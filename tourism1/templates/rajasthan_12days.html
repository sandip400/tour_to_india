Explore the Wonders of Rajasthan

A 12-day journey through royal palaces, majestic forts, and vibrant culture

Rajasthan: The Land of Kings

Rajasthan offers a perfect blend of royal heritage, vibrant culture, and breathtaking landscapes. From the pink city of Jaipur to the golden sands of Jaisalmer, this 12-day comprehensive itinerary covers the best of what this colorful northwestern Indian state has to offer.

Highlights of the Tour

Magnificent forts and palaces
Authentic Rajasthani cuisine
Thar Desert and camel safari
Wildlife safari in Ranthambore
Traditional arts and handicrafts
Folk music and cultural performances

Quick Facts

  • Duration: 12 Days
  • Starting Point: Jaipur
  • Ending Point: Jaipur
  • Languages: Hindi, Rajasthani, English
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹)

Day 1

Arrival in Jaipur & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Jaipur International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in the city center. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Jai Mahal Palace or ITC Rajputana for luxury stays; Dera Rawatsar or Umaid Bhawan for heritage stays; Hotel Diggi Palace or Jas Vilas for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Albert Hall Museum

Enjoy your first Rajasthani meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Spice Court' or 'Niros'. Visit the Albert Hall Museum, the oldest museum in Rajasthan, housing a rich collection of artifacts including paintings, jewelry, carpets, and sculptures.

Lunch recommendation: Try Dal Baati Churma, a traditional Rajasthani dish consisting of baked wheat balls served with lentil curry and sweetened cereal.

Evening: Johri Bazaar & Bapu Bazaar

Explore the vibrant markets of Jaipur, known for textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts. Johri Bazaar is famous for its jewelry shops, while Bapu Bazaar offers traditional Rajasthani textiles, juttis (footwear), and souvenirs.

Evening activity: Enjoy a traditional Rajasthani dinner at Chokhi Dhani, a village-themed resort that offers authentic cuisine along with folk music and dance performances.

Albert Hall Museum

Johri Bazaar

Day 1 Tips
  • Jaipur is known as the "Pink City" due to the distinctive color of its buildings.
  • Bargaining is expected in the markets, so don't hesitate to negotiate prices.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Wear comfortable footwear as you'll be doing a lot of walking in the markets.
Day 2

Jaipur Forts & Palaces

Morning: Amber Fort

Visit the magnificent Amber Fort, located 11 km from Jaipur. This majestic fort-palace complex is built from pale yellow and pink sandstone and white marble. Explore its ornate halls, courtyards, and the stunning Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace).

Activity: Take an elephant ride up to the fort entrance (subject to availability) or opt for a jeep ride.

Afternoon: Jal Mahal & Lunch

On your way back to the city, stop at Jal Mahal (Water Palace), a palace located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in the city.

Lunch: Try Rajasthani thali at LMB (Laxmi Misthan Bhandar) or Handi Restaurant for an authentic culinary experience.

Evening: City Palace & Jantar Mantar

Visit the City Palace, a complex of courtyards, gardens, and buildings in the heart of Jaipur. Continue to Jantar Mantar, an astronomical observation site built in the early 18th century, featuring the world's largest stone sundial.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at 1135 AD, a royal dining experience inside Amber Fort, or at Peacock Rooftop Restaurant for panoramic city views.

Day 3

Jaipur to Pushkar

Morning: Hawa Mahal

Visit Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), a five-story palace with a unique honeycomb facade of 953 small windows. This architectural marvel was designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals while remaining unseen.

Activity: Visit early morning for the best photographs as the sun illuminates the pink sandstone structure.

Afternoon: Drive to Pushkar

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Pushkar (approximately 2.5 hours, 150 km), a sacred town built around a holy lake. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Pushkar Palace or Pushkar Resorts for luxury; Hotel Mama Luna or Inn Seventh Heaven for mid-range options.

Evening: Pushkar Lake & Ghats

Explore the sacred Pushkar Lake surrounded by 52 bathing ghats where pilgrims come to take a holy dip. Walk around the lake and visit some of the numerous temples that dot its perimeter.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Sunset Cafe or La Pizzeria for a mix of Indian and international cuisine with lake views.

Hawa Mahal

Pushkar Lake

About Pushkar

Pushkar is one of the oldest cities in India and a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus. The town is built around a sacred lake said to have been created by Lord Brahma. It houses one of the few Brahma temples in the world.

Besides its religious significance, Pushkar is known for its annual camel fair (held in November), vibrant markets, and laid-back atmosphere that attracts both pilgrims and travelers seeking spiritual experiences.

Cultural Advisory

Pushkar is a sacred town, so please respect local customs. Alcohol and non-vegetarian food are prohibited within the town limits. Dress modestly, especially around the lake and temples. Remove shoes before entering temples.

Day 4

Pushkar to Jodhpur

Morning: Brahma Temple & Pushkar Market

Visit the Brahma Temple, one of the very few temples in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma. Explore the colorful Pushkar Market, known for its textiles, silver jewelry, leather goods, and handicrafts.

Activity: Take a short hike up to Savitri Temple for panoramic views of Pushkar and the surrounding Aravalli Hills.

Afternoon: Drive to Jodhpur

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Jodhpur (approximately 4-5 hours, 200 km), known as the "Blue City" for its blue-painted houses. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Umaid Bhawan Palace or Taj Hari Mahal for luxury; Ratan Vilas or Pal Haveli for heritage stays; Zostel Jodhpur or Moustache Hostel for budget options.

Evening: Clock Tower & Sardar Market

Visit the Clock Tower and explore the bustling Sardar Market surrounding it. This vibrant market is perfect for shopping for spices, textiles, silver jewelry, and handicrafts.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Indique or On The Rocks for rooftop dining with views of Mehrangarh Fort.

Brahma Temple

Jodhpur Blue City

About Jodhpur

Jodhpur, the second largest city in Rajasthan, was founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, chief of the Rathore clan. The city is known for its blue-painted houses, which were traditionally homes of Brahmins but now extend throughout the old city.

Dominated by the massive Mehrangarh Fort that towers over the city, Jodhpur offers a blend of modern urban infrastructure and old-world charm. The city is also known for its furniture industry, handicrafts, glass bangles, and textiles.

Day 5

Jodhpur Exploration

Morning: Mehrangarh Fort

Visit the imposing Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India, perched 400 feet above the city. Explore its palaces, courtyards, and museums housing royal palanquins, arms, paintings, and textiles.

Activity: Don't miss the panoramic views of the blue city from the fort ramparts. Consider taking an audio guide for detailed historical insights.

Afternoon: Jaswant Thada & Umaid Bhawan Palace

Visit Jaswant Thada, a marble memorial built in 1899 in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II. Continue to Umaid Bhawan Palace, part of which is a luxury hotel while another section houses a museum displaying royal artifacts.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Cafe Mehran near Mehrangarh Fort or at Jhankar Choti Haveli for traditional Rajasthani cuisine.

Evening: Blue City Walking Tour

Take a guided walking tour through the narrow lanes of the old Blue City. Explore the local lifestyle, architecture, and hidden gems that are not accessible by vehicle.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Indigo Restaurant or Darikhana for authentic Rajasthani cuisine with a modern twist.

Day 6

Jodhpur to Jaisalmer

Morning: Bishnoi Village Safari

Take a jeep safari to the Bishnoi villages around Jodhpur. The Bishnoi community is known for their deep reverence for nature and wildlife conservation. Witness their traditional lifestyle, crafts, and ceremonies.

Activity: Interact with local artisans and witness pottery making, weaving, and block printing demonstrations.

Afternoon: Drive to Jaisalmer

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Jaisalmer (approximately 4-5 hours, 285 km), known as the "Golden City" due to its yellow sandstone architecture. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Suryagarh or The Gulaal for luxury; Hotel Killa Bhawan or Hotel Pleasant Haveli for heritage stays; Zostel Jaisalmer or Moustache Hostel for budget options.

Evening: Gadisar Lake

Visit Gadisar Lake, an artificial reservoir built in the 14th century that once supplied water to the entire city. Enjoy a peaceful boat ride on the lake and watch the sunset.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Trio or The Trio Restaurant for authentic Rajasthani cuisine with rooftop views of the fort.

Bishnoi Village

Gadisar Lake

About Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer, founded in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal, is a city in the heart of the Thar Desert. Known as the "Golden City" due to its yellow sandstone architecture, it's dominated by the Jaisalmer Fort, a living fort where about a quarter of the city's population still resides.

The city is renowned for its intricate architecture, ornate havelis (mansions), desert landscapes, and vibrant culture. Jaisalmer's proximity to the Pakistan border has made it an important center for military presence.

Day 7

Jaisalmer Fort & Desert Safari

Morning: Jaisalmer Fort

Explore Jaisalmer Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest fully preserved fortified cities in the world. Visit Raj Mahal (Royal Palace), Jain temples, and walk through the narrow lanes filled with shops, restaurants, and homes.

Activity: Visit Patwon Ki Haveli, the most elaborate and magnificent haveli in Jaisalmer, known for its intricate carvings and mirror work.

Afternoon: Sam Sand Dunes

Drive to Sam Sand Dunes (approximately 45 minutes, 45 km from Jaisalmer) for a desert safari experience. Enjoy a camel ride across the dunes and witness the spectacular sunset over the Thar Desert.

Note: Wear comfortable clothing and carry sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat for protection from the desert sun.

Evening: Desert Camp & Cultural Program

Enjoy a cultural evening at a desert camp with traditional Rajasthani folk music and dance performances. Experience authentic Rajasthani cuisine under the starlit sky.

Option: You can choose to stay overnight at a desert camp for a complete desert experience or return to your hotel in Jaisalmer.

Jaisalmer Fort

Desert Safari Options

There are several types of desert safaris available in Jaisalmer:

  • Camel Safari: Traditional way to explore the desert, ranging from 1-hour rides to multi-day expeditions
  • Jeep Safari: Faster and more comfortable option for covering larger areas of the desert
  • Quad Biking: For adventure enthusiasts looking for an adrenaline rush
  • Desert Camping: Overnight stay in Swiss tents or mud huts with cultural programs and meals

Prices vary depending on the duration and inclusions. Most desert camps offer packages that include transportation, camel rides, cultural programs, dinner, and accommodation if staying overnight.

Day 8

Jaisalmer to Bikaner

Morning: Abandoned Villages

Visit the abandoned villages of Kuldhara and Khaba, located about 20 km from Jaisalmer. These villages were mysteriously abandoned in the 19th century and are now considered haunted by locals.

Activity: Explore the ruins and learn about the legends and stories associated with these ghost villages.

Afternoon: Drive to Bikaner

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Bikaner (approximately 5-6 hours, 330 km), known for its magnificent forts, palaces, and camel breeding farm. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Narendra Bhawan or Laxmi Niwas Palace for luxury; Hotel Jaswant Bhawan or Hotel Harasar Haveli for mid-range options.

Evening: Local Cuisine Exploration

Explore the local cuisine of Bikaner, famous for its savory snacks and sweets. Visit the old city area to sample traditional Bikaneri bhujia, kachori, and sweets.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Gallops Restaurant or Hotel Bhairon Vilas for authentic Rajasthani cuisine.

Kuldhara Village

Bikaner City

About Bikaner

Bikaner was founded in 1488 by Rao Bika, a Rajput prince. The city is known for its imposing forts and palaces, built of red and yellow sandstone. Bikaner's strategic position on the ancient caravan routes made it a prominent trading center.

The city is famous for its camel breeding farm, the largest in Asia, as well as its savory snacks, especially Bikaneri bhujia. The old city is surrounded by a wall with five gates and has narrow streets lined with havelis and temples.

Day 9

Bikaner to Udaipur

Morning: Junagarh Fort & Camel Breeding Farm

Visit Junagarh Fort, one of the few major forts in Rajasthan that is not built on a hilltop. Explore its palaces, temples, and pavilions. Continue to the National Research Centre on Camel (Camel Breeding Farm) to learn about camel breeding and research.

Activity: Watch camel milk processing and sample camel milk products if available.

Afternoon: Drive to Udaipur

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Udaipur (approximately 7-8 hours, 400 km), known as the "City of Lakes" and the "Venice of the East." Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Taj Lake Palace or The Oberoi Udaivilas for luxury; Jagat Niwas Palace or Lake Pichola Hotel for heritage stays with lake views.

Evening: Lake Pichola

Relax and enjoy the serene views of Lake Pichola. If time permits, take a boat ride on the lake to see the City Palace, Jag Mandir, and other landmarks from the water.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Ambrai Restaurant or Tribute Restaurant for lakeside dining with views of the illuminated City Palace.

Junagarh Fort

Lake Pichola

About Udaipur

Udaipur, founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, is often called the "City of Lakes" due to its sophisticated lake system. The city served as the capital of the Mewar Kingdom and is known for its palaces, temples, havelis, and narrow, colorful streets.

Surrounded by the Aravalli Range, Udaipur is known for its natural beauty, historic architecture, and cultural heritage. The city has been a popular filming location, most notably for the James Bond film "Octopussy" and has been ranked as one of the most romantic cities in India.

Day 10

Udaipur Exploration

Morning: City Palace & Jagdish Temple

Visit the magnificent City Palace, a complex of palaces built over 400 years, showcasing the fusion of Rajasthani and Mughal architectural styles. Continue to Jagdish Temple, a large Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its intricate carvings.

Activity: Take a guided tour of the City Palace Museum to learn about the history and culture of Mewar.

Afternoon: Saheliyon Ki Bari & Fateh Sagar Lake

Visit Saheliyon Ki Bari (Garden of the Maidens), a beautiful garden with fountains, kiosks, marble elephants, and a lotus pool. Continue to Fateh Sagar Lake for a boat ride and visit the Maharana Pratap Memorial on Moti Magri hill.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Savage Garden or Upre by 1559 AD for panoramic views of the city.

Evening: Cultural Performance

Attend a cultural performance at Bagore Ki Haveli, showcasing traditional Rajasthani folk dance and music, including the famous puppet show and Dharohar dance.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Jagat Niwas Palace Restaurant or Raas Leela for authentic Rajasthani cuisine with lake views.

Day 11

Udaipur to Ranthambore

Morning: Monsoon Palace

Visit Monsoon Palace (Sajjangarh Palace), perched on a hilltop overlooking Fateh Sagar Lake. This palace offers panoramic views of the city, lakes, and surrounding countryside.

Activity: Enjoy the scenic drive up to the palace and take in the breathtaking views of Udaipur from this vantage point.

Afternoon: Drive to Ranthambore

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Ranthambore (approximately 6-7 hours, 400 km), home to one of India's largest tiger reserves. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Oberoi Vanyavilas or Taj Sawai Madhopur Lodge for luxury; Ranthambore Regency or Tiger Den Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: Rest & Relaxation

After the long drive, spend the evening relaxing at your hotel. Get briefed about the next day's safari and prepare for an early morning start.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel's restaurant, which typically serves a mix of Indian and international cuisine.

Monsoon Palace

Ranthambore National Park

About Ranthambore

Ranthambore National Park is one of the largest and most famous national parks in northern India. The park was a former hunting ground of the Maharajas of Jaipur and was declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1957 and a national park in 1981.

The park is known for its population of Bengal tigers and is one of the best places in India to see these majestic cats in their natural habitat. Besides tigers, the park is home to leopards, nilgai, wild boar, sambar, hyena, sloth bear, and various bird species.

Safari Tips

For the best wildlife viewing experience at Ranthambore:

  • Morning safaris (6:00 AM - 9:30 AM) typically offer better sightings
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing (avoid bright colors)
  • Bring binoculars and a camera with zoom lens
  • Maintain silence during the safari to avoid disturbing wildlife
  • Follow all instructions given by your guide and driver
Day 12

Ranthambore to Jaipur & Departure

Morning: Wildlife Safari

Wake up early for a morning safari in Ranthambore National Park. Explore the diverse wildlife, including the majestic Bengal tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and various deer species. The park also houses the impressive Ranthambore Fort, which offers a historical backdrop to your wildlife adventure.

Activity: Keep your camera ready for wildlife photography, but remember to enjoy the experience beyond the lens as well.

Afternoon: Return to Jaipur

After breakfast and check-out, drive back to Jaipur (approximately 3-4 hours, 180 km). Depending on your departure time, you may check into a hotel for a short rest or head directly to the airport.

En route: Reflect on the diverse experiences of your Rajasthan journey, from royal palaces to desert landscapes and wildlife.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Jaipur for souvenirs like block-printed textiles, blue pottery, gemstone jewelry, or Rajasthani miniature paintings.

Departure: Transfer to Jaipur International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Winter months with pleasant temperatures ideal for sightseeing
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional rainfall, offering lush green landscapes
  • Avoid April to June: Summer months with extremely high temperatures (often exceeding 40°C/104°F)
  • For wildlife at Ranthambore: October to June, with peak tiger sightings in March to May
  • For festivals: Diwali (Oct/Nov), Pushkar Camel Fair (Nov), Desert Festival in Jaisalmer (Jan/Feb)

Transportation

  • Air: International airports in Jaipur and Udaipur with good connectivity
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Road: Well-maintained highways and state roads
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Recommended: Hire a driver for the entire trip for convenience and flexibility
  • Special: Camel safaris in Jaisalmer, boat rides in Udaipur, jeep safaris in Ranthambore

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for summer, warm layers for winter nights
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for fort visits
  • Sun protection: Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, scarf
  • For desert areas: Moisturizer, lip balm, wet wipes
  • For wildlife safari: Binoculars, zoom lens camera, neutral-colored clothing
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, water purification tablets
  • Other: Power bank, universal adapter, water bottle

Food & Cuisine

Rajasthan offers a rich culinary experience with dishes that are flavorful and often spicy:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Dal Baati Churma (baked wheat balls with lentil curry)
  • Gatte ki Sabzi (gram flour dumplings in yogurt curry)
  • Laal Maas (spicy mutton curry)
  • Ker Sangri (desert beans and berries)
  • Pyaaz Kachori (onion-filled pastry)
  • Bajre ki Roti (millet bread)
Desserts & Snacks
  • Ghewar (disc-shaped sweet cake)
  • Mawa Kachori (sweet dumpling)
  • Dil Khushal (stuffed pastry)
  • Bikaneri Bhujia (spicy snack)
  • Mirchi Bada (chili fritters)
  • Lassi (yogurt drink)

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi and Rajasthani are primary languages. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote desert areas
  • Bargaining: Expected in markets and with auto-rickshaw drivers
  • Dress code: Conservative dress is appreciated, especially at religious sites
  • Photography: May be restricted or require fees at certain monuments
  • Water: Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
