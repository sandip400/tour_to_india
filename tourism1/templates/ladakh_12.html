12-Day Ladakh Adventure

Discover the breathtaking landscapes and vibrant culture of Ladakh

About This Itinerary

Ladakh, known as "The Land of High Passes," is a region in northern India that's famous for its remote mountain beauty, Buddhist monasteries, and unique culture. This 12-day itinerary is designed to help you acclimatize properly to the high altitude while experiencing the best Ladakh has to offer—from ancient monasteries and pristine lakes to dramatic landscapes and local experiences.

This journey takes you through Leh, Nubra Valley, Pangong Lake, and Tso Moriri, with carefully planned days to help you adjust to the altitude and enjoy this Himalayan wonderland safely.

1Arrive in Leh (3,500m) - Rest & Acclimatization

Morning

Arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh. The flight into Leh offers stunning aerial views of the Himalayan range. Remember that Leh sits at 3,500 meters (11,500 ft), so acclimatization is essential.

Afternoon & Evening

Complete check-in at your hotel and spend the entire day resting. Drink plenty of water and avoid any physical exertion. You might experience mild altitude sickness symptoms which should subside with rest.

Day 1 Tips:

  • Book a morning flight to Leh to give yourself maximum time to rest on arrival day
  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol for better acclimatization
  • Avoid sleeping during the daytime
  • Take altitude sickness medication if prescribed by your doctor

2Leh - Gentle Exploration

Morning

Continue slow acclimatization with a late breakfast at your hotel. Take a gentle walk around Leh Main Bazaar, exploring the local shops and handicrafts. Visit Leh Palace, which overlooks the town.

Afternoon

Have lunch at a local restaurant in Leh Market. Visit the Shanti Stupa - a beautiful white-domed stupa on a hilltop that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and Leh town.

Evening

Return to your hotel for dinner. Continue hydrating and resting well.

Day 2 Tips:

  • Walk slowly and avoid climbing too many steps at once
  • Continue drinking plenty of water
  • Apply sunscreen as UV radiation is intense at high altitudes
  • Try local butter tea (po cha) which can help with acclimatization

3Leh Monastery Tour

Morning

After breakfast, visit Thiksey Monastery, one of the most beautiful monasteries resembling Potala Palace in Tibet. Explore the prayer rooms, temples, and the giant Buddha statue. Then proceed to Hemis Monastery, the largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh.

Afternoon

After lunch, visit Shey Palace and Monastery, the former summer palace of the kings of Ladakh. Continue to Stok Palace Museum, which houses royal artifacts and the queen's crown.

Evening

Return to Leh by early evening. Explore Leh Market for souvenir shopping.

Day 3 Tips:

  • Dress modestly when visiting monasteries (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Ask permission before photographing monks or prayer halls
  • Carry small change for donations at monasteries
  • Respect local customs and remove shoes when entering temples

4Leh to Nubra Valley via Khardung La (5,359m)

Morning

After breakfast, begin your journey to Nubra Valley via Khardung La, one of the world's highest motorable passes at 5,359m (17,582 ft). Take photographs and spend a short time at the pass before descending to lower altitude.

Afternoon

Arrive in Diskit, the capital of Nubra Valley. Visit the impressive Diskit Monastery and the 32-meter tall Maitreya Buddha statue that overlooks the Shyok River valley.

Evening

Check into your accommodation in Nubra Valley (Diskit/Hunder). Enjoy the beautiful views of the valley.

Day 4 Tips:

  • Start early as permits are checked along the way
  • Spend minimal time at Khardung La to avoid altitude sickness
  • Carry warm clothes as it can be very cold at the pass
  • Pack motion sickness pills if you're susceptible to mountain roads

5Nubra Valley Exploration

Morning

After breakfast, visit the Hunder Sand Dunes, a unique desert landscape between mountains. Take a short Bactrian (double-humped) camel ride, a reminder of the ancient Silk Route that passed through this region.

Afternoon

Drive to Panamik village, known for its hot springs which are believed to have medicinal properties. Visit the Yarab Tso Lake (hidden lake) near Sumur village.

Evening

Return to your accommodation in Nubra Valley. Enjoy dinner under the starlit sky.

Day 5 Tips:

  • Carry cash as ATMs are scarce in Nubra Valley
  • Apply sunscreen as the reflection from sand can cause sunburn
  • Respect the local Balti and Ladakhi culture
  • Consider bringing a swimsuit if you want to bathe in the hot springs at Panamik

6Nubra Valley to Pangong Lake (4,350m)

Morning

After breakfast, check out and start your journey to Pangong Lake via the Shyok River route (if open) or via the longer Leh route depending on road conditions.

Afternoon

If taking the Shyok route, reach Pangong Lake by early afternoon. Marvel at the changing colors of this saltwater lake stretching from India to Tibet. The lake famously appeared in the movie "3 Idiots" and is known for its spectacular blue waters.

Evening

Check into your accommodation near the lake (Spangmik, Man, or Merak villages). Enjoy the sunset over the mountains reflecting in the crystal-clear blue waters of Pangong.

Day 6 Tips:

  • The Shyok route can be risky during monsoons due to water crossings
  • Accommodations at Pangong are mostly tented camps with basic facilities
  • Nights get very cold even in summer—pack warm clothes
  • Charging facilities may be limited, so carry power banks

7Pangong Lake to Leh via Chang La (5,360m)

Morning

Wake up early to witness the spectacular sunrise over Pangong Lake. After breakfast, begin your journey back to Leh via Chang La Pass, another high-altitude mountain pass at 5,360m (17,590 ft).

Afternoon

On the way, visit Thiksey Monastery if you didn't visit on Day 3. Alternatively, stop at Sindhu Ghat where you can see the mighty Indus River flowing peacefully.

Evening

Reach Leh by evening. Rest and recover at your hotel. Enjoy dinner at one of Leh's popular restaurants.

Day 7 Tips:

  • Start early to avoid traffic at Chang La pass
  • Visit the Chang La Baba Temple at the pass
  • Make a stop at Harong Wetlands to spot migratory birds
  • Drink hot ginger tea at Chang La to combat the cold

8Leh to Tso Moriri Lake (4,530m)

Morning

After breakfast, begin your journey to Tso Moriri Lake in the Changthang region. Drive through the scenic Indus Valley and cross Chumathang, known for its hot springs.

Afternoon

Visit Tso Kar (a smaller salt lake) on the way if time permits. Continue to Tso Moriri, a high-altitude mountain lake in the Changthang Plateau. This region is home to nomadic tribes and unique wildlife including kiangs (Tibetan wild ass).

Evening

Check into your accommodation at Korzok village near Tso Moriri. Enjoy the serene surroundings and spectacular lake views.

Day 8 Tips:

  • The journey to Tso Moriri is long, so start early
  • Permits are checked at multiple points, keep them handy
  • Accommodation options are very limited—mostly basic homestays or tented camps
  • Nights are extremely cold at Tso Moriri even in summer

9Tso Moriri to Leh

Morning

Wake up early to catch the beautiful sunrise over Tso Moriri. After breakfast, visit the Korzok Monastery, one of the highest situated Buddhist monasteries in the world.

Afternoon

Begin your return journey to Leh. Take a different route if possible, via Taglang La pass (5,328m) and the More Plains, to experience diverse landscapes.

Evening

Arrive in Leh by evening. Rest at your hotel after the long drive.

Day 9 Tips:

  • Take time to observe the unique birdlife around Tso Moriri in the morning
  • Look out for marmots and pikas on the More Plains
  • The road conditions can be challenging—ensure your vehicle is in good condition
  • Carry extra snacks as there are few food options along this route

10Sham Valley Tour - The Lower Indus Valley

Morning

After breakfast, embark on a tour of the Sham Valley, also known as "Apricot Valley." First, visit the Magnetic Hill, an optical illusion where vehicles appear to roll uphill. Continue to Sangam (confluence) where the Indus and Zanskar rivers meet.

Afternoon

Visit the Alchi Monastery complex, dating back to the 11th century and known for its unique Kashmiri-influenced Buddhist art. Next, visit the Likir Monastery which houses a 75 ft tall golden Maitreya Buddha statue.

Evening

On the return journey, stop at Basgo Castle and Monastery, once an important political and cultural center. Return to Leh by evening.

Day 10 Tips:

  • The Sham Valley is at a lower altitude and perfect for further acclimatization
  • Alchi is unique as one of the few monasteries on flat ground in Ladakh
  • Try fresh apricots from the valley if in season (July-August)
  • Consider visiting the Nimmu village market for local produce

11Leh - Cultural Immersion Day

Morning

After breakfast, visit the Central Asian Museum in Leh to learn about the region's rich trading history on the Silk Route. Stop by the Ecology Centre to understand Ladakh's unique ecosystem and conservation efforts.

Afternoon

Participate in a cooking class to learn traditional Ladakhi dishes like thukpa (noodle soup), momos (dumplings), and skyu (pasta with vegetables). Alternatively, visit local handicraft centers where you can observe traditional weaving and painting.

Evening

Enjoy cultural performances featuring traditional Ladakhi dance and music at a local cultural center or your hotel. Have a farewell dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 11 Tips:

  • Shop for souvenirs like prayer flags, thangkas (Buddhist paintings), and pashmina shawls
  • Visit the Tibetan Refugee Market for authentic crafts
  • Consider donating to local conservation or cultural preservation projects
  • Exchange contact details with new local friends you've made

12Departure from Leh

Morning

Depending on your flight time, you might have a few hours for last-minute shopping or relaxation. Check out from your hotel and transfer to Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport for your departure flight.

Day 12 Tips:

  • Flights from Leh are frequently delayed or cancelled due to weather conditions, so keep buffer days if possible
  • Reach the airport at least 2 hours before departure as security procedures are stringent
  • Some hotels offer late checkout—check in advance if you have an afternoon flight
  • Keep important medications in your carry-on luggage

Essential Information for Ladakh

  • Best Time to Visit: May to September (summer months) when mountain passes are open
  • Inner Line Permits: Required for visiting restricted areas like Nubra Valley, Pangong Lake, and Tso Moriri
  • Altitude Considerations: Ladakh's high altitude requires proper acclimatization—follow the itinerary's rest days
  • What to Pack: Warm clothes (even in summer), sun protection, moisturizer, lip balm, good walking shoes, altitude sickness medication
  • Connectivity: Mobile and internet connectivity is limited outside Leh; BSNL works best in remote areas
  • Electricity: Power cuts are common; carry a power bank
  • Health: No specific vaccinations are required, but consult a travel doctor before your trip

This 12-day Ladakh itinerary is designed to help you experience the region's highlights while allowing for proper acclimatization. Adjust as needed based on your interests and travel style. Julley! (Hello/Goodbye in Ladakhi)
