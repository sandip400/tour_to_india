Kashmir: Heaven on Earth

Kashmir offers a mesmerizing blend of snow-capped mountains, pristine lakes, lush meadows, and rich cultural heritage. From the serene Dal Lake in Srinagar to the majestic mountains of Gulmarg, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northern Indian paradise has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Srinagar & Houseboat Check-in

Morning: Arrival & Transfer

Arrive at Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport, Srinagar. Meet your driver and transfer to the iconic Dal Lake. Check into your traditional Kashmiri houseboat, a unique floating accommodation experience.

Accommodation: Luxury houseboats like Butt's Clermont Houseboats or Sukoon Houseboat; mid-range options include New Jacqueline Heritage Houseboats.

Afternoon: Lunch & Shikara Ride

Enjoy your first Kashmiri meal on the houseboat. Experience a relaxing Shikara (traditional wooden boat) ride on Dal Lake, visiting the floating gardens and the local floating market.

Lunch recommendation: Try Kashmiri specialties like Rogan Josh (lamb curry), Dum Aloo (potato curry), and Kahwa (traditional green tea).

Evening: Sunset on Dal Lake

Witness the magical sunset over Dal Lake from your houseboat. Enjoy a traditional Kashmiri dinner on the houseboat, often accompanied by live traditional music.

Evening activity: Stroll along the Boulevard Road that runs alongside Dal Lake, offering beautiful views and shopping opportunities.

Shikara Ride

Day 1 Tips
  • Bargain politely for your Shikara ride if not included in your package.
  • Carry a light jacket as evenings can be cool even in summer.
  • Most houseboats provide meals, but confirm in advance.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly.
Day 2

Srinagar Mughal Gardens Tour

Morning: Nishat & Shalimar Gardens

After breakfast, visit the famous Mughal Gardens. Start with Nishat Bagh (Garden of Joy), the second largest Mughal garden in Kashmir. Continue to Shalimar Bagh (Abode of Love), built by Emperor Jahangir for his wife Nur Jahan.

Activity: Enjoy the terraced gardens with fountains and panoramic views of Dal Lake and surrounding mountains.

Afternoon: Chashme Shahi & Pari Mahal

Visit Chashme Shahi (Royal Spring), known for its fresh water spring. Continue to Pari Mahal (Fairy Palace), a terraced garden with beautiful architecture and panoramic views of Srinagar city.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Wazwan meal at Ahdoos or Mughal Darbar restaurants in the city.

Evening: Old City Tour

Explore the old city of Srinagar. Visit Jamia Masjid, one of the largest mosques in Kashmir with unique Indo-Saracenic architecture. Continue to Khanqah-e-Moula, a wooden shrine dedicated to Mir Syed Ali Hamdani.

Shopping: Visit the local markets for traditional Kashmiri handicrafts like Pashmina shawls, carpets, and walnut wood carvings.

Day 3

Srinagar to Gulmarg

Morning: Drive to Gulmarg

After breakfast, check out from your houseboat and drive to Gulmarg (approximately 2 hours). Enjoy the scenic drive through pine forests and picturesque villages. Gulmarg, known as the "Meadow of Flowers," is a stunning hill station at an altitude of 2,650 meters.

En route: Stop at a few viewpoints for photography and at Tangmarg for a tea break.

Afternoon: Check-in & Gondola Ride

Check into your hotel in Gulmarg. After lunch, experience the famous Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world. The ride is divided into two phases - first phase takes you to Kongdori and the second phase goes up to Apharwat Peak at 4,200 meters.

Accommodation: The Khyber Himalayan Resort & Spa for luxury; Hotel Highlands Park or Pine Palace for mid-range options.

Evening: Explore Gulmarg

Explore the beautiful meadows of Gulmarg. Visit the St. Mary's Church, a Victorian architectural marvel built in 1902. In winter, enjoy skiing (Gulmarg is India's premier ski destination); in summer, try activities like golf at the world's highest golf course, horse riding, or simply stroll around the meadows.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel, many of which offer excellent Kashmiri cuisine.

Gulmarg Gondola with Snow-capped Mountains

Important Information
  • Book Gondola tickets in advance to avoid long queues, especially in peak season.
  • Carry warm clothes even in summer as temperatures can drop significantly at higher altitudes.
  • The second phase of the Gondola may be closed during bad weather.
  • Oxygen levels are lower at Apharwat Peak, so move slowly if you're not accustomed to high altitudes.
  • Carry sunscreen and sunglasses as UV radiation is stronger at high altitudes.
Seasonal Activities

Winter (Dec-Mar): Skiing, snowboarding, snow sledding

Summer (Apr-Oct): Golf, trekking, horse riding, mountain biking

Gulmarg transforms completely with the seasons, offering unique experiences year-round!

Day 4

Gulmarg to Pahalgam

Morning: Gulmarg Activities

Enjoy a morning walk around Gulmarg's meadows. Visit Strawberry Valley and enjoy the beautiful landscape. Depending on the season, you can go for a short trek or try other activities like ATV rides.

Activity: Visit the Maharani Temple and enjoy panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Afternoon: Drive to Pahalgam

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Pahalgam (approximately 3-4 hours). The journey takes you through beautiful landscapes, saffron fields (in season), and traditional Kashmiri villages.

En route: Stop at Avantipur Ruins, ancient temple ruins dating back to the 9th century, dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Evening: Arrival in Pahalgam

Arrive in Pahalgam, known as the "Valley of Shepherds," situated at the confluence of the Lidder River and Sheshnag Lake. Check into your hotel and relax. Take an evening stroll along the Lidder River.

Accommodation: Pahalgam Hotel, The Pine & Peak, or Kolahoi Green Resort for luxury; Hotel Mount View or Hotel Heevan Pahalgam for mid-range options.

Day 5

Pahalgam Exploration

Morning: Aru Valley

After breakfast, visit Aru Valley (15 km from Pahalgam), a picturesque meadow surrounded by mountains. Enjoy horse riding or a short trek in the valley. The starting point of the famous Kolahoi Glacier trek is located here.

Activity: Photography, horse riding, or simply enjoying the serene environment.

Afternoon: Betaab Valley

Visit Betaab Valley, named after the Bollywood movie "Betaab" which was shot here. The valley is surrounded by pine forests and snow-capped mountains with a beautiful stream flowing through it.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at one of the small restaurants in Pahalgam market.

Evening: Chandanwari & Lidder River

Visit Chandanwari (16 km from Pahalgam), the starting point of the Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage. Enjoy the beautiful views of the Lidder River and surrounding mountains. Return to Pahalgam and spend the evening relaxing by the river or exploring the local market.

Shopping: Look for Kashmiri handicrafts, especially embroidered items and traditional Kashmiri Pherans (traditional outerwear).

Day 6

Pahalgam to Sonamarg

Morning: Drive to Sonamarg

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Sonamarg (approximately 3-4 hours). Sonamarg, meaning "Meadow of Gold," is a stunning valley located at an altitude of 2,800 meters, known for its snow-covered mountains and beautiful meadows.

En route: Enjoy the scenic drive through the Kashmir Valley, with stops at viewpoints for photography.

Afternoon: Check-in & Local Exploration

Check into your hotel in Sonamarg. After lunch, explore the beautiful meadows and surroundings of Sonamarg. Visit the Thajiwas Glacier, located just 3 km from Sonamarg. You can either walk or take a pony ride to reach the glacier.

Accommodation: The Villa Himalaya, Sonamarg; Snowland Hotel; or Barzman Hotel for comfortable stays.

Evening: Sindh River

Spend the evening by the Sindh River that flows through Sonamarg. Enjoy the beautiful sunset views over the mountains. Return to your hotel for dinner and overnight stay.

Activity: Photography, riverside walk, or simply relaxing in the serene environment.

About Sonamarg

Sonamarg, also known as the "Meadow of Gold," is a beautiful hill station located in the Ganderbal district of Jammu and Kashmir. It serves as the gateway to Ladakh and is surrounded by majestic glaciers and serene lakes. The Sindh River flows through the valley, adding to its picturesque beauty.

Elevation: 2,800 meters (9,186 ft)
Cool climate even in summer
Popular for trekking and glacier visits
Day 7

Sonamarg to Srinagar & Departure

Morning: Sonamarg Exploration

Enjoy an early morning walk in Sonamarg to soak in the beautiful surroundings one last time. Visit Baltal, the base camp for Amarnath Yatra via the Baltal route, located about 15 km from Sonamarg.

Activity: Short nature walk or photography session before departure.

Afternoon: Return to Srinagar

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive back to Srinagar (approximately 2-3 hours). On arrival, you can visit any places you missed earlier or do some last-minute shopping.

Shopping: Visit the local markets in Srinagar like Lal Chowk or the handicraft emporiums for souvenirs.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport for your departure flight. If your flight is the next day, you can stay overnight in a hotel or houseboat in Srinagar.

Note: Airport security in Srinagar is stringent. Arrive at least 3 hours before your flight departure time.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Blooming flowers, pleasant weather
  • Summer (June-August): Peak tourist season, ideal for all activities
  • Autumn (September-November): Golden landscapes, fewer tourists
  • Winter (December-February): Heavy snowfall, perfect for snow activities
  • Avoid visiting during heavy rainfall (typically late July-August)

Transportation

  • Air: Direct flights to Srinagar from major Indian cities
  • Road: Well-connected by road from Jammu and other northern cities
  • Local Transport: Taxis, shared cabs, and auto-rickshaws in Srinagar
  • Shikara: Traditional boats for transportation on Dal Lake
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers for varying temperatures; modest attire recommended
  • Winter: Heavy woolens, thermals, gloves, mufflers, snow boots
  • Summer: Light woolens for evenings, comfortable walking shoes
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, lip balm
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Kashmir offers a unique culinary experience with influences from Central Asian, Persian, and Indian cuisines:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Wazwan (multi-course meal)
  • Rogan Josh (lamb curry)
  • Yakhni (yogurt-based curry)
  • Dum Aloo (potato curry)
  • Modur Pulao (sweet rice)
  • Kahwa (saffron tea)
Dining Recommendations
  • Ahdoos Restaurant (Srinagar)
  • Mughal Darbar (Srinagar)
  • Shamyana Restaurant (Gulmarg)
  • Dana Pani Restaurant (Pahalgam)
  • Houseboat dining experiences
  • Local bakeries for Kashmiri breads

Practical Information

  • Language: Kashmiri, Urdu, and Hindi are commonly spoken. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities but may be limited in remote areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Check current travel advisories before planning your trip
  • Local Customs: Respect local traditions and dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Bargaining: Polite bargaining is expected in local markets, especially for handicrafts
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
