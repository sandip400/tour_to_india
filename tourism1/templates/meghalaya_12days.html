Meghalaya: The Abode of Clouds

Meghalaya offers a perfect blend of natural wonders, living traditions, and unique landscapes. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary covers not just the popular destinations but also hidden gems of this northeastern Indian state, allowing you to fully immerse in its pristine beauty and rich culture.

Authentic Northeastern cuisine

Day 1

Arrival in Guwahati & Drive to Shillong

Morning: Arrival at Guwahati

Arrive at Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, Assam. Meet your driver and prepare for the journey to Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya.

Travel tip: Guwahati is the nearest airport to Meghalaya. Pre-arrange your transportation to Shillong for a hassle-free experience.

Afternoon: Drive to Shillong

Embark on a scenic 3-hour drive (approximately 100 km) from Guwahati to Shillong. En route, stop at Umiam Lake (Barapani), a vast reservoir surrounded by hills, for photographs and a quick refreshment.

Attraction: Umiam Lake offers breathtaking panoramic views and watersport activities (if time permits).

Evening: Check-in & Police Bazaar

Check into your hotel in Shillong. After freshening up, visit Police Bazaar, the main commercial hub of Shillong, for shopping and dinner. Experience the vibrant local atmosphere and try some Khasi cuisine.

Accommodation: Tripura Castle, Hotel Polo Towers, or Ri Kynjai for luxury stays; Hotel Alpine Continental or Hotel Rockski for mid-range options.

Day 2

Shillong Exploration

Morning: Elephant Falls & Shillong Peak

Start your day with a visit to Elephant Falls, a three-tier waterfall located on the outskirts of the city. Next, head to Shillong Peak, the highest point in Shillong offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

Activity: At Shillong Peak, which is a sacred site for the local Khasi tribe, enjoy the magnificent bird's-eye view of Shillong and take memorable photographs.

Afternoon: Cathedral & Don Bosco Museum

Visit the Mary Help of Christians Cathedral, an architectural marvel with beautiful stained glass windows. Continue to Don Bosco Museum, a seven-story museum showcasing the culture, heritage, and history of the northeastern states of India.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant like Cafe Shillong or ML05 Cafe for authentic Khasi cuisine or continental options.

Evening: Ward's Lake & Local Market

Spend the evening at Ward's Lake, a picturesque man-made lake with a botanical garden where you can enjoy boating or a leisurely walk. Later, explore the local markets for traditional Khasi handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Dinner: Try Jadoh (rice and meat dish) or Dohneiihong (pork with black sesame) at local eateries like Trattoria or City Hut Dhaba.

Day 3

Day Trip to Mawphlang & Laitlum Canyon

Morning: Mawphlang Sacred Forest

Take a day trip to Mawphlang Sacred Grove (about 25 km from Shillong). Take a guided tour through this ancient forest, considered sacred by the Khasi people and preserved for centuries through traditional conservation practices.

Activity: Learn about the rich biodiversity and cultural significance of the forest while exploring its trails with a local guide.

Afternoon: David Scott Trail

Experience a part of the famous David Scott Trail, a historic 16 km trail built during the British era. You can walk a small section of this trail that offers beautiful views of the countryside and rolling hills.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local eatery near Mawphlang village.

Evening: Laitlum Canyon

Visit Laitlum Canyon ("End of Hills"), a hidden gem offering breathtaking views of deep gorges and valleys. The panoramic views of the East Khasi Hills from this vantage point are truly spectacular, especially during sunset.

Return: Drive back to Shillong for dinner and overnight stay at your hotel.

Mawphlang Sacred Grove

Sacred Groves of Meghalaya

Sacred groves are forest fragments that are communally protected with spiritual and ecological significance. Meghalaya has the highest concentration of these groves in India, with over 100 documented locations.

The Mawphlang Sacred Grove, spanning about 80 hectares, is one of the most famous. It houses diverse plant species, some of which are extinct elsewhere. The Khasi people believe these forests are the abode of their deities and ancestral spirits, and taking anything out of the grove (even a leaf or twig) is strictly prohibited and believed to bring misfortune.

Cultural Advisory

When visiting sacred groves, please respect local customs and beliefs. Do not take anything from the forest, stay on designated paths, and follow your guide's instructions. Photography may be restricted in certain areas.

Day 4

Shillong to Cherrapunji

Morning: Shillong Local Exploration

Visit any remaining attractions in Shillong, such as the Butterfly Museum or the Air Force Museum. You can also take some time to explore local cafes and shops before departing for Cherrapunji.

Activity: Try some local Khasi tea or coffee at one of Shillong's charming cafes.

Afternoon: Drive to Cherrapunji

Check out from your Shillong hotel and drive to Cherrapunji (Sohra), one of the wettest places on Earth, approximately 54 km from Shillong. En route, stop at various viewpoints to admire the cascading waterfalls and lush green valleys.

Accommodation: Polo Orchid Resort or Kutmadan Resort for comfort; Sa-I-Mika Park or Coniferous Resort for budget options.

Evening: Seven Sisters Waterfall

Visit the Seven Sisters Waterfall (Nohsngithiang Falls), a seven-segmented waterfall that plunges down from a height of 315 meters. Enjoy the sunset views as the last rays of the sun illuminate the waterfall.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your resort or at a local restaurant in Cherrapunji, trying Khasi specialties like Tungrymbai (fermented soybean dish) or Nakham Bitchi (dried fish chutney).

About Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji (locally known as Sohra) once held the record for receiving the highest rainfall on Earth and is now the second wettest place after nearby Mawsynram. The region is known for its stunning landscapes, deep gorges, majestic waterfalls, and the unique living root bridges cultivated by the Khasi tribe.

The area's limestone caves, dense forests, and breathtaking viewpoints offer visitors a glimpse into nature's raw beauty and the traditional way of life of the indigenous Khasi people.

Day 5

Cherrapunji Exploration

Morning: Nohkalikai Falls & Eco Park

Visit Nohkalikai Falls, India's tallest plunge waterfall with a height of 340 meters. Learn about the tragic legend behind the waterfall's name and enjoy the breathtaking views from the viewing platform. Continue to Eco Park for panoramic views of the Bangladesh plains.

Activity: Photography and admiring the magnificent views of the waterfall and surrounding landscapes.

Afternoon: Mawsmai Cave & Arwah Cave

Explore Mawsmai Cave, a limestone cave with impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations. The illuminated sections of the cave are accessible to tourists. If time permits, visit the less crowded Arwah Cave, known for its fossils and spacious chambers.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near the caves.

Evening: Garden of Caves & Dainthlen Falls

Visit the Garden of Caves, a unique natural garden with caves and rock formations. Continue to Dainthlen Falls, associated with a Khasi legend about killing a great serpent. Both sites offer great views and photo opportunities.

Dinner: Return to your resort for dinner and relaxation.

Nohkalikai Falls

Mawsmai Cave

The Legend of Nohkalikai

Nohkalikai Falls derives its name from a tragic Khasi legend. 'Noh' means 'jump' and 'Ka Likai' refers to a woman named Likai. According to the legend, Likai was a widow who remarried but her new husband was jealous of her attention to her daughter from her first marriage.

One day, while Likai was out working, her husband killed her daughter, cooked her meat, and saved her head and bones. When Likai returned home, she ate the meal, later finding her daughter's fingers. Realizing what had happened, in her grief, she jumped off the cliff, giving the waterfall its name.

Day 6

Nongriat Trek

Morning: Trek to Nongriat

Drive to Tyrna village (about 12 km from Cherrapunji) and begin the trek to Nongriat village to see the famous Double Decker Living Root Bridge. The trek involves descending approximately 3,500 steps into the valley.

Activity: The trek takes about 2-3 hours each way and requires good physical fitness. Carry water, snacks, and wear proper trekking shoes.

Afternoon: Double Decker Root Bridge & Rainbow Falls

Explore the Double Decker Living Root Bridge, a unique bio-engineering marvel created by the Khasi tribe. For those with extra energy, continue the trek to Rainbow Falls, a beautiful waterfall about 1-2 hours further from Nongriat.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a small eatery in Nongriat village.

Evening: Return to Cherrapunji

Trek back to Tyrna village, which involves climbing up the 3,500 steps. Drive back to your resort in Cherrapunji for a well-deserved rest after the day's physical exertion.

Alternative: For those who prefer, there's an option to stay overnight in a homestay in Nongriat village to fully experience the local way of life. This option requires prior arrangement.

Living Root Bridges

Living root bridges are unique bio-engineering marvels created by the Khasi and Jaintia tribes of Meghalaya. The tribes train the aerial roots of the Indian rubber tree (Ficus elastica) to grow along hollow betel nut trunks that span streams and rivers.

Over 15-30 years, these roots form a sturdy, living bridge that can support the weight of several people at once. Some of these bridges are over 150 years old and grow stronger with time, unlike conventional bridges that weaken.

The Double Decker Root Bridge in Nongriat village is a rare example where roots have been trained to form two bridges, one above the other.

Day 7

Cherrapunji to Mawsynram

Morning: Relaxed Start

After the physical exertion of the previous day, enjoy a relaxed morning at your resort. Take in the beautiful views of the Cherrapunji landscape and prepare for your journey to Mawsynram.

Activity: Capture some final photographs of Cherrapunji's scenic beauty before departing.

Afternoon: Drive to Mawsynram

Check out from your Cherrapunji accommodation and drive to Mawsynram (approximately 40 km, 1.5-hour drive), currently recognized as the wettest place on Earth. The drive offers stunning views of valleys and waterfalls.

Accommodation: Mawsynram has limited accommodation options. Stay at local homestays or guesthouses for an authentic experience.

Evening: Mawjymbuin Cave

Visit Mawjymbuin Cave, known for its unique stalagmite formation that resembles a 'Shivalinga' (a symbol of Lord Shiva in Hindu mythology). Explore the cave and the surrounding area, which offers a glimpse into local geological features.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple home-cooked meal at your homestay, sampling local Khasi dishes.

The Wettest Place on Earth

Mawsynram currently holds the Guinness World Record for being the wettest place on Earth, receiving an average annual rainfall of about 11,872 mm (467.4 inches). This small village beats the nearby Cherrapunji (Sohra) which previously held this title.

The extraordinary rainfall is due to the village's location, which forces the moisture-laden air from the Bay of Bengal to rise and condense, resulting in heavy precipitation. The local Khasi people have adapted to this extreme climate with unique lifestyle practices, including the use of knup (a traditional bamboo and grass rain shield) and the cultivation of betel nut and bay leaf trees that thrive in wet conditions.

Day 8

Mawsynram to Mawlynnong

Morning: Mawsynram Exploration

Explore the village of Mawsynram and its surrounding areas. Visit the Mawsynram Viewpoint for panoramic views of the landscape. If the weather permits, take a short hike to see some of the natural rock formations in the area.

Activity: Learn about how locals adapt to living in the wettest place on Earth, with unique rainwater harvesting systems and traditional architecture.

Afternoon: Drive to Mawlynnong

Check out from your Mawsynram accommodation and drive to Mawlynnong (approximately 80 km, 3-hour drive), recognized as "Asia's Cleanest Village" by Discover India Magazine in 2003.

Accommodation: Mawlynnong has limited accommodation options. Stay at local homestays like Hala Tyngkong Homestay or Ilajong Guest House for an authentic experience.

Evening: Village Exploration

Explore the pristine village of Mawlynnong, known for its cleanliness, community-based sanitation system, and unique living practices. Visit the Sky View platform, a bamboo structure offering panoramic views of the Bangladesh plains.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple homemade dinner at your homestay, typically consisting of local Khasi dishes prepared with fresh, organic ingredients.

Mawlynnong Village

About Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong gained international recognition as "Asia's Cleanest Village" and "God's Own Garden." The village showcases the Khasi community's commitment to cleanliness and environmental preservation.

All homes in Mawlynnong use eco-friendly disposal systems, and waste is collected in bamboo dustbins placed throughout the village. The community works together to maintain cleanliness, with even young children participating in daily cleaning routines.

The village also promotes gender equality and is known for its matrilineal society where property and wealth are passed down through the female line, and children take their mother's surname.

Day 9

Mawlynnong & Dawki

Morning: Living Root Bridge

Visit the Single Decker Living Root Bridge at Riwai, a short walk from Mawlynnong. This natural bridge, formed by training the roots of the Indian rubber tree, is over a century old and demonstrates the ingenious engineering skills of the Khasi tribe.

Activity: Walk across the bridge and follow the trail that leads to a stream where you can dip your feet in the cool water.

Afternoon: Dawki & Umngot River

Drive to Dawki (approximately 35 km from Mawlynnong) to see the crystal-clear Umngot River. The river's transparency is so remarkable that boats appear to be floating on air rather than water.

Activity: Take a boat ride on the Umngot River and enjoy the serene atmosphere. Visit the Bangladesh border which is very close to Dawki.

Evening: Shnongpdeng

Visit Shnongpdeng, a riverside village known for water activities like snorkeling, cliff jumping, and kayaking. Enjoy the scenic beauty of the river and surrounding mountains as the sun sets.

Stay: You can choose to stay overnight in Dawki or Shnongpdeng in a riverside camp, or return to Mawlynnong for the night.

Umngot River at Dawki

The Crystal Clear Umngot River

The Umngot River is often described as one of the clearest rivers in India and possibly Asia. The water is so transparent that the bottom of the river is clearly visible, even at depths of 12-15 feet. When viewed from above, boats on the river appear to be floating in mid-air rather than on water.

This natural wonder forms a natural border between the Khasi and Jaintia Hills and flows into Bangladesh. The pristine water quality is a result of the unique geological features of the region and limited industrial development in the area.

Besides boat rides, visitors can enjoy swimming, kayaking, and fishing in designated areas. The surrounding riverside villages offer glimpses into the traditional lifestyle of the local communities.

Day 10

Dawki to Jowai

Morning: River Activities

If staying in Dawki or Shnongpdeng, enjoy some morning activities on the Umngot River, such as swimming, kayaking, or simply relaxing by the riverside. If you've returned to Mawlynnong, drive back to Dawki for a final visit.

Activity: Early morning is the best time to see the crystal-clear waters when there are fewer tourists and boats.

Afternoon: Drive to Jowai

After lunch, begin your journey to Jowai (approximately 60 km, 2-hour drive), the headquarters of West Jaintia Hills district and home to the Pnar tribe. Check into your accommodation upon arrival.

Accommodation: Hotel Sumo, Sky View Hotel, or local guesthouses are options in Jowai.

Evening: Thadlaskein Lake

Visit Thadlaskein Lake, a man-made lake associated with historical legends of the Pnar tribe. The serene setting offers a peaceful evening stroll and beautiful sunset views.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant in Jowai, trying Jaintia cuisine which has its own unique dishes distinct from Khasi cuisine.

About Jowai & Jaintia Hills

Jowai is the headquarters of West Jaintia Hills district and the cultural center of the Pnar (a subtribe of the Khasi) people. The region is known for its picturesque landscapes, waterfalls, caves, and rich cultural heritage.

The Jaintia Hills have a fascinating history, having been an independent kingdom known as the Jaintia Kingdom until the 19th century when it came under British rule. The area is known for its matrilineal society, traditional dance forms like the Ka Shad PheiÃ± Iawbei, and unique festivals like Behdienkhlam (celebrated to drive away plague and pray for good health and harvest).

The region is also rich in natural resources, particularly coal and limestone, though mining has been regulated in recent years due to environmental concerns.

Day 11

Jowai to Shillong

Morning: Krangsuri Falls

Visit Krangsuri Falls, one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Meghalaya, located about 30 km from Jowai. The emerald blue pool at the base of the waterfall is perfect for swimming, and the surrounding area offers opportunities for picnicking and relaxation.

Activity: Take a swim in the clear blue waters of the pool (if permitted and if you're comfortable swimming).

Afternoon: Nartiang Monoliths

Visit Nartiang, known for having one of the largest collections of monoliths in one place in the world. These stone structures were erected by the Jaintia kings to commemorate important events and people.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local eatery in Nartiang or Jowai before departing for Shillong.

Evening: Return to Shillong

Drive back to Shillong (approximately 65 km, 2-hour drive). Check into your hotel and spend the evening relaxing or exploring the city's vibrant nightlife, including cafes and live music venues that Shillong is famous for.

Accommodation: Return to one of the hotels in Shillong where you stayed earlier in the trip.

Shillong: The Rock Capital of India

Shillong is often referred to as the "Rock Capital of India" due to its vibrant music culture and the popularity of Western music, particularly rock and roll, among its residents. The city has produced numerous talented musicians and bands over the years.

The love for music in Shillong can be traced back to the influence of American soldiers stationed in Northeast India during World War II, who introduced Western music to the locals. This, combined with the missionary education system that encouraged music, led to the deep-rooted music culture in the city.

If you're interested in experiencing this aspect of Shillong's culture, consider visiting popular venues like Cloud 9 or Cafe Shillong, where you might catch live performances by local bands during weekends.

Day 12

Shillong to Guwahati & Departure

Morning: Last-Minute Shopping

Spend the morning doing some last-minute shopping in Shillong. Visit the Bara Bazaar (also known as Iewduh) or Police Bazaar to purchase souvenirs, local handicrafts, and specialties like Khasi traditional attire, bamboo items, or organic honey.

Activity: Don't miss trying some local snacks and sweets to take back home.

Afternoon: Drive to Guwahati

After lunch and check-out from your hotel, begin your journey back to Guwahati (approximately 100 km, 3-hour drive). En route, make a final stop at Umiam Lake for one last view of this magnificent water body.

En route: Reflect on the diverse experiences of your Meghalaya journey, from the crystal-clear rivers to the living root bridges and rich tribal culture.

Evening: Departure

Arrive in Guwahati and, depending on your departure time, either transfer directly to Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport or visit some local attractions in Guwahati like the Kamakhya Temple or Assam State Museum if time permits.

Departure: Bid farewell to the beautiful Northeast as you depart for your onward journey, carrying memories of Meghalaya's pristine landscapes and unique culture.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: Ideal months with pleasant weather and clear skies
  • June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall (great for waterfall viewing but trekking can be challenging)
  • December to February: Cold but clear, with temperatures dropping to 4-5Â°C in some areas
  • For river activities: October to March when waters are clearest
  • For cultural festivals: November (Wangala) and April (Shad Suk Mynsiem)

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airport is in Guwahati, Assam (3 hours from Shillong)
  • Train: Nearest major railway station is in Guwahati
  • Road: Well-connected via NH-40 from Guwahati
  • Local Transport: Shared sumos, taxis, and local buses available
  • Recommended: Hire a private car with driver for the entire trip
  • For trekking: Local guides necessary for living root bridge treks

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Lightweight with warm layers for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Rain gear: Waterproof jacket, umbrella, waterproof covers for bags
  • For treks: Backpack, water bottle, energy bars, first aid kit
  • Documentation: ID proof (required for domestic tourists)
  • Health: Personal medications, insect repellent
  • Other: Power bank, camera with waterproof case, torch

Food & Cuisine

Meghalaya offers a unique culinary experience with dishes that showcase local ingredients and traditional cooking methods:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Jadoh (rice and meat dish)
  • Dohneiihong (pork with black sesame)
  • Tungrymbai (fermented soybean dish)
  • Nakham Bitchi (dried fish chutney)
  • Putharo (rice pancakes)
  • Kyat (local rice beer)
Dining Tips
  • Most villages offer simple, home-cooked meals
  • Shillong has a range of restaurants serving local and international cuisine
  • Try orange honey and tea from Khasi Hills
  • Carry snacks for long drives and treks
  • Vegetarian options are limited in rural areas
  • Try the local pineapples and oranges when in season

Practical Information

  • Language: Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo are the main languages. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs available in Shillong but limited in rural areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Power outages can occur, so carry a power bank
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Shillong; limited in remote areas
  • Registration: All tourists need to register on the Meghalaya Tourism app
  • Protected areas: Some areas require special permits for foreign nationals
  • Local customs: Respect tribal customs and ask before photographing people
  • Plastic ban: Single-use plastics are banned in many areas of Meghalaya
  • Emergency: Limited medical facilities outside Shillong; carry basic medicines
