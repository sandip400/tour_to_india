Culture of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has a rich and diverse culture, shaped by its history, literature, art, music, festivals, and traditions. Here's an overview of some key aspects of its culture:

1. Language

  • Hindi is the official language of Madhya Pradesh, but various dialects are spoken across the state including Malvi, Nimadi, Bundeli, and Bagheli. The tribal communities have their own languages and dialects such as Bhili, Gondi, and Korku.

2. Literature

  • Madhya Pradesh has a rich literary tradition with notable contributions from writers like Harishankar Parsai, Makhanlal Chaturvedi, Subhadra Kumari Chauhan, and Bhavani Prasad Mishra.
  • The state has been home to many Sanskrit scholars and has contributed significantly to Hindi literature.


3. Art and Craft

  • The state is known for its diverse range of art forms, from Gond and Bhil tribal paintings to Batik prints and Chanderi sarees.
  • Bell metal craft of Bastar, terracotta of Malwa, and bamboo craft of tribal regions are famous handicrafts.

4. Music

  • Madhya Pradesh has a rich musical heritage with the Dhrupad style of classical music originating from Gwalior.
  • The Tansen Music Festival in Gwalior celebrates the legacy of Tansen, one of the nine jewels of Emperor Akbar's court.
  • Folk music traditions include Maand, Fag, and various tribal music forms.
  • Traditional instruments like dholak, manjira, and ektara are widely used in folk performances.

5. Dance

  • Madhya Pradesh is home to numerous folk dance forms including Jawara, Matki, Phulpati, and Grida.
  • Tribal dances like Saila, Karma, Kaksar, and Bhagoria are performed during festivals and celebrations.
  • The Khajuraho Dance Festival is an internationally acclaimed event showcasing classical dance forms.

6. Cuisine

  • Madhya Pradesh cuisine varies by region, with Indori poha, jalebi, and kachori being famous breakfast items.
  • Bhutte ka kees (grated corn dish), dal bafla (baked wheat balls with dal), and chakki ki shaak are popular dishes.
  • Tribal cuisines feature ingredients like mahua, tendu leaves, and forest produce.
  • Street food in cities like Indore is famous throughout India.


7. Theatre and Cinema

  • Madhya Pradesh has a vibrant theatre tradition with Habib Tanvir's Naya Theatre being a significant contribution.
  • The Bharat Bhavan in Bhopal is a cultural center that promotes theatre, visual arts, and literature.
  • The state has produced notable film personalities like Raghubir Yadav and Rajpal Yadav.

8. Architecture

  • Madhya Pradesh's architectural style blends Hindu, Islamic, Jain, and Buddhist influences. Notable structures include the Khajuraho Temples, Sanchi Stupa, Gwalior Fort, and various palaces in Orchha and Mandu.

9. Religious Diversity

  • The state is home to a variety of religions, with Hinduism, Islam, Jainism, Buddhism, and tribal faiths having a significant presence. This diversity is reflected in the state's festivals, rituals, and cultural practices.

10. Folk Traditions

  • Madhya Pradesh is famous for its rich folk traditions, including Maach (a form of folk theatre), Mela (fairs), and various tribal rituals.
  • The Bhagoria festival is a unique tribal tradition that serves as a matchmaking event for young people.

11. Philosophy

  • The state has been a center for various philosophical traditions, including Buddhism (Sanchi), Jainism (Khajuraho), and various Hindu schools of thought.
  • Tribal philosophies and belief systems add to the rich tapestry of thought in the region.

12. Handicrafts

  • Madhya Pradesh is known for its handicrafts such as Chanderi and Maheshwari textiles, Gond paintings, bell metal craft, bamboo work, and stone carving.


Madhya Pradesh's culture is deeply rooted in its history, and it continues to evolve while maintaining a close connection to its heritage. It's a region where modernity and tradition coexist beautifully!



Tradition of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has a rich and diverse tradition deeply rooted in its history, culture, and social practices. Here's an overview of some key traditional aspects of the state:

1. Clothing Tradition

Men

  • Dhoti and Kurta: Traditional men wear white or colored dhoti with a kurta, especially during religious ceremonies, weddings, and festivals.
  • Bandi and Pagdi: Worn for special occasions, especially in rural areas.

Women

  • Saree: Women traditionally wear sarees like Chanderi, Maheshwari, and Bagh prints. Different tribal communities have their own distinctive styles.
  • Lehenga-Choli: Popular in tribal regions and during festivals, often with mirror work and embroidery.

2. Wedding Traditions

  • Mandap Muhurat: The auspicious time for setting up the wedding mandap.
  • Haldi Ceremony: Turmeric paste is applied to both bride and groom for glowing skin.
  • Kanya Daan: The ritual where the bride's parents give her away to the groom.
  • Saptapadi: The couple takes seven rounds around the sacred fire, making vows.
  • Tribal Weddings: Tribal communities like Bhil, Gond, and Baiga have their own unique wedding customs and rituals.

3. Music & Dance Traditions

  • Dhrupad: One of the oldest forms of classical music, with Gwalior being a major center.
  • Maand: A folk music tradition popular in Malwa region.
  • Tribal Music: Each tribal group has its own music traditions with unique instruments.
  • Jawara Dance: A harvest dance performed during Navratri.
  • Matki Dance: Women balance earthen pots on their heads while dancing.
  • Tribal Dances: Saila, Karma, Kaksar, and Bhagoria dances performed by various tribes.

4. Social & Religious Traditions

  • Melas & Haats: Traditional fairs and weekly markets where people gather for trade and social interaction.
  • Tribal Gatherings: Events like Bhagoria where young people meet potential partners.
  • Narmada Parikrama: The sacred circumambulation of the Narmada River, covering about 2,600 km.
  • Simhastha Kumbh Mela: Held every 12 years in Ujjain, one of the largest religious gatherings.

5. Architectural & Handicraft Traditions

  • Temple Architecture: Khajuraho temples showcase the Nagara style with intricate carvings.
  • Gond Painting: Traditional tribal art with distinctive patterns and natural colors.
  • Chanderi & Maheshwari Weaving: Traditional textile crafts producing fine sarees and fabrics.
  • Bell Metal Craft: Intricate metal work using the lost-wax technique.
  • Bamboo & Wood Craft: Utilitarian and decorative items made by tribal artisans.

6. Sports & Games

  • Mallakhamb: Traditional pole gymnastics that originated in Maharashtra but is popular in MP.
  • Kabaddi & Kho-Kho: Traditional rural games played widely.
  • Archery: Traditional sport among tribal communities, especially Bhils who are known for their archery skills.
  • Wrestling: Traditional mud wrestling is popular in rural areas.


Madhya Pradesh's traditions beautifully combine history, art, food, music, and festivals, making it one of the most culturally vibrant states in India.



Festivals of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh is known for its vibrant and diverse festivals, which reflect its rich cultural heritage, religious harmony, and artistic traditions. Here's a list of some of the major festivals celebrated in the state:

1. Lokrang Festival

  • A cultural festival celebrating folk arts, crafts, music, and dance from across India.
  • Held annually in Bhopal, it features performances, exhibitions, and workshops.
  • Artisans from different states showcase their crafts and traditional skills.
  • Food stalls offer regional cuisines from across the country.

üìç Time: January (Republic Day week)


2. Khajuraho Dance Festival

  • A week-long classical dance festival held against the backdrop of the magnificent Khajuraho temples.
  • Features performances of classical dance forms like Kathak, Bharatanatyam, Odissi, Kuchipudi, and Manipuri.
  • Attracts dancers and audiences from across the world.

üìç Time: February


3. Tansen Music Festival

  • Dedicated to Tansen, one of the nine jewels of Emperor Akbar's court and a musical genius.
  • Held near Tansen's tomb in Gwalior, it features performances by renowned classical musicians.
  • The festival preserves and promotes the Dhrupad style of classical music.

üìç Time: December


4. Bhagoria Festival

  • A tribal festival celebrated by the Bhil tribe, primarily in the Jhabua and Alirajpur districts.
  • Serves as a matchmaking event where young men and women choose their partners.
  • Features colorful markets, tribal dances, and music.
  • Young people apply colors on each other to express interest.

üìç Time: March (before Holi)


5. Simhastha Kumbh Mela

  • One of the four Kumbh Melas, held every 12 years in Ujjain.
  • Millions of pilgrims gather to take a holy dip in the Shipra River.
  • Features processions of sadhus, religious discourses, and cultural events.

üìç Time: Every 12 years (last held in 2016)

6. Malwa Utsav

  • A cultural festival celebrating the art, craft, music, and cuisine of the Malwa region.
  • Held in Indore, it features folk performances, art exhibitions, and food stalls.

üìç Time: April


7. Navratri & Dussehra

  • Celebrated with great enthusiasm across the state.
  • Features Garba and Dandiya Raas dances in urban areas.
  • Tribal communities perform Jawara dance during this period.
  • Culminates in Dussehra with the burning of Ravana effigies.

üìç Time: September-October

8. Diwali

  • The festival of lights is celebrated throughout the state with homes and streets decorated with oil lamps and electric lights.
  • Special prayers are offered to Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity.
  • Fireworks displays and family gatherings are common.

üìç Time: October-November


9. Tribal Festivals

  • Bhagoriya: A tribal fair and festival celebrated by the Bhil tribe.
  • Karma Festival: Celebrated by the Gond and Baiga tribes to worship the Karam tree.
  • Madai Festival: A tribal gathering featuring rituals, dances, and markets.

Foods of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has a rich and diverse culinary tradition that varies across its different regions. The cuisine is influenced by neighboring states and has its own unique flavors and specialties. Here's a look at some of the most iconic foods of Madhya Pradesh:

1. Staple Food of Madhya Pradesh

  • Wheat & Rice: Wheat is the primary staple in most regions, while rice is more common in eastern parts.
  • Jowar & Bajra: Millet varieties used in rural and tribal areas, especially for rotis.

2. Regional Breads

  • Bafla: Wheat dough balls that are first boiled and then baked in traditional ovens.
  • Bhakri: Thick flatbread made from jowar or bajra flour.
  • Puri: Deep-fried bread served with various curries.

3. Famous Madhya Pradesh Dishes

Indore Specialties

  • Poha-Jalebi: A popular breakfast combination of flattened rice with sweet jalebis.
  • Indori Sabudana Khichdi: A savory dish made from tapioca pearls, peanuts, and spices.
  • Bhutte Ka Kees: Grated corn cooked with milk, spices, and garnished with lime juice.

Malwa Region Specialties

  • Dal Bafla: Wheat dough balls served with ghee and dal, a signature dish of MP.
  • Chakki Ki Shaak: A spicy curry made from wheat flour dumplings.
  • Mawa Bati: Sweet dumplings stuffed with khoya (milk solids) and dry fruits.

Bundelkhand Specialties

  • Kadaknath Chicken: A special black-meat chicken native to Jhabua district, known for its medicinal properties.
  • Rogan Josh: A spicy meat curry popular in the region.
  • Keema Pav: Minced meat served with bread rolls.

Tribal Cuisine

  • Mahua Dishes: Various preparations using the mahua flower, including drinks and sweets.
  • Tribal Meat Preparations: Using unique cooking methods and forest herbs.

4. Sweets & Desserts

  • Malpua: Sweet pancakes soaked in sugar syrup, often served with rabri.
  • Jalebi: Crispy, pretzel-shaped sweets soaked in sugar syrup.
  • Mawa Bati: Sweet dumplings filled with khoya and dry fruits.
  • Khopra Pak: A coconut-based sweet popular in the Malwa region.
  • Lavang Lata: A spiral-shaped pastry filled with khoya and flavored with cloves.
  • Imarti: Similar to jalebi but made with urad dal flour and has a more complex pattern.

5. Street Food of Madhya Pradesh

  • Indori Chaat: Various savory snacks including kachoris, samosas, and dahi vada.
  • Bhutte Ka Kees: Spiced grated corn, a popular street food in Indore.
  • Garadu: Spicy fried yam, a winter specialty in Indore.
  • Khopra Patties: Coconut-based snack popular in the Malwa region.
  • Dal Kachori: Deep-fried pastry filled with spiced lentils.

6. Famous Drinks

  • Shikanji: A refreshing lemonade with spices and herbs.
  • Mahua Drink: A traditional tribal alcoholic beverage made from mahua flowers.
  • Lassi: Sweetened yogurt drink, often flavored with fruits or spices.

7. Special Festival Foods

  • Lapsi: A sweet porridge made from broken wheat, popular during festivals.
  • Kheer: Rice pudding prepared during celebrations and religious occasions.
  • Peda: Milk-based sweet, especially famous from Dharampuri in Dhar district.

History of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has a rich and diverse history that spans ancient civilizations, medieval kingdoms, British colonial rule, and the modern era. The region has played a significant role in India's political, cultural, and religious history.

1. Prehistoric & Ancient Period (Before 6th Century CE)

  • Prehistoric Era: The Bhimbetka rock shelters (UNESCO World Heritage Site) contain some of the oldest known rock art in India, dating back to approximately 10,000 BCE.
  • Maurya Empire (322-185 BCE): Emperor Ashoka built the Great Stupa at Sanchi, one of the oldest stone structures in India.
  • Gupta Empire (320-550 CE): The region flourished under Gupta rule, with advancements in art, science, and literature.

2. Medieval Period (6th - 16th Century)

Paramara Dynasty (9th-14th Century):

  • Ruled from Dhar and Mandu, with King Bhoja being the most famous ruler.
  • Contributed significantly to art, architecture, and literature.
  • Built the magnificent structures at Mandu, including Jahaz Mahal.

Chandela Dynasty (9th-13th Century):

  • Ruled the Bundelkhand region and built the famous Khajuraho temples.
  • Known for their architectural achievements and patronage of arts.

Delhi Sultanate (13th-16th Century):

  • Various parts of Madhya Pradesh came under the control of the Delhi Sultanate.
  • Alauddin Khilji captured Mandu in 1305, and the region became an important province.

3. Mughal Rule (16th - 18th Century)

  • Most of present-day Madhya Pradesh came under Mughal control during Akbar's reign.
  • The region was divided into the Malwa and Gondwana subahs (provinces).
  • Gond kingdoms in eastern MP maintained semi-independent status by paying tribute.

4. Maratha Period & British Colonial Rule (18th - 20th Century)

Maratha Influence (18th Century)

  • The Marathas under the Holkars of Indore, Scindias of Gwalior, and Bhonsles of Nagpur controlled large parts of the region.
  • They established semi-autonomous states that later became princely states under British rule.

British Colonial Period (19th - 20th Century)

  • The British established control through a series of wars and treaties with local rulers.
  • The region was divided into the Central Provinces and Berar, and various princely states.
  • Tribal revolts against British rule occurred, including the Bhil uprising and the 1857 rebellion.

Freedom Movement

  • Tribal Movements: Various tribal communities led resistance movements against British policies.
  • Jungle Satyagraha: Protests against forest laws that restricted tribal access to forest resources.
  • Prominent Leaders: Shankar Shah, Raghunath Shah, and Tantya Bhil were important freedom fighters from the region.
  • Civil Disobedience Movement: The region actively participated in Gandhi's movements.
  • Princely States: Movements for responsible government emerged in princely states like Indore and Gwalior.

5. Post-Independence Period (1947 - Present)

Formation of Madhya Pradesh

  • After independence, the princely states in the region were merged to form Madhya Bharat, Vindhya Pradesh, and Bhopal state.
  • In 1956, these states were reorganized, and Madhya Pradesh was created as a state of the Indian Union.
  • Bhopal was chosen as the capital of the new state.

Recent Developments

  • In 2000, the southeastern portion of Madhya Pradesh was separated to form the new state of Chhattisgarh.
  • The state has seen significant industrial and agricultural development in recent decades.
  • Tourism has grown with increased focus on the state's rich heritage, wildlife, and natural beauty.

6. Present-Day Madhya Pradesh

  • Cultural Heritage: The state preserves its rich cultural heritage through various initiatives and festivals.
  • Economic Growth: Agriculture, industry, tourism, and IT sectors are key contributors to the economy.
  • Tourist Attractions: Khajuraho Temples, Sanchi Stupa, Bhimbetka Caves, national parks, and palaces attract visitors from around the world.


Madhya Pradesh has witnessed ancient civilizations, medieval kingdoms, Mughal and Maratha rule, British colonization, and modern development. It remains a center of culture, heritage, and natural beauty in India. üö©üìú
