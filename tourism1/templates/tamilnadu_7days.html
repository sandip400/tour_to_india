Tamil Nadu: A Cultural Treasure

Tamil Nadu offers a perfect blend of ancient temples, colonial heritage, and stunning natural landscapes. From the bustling streets of Chennai to the serene hills of Kodaikanal, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this southern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Chennai & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chennai International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Chennai. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Leela Palace or ITC Grand Chola for luxury stays; Taj Coromandel or Trident Chennai for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Marina Beach

Enjoy your first Tamil meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Saravana Bhavan' or 'Murugan Idli Shop'. Visit Marina Beach, the second-longest urban beach in the world, and enjoy the sea breeze.

Lunch recommendation: Try South Indian classics like Idli, Dosa, Sambar, and Filter Coffee.

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the iconic Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Mylapore, a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture. Explore the surrounding area with its traditional markets and streets. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Evening activity: If available, catch a classical Carnatic music or Bharatanatyam dance performance at Kalakshetra or Music Academy.

Marina Beach

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples (shoulders and knees covered).
  • Marina Beach is best visited in the evening when it's cooler and livelier.
Day 2

Chennai to Mahabalipuram

Morning: Chennai Heritage Tour

Start your day with a visit to Fort St. George, the first English fortress in India, now housing the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly. Continue to San Thome Basilica, built over the tomb of St. Thomas the Apostle.

Activity: Explore the Government Museum and National Art Gallery in Egmore.

Afternoon: Drive to Mahabalipuram

After lunch, drive to Mahabalipuram (also known as Mamallapuram), approximately 60 km south of Chennai along the scenic East Coast Road. Check into your hotel.

Accommodation: Radisson Blu Resort Temple Bay or InterContinental Chennai Mahabalipuram Resort for luxury; Ideal Beach Resort or Chariot Beach Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: Beach Sunset & Dinner

Relax at Mahabalipuram Beach and watch the sunset. Enjoy a seafood dinner at one of the beachside restaurants.

Dinner: Try fresh seafood preparations like prawn curry, fish fry, and crab masala at the local restaurants.

Day 3

Mahabalipuram to Pondicherry

Morning: UNESCO Heritage Sites

Explore the UNESCO World Heritage monuments of Mahabalipuram, including the Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance (a massive rock relief), the Five Rathas (monolithic temples shaped as chariots), and Krishna's Butter Ball (a giant balancing rock).

Note: Hire a local guide to understand the historical and mythological significance of these 7th-century monuments.

Afternoon: Drive to Pondicherry

After lunch, drive to Pondicherry (now officially called Puducherry), approximately 95 km south of Mahabalipuram. Check into your hotel in Pondicherry.

Accommodation: La Villa, Palais de Mahe, or Le Pondy for heritage and luxury stays; Hotel Atithi or The Promenade for mid-range options.

Evening: French Quarter Walk

Take a leisurely walk through the charming French Quarter (White Town) with its colonial buildings, boutiques, and cafes. Stroll along the famous Promenade Beach at sunset.

Dinner: Enjoy French or Franco-Tamil fusion cuisine at restaurants like Villa Shanti, Le Dupleix, or Carte Blanche.

Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram

Important Information
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes and sun protection for exploring Mahabalipuram monuments.
  • The monuments are open from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • Camera fees may apply at some monuments.
  • Pondicherry has a distinct French colonial charm, especially in the French Quarter.
  • The city is divided into French (White Town) and Tamil quarters, each with its own character.
About Pondicherry

Pondicherry was a French colonial settlement until 1954 and still retains much of its French heritage. The city is known for its French Quarter with tree-lined streets, colonial mansions, and French-style cafes and bakeries. It's also home to Auroville, an experimental township dedicated to human unity.

Day 4

Pondicherry to Thanjavur

Morning: Auroville & Sri Aurobindo Ashram

Visit Auroville, the international community founded in 1968, and see the Matrimandir (requires advance booking for inner chamber). Continue to Sri Aurobindo Ashram, a spiritual community founded by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother.

Note: Maintain silence at the Ashram and dress modestly.

Afternoon: Drive to Thanjavur

After lunch, embark on a longer drive to Thanjavur (formerly Tanjore), approximately 170 km southwest of Pondicherry. Check into your hotel in Thanjavur by evening.

Accommodation: Svatma or Ideal River View Resort for luxury; Sangam Hotel or Hotel Parisutham for mid-range options.

Evening: Rest & Relaxation

Rest at your hotel after the long drive. Enjoy a traditional Thanjavur dinner at your hotel or a nearby restaurant.

Try: Thanjavur's special dishes like Puliyodharai (tamarind rice), Vathal Kuzhambu (spicy gravy), and Thavala Adai (rice pancakes).

Day 5

Thanjavur to Madurai

Morning: Brihadeeswarar Temple

Visit the magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century. Marvel at the 216-feet tall vimana (tower) and the massive Nandi (bull) statue carved from a single stone.

Also visit: The Thanjavur Royal Palace and Art Gallery, which houses an impressive collection of Chola bronzes and other artifacts.

Afternoon: Thanjavur Art

Visit a workshop to see the famous Thanjavur painting technique, known for its use of gold foil and embedded precious stones. You can also see demonstrations of the traditional bronze casting process that has been practiced here for centuries.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional South Indian thali (meal served on banana leaf) at a local restaurant.

Evening: Drive to Madurai

Drive to Madurai, approximately 150 km southwest of Thanjavur. Check into your hotel in Madurai.

Accommodation: Heritage Madurai, The Gateway Hotel Pasumalai, or Taj Madurai for luxury; JC Residency or Fortune Pandiyan Hotel for mid-range options.

Day 6

Madurai to Kodaikanal

Morning: Meenakshi Amman Temple

Visit the iconic Meenakshi Amman Temple, a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture with its towering gopurams (gateway towers) covered with thousands of colorful sculptures. Explore the thousand-pillar hall and the temple tank.

Note: Non-Hindus are not allowed in the innermost sanctum but can explore most parts of the temple complex.

Afternoon: Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace

Visit the Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace, built in 1636 by King Thirumalai Nayak in Indo-Saracenic style. Admire the massive pillars, stucco work, and the grand courtyard.

Lunch: Try Madurai's famous Kari Dosa (meat dosa) or Parottas with Mutton Curry at restaurants like Kumar Mess or Amma Mess.

Evening: Drive to Kodaikanal

Drive to Kodaikanal, a hill station located about 120 km northwest of Madurai. Enjoy the scenic drive as you ascend into the Western Ghats. Check into your hotel in Kodaikanal.

Accommodation: The Carlton, Kodai - By The Lake, or Sterling Kodai Lake for luxury; Hotel Kodai International or Hotel Tamil Nadu for mid-range options.

About Kodaikanal

Kodaikanal, often referred to as the "Princess of Hill Stations," is situated at an altitude of 2,133 meters (7,000 feet) in the Palani Hills. The name Kodaikanal means "Gift of the Forest" in Tamil. The hill station is known for its cool climate, mist-covered mountains, the star-shaped Kodaikanal Lake, and beautiful viewpoints.

Elevation: 2,133 meters (7,000 ft)
Cool climate throughout the year
Famous for pine forests and waterfalls
Day 7

Kodaikanal to Chennai & Departure

Morning: Kodaikanal Lake & Viewpoints

Take a boat ride on the star-shaped Kodaikanal Lake. Visit Coaker's Walk, a 1-kilometer paved pedestrian path offering stunning views of the valleys and hills. Continue to Pillar Rocks, three granite boulders standing 400 feet high.

Also visit: Bryant Park, a beautifully maintained botanical garden near the lake.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, begin your return journey to Chennai. You can either drive directly to Chennai (approximately 8-9 hours) or drive to Madurai (3 hours) and take an evening flight to Chennai.

Alternative: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Chennai.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Chennai. T. Nagar and Pondy Bazaar are good options for textiles and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Chennai International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Coastal areas: October to March (winter months)
  • Hill stations: March to June (summer) and September to November (autumn)
  • Temple towns: Year-round, but avoid summer (April-June) due to extreme heat
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) in most areas

Transportation

  • Air: Chennai, Madurai, and Trichy have airports with good connectivity
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities and towns
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in cities; buses and auto-rickshaws widely available
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for coastal areas; warm layers for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Tamil Nadu offers a diverse vegetarian and non-vegetarian culinary experience:

Vegetarian Specialties
  • Idli (steamed rice cakes)
  • Dosa (rice and lentil crepes)
  • Sambar (lentil and vegetable stew)
  • Rasam (tamarind soup)
  • Pongal (rice and lentil dish)
  • Filter Coffee
Non-Vegetarian Specialties
  • Chettinad Chicken/Mutton
  • Fish Curry
  • Prawn Masala
  • Madurai Kari Dosa
  • Dindigul Biryani
  • Kongu Nadu Meat Dishes

Practical Information

  • Language: Tamil is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs

  • Temple etiquette: Remove shoes before entering temples, dress modestly, and follow local customs
