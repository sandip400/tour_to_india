Day 1

Arrival in Mumbai & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Mumbai. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Mahal Palace or The Oberoi for luxury stays; Trident Nariman Point or Fariyas Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & South Mumbai

Enjoy your first Maharashtrian meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Trishna' or 'Britannia & Co.'. Visit the Gateway of India, a magnificent basalt arch overlooking the Arabian Sea, and the adjacent Taj Mahal Palace Hotel.

Lunch recommendation: Try Maharashtrian classics like Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, or coastal seafood specialties.

Evening: Marine Drive

Visit the iconic Marine Drive at sunset for spectacular views. Take a leisurely stroll along this 3.6-kilometer-long boulevard, also known as the "Queen's Necklace" for its nighttime appearance. Enjoy dinner at one of the restaurants along the promenade.

Evening activity: If available, catch a Bollywood movie at a historic cinema like Regal or Eros.

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Mumbai's local trains are crowded during rush hours, avoid if possible.
  • South Mumbai is best explored on foot to appreciate the colonial architecture.
Day 2

Mumbai Heritage Tour

Morning: Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus & Crawford Market

Start your day with a visit to the UNESCO World Heritage Site Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (formerly Victoria Terminus), a historic railway station with stunning Victorian Gothic architecture. Continue to Crawford Market, a colonial-era market selling everything from fruits to household items.

Activity: Sample some local street food like Batata Vada or Pav Bhaji at nearby stalls.

Afternoon: Kala Ghoda & Museums

Explore the Kala Ghoda Art District, home to several art galleries and the Jehangir Art Gallery. Visit the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (formerly Prince of Wales Museum) with its extensive collection of art, archaeology, and natural history.

Lunch: Try the Parsi cuisine at Jimmy Boy or Britannia & Co. in the nearby Fort area.

Evening: Bandra & Juhu

Head to the suburbs of Bandra to see the Bandra-Worli Sea Link and Bandstand Promenade. Continue to Juhu Beach for a relaxing evening by the sea and enjoy Mumbai's famous street food like Bhel Puri and Pani Puri.

Dinner: Try coastal Maharashtrian cuisine at Highway Gomantak or Gajalee.

Day 3

Mumbai to Lonavala

Morning: Departure to Lonavala

After breakfast, check out from your Mumbai hotel and drive to Lonavala (approximately 2 hours), a picturesque hill station in the Western Ghats. The drive offers beautiful views of the mountains and valleys.

Note: You can hire a private car or take a train from Mumbai to Lonavala. Trains take approximately 2-2.5 hours.

Afternoon: Bhushi Dam & Tiger's Leap

After checking into your hotel, visit Bhushi Dam, a popular picnic spot with cascading waterfalls during monsoon. Continue to Tiger's Leap, a cliff-top with panoramic views of the valley, resembling a leaping tiger.

Lunch: Try Maharashtrian thali at a local restaurant like Rama Krishna or Buvachi Bhaji.

Evening: Karla Caves

Visit the ancient Karla Caves, a complex of rock-cut cave shrines dating back to the 2nd century BC. These Buddhist caves feature impressive chaitya halls and intricate carvings.

Accommodation: The Machan, Rhythm Lonavala, or Della Resorts for luxury stays; Hotel Kumar Plaza or United-21 for mid-range options.

Lonavala Western Ghats

Important Information
  • Lonavala is most beautiful during monsoon (June-September), but some activities may be restricted due to heavy rainfall.
  • Don't forget to buy Lonavala's famous chikki (hard candy made from jaggery and nuts) and chocolate fudge.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as most attractions involve walking on uneven terrain.
  • Carry a light jacket as evenings can be cool, especially in winter months.
Western Ghats

The Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the world's eight "hottest hotspots" of biological diversity. The area around Lonavala offers stunning landscapes, diverse flora and fauna, and numerous trekking opportunities.

Day 4

Lonavala to Pune

Morning: Lohagad Fort

After breakfast, visit Lohagad Fort, one of the many hill forts of Maharashtra. The fort, dating back to the 18th century, offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and Pawna Lake.

Note: The trek to the fort is moderate and takes about 30-45 minutes from the base.

Afternoon: Drive to Pune

Check out from your Lonavala hotel and drive to Pune (approximately 1.5 hours). Pune, known as the "Oxford of the East," is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage.

Lunch: Stop at one of the dhabas (roadside restaurants) on the way for authentic Maharashtrian food.

Evening: Pune Exploration

Check into your hotel in Pune. Visit Shaniwar Wada, an 18th-century fortified palace and the seat of the Peshwa rulers. Explore the nearby Laxmi Road, a bustling market area perfect for shopping and sampling local street food.

Accommodation: Conrad Pune, JW Marriott, or The Ritz-Carlton for luxury stays; Hotel Shreyas or Ginger Pune for mid-range options.

About Pune

Pune is Maharashtra's cultural capital and the second-largest city in the state after Mumbai. It was once the center of power for the Maratha Empire under the Peshwas. Today, it's a thriving metropolis known for its educational institutions, IT industry, and rich cultural heritage.

Day 5

Pune to Aurangabad

Morning: Pune Temples

Start your day with a visit to the famous Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple, one of the most visited Ganesh temples in Maharashtra. Continue to the Pataleshwar Cave Temple, a rock-cut cave temple dating back to the 8th century.

Also visit: Aga Khan Palace, a historical landmark associated with India's freedom movement and now a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi.

Afternoon: Travel to Aurangabad

After lunch, travel to Aurangabad. The fastest option is to take a flight (approximately 1 hour). Alternatively, you can drive (approximately 5-6 hours) or take a train (6-7 hours).

Note: If flying, book an afternoon flight to allow time for morning sightseeing in Pune.

Evening: Arrival in Aurangabad

Check into your hotel in Aurangabad. If time permits, visit the local markets for some shopping or relax at your hotel.

Accommodation: Vivanta Aurangabad, Lemon Tree Hotel, or Ambassador Ajanta for comfortable stays.

Day 6

Ellora & Ajanta Caves

Morning: Ellora Caves

Start early for a visit to the Ellora Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring 34 rock-cut temples and monasteries dating from the 6th to 10th century. The caves represent Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments and artwork.

Highlight: Don't miss Cave 16, the magnificent Kailasa Temple, carved from a single rock and considered one of the most remarkable cave temples in India.

Afternoon: Lunch & Travel to Ajanta

Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Ellora. Then drive to the Ajanta Caves (approximately 2 hours), another UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Note: Ajanta Caves are closed on Mondays, so plan your visit accordingly.

Evening: Ajanta Caves

Explore the Ajanta Caves, a series of 30 Buddhist cave monuments dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE. These caves are renowned for their exquisite paintings and sculptures depicting the life of Buddha.

Return: Drive back to Aurangabad in the evening.

Day 7

Aurangabad to Mumbai & Departure

Morning: Aurangabad Sightseeing

Visit Bibi Ka Maqbara, also known as the "Taj of the Deccan," a beautiful mausoleum built in the 17th century. Continue to Aurangabad Caves, a series of 12 Buddhist caves dating back to the 6th and 7th centuries.

Also visit: Panchakki, a 17th-century water mill known for its underground water channel.

Afternoon: Return to Mumbai

After lunch, take an afternoon flight back to Mumbai (approximately 1 hour). Alternatively, you can take a train or drive, but these options will take significantly longer.

Note: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Mumbai.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Mumbai. Colaba Causeway and Linking Road are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Mumbai & Pune: October to March (winter months)
  • Lonavala: Monsoon (June to September) for waterfalls; October to February for pleasant weather
  • Aurangabad: October to March
  • Avoid summer months (April to June) as temperatures can soar above 40Â°C

Transportation

  • Air: Mumbai and Pune have international airports; Aurangabad has domestic connections
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs, auto-rickshaws, local buses
  • Consider hiring a driver for day trips and intercity travel

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for cities; light jacket for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Maharashtra offers a diverse culinary experience:

Mumbai Street Food
  • Vada Pav (potato fritter in a bun)
  • Pav Bhaji (spiced vegetable curry)
  • Bhel Puri & Sev Puri (savory snacks)
  • Mumbai Sandwich
  • Pani Puri (hollow crisp filled with flavored water)
Traditional Maharashtrian
  • Puran Poli (sweet flatbread)
  • Misal Pav (spicy sprouts curry)
  • Modak (sweet dumpling)
  • Tambda & Pandhra Rassa (red & white curry)
  • Sabudana Khichdi (tapioca pearl dish)

Practical Information

  • Language: Marathi is the official language. Hindi and English are widely understood
