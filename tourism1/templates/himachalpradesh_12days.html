Himachal Pradesh: The Land of Snow

Himachal Pradesh offers breathtaking mountain landscapes, lush valleys, ancient temples, and vibrant culture. From the colonial charm of Shimla to the adventure hub of Manali and the spiritual aura of Dharamshala, this 12-day itinerary covers the best of what this northern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Shimla

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chandigarh Airport or Railway Station. Take a scenic 4-hour drive to Shimla through winding mountain roads. Check into your hotel and take some time to rest and acclimatize to the altitude.

Accommodation: The Oberoi Cecil, Wildflower Hall, or Clarke's Hotel for luxury stays; Hotel Willow Banks or Radisson Hotel Shimla for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Mall Road Stroll

Take a leisurely walk along the famous Mall Road, the main street of Shimla. Explore the colonial-era buildings, shops, and cafes. Visit the historic Christ Church, one of the oldest churches in North India.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Himachali dishes at Himachali Rasoi or enjoy continental cuisine at Cafe Sol.

Evening: The Ridge & Lakkar Bazaar

Visit The Ridge, a large open space in the heart of Shimla offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. Explore Lakkar Bazaar, famous for its wooden crafts and souvenirs.

Evening activity: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and experience the vibrant nightlife of Shimla.

Mall Road, Shimla

Christ Church, Shimla

Day 1 Tips
  • Shimla is at an altitude of 2,200 meters (7,200 ft). Take it easy on your first day to acclimatize.
  • Mall Road is a pedestrian-only zone, so be prepared for walking.
  • Carry a light jacket as evenings can be cool even in summer.
  • Try the famous Shimla water, known for its purity.
Day 2

Shimla Exploration

Morning: Viceregal Lodge

After breakfast, visit the Viceregal Lodge (now Indian Institute of Advanced Study), a magnificent British-era building with beautiful gardens. Take a guided tour to learn about its historical significance.

Activity: Explore the botanical gardens surrounding the lodge.

Afternoon: Jakhu Temple

Visit Jakhu Temple, dedicated to Lord Hanuman, situated at the highest point in Shimla. The temple is famous for its 108-feet tall Hanuman statue and offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Note: Be cautious of the monkeys around the temple area. Avoid carrying food items or shiny objects.

Evening: Gaiety Theatre & Shopping

Visit the historic Gaiety Theatre, a Victorian-Gothic style building on Mall Road. Spend the evening shopping for local handicrafts, woolens, and souvenirs at the Lower Bazaar.

Dinner: Try Himachali Dham (traditional feast) at Ashiana Restaurant or enjoy dinner with a view at The Oberoi Cecil.

Day 3

Shimla to Kufri & Toy Train Experience

Morning: Kufri Excursion

After breakfast, drive to Kufri (around 14 km from Shimla), a small hill station known for its scenic beauty and adventure activities. Visit the Himalayan Wildlife Zoo, home to various Himalayan species.

Activity: Enjoy horse riding to Mahasu Peak for panoramic views of the Himalayas (seasonal).

Afternoon: Adventure Activities

Engage in adventure activities like skiing (in winter) or try the exciting toboggan slide. Visit the Kufri Fun World, an amusement park with various rides and activities.

Lunch: Enjoy a picnic lunch amidst nature or dine at one of the local restaurants.

Evening: Toy Train Experience

Return to Shimla and experience a short journey on the famous Kalka-Shimla Toy Train, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The narrow-gauge railway offers breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.

Evening activity: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and take a final stroll along Mall Road.

Scenic Kufri Landscape

Kalka-Shimla Toy Train

The Kalka-Shimla Railway, built in 1903, is an engineering marvel with 103 tunnels and 800+ bridges. The complete journey from Kalka to Shimla takes about 5-6 hours, but you can experience a shorter ride between Shimla and nearby stations like Barog or Taradevi.

Day 4

Shimla to Manali

Morning: Departure to Manali

After an early breakfast, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Manali (approximately 250 km, 7-8 hours drive). The route takes you through beautiful landscapes, pine forests, and alongside the Beas River.

Stops en route: Sundernagar Lake, Pandoh Dam, and Kullu Valley.

Afternoon: Kullu Valley

Stop at Kullu, known for its shawl industry and adventure activities. Visit the Kullu Shawl Factory and shop for authentic Kullu shawls, caps, and other woolen items.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a riverside restaurant in Kullu.

Evening: Arrival in Manali

Reach Manali by evening and check into your hotel. Take some time to rest and acclimatize to the higher altitude. If time permits, take a short stroll along Mall Road in Manali.

Accommodation: The Himalayan, Span Resort & Spa, or Manu Allaya Resort for luxury stays; Honeymoon Inn Manali or Johnson Lodge for mid-range options.

Day 5

Manali Local Sightseeing

Morning: Hadimba Temple & Vashisht

Start your day with a visit to the ancient Hadimba Temple, a unique wooden temple dedicated to Goddess Hadimba. Continue to Vashisht Village, known for its hot water springs and temples dedicated to sage Vashisht.

Activity: Take a dip in the natural hot springs at Vashisht, known for their medicinal properties.

Afternoon: Manu Temple & Tibetan Monastery

Visit the Manu Temple, dedicated to sage Manu, the creator of human race according to Hindu mythology. Explore the Tibetan Monastery and the handicraft center to witness Tibetan culture and art.

Lunch: Enjoy traditional Himachali cuisine at Johnson's Cafe or Drifters' Inn.

Evening: Old Manali & Mall Road

Explore Old Manali, known for its laid-back atmosphere, cafes, and shops. Cross the Manalsu River via a wooden bridge to reach this charming area. Spend the evening at Mall Road, shopping for souvenirs and enjoying the vibrant atmosphere.

Dinner: Try trout fish, a local specialty, at one of the riverside restaurants in Try trout fish, a local specialty, at one of the riverside restaurants in Old Manali or enjoy international cuisine at Casa Bella Vista.

Day 6

Solang Valley Adventure

Morning: Solang Valley

After breakfast, drive to Solang Valley (around 14 km from Manali), a picturesque valley offering panoramic views of glaciers and snow-capped mountains. It's a popular spot for adventure activities.

Summer activities: Paragliding, zorbing, horse riding, and ATV rides.

Winter activities: Skiing, snowboarding, and snow scooter rides.

Afternoon: Gondola Ride

Take a gondola (cable car) ride to the top of the mountain for breathtaking views of the valley and surrounding peaks. The first phase takes you to a mid-way point with restaurants and souvenir shops.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at one of the restaurants at the mid-way point or back in the valley.

Evening: Riverside Relaxation

Return to Manali and spend the evening relaxing by the Beas River. Many riverside cafes and sitting areas offer a peaceful environment to unwind after an adventurous day.

Dinner: Enjoy a bonfire dinner at Johnson's Cafe or River Music Cafe with live music.

Day 7

Rohtang Pass Excursion

Early Morning: Departure to Rohtang Pass

Start very early (around 5-6 AM) for Rohtang Pass (51 km from Manali), as it takes about 2-3 hours to reach and permits are limited. The pass, at an altitude of 3,978 meters (13,050 ft), offers spectacular views of glaciers and snow-capped peaks.

Note: Rohtang Pass requires a permit which should be obtained a day in advance. Your hotel can help arrange this. The pass is usually open from May to November, depending on weather conditions.

Morning to Afternoon: Snow Activities

Enjoy various snow activities at Rohtang Pass like skiing, snow scooter rides, and sledging (depending on snow conditions). Take in the breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.

Lunch: Carry packed lunch or eat at one of the small dhabas (food stalls) at Rohtang.

Evening: Return to Manali

Begin your return journey to Manali by early afternoon to avoid traffic congestion and reach before dark. On the way back, stop at Gulaba or Marhi for tea and snacks.

Evening activity: Relax at your hotel or explore the local markets for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Snow-covered Rohtang Pass

Important Information
  • Rohtang Pass requires a permit which costs around â‚¹500 per vehicle.
  • The number of vehicles allowed per day is limited, so obtain your permit a day in advance.
  • Carry warm clothes, sunglasses, sunscreen, and adequate water.
  • Altitude sickness is common, so carry necessary medication if you're prone to it.
  • The road to Rohtang can be congested during peak season, so start early.
Rohtang Pass

Rohtang Pass, meaning "pile of corpses" in Tibetan, got its name due to the number of people who lost their lives trying to cross it in the past. Today, it's a popular tourist destination offering breathtaking views of the Himalayan range and is the gateway to Lahaul and Spiti valleys.

Day 8

Manali to Kasol

Morning: Departure to Kasol

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Kasol (approximately 80 km, 3-4 hours drive). Kasol is a picturesque village in Parvati Valley, known for its hippie culture, Israeli influence, and stunning natural beauty.

En route: Stop at Naggar Castle, a medieval castle built in the 15th century that now serves as a heritage hotel and museum.

Afternoon: Arrival & Exploration

Reach Kasol by afternoon and check into your accommodation. After lunch, explore the main market area of Kasol, known for its bohemian vibe, cafes, and shops selling handicrafts and clothing.

Lunch: Try Israeli cuisine at one of the many cafes like Moon Dance Cafe or Evergreen Cafe.

Evening: Chalal Trek

Take a short and easy trek to Chalal village (approximately 30 minutes one way), located across a suspension bridge over the Parvati River. The trek offers beautiful views of the valley and river.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at one of the riverside cafes in Kasol, offering a variety of cuisines from Israeli to Italian and Indian.

Kasol Village

About Kasol

Kasol, often referred to as "Mini Israel of India," is a small village in Himachal Pradesh's Parvati Valley. It's known for its scenic beauty, hippie culture, and as a base for treks to places like Kheerganga and Tosh. The village is situated along the banks of the Parvati River and offers a relaxed atmosphere with numerous cafes serving various international cuisines.

Day 9

Kasol & Manikaran

Morning: Leisure in Kasol

Enjoy a leisurely morning in Kasol. Take a walk along the Parvati River, relax at a riverside cafe, or explore more of the local market. Kasol is known for its laid-back atmosphere, perfect for a relaxed morning.

Activity: Try yoga or meditation by the riverside, offered by several centers in Kasol.

Afternoon: Manikaran Sahib

Visit Manikaran Sahib (approximately 4 km from Kasol), a pilgrimage site for both Hindus and Sikhs. The place is famous for its hot springs and the Manikaran Sahib Gurudwara. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati once spent 1,100 years here.

Experience: Take a dip in the hot springs, known for their medicinal properties. Visit the Gurudwara and enjoy the langar (community meal).

Evening: Tosh Village (Optional)

For those interested, take a short trip to Tosh Village (approximately 18 km from Kasol), a beautiful hamlet offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. The village is known for its pristine beauty and traditional Himachali houses.

Dinner: Return to Kasol for dinner or enjoy a meal at one of the cafes in Tosh if you decide to stay longer.

Day 10

Kasol to Dharamshala

Morning: Departure to Dharamshala

After breakfast, check out from your accommodation and begin your journey to Dharamshala (approximately 250 km, 7-8 hours drive). The route takes you through beautiful landscapes, including the Kangra Valley.

En route: Stop at Baijnath Temple, an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, built in the early 13th century.

Afternoon: Palampur Tea Gardens

Stop at Palampur, known as the "Tea Capital of North India." Visit the tea gardens and a tea factory to learn about the tea-making process. Enjoy a cup of freshly brewed Kangra tea.

Lunch: Have lunch at a local restaurant in Palampur.

Evening: Arrival in Dharamshala

Reach Dharamshala by evening and check into your hotel. If time permits, take a short stroll around the area to get a feel of this beautiful hill station, known for its Tibetan influence and as the residence of the Dalai Lama.

Accommodation: Fortune Park Moksha, Hyatt Regency Dharamshala Resort, or Pride Surya Mountain Resort for luxury stays; Hotel Bhagsu or Spring Valley Resort for mid-range options.

Day 11

McLeodganj Exploration

Morning: Namgyal Monastery & Tsuglagkhang Complex

Visit the Namgyal Monastery and Tsuglagkhang Complex (Dalai Lama's Temple) in McLeodganj, the heart of the Tibetan community in exile. Explore the monastery, temple, and museum to learn about Tibetan culture, history, and Buddhism.

Activity: Attend a meditation session or Buddhist philosophy class offered at various centers in McLeodganj.

Afternoon: McLeodganj Market & Lunch

Explore the vibrant markets of McLeodganj, known for Tibetan handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. Visit the Tibetan Institute of Performing Arts to learn about Tibetan music, dance, and theater.

Lunch: Try authentic Tibetan cuisine like momos, thukpa, and butter tea at restaurants like Tibet Kitchen or Lung Ta.

Evening: Bhagsu Waterfall & Sunset Point

Visit the Bhagsu Waterfall, a popular tourist spot near McLeodganj. The waterfall is surrounded by cafes and offers a refreshing atmosphere. Later, head to Naddi Sunset Point to witness a spectacular sunset over the Dhauladhar range.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at one of the rooftop restaurants in McLeodganj with views of the mountains.

Day 12

Dharamshala & Departure

Morning: Dharamshala Cricket Stadium

Visit the HPCA Cricket Stadium, one of the most scenic cricket stadiums in the world, set against the backdrop of the Dhauladhar range. Take a tour of the stadium and enjoy the panoramic views.

Activity: Visit the War Memorial nearby, dedicated to the brave soldiers of Himachal Pradesh.

Afternoon: Last-Minute Shopping

Spend some time shopping for souvenirs, Tibetan handicrafts, and local products. The Kotwali Bazaar in Dharamshala is a good place for last-minute shopping.

Lunch: Enjoy a farewell lunch at a local restaurant.

Evening: Departure

Depending on your onward journey, depart from Dharamshala. The nearest airport is Gaggal Airport (about 13 km from Dharamshala) with limited flights. Alternatively, you can drive to Pathankot (85 km) for train connections or to Chandigarh (250 km) for more flight options.

Note: If your flight is the next day, you can stay an extra night in Dharamshala or drive to Chandigarh and stay there before your departure.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March to May): Pleasant weather, blooming flowers, ideal for most activities
  • Summer (June to August): Warm days, cool nights, perfect for escaping the plains' heat
  • Autumn (September to November): Clear skies, excellent views, comfortable temperatures
  • Winter (December to February): Snowfall in many areas, ideal for snow activities and winter sports
  • Avoid monsoon season (July to mid-September) as landslides are common

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airports are at Chandigarh, Dharamshala (Gaggal), and Kullu (Bhuntar)
  • Train: Nearest major railway stations are at Chandigarh, Pathankot, and Kalka
  • Road: Well-connected by road to major cities, but mountain roads can be challenging
  • Local Transport: Taxis, local buses, and shared jeeps are available
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers are key; pack for varying temperatures
  • Summer: Light woolens, comfortable walking shoes, rain jacket
  • Winter: Heavy woolens, thermal wear, gloves, muffler, snow boots
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, sunscreen, hat, moisturizer, lip balm
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills, basic first aid
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
