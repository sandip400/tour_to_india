Assam: Land of Tea, Wildlife, and Heritage

Assam offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse wildlife. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the best of what this northeastern Indian state has to offer, from the bustling city of Guwahati to the majestic national parks, ancient temples, and the world's largest river island.

Highlights of the Tour

Ancient temples and Ahom monuments
Authentic Assamese cuisine
One-horned rhinos in Kaziranga
Asia's first oil refinery in Digboi
Mighty Brahmaputra River
Tea plantation tours
Vibrant cultural experiences
Tiger spotting in Manas National Park

Day 1

Arrival in Guwahati & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Guwahati. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Vivanta Guwahati or Radisson Blu for luxury stays; Hotel Brahmaputra Ashok or Kiranshree Grand for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Kamakhya Temple

Enjoy your first Assamese meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Paradise' or 'Maihang'. Visit the sacred Kamakhya Temple, one of the most revered Shakti Peethas in India, located atop Nilachal Hill.

Lunch recommendation: Try Assamese classics like Masor Tenga (sour fish curry), Khar (traditional alkaline dish), and Pitha (rice cakes).

Evening: Brahmaputra Riverfront

Visit the Brahmaputra Riverfront at sunset for spectacular views. Take a leisurely stroll along the Umananda Ghat. Enjoy dinner at Pan Bazar or Fancy Bazar, Guwahati's food and shopping hubs.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance showcasing Bihu dance at Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra.

Kamakhya Temple

Brahmaputra River

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Dress modestly when visiting Kamakhya Temple.
  • The view of the Brahmaputra River is best during sunset.
Day 2

Guwahati Cultural Tour

Morning: Assam State Museum

Start your day with a visit to the Assam State Museum, which houses a rich collection of sculptures, artifacts, and handicrafts showcasing the cultural heritage of Assam and the Northeast.

Activity: Explore the various galleries featuring archaeological finds, ethnographic items, and traditional art forms.

Afternoon: Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra

Visit Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra, a cultural institution that preserves and promotes the cultural heritage of Assam. Explore the museum, library, and open-air theater showcasing the life and works of Srimanta Sankardev, the Vaishnavite saint and reformer.

Note: Don't miss the Heritage Museum and the Artists' Village within the complex.

Evening: Fancy Bazar & River Cruise

Explore Fancy Bazar, one of the oldest and busiest markets in Guwahati. Take an evening cruise on the Brahmaputra River for a different perspective of the city.

Dinner: Try Assamese street food like Ghila Pitha, Luchi-Aloor Dom, and Jolpan at local eateries.

Day 3

Guwahati to Kaziranga National Park

Morning: Departure to Kaziranga

After breakfast, depart from Guwahati to Kaziranga National Park (approximately 4-5 hours by road). Enjoy the scenic drive through the Assamese countryside, with tea gardens and small villages dotting the landscape.

Note: Make a stop at Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary en route if time permits, known for having the highest density of one-horned rhinos in the world.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Kaziranga and check into your resort or lodge. Have lunch at the resort or at a local restaurant serving authentic Assamese cuisine.

Accommodation options: Diphlu River Lodge, Infinity Resort Kaziranga, or Wild Grass Lodge for a comfortable stay close to nature.

Evening: Orientation & Cultural Program

Attend an orientation session about Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to two-thirds of the world's one-horned rhinoceros population. Enjoy a cultural program showcasing traditional Assamese or tribal dances at your resort.

Evening activity: Visit the Orchid and Biodiversity Park near Kaziranga to see rare orchid species and learn about local flora.

Kaziranga National Park

Important Information
  • Kaziranga National Park is open from November 1 to April 30.
  • The park is divided into four ranges: Central (Kohora), Western (Bagori), Eastern (Agoratoli), and Burapahar.
  • Pre-book your safari permits to avoid last-minute hassles.
  • Carry binoculars, camera with zoom lens, and field guides for a better wildlife experience.
  • Dress in earth tones (olive green, brown, khaki) for safari.
Wildlife Spotting

Kaziranga is home to the "Big Five": One-horned Rhinoceros, Royal Bengal Tiger, Asian Elephant, Wild Water Buffalo, and Swamp Deer. The park also hosts over 400 bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

Day 4

Kaziranga Safari Day

Early Morning: Jeep Safari

Wake up early for a morning jeep safari in the Central Range (Kohora) of Kaziranga National Park. This is the best time to spot wildlife as animals are most active during the early hours. Look out for the one-horned rhinoceros, elephants, wild buffaloes, and if you're lucky, the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger.

Note: The safari starts at around 5:30 AM and lasts for about 2-3 hours.

Late Morning: Breakfast & Relaxation

Return to your resort for a hearty breakfast. Spend some time relaxing at the resort, sharing your wildlife sightings with fellow travelers, or reviewing your photographs.

Activity: Visit the Kaziranga National Orchid and Biodiversity Park to see rare orchid species and medicinal plants.

Afternoon: Elephant Safari

Experience an elephant safari in the Western Range (Bagori) of the park. Riding on an elephant gives you a different perspective and allows you to get closer to the rhinos and other wildlife.

Note: Elephant safaris need to be booked well in advance as they are limited and in high demand.

Evening: Birdwatching & Cultural Show

Visit the Eastern Range (Agoratoli) for birdwatching, especially around the Sohola Lake area. Return to your resort for dinner and enjoy a cultural show featuring traditional Assamese or Mising tribal dances.

Dinner: Try the traditional Assamese thali at your resort, featuring dishes like Masor Tenga, Aloo Pitika, and Khar.

Day 5

Kaziranga to Majuli Island

Morning: Drive to Nimati Ghat

After breakfast, check out from your Kaziranga accommodation and drive to Nimati Ghat near Jorhat (approximately 2-3 hours). From here, you'll take a ferry to Majuli, the world's largest river island.

Note: The ferry ride takes about 1-1.5 hours and offers beautiful views of the Brahmaputra River. Ferries usually operate from morning till afternoon.

Afternoon: Ferry to Majuli & Check-in

Take the ferry to Majuli Island. Upon arrival, check into your guesthouse or eco-resort. Majuli is known for its unique culture, Vaishnavite monasteries (Satras), and migratory birds.

Accommodation: La Maison de Ananda, Mepo Okum Eco Resort, or traditional homestays for an authentic experience.

Evening: Explore Satras

Visit some of the famous Satras (Vaishnavite monasteries) like Auniati Satra and Kamalabari Satra. These monasteries are centers of traditional art, dance, and culture, preserving ancient traditions for centuries.

Evening activity: Witness a traditional mask-making demonstration or a Sattriya dance performance, recognized as one of India's classical dance forms.

Ferry to Majuli

Sattriya Dance Performance

About Majuli

Majuli is the world's largest river island, situated in the Brahmaputra River. It's a hub of Assamese culture and is famous for its Satras (Vaishnavite monasteries), which preserve ancient religious traditions, art forms, and artifacts. The island is also known for its vibrant tribal culture, mask making, pottery, and handloom.

Area: Approximately 350 sq km (shrinking due to erosion)
Home to Mising, Deori, and Sonowal Kachari tribes
Important bird area with migratory birds in winter
Day 6

Exploring Majuli Island

Morning: Village Tour

Rent a bicycle to explore more of Majuli Island. Visit local villages to see traditional pottery making by the Kumar community and mask making at Samaguri Satra. Interact with locals and learn about their way of life.

Activity: Try your hand at pottery making or participate in a traditional handloom weaving demonstration.

Afternoon: Cultural Immersion

Visit more Satras including Bengenaati Satra and Uttar Kamalabari Satra. Each Satra has its own unique traditions and specialties in art, music, and dance forms.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Mising tribal lunch at a local homestay, featuring dishes like fish wrapped in banana leaves, bamboo shoot pickle, and rice beer.

Evening: Sunset by the Brahmaputra

Head to the riverbank to witness a spectacular sunset over the Brahmaputra River. The island offers stunning views of the river and surrounding landscapes.

Evening activity: Participate in a community cultural program where you can learn traditional Bihu dance steps and enjoy folk music performances.

Mask Making in Majuli

Mising Tribal Village

Mask Making Tradition

The mask-making tradition of Majuli, particularly at Samaguri Satra, is a unique cultural heritage. These masks, made from bamboo frames covered with cloth, clay, and cow dung, are used in traditional Bhaona performances (religious plays). The masks represent various characters from Hindu mythology and are an integral part of the island's cultural identity.

Masks represent gods, demons, animals, and mythological characters
Made using traditional techniques passed down for generations
Some masks have movable parts for realistic expressions
Day 7

Majuli to Sivasagar

Morning: Birdwatching

Wake up early for a birdwatching session along the riverbanks. Majuli is home to numerous migratory and resident bird species, especially during the winter months.

Activity: Visit a local Mising village to see their unique stilt houses and learn about their sustainable lifestyle adapted to the flood-prone environment.

Afternoon: Ferry Back & Drive to Sivasagar

Take the ferry back to Nimati Ghat and then drive to Sivasagar (approximately 1 hour), the ancient capital of the Ahom Kingdom that ruled Assam for nearly 600 years. Check into your hotel in Sivasagar.

Accommodation: Hotel Brahmaputra, Hotel Shiva Palace, or similar mid-range options in Sivasagar.

Evening: Sivasagar Exploration

Begin exploring Sivasagar by visiting the iconic Shiva Dol, one of the tallest Shiva temples in India. Also visit the adjacent Vishnu Dol and Devi Dol, forming a complex of three temples.

Dinner: Try local Assamese cuisine at restaurants like Kunjaban or Atithi, known for their authentic flavors.

Birdwatching in Majuli

Shiva Dol, Sivasagar

Ahom Dynasty

The Ahom Dynasty ruled Assam for nearly 600 years (1228-1826), one of the longest-ruling dynasties in Indian history. Sivasagar (formerly Rangpur) served as their capital, and the region is dotted with monuments showcasing their architectural prowess and cultural heritage.

Talatal Ghar: Multi-storied royal palace with underground tunnels
Joysagar: One of the largest man-made tanks in India
Rang Ghar: Two-storied oval-shaped pavilion, Asia's oldest amphitheater
Day 8

Sivasagar to Dibrugarh

Morning: Ahom Monuments

Visit the key Ahom monuments in Sivasagar. Start with Talatal Ghar, a seven-storied palace with three underground floors. Continue to Rang Ghar, an amphitheater used by Ahom kings to watch buffalo fights and other sports. Finally, visit Kareng Ghar, another royal palace.

Also visit: Joysagar, one of the largest man-made tanks in India, built by the Ahom kings in 1697.

Afternoon: Drive to Dibrugarh

After lunch, drive to Dibrugarh (approximately 1.5 hours), known as the "Tea City of India." Check into your hotel in Dibrugarh.

Accommodation: Mancotta Heritage Chang Bungalow, Hotel Tea County, or similar comfortable options in Dibrugarh.

Evening: Tea Garden Visit

Visit a nearby tea estate to learn about tea cultivation, processing, and the history of tea in Assam. Enjoy a tea tasting session to appreciate the distinctive flavors of Assam tea.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try local cuisine at restaurants like Mancotta Heritage Dining or Bongaigaon Restaurant.

Rang Ghar, Sivasagar

Tea Garden, Dibrugarh

Assam Tea Heritage

Assam is the world's largest tea-growing region and produces some of the finest black teas. The tea industry in Assam dates back to the 1830s when the British discovered the indigenous tea plants growing wild in the region. Today, Assam tea is known for its bold, malty flavor and bright color, making it perfect for breakfast blends.

Grown in the lowlands of the Brahmaputra Valley
Thrives in the region's hot, humid climate
Known for its strong, full-bodied flavor
Day 9

Dibrugarh to Digboi

Morning: Tea Factory Tour

Visit a tea factory to see the complete tea-making process from plucking to packaging. Learn about the different grades of tea and the factors that influence tea quality.

Activity: Try your hand at tea plucking in the garden and learn the technique from experienced tea pluckers.

Afternoon: Drive to Digboi

After lunch, drive to Digboi (approximately 2 hours), known as the "Oil City of Assam." Digboi is home to Asia's first oil refinery, established in 1901, and has a rich industrial heritage.

Accommodation: Digboi Club, Hotel Raj Palace, or similar options in Digboi.

Evening: Digboi Oil Town Exploration

Explore the historic oil town of Digboi, with its colonial-era bungalows and industrial heritage. Visit the Digboi Centenary Museum to learn about the history of oil exploration in India.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at the Digboi Club, a colonial-era establishment with a rich history and ambiance.

Tea Factory Tour

Digboi Oil Refinery

Digboi: The Oil Heritage

Digboi holds the distinction of being home to Asia's first oil refinery and one of the world's oldest continuously operating refineries. The name "Digboi" is said to have originated from the phrase "dig-boy-dig," which British engineers would shout to workers as they dug for oil. The town's history is deeply intertwined with the story of oil exploration in India.

First oil well drilled in 1889
Refinery established in 1901
War Cemetery commemorates Allied soldiers of WWII
Day 10

Digboi to Nameri National Park

Morning: Digboi War Cemetery

Visit the Digboi War Cemetery, which contains graves of Allied soldiers who died during World War II. The cemetery is beautifully maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission.

Also visit: The still-operational oil wells and the historic Digboi Club, a reminder of the British colonial era.

Afternoon: Drive to Nameri

After lunch, embark on a long drive to Nameri National Park (approximately 6-7 hours). The journey takes you through changing landscapes, from tea gardens to forested hills.

Accommodation: Eco Camp Nameri, Nameri Jungle Camp, or similar eco-friendly accommodations near the park.

Evening: Arrival & Orientation

Arrive at your accommodation near Nameri National Park. Attend an orientation session about the park, its biodiversity, and the activities planned for the next day.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple but delicious dinner at your camp, often featuring local ingredients and recipes.

Digboi War Cemetery

Nameri Eco Camp

Nameri National Park

Nameri National Park, located at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas, is a biodiversity hotspot known for its pristine forests, diverse wildlife, and the crystal-clear Jia Bhoreli River flowing through it. The park is contiguous with Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary in Arunachal Pradesh, forming a large conservation area.

Home to tigers, elephants, and over 300 bird species
Known for the endangered Golden Mahseer fish
Offers excellent river rafting opportunities
Day 11

Nameri to Manas National Park

Morning: Nameri Exploration

Start your day with an early morning nature walk in Nameri National Park, accompanied by a forest guide. The park is known for its rich birdlife, including the endangered White-winged Wood Duck.

Activity: If interested, try angling for the Golden Mahseer in the Jia Bhoreli River (catch and release only) or enjoy a river rafting experience.

Afternoon: Drive to Manas

After lunch, drive to Manas National Park (approximately 5-6 hours), a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India's most beautiful wildlife reserves, situated at the foothills of the Himalayas.

Accommodation: Manas Jungle Lodge, Bansbari Lodge, or similar accommodations near the park entrance.

Evening: Arrival & Cultural Program

Check into your accommodation near Manas National Park. Attend a cultural program showcasing the traditions of the local Bodo tribe, known for their colorful dances and music.

Dinner: Enjoy a dinner featuring local Bodo cuisine, known for its unique flavors and ingredients.

Birdwatching in Nameri

Manas National Park

Manas National Park

Manas National Park, named after the Manas River that flows through it, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, a Project Tiger Reserve, an Elephant Reserve, and a Biosphere Reserve. The park is known for its exceptional biodiversity and is home to some of India's most endangered species.

Home to rare species like Golden Langur and Pygmy Hog
Spectacular backdrop of the Himalayan foothills
Rich cultural heritage of the indigenous Bodo tribe
Day 12

Manas to Guwahati & Departure

Early Morning: Manas Safari

Wake up early for a jeep safari in Manas National Park. Look out for tigers, elephants, rhinos, and various deer species. The park is also home to unique species like the Golden Langur and Pygmy Hog.

Note: The safari starts at around 6:00 AM and lasts for about 2-3 hours.

Late Morning: Return Journey

After breakfast, check out from your accommodation and begin your return journey to Guwahati (approximately 4-5 hours). You may want to break the journey with a stop at a local restaurant for lunch.

Alternative: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Guwahati.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Guwahati. Pan Bazar and Fancy Bazar are good options for souvenirs like Assamese silk (Muga, Eri, Pat), handloom products, and tea.

Departure: Transfer to Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to April: Ideal time to visit Assam with pleasant weather
  • November to February: Best time for wildlife viewing in national parks
  • April: Experience the Bihu festival, Assam's most important cultural celebration
  • Avoid the monsoon season (May to September) as heavy rainfall can cause flooding

Transportation

  • Air: Guwahati is well-connected to major Indian cities; Dibrugarh and Jorhat also have airports
  • Train: Good rail connectivity with major stations at Guwahati, Jorhat, and Dibrugarh
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in Guwahati; shared taxis and buses for intercity travel
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience
  • Ferry services are essential for visiting river islands like Majuli

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes; warm layers for winter months
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • Safari gear: Earth-toned clothing, hat, sunglasses, binoculars
  • Protection: Sunscreen, insect repellent, rain gear
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera with zoom lens, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Assamese cuisine is characterized by minimal use of spices and a focus on fresh ingredients:

Main Dishes
  • Masor Tenga (sour fish curry)
  • Khar (prepared with raw papaya and pulses)
  • Aloo Pitika (mashed potato)
  • Ou Tenga Masor Jul (elephant apple fish curry)
  • Paro Manxo (pigeon meat curry)
  • Xaak Aru Bhaji (leafy vegetables)
Snacks & Sweets
  • Pitha (rice cakes in various forms)
  • Laru (sweet balls made of coconut or rice)
  • Jolpan (traditional breakfast items)
  • Kola Khar (banana stem preparation)
  • Til Pitha (sesame rice cake)
  • Narikol Laru (coconut sweet)

Practical Information

  • Language: Assamese is the primary language. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Assam is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Festivals: Bihu (April, October, January), Ambubachi Mela (June), and Tea Festival (November) are major celebrations
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
