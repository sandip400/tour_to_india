Karnataka: A Land of Royal Heritage

Karnataka offers a perfect blend of ancient temples, royal palaces, lush coffee plantations, and pristine beaches. From the tech hub of Bengaluru to the majestic ruins of Hampi, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this southern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Bengaluru & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru. Transfer to your hotel in central Bengaluru. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Leela Palace or Taj West End for luxury stays; The Park or Radisson Blu for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Enjoy your first Karnataka meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Vidyarthi Bhavan' or 'MTR'. Visit the Lalbagh Botanical Garden, a 240-acre garden with a diverse collection of tropical plants and a magnificent glasshouse.

Lunch recommendation: Try Karnataka classics like Bisi Bele Bath, Masala Dosa, and Filter Coffee.

Evening: Cubbon Park & MG Road

Visit Cubbon Park, a 300-acre green space in the heart of the city. Explore the Government Museum and Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum. End your day with shopping and dinner at MG Road, one of Bengaluru's most vibrant commercial streets.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance at Ranga Shankara or Chowdiah Memorial Hall.

Day 1 Tips
  • Bengaluru traffic can be heavy, so plan your movements accordingly.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Lalbagh has a small entry fee and is closed during lunch hours.
  • The weather in Bengaluru is pleasant year-round, but carry a light jacket for evenings.
Day 2

Bengaluru to Mysuru

Morning: Drive to Mysuru

After breakfast, drive to Mysuru (approximately 3-4 hours). The journey takes you through scenic countryside and small towns. En route, you can stop at Srirangapatna, an island fortress town with historical significance.

Activity: Visit Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace and Dariya Daulat Bagh in Srirangapatna.

Afternoon: Mysuru Palace

Check into your hotel in Mysuru. After lunch, visit the magnificent Mysuru Palace, one of the most visited monuments in India. The palace is a splendid example of Indo-Saracenic architecture with intricate carvings, stained glass, and mirror work.

Note: Photography inside the palace requires a separate ticket. The palace is illuminated on Sundays and public holidays.

Evening: Devaraja Market & Dinner

Explore the vibrant Devaraja Market, a traditional market selling fruits, vegetables, flowers, spices, and souvenirs. It's a great place to experience local life and pick up some Mysore Pak (a local sweet) and sandalwood products.

Dinner: Try authentic Mysuru cuisine at restaurants like Mylari or Hotel RRR for their famous Mysore Masala Dosa.

Day 3

Mysuru to Coorg

Morning: Chamundi Hills

Start your day with a visit to Chamundi Hills, located 13 km from Mysuru city. Visit the Chamundeshwari Temple at the top and the massive Nandi (bull) statue on the way. Enjoy panoramic views of Mysuru city from the hilltop.

Note: You can either drive up or climb the 1,000 steps to the top. The temple gets crowded during festivals.

Afternoon: Drive to Coorg

After lunch, drive to Coorg (Kodagu), known as the "Scotland of India" (approximately 3-4 hours). The journey takes you through coffee plantations, spice gardens, and misty hills. Check into your resort or homestay in Coorg.

Accommodation: Taj Madikeri, Orange County, or a coffee plantation homestay for an authentic experience.

Evening: Plantation Walk

Take a leisurely walk around a coffee plantation to learn about coffee cultivation and processing. Many homestays and resorts offer guided tours of their estates.

Dinner: Try Kodava cuisine, known for its unique flavors and spices. Don't miss Pandi Curry (pork curry) and Kadambuttu (rice dumplings).

Coorg Coffee Plantation

Important Information
  • Coorg has a cool climate, so pack accordingly with light woolens.
  • Mobile network can be patchy in some areas of Coorg.
  • Carry insect repellent as you'll be in a plantation area.
  • Respect local Kodava culture and traditions.
  • The best time to visit is from October to March.
Coorg Specialties

Coorg is famous for its coffee, but don't miss out on other local specialties like honey, spices (especially black pepper and cardamom), and homemade wines. Many plantations sell these products directly to visitors.

Day 4

Coorg Exploration

Morning: Abbey Falls

Visit Abbey Falls, a beautiful waterfall nestled amidst coffee plantations and spice estates. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery and offer a refreshing experience. A hanging bridge in front of the falls provides a good view.

Note: The path to the falls involves a short trek through coffee plantations. Wear comfortable shoes and carry water.

Afternoon: Talakaveri & Golden Temple

Visit Talakaveri, the source of the River Cauvery, located on the slopes of Brahmagiri Hills. The site has a temple and a tank with a spring that is considered the origin of the river. Later, visit the Namdroling Monastery (Golden Temple) in Bylakuppe, the second largest Tibetan settlement outside Tibet.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local restaurant near Bylakuppe.

Evening: Madikeri Fort

Explore Madikeri Fort, built in the 17th century and later rebuilt by Tipu Sultan. The fort houses a temple, a church, a prison, and a museum. Enjoy the sunset from Raja's Seat, a seasonal garden with a viewing platform offering panoramic views of the valleys and hills.

Dinner: Try more Kodava specialties at a local restaurant in Madikeri town.

Day 5

Coorg to Chikmagalur

Morning: Dubare Elephant Camp

Visit Dubare Elephant Camp, where you can interact with elephants, help bathe them, and learn about these gentle giants. The camp is located on the banks of the River Cauvery and offers a unique wildlife experience.

Note: The elephant activities are usually scheduled in the morning. Arrive early to make the most of your visit.

Afternoon: Drive to Chikmagalur

After lunch, drive to Chikmagalur (approximately 3-4 hours), another coffee-growing region in Karnataka. The journey takes you through scenic Western Ghats with beautiful landscapes. Check into your hotel or homestay in Chikmagalur.

Accommodation: The Gateway Chikmagalur, Trivik Hotels & Resorts, or a coffee estate homestay.

Evening: Mullayanagiri Sunset

If time permits, drive to Mullayanagiri, the highest peak in Karnataka, to catch the sunset. The viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Western Ghats and surrounding coffee estates.

Dinner: Return to your accommodation for dinner or try local cuisine at restaurants like Siri Coffee or The Deck.

About Chikmagalur

Chikmagalur, which means "The Land of the Younger Daughter" in Kannada, is known for its coffee plantations, hills, and pleasant climate. It's said that coffee was first introduced to India when a Sufi saint, Baba Budan, smuggled seven coffee beans from Yemen to the hills of Chikmagalur in the 17th century.

Home to Karnataka's highest peak
Known as the "Coffee Land of Karnataka"
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Day 6

Chikmagalur to Hampi

Morning: Baba Budangiri

Visit Baba Budangiri, a mountain range named after the Sufi saint who introduced coffee to India. The area has religious significance for both Hindus and Muslims. Explore the caves and the shrine of Baba Budan.

Also visit: Manikyadhara Falls, a beautiful waterfall near Baba Budangiri.

Afternoon: Drive to Hampi

After lunch, embark on a long drive to Hampi (approximately 6-7 hours). The journey takes you through changing landscapes from the green hills of Chikmagalur to the boulder-strewn terrain of Hampi. Check into your hotel in Hampi or nearby Hospet.

Accommodation: Evolve Back Kamalapura Palace, Heritage Resort Hampi, or Hyatt Place Hampi.

Evening: Hampi Bazaar

If you arrive before sunset, take a stroll around Hampi Bazaar and Virupaksha Temple area. The temple is one of the oldest functioning temples in India and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Dinner: Try local cuisine at Mango Tree Restaurant or Laughing Buddha, popular eateries in Hampi.

About Hampi

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The ruins of Hampi spread over an area of 25 square kilometers and include temples, palaces, aquatic structures, ancient market streets, and royal pavilions.

The unique landscape of Hampi, with massive boulders balanced precariously, adds to its mystical charm.

Day 7

Hampi to Bengaluru & Departure

Morning: Hampi Ruins Exploration

Spend the morning exploring the magnificent ruins of Hampi. Visit key attractions like the Vittala Temple with its famous Stone Chariot, the Royal Enclosure, Hazara Rama Temple, and the Elephant Stables.

Note: Hire a local guide to understand the historical significance of the ruins. The site is vast, so prioritize the main attractions.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, begin your journey back to Bengaluru (approximately 6-7 hours by road). Alternatively, you can take a train from Hospet to Bengaluru or a flight from Hubli Airport (about 3 hours from Hampi).

Option: If your flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Bengaluru.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Bengaluru. Commercial Street and MG Road are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Kempegowda International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Bengaluru: Year-round, but October to February is most pleasant
  • Mysuru: September to March, especially during Dasara festival (October)
  • Coorg & Chikmagalur: October to March (avoid monsoon season from June to September)
  • Hampi: November to February (summers can be extremely hot)

Transportation

  • Air: Bengaluru is well-connected to major cities. Mysuru and Hubli also have airports
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities and towns
  • Road: Good highway connectivity, but hill roads can be winding
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in cities; local buses and taxis in smaller towns
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for Bengaluru and Hampi; light woolens for Coorg and Chikmagalur
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Practical Information

  • Language: Kannada is the official language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
