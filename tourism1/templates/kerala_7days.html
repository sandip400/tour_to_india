Kerala: God's Own Country

Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and serene experiences. From the historic port city of Kochi to the tranquil backwaters of Alleppey, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this southern Indian state has to offer.

Highlights of the Tour

Colonial architecture in Fort Kochi
Authentic Kerala cuisine
Houseboat stay in Alleppey
Tea plantations in Munnar
Wildlife in Periyar
Ayurvedic treatments

Day 1

Arrival in Kochi & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Cochin International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in Fort Kochi. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Brunton Boatyard or Taj Malabar for luxury stays; Old Harbour Hotel or Eighth Bastion for heritage options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Fort Kochi

Enjoy your first Kerala meal at a traditional restaurant. Visit the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets along the shore, a legacy of ancient Chinese explorers and traders.

Lunch recommendation: Try Kerala Sadhya (traditional meal served on banana leaf) or Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the historic St. Francis Church, the oldest European church in India. Explore Jew Town and the Paradesi Synagogue, showcasing Kerala's religious diversity. End your day with a traditional Kathakali dance performance.

Evening activity: Kathakali performance at Kerala Kathakali Centre (arrive early to see the elaborate makeup application).

Chinese Fishing Nets

Kathakali Performance

Day 1 Tips
  • Fort Kochi is best explored on foot or by renting a bicycle.
  • Book your Kathakali performance in advance during peak season.
  • The best time to photograph the Chinese Fishing Nets is during sunset.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
Day 2

Kochi Heritage Tour

Morning: Mattancherry Palace

Start your day with a visit to the Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace), known for its Kerala murals and portraits of the Rajas of Kochi. Continue to the Spice Market to learn about Kerala's historic spice trade.

Activity: Purchase some authentic Kerala spices as souvenirs.

Afternoon: Folklore Museum & Santa Cruz Basilica

Visit the Kerala Folklore Museum, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the state. Continue to the Santa Cruz Basilica, one of the oldest and most impressive churches in India.

Lunch: Try a traditional Syrian Christian meal at one of the local restaurants.

Evening: Sunset Cruise & Dinner

Take a sunset cruise in the Kochi harbor to see the city from a different perspective. Enjoy dinner at a seafood restaurant by the waterfront.

Dinner: Fresh seafood at restaurants like Fort House or Fusion Bay.

Day 3

Kochi to Munnar

Morning: Drive to Munnar

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Munnar, a picturesque hill station known for its tea plantations (approximately 4-5 hours drive). Enjoy the scenic route as you ascend into the Western Ghats.

En route: Stop at Cheeyappara and Valara waterfalls for photographs and refreshments.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive in Munnar and check into your hotel. Take some time to relax and enjoy the cool climate and beautiful surroundings.

Accommodation: Windermere Estate or Tea County for luxury stays; KTDC Tea Castle or Deshadan Mountain Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: Tea Museum & Plantation Visit

Visit the Tata Tea Museum to learn about the history and process of tea production in Munnar. Take a guided tour of a nearby tea plantation to see how tea is grown and harvested.

Evening activity: Tea tasting session to sample different varieties of local tea.

Munnar Tea Plantations

Important Information
  • The temperature in Munnar is significantly cooler than Kochi, especially in the evenings. Carry light woolens or jackets.
  • The road to Munnar has many curves and turns. If you're prone to motion sickness, take appropriate medication.
  • Munnar is at an elevation of approximately 1,600 meters (5,200 feet) above sea level.
  • The Tata Tea Museum is closed on Mondays.
Photography Tips

Munnar offers some of the most photogenic landscapes in Kerala. The rolling tea plantations are best photographed in the early morning or late afternoon when the light is soft. The mist that often covers the hills in the morning creates a magical atmosphere for photography.

Day 4

Munnar Exploration

Morning: Eravikulam National Park

Visit Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr (mountain goat). Take a guided trek through the park to enjoy the beautiful landscapes and possibly spot some wildlife.

Note: The park is closed during the breeding season of the Nilgiri Tahr, usually from January to March.

Afternoon: Top Station

After lunch, drive to Top Station, the highest point in Munnar offering panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the valley of Tamil Nadu. On a clear day, you can see as far as the plains of Tamil Nadu.

En route: Stop at Mattupetty Dam and Echo Point for more scenic views and activities.

Evening: Ayurvedic Spa & Dinner

Return to your hotel and indulge in a traditional Ayurvedic massage or treatment, a perfect way to relax after a day of exploration. Enjoy dinner at your hotel or a local restaurant.

Dinner: Try traditional Kerala dishes like Appam with Stew or Puttu with Kadala Curry.

Day 5

Munnar to Thekkady

Morning: Drive to Thekkady

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Thekkady, home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary (approximately 3-4 hours drive). The route takes you through spice plantations and small villages.

En route: Stop at a spice plantation for a guided tour to learn about various spices grown in Kerala.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive in Thekkady and check into your hotel. Have lunch and take some time to relax.

Accommodation: Spice Village or Cardamom County for eco-friendly luxury; KTDC Aranya Nivas or Periyar House for mid-range options.

Evening: Periyar Lake Boat Cruise

Take a boat cruise on Periyar Lake inside the wildlife sanctuary. This is one of the best ways to spot wildlife including elephants, deer, and various bird species coming to the lake for water.

Evening activity: Attend a Kathakali or Kalaripayattu (ancient martial art form) performance at a local cultural center.

Periyar Lake

Important Information
  • Book your boat cruise in advance as tickets can sell out, especially during peak season.
  • The best time for wildlife spotting is early morning or late afternoon.
  • Photography is allowed but flash photography is prohibited.
  • Carry binoculars if possible for better wildlife viewing.
Wildlife Spotting

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary is home to elephants, tigers, gaur, sambar deer, and various bird species. While tiger sightings are rare, you're likely to spot elephants, especially during the boat cruise. Keep your eyes peeled and maintain silence for the best wildlife viewing experience.

Day 6

Thekkady to Alleppey

Morning: Nature Walk

Take an early morning guided nature walk in the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary. These walks are led by trained tribal guides who share their knowledge of the forest ecosystem and medicinal plants.

Alternative: If available, you can opt for a bamboo rafting experience on Periyar Lake.

Afternoon: Drive to Alleppey

After lunch, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Alleppey, known as the "Venice of the East" for its network of backwaters (approximately 4-5 hours drive).

En route: Stop at Plantation County in Kumily for a spice garden tour.

Evening: Houseboat Check-in

Arrive in Alleppey and board your private houseboat (kettuvallam) for an overnight stay. Cruise through the serene backwaters, observing village life along the shores.

Experience: Enjoy a traditional Kerala dinner prepared by the onboard chef as you spend the night on the tranquil backwaters.

About Alleppey Backwaters

Alleppey (Alappuzha) is famous for its intricate network of backwaters, lagoons, and canals. The backwaters are a unique ecosystem where freshwater from rivers meets the seawater from the Arabian Sea. A houseboat stay is one of the most authentic ways to experience Kerala's backwater culture.

900 km of interconnected waterways
Traditional rice farming below sea level
Rich in aquatic life and bird species
Day 7

Alleppey to Kochi & Departure

Morning: Backwater Sunrise

Wake up early to witness the sunrise over the backwaters. Enjoy a traditional Kerala breakfast on the houseboat as you continue cruising through the narrow canals.

Activity: Observe the early morning activities of the villagers along the backwaters, including fishing and farming.

Mid-morning: Houseboat Check-out

Return to the dock and disembark from your houseboat. If time permits, take a short tour of Alleppey town, visiting the beach and the 150-year-old pier extending into the sea.

Optional: Visit the International Coir Museum to learn about the coir industry, an important part of Kerala's economy.

Afternoon: Return to Kochi

Drive back to Kochi (approximately 1.5-2 hours). Depending on your departure time, you may have some free time for last-minute shopping or relaxation.

Shopping: Lulu Mall or MG Road for souvenirs, spices, and Kerala handicrafts.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Cochin International Airport for your onward journey, taking with you memories of God's Own Country.

Note: If your flight departs the next day, we can arrange an additional night's stay in Kochi.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Peak Season: October to February (winter months)
  • Monsoon: June to September (ideal for Ayurvedic treatments)
  • Summer: March to May (hot and humid in lowlands, pleasant in hill stations)
  • Avoid the heavy monsoon months (July-August) if you plan extensive outdoor activities

Transportation

  • Air: Cochin International Airport is well-connected to major cities
  • Train: Extensive rail network with stations in major towns
  • Local Transport: State-run KSRTC buses, private buses, auto-rickshaws
  • Consider hiring a car with driver for the entire trip for convenience
  • Water transport is common in the backwater regions

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for lowlands; light woolens for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • Protection: Sunscreen, insect repellent, hat, umbrella
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Binoculars for wildlife viewing

Food & Cuisine

Kerala cuisine is known for its spicy and flavorful dishes with coconut as a key ingredient:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Kerala Sadhya (traditional feast)
  • Appam with Stew
  • Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish)
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Kerala Prawn Curry
  • Beef Fry (Erachi Ularthiyathu)
Beverages & Desserts
  • Tender Coconut Water
  • Sulaimani Tea (black tea with spices)
  • Kaapi (filter coffee)
  • Payasam (sweet pudding)
  • Unniyappam (sweet fried snack)
  • Ada Pradhaman (rice flakes pudding)

Practical Information

  • Language: Malayalam is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Kerala is one of the safest states in India for tourists
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing is appreciated, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
  • Alcohol: Available in licensed establishments and some houseboats
