Maharashtra: A Complete Experience

This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the diverse landscapes and rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra. From the bustling metropolis of Mumbai to the serene beaches of the Konkan coast, ancient cave temples, and historic forts, this journey covers the best of what this western Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Mumbai & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Mumbai. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Mahal Palace or The Oberoi for luxury stays; Trident Nariman Point or Fariyas Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & South Mumbai

Enjoy your first Maharashtrian meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Trishna' or 'Britannia & Co.'. Visit the Gateway of India, a magnificent basalt arch overlooking the Arabian Sea, and the adjacent Taj Mahal Palace Hotel.

Lunch recommendation: Try Maharashtrian classics like Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, or coastal seafood specialties.

Evening: Marine Drive

Visit the iconic Marine Drive at sunset for spectacular views. Take a leisurely stroll along this 3.6-kilometer-long boulevard, also known as the "Queen's Necklace" for its nighttime appearance. Enjoy dinner at one of the restaurants along the promenade.

Evening activity: If available, catch a Bollywood movie at a historic cinema like Regal or Eros.

Gateway of India

Marine Drive

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Mumbai's local trains are crowded during rush hours, avoid if possible.
  • South Mumbai is best explored on foot to appreciate the colonial architecture.
Day 2

Mumbai Heritage Tour

Morning: Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus & Crawford Market

Start your day with a visit to the UNESCO World Heritage Site Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (formerly Victoria Terminus), a historic railway station with stunning Victorian Gothic architecture. Continue to Crawford Market, a colonial-era market selling everything from fruits to household items.

Activity: Sample some local street food like Batata Vada or Pav Bhaji at nearby stalls.

Afternoon: Kala Ghoda & Museums

Explore the Kala Ghoda Art District, home to several art galleries and the Jehangir Art Gallery. Visit the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (formerly Prince of Wales Museum) with its extensive collection of art, archaeology, and natural history.

Lunch: Try the Parsi cuisine at Jimmy Boy or Britannia & Co. in the nearby Fort area.

Evening: Bandra & Juhu

Head to the suburbs of Bandra to see the Bandra-Worli Sea Link and Bandstand Promenade. Continue to Juhu Beach for a relaxing evening by the sea and enjoy Mumbai's famous street food like Bhel Puri and Pani Puri.

Dinner: Try coastal Maharashtrian cuisine at Highway Gomantak or Gajalee.

Day 3

Mumbai to Lonavala

Morning: Departure to Lonavala

After breakfast, check out from your Mumbai hotel and drive to Lonavala (approximately 2 hours), a picturesque hill station in the Western Ghats. The drive offers beautiful views of the mountains and valleys.

Note: You can hire a private car or take a train from Mumbai to Lonavala. Trains take approximately 2-2.5 hours.

Afternoon: Bhushi Dam & Tiger's Leap

After checking into your hotel, visit Bhushi Dam, a popular picnic spot with cascading waterfalls during monsoon. Continue to Tiger's Leap, a cliff-top with panoramic views of the valley, resembling a leaping tiger.

Lunch: Try Maharashtrian thali at a local restaurant like Rama Krishna or Buvachi Bhaji.

Evening: Karla Caves

Visit the ancient Karla Caves, a complex of rock-cut cave shrines dating back to the 2nd century BC. These Buddhist caves feature impressive chaitya halls and intricate carvings.

Accommodation: The Machan, Rhythm Lonavala, or Della Resorts for luxury stays; Hotel Kumar Plaza or United-21 for mid-range options.

Lonavala Western Ghats

Important Information
  • Lonavala is most beautiful during monsoon (June-September), but some activities may be restricted due to heavy rainfall.
  • Don't forget to buy Lonavala's famous chikki (hard candy made from jaggery and nuts) and chocolate fudge.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as most attractions involve walking on uneven terrain.
  • Carry a light jacket as evenings can be cool, especially in winter months.
Day 4

Lonavala to Pune

Morning: Lohagad Fort

After breakfast, visit Lohagad Fort, one of the many hill forts of Maharashtra. The fort, dating back to the 18th century, offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and Pawna Lake.

Note: The trek to the fort is moderate and takes about 30-45 minutes from the base.

Afternoon: Drive to Pune

Check out from your Lonavala hotel and drive to Pune (approximately 1.5 hours). Pune, known as the "Oxford of the East," is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage.

Lunch: Stop at one of the dhabas (roadside restaurants) on the way for authentic Maharashtrian food.

Evening: Pune Exploration

Check into your hotel in Pune. Visit Shaniwar Wada, an 18th-century fortified palace and the seat of the Peshwa rulers. Explore the nearby Laxmi Road, a bustling market area perfect for shopping and sampling local street food.

Accommodation: Conrad Pune, JW Marriott, or The Ritz-Carlton for luxury stays; Hotel Shreyas or Ginger Pune for mid-range options.

Lohagad Fort

About Pune

Pune is Maharashtra's cultural capital and the second-largest city in the state after Mumbai. It was once the center of power for the Maratha Empire under the Peshwas. Today, it's a thriving metropolis known for its educational institutions, IT industry, and rich cultural heritage.

Day 5

Pune Exploration

Morning: Pune Temples

Start your day with a visit to the famous Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple, one of the most visited Ganesh temples in Maharashtra. Continue to the Pataleshwar Cave Temple, a rock-cut cave temple dating back to the 8th century.

Also visit: Aga Khan Palace, a historical landmark associated with India's freedom movement and now a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi.

Afternoon: Koregaon Park & Osho Ashram

After lunch, visit the upscale neighborhood of Koregaon Park, known for its tree-lined avenues and trendy cafes. Explore the Osho International Meditation Resort, a spiritual center founded by the controversial guru Osho (formerly known as Rajneesh).

Note: The Osho Ashram requires visitors to wear maroon robes, which can be purchased at the entrance. There's also an HIV test required for entry.

Evening: Pune University & Bund Garden

Visit the beautiful campus of Savitribai Phule Pune University (formerly University of Pune). End your day with a relaxing stroll at Bund Garden, a popular recreational area along the Mula-Mutha River.

Dinner: Try authentic Puneri cuisine at Shreyas or Durvankur, known for their traditional Maharashtrian thalis.

Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple

Pune Food Guide

Pune is famous for its unique culinary offerings. Don't miss these local specialties:

  • Misal Pav: A spicy sprouts curry served with bread rolls
  • Mastani: A rich, thick milkshake topped with ice cream and dry fruits
  • Vada Pav: A potato fritter in a bun, often called the Indian burger
  • Puran Poli: A sweet flatbread stuffed with a mixture of jaggery and lentils
  • Bakarwadi: A spicy, crispy snack invented by Pune's famous Chitale Bandhu
Day 6

Pune to Kolhapur

Morning: Drive to Kolhapur

After breakfast, check out from your Pune hotel and drive to Kolhapur (approximately 4 hours). Kolhapur is a historic city known for its temples, palaces, and distinctive cuisine.

En route: Stop at Panhala Fort, a massive fort located about 20 km from Kolhapur. This 12th-century fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Afternoon: Check-in & Lunch

Check into your hotel in Kolhapur. Enjoy a traditional Kolhapuri lunch, known for its spicy and flavorful dishes.

Lunch recommendation: Try the famous Kolhapuri Tambda Rassa (red curry) and Pandhra Rassa (white curry) with Bhakri (millet flatbread).

Evening: Mahalaxmi Temple

Visit the Mahalaxmi Temple, one of the Shakti Peethas and the most important temple in Kolhapur. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi and features beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.

Accommodation: The Pavilion, Sayaji Hotel, or Hotel Shalini Palace for comfortable stays.

Panhala Fort

Mahalaxmi Temple

About Kolhapur

Kolhapur is a historic city in southern Maharashtra known for its rich cultural heritage. It was once the capital of the Maratha princely state of Kolhapur and is famous for its temples, palaces, wrestling tradition, and distinctive cuisine. The city is also known for its Kolhapuri chappals (leather sandals) and jewelry.

Day 7

Kolhapur to Ratnagiri

Morning: New Palace & Rankala Lake

Visit the New Palace (Maharaja's Palace), a beautiful palace built in the 19th century that now houses a museum with a collection of royal artifacts. Continue to Rankala Lake, a popular recreational spot with boating facilities.

Also visit: Town Hall Museum, which houses a collection of artifacts related to the history of Kolhapur.

Afternoon: Drive to Ratnagiri

After lunch, check out from your Kolhapur hotel and drive to Ratnagiri (approximately 3-4 hours), a coastal town in the Konkan region known for its beautiful beaches, historical sites, and as the birthplace of Lokmanya Tilak.

En route: Enjoy the scenic drive through the Western Ghats and the Konkan coastline.

Evening: Ratnagiri Beach

Check into your hotel in Ratnagiri. Visit Ratnagiri Beach for a relaxing evening by the Arabian Sea. Enjoy the sunset and the cool sea breeze.

Accommodation: Blue Ocean Resort & Spa, Hotel Kohinoor Palace, or Hotel Sea View for comfortable stays.

New Palace, Kolhapur

Ratnagiri Beach

Konkan Cuisine

The Konkan region is famous for its distinctive coastal cuisine, characterized by the use of coconut, kokum (a souring agent), and a variety of seafood. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Kombdi Vade: Spicy chicken curry served with deep-fried bread
  • Sol Kadhi: A pink-colored digestive drink made from kokum and coconut milk
  • Malvani Fish Curry: A spicy fish curry made with a special Malvani masala
  • Bangda Fry: Spicy mackerel fry
  • Modak: Sweet dumplings filled with coconut and jaggery
Day 8

Ratnagiri to Ganpatipule

Morning: Ratnagiri Sightseeing

Visit Thibaw Palace, the residence of the exiled last king of Burma, King Thibaw. Continue to Lokmanya Tilak's birthplace, which has been converted into a memorial museum dedicated to the freedom fighter.

Also visit: Ratnadurg Fort, a sea fort offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.

Afternoon: Drive to Ganpatipule

After lunch, drive to Ganpatipule (approximately 1 hour), a small coastal town known for its pristine beaches and the ancient Ganpati Temple.

En route: Stop at Jaigad Fort, a 17th-century sea fort offering beautiful views of the Arabian Sea and the Jaigad Creek.

Evening: Ganpatipule Temple & Beach

Visit the 400-year-old Swayambhu Ganpati Temple, one of the most important Ganesh temples in Maharashtra. The temple is located right on the beach, offering a unique spiritual experience. Spend the evening relaxing on the pristine Ganpatipule Beach.

Accommodation: MTDC Resort Ganpatipule, Abhishek Beach Resort, or Blue Ocean Resort for comfortable stays.

Thibaw Palace

Ganpatipule Temple

Beach Activities

Ganpatipule is known for its pristine beaches and clear waters. Here are some activities you can enjoy:

  • Swimming in the shallow waters (always follow safety guidelines)
  • Beach walks during sunrise and sunset
  • Water sports like banana boat rides and jet skiing
  • Beachside camel and horse rides
  • Fresh seafood at beachside shacks

Note: The sea can be rough during monsoon (June-September). Swimming is not advisable during this period.

Day 9

Ganpatipule to Nashik

Morning: Beach Time

Spend the morning relaxing on Ganpatipule Beach. Take a morning walk along the shore, swim in the clear waters, or simply enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Activity: Visit the Prachin Konkan Museum, an open-air museum showcasing the traditional Konkan lifestyle, architecture, and culture.

Afternoon: Drive to Nashik

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Nashik (approximately 6-7 hours), one of the holiest cities in India and the wine capital of the country.

En route: The drive takes you through the scenic Western Ghats and rural Maharashtra. Make stops as needed for refreshments and to enjoy the views.

Evening: Arrival in Nashik

Check into your hotel in Nashik. If time permits, take a stroll along the Godavari River at Ramkund, a sacred bathing ghat.

Accommodation: The Gateway Hotel Ambad, Express Inn, or Ginger Nashik for comfortable stays.

Godavari River, Nashik

About Nashik

Nashik is one of the holiest cities in India, situated on the banks of the Godavari River. It hosts the Kumbh Mela every 12 years and is mentioned in the Ramayana as the place where Lord Rama spent a significant part of his exile. In recent years, Nashik has also emerged as India's wine capital, with numerous vineyards and wineries in and around the city.

India's wine capital
Home to numerous ancient temples
Located on the banks of the holy Godavari River
Day 10

Nashik to Aurangabad

Morning: Wine Tour

Visit one of Nashik's famous wineries like Sula Vineyards or York Winery. Take a guided tour of the vineyard and winery to learn about the wine-making process. Enjoy a wine tasting session featuring different varieties of wines produced there.

Note: Most wineries open around 11:00 AM for tours and tastings. It's advisable to book in advance.

Afternoon: Trimbakeshwar Temple

Visit the Trimbakeshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (sacred abodes of Lord Shiva) in India. The temple is located about 30 km from Nashik city and is known for its unique three-faced lingam representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Maharashtrian lunch at a local restaurant near the temple.

Evening: Drive to Aurangabad

After visiting the temple, drive to Aurangabad (approximately 4 hours), the gateway to the Ajanta and Ellora Caves. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Vivanta Aurangabad, Lemon Tree Hotel, or Ambassador Ajanta for comfortable stays.

Sula Vineyards

Trimbakeshwar Temple

Nashik Wine Guide

Nashik is known as India's wine capital, with over 50 wineries in the region. Here's what you need to know about wine tourism in Nashik:

  • Best Wineries: Sula Vineyards, York Winery, Soma Vineyards, Grover Zampa
  • Wine Varieties: Chenin Blanc, Sauvignon Blanc, Cabernet Sauvignon, Shiraz
  • Tours: Most wineries offer guided tours explaining the wine-making process
  • Tastings: Wine tasting sessions are available at most wineries for a nominal fee
  • Best Time: November to March is ideal for vineyard visits
Day 11

Ellora & Ajanta Caves

Morning: Ellora Caves

Start early for a visit to the Ellora Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring 34 rock-cut temples and monasteries dating from the 6th to 10th century. The caves represent Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments and artwork.

Highlight: Don't miss Cave 16, the magnificent Kailasa Temple, carved from a single rock and considered one of the most remarkable cave temples in India.

Afternoon: Lunch & Travel to Ajanta

Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Ellora. Then drive to the Ajanta Caves (approximately 2 hours), another UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Note: Ajanta Caves are closed on Mondays, so plan your visit accordingly.

Evening: Ajanta Caves

Explore the Ajanta Caves, a series of 30 Buddhist cave monuments dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE. These caves are renowned for their exquisite paintings and sculptures depicting the life of Buddha.

Return: Drive back to Aurangabad in the evening.

Day 12

Aurangabad to Mumbai & Departure

Morning: Aurangabad Sightseeing

Visit Bibi Ka Maqbara, also known as the "Taj of the Deccan," a beautiful mausoleum built in the 17th century. Continue to Aurangabad Caves, a series of 12 Buddhist caves dating back to the 6th and 7th centuries.

Also visit: Panchakki, a 17th-century water mill known for its underground water channel.

Afternoon: Return to Mumbai

After lunch, take an afternoon flight back to Mumbai (approximately 1 hour). Alternatively, you can take a train or drive, but these options will take significantly longer.

Note: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Mumbai.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Mumbai. Colaba Causeway and Linking Road are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Mumbai & Pune: October to March (winter months)
  • Lonavala: Monsoon (June to September) for waterfalls; October to February for pleasant weather
  • Konkan Coast: November to February
  • Aurangabad: October to March
  • Avoid summer months (April to June) as temperatures can soar above 40°C

Transportation

  • Air: Mumbai and Pune have international airports; Aurangabad has domestic connections
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs, auto-rickshaws, local buses
  • Consider hiring a driver for day trips and intercity travel

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for cities; light jacket for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Maharashtra offers a diverse culinary experience:

Mumbai Street Food
  • Vada Pav (potato fritter in a bun)
  • Pav Bhaji (spiced vegetable curry)
  • Bhel Puri & Sev Puri (savory snacks)
  • Mumbai Sandwich
  • Pani Puri (hollow crisp filled with flavored water)
Traditional Maharashtrian
  • Puran Poli (sweet flatbread)
  • Misal Pav (spicy sprouts curry)
  • Modak (sweet dumpling)
  • Tambda & Pandhra Rassa (red & white curry)
  • Sabudana Khichdi (tapioca pearl dish)

Practical Information

  • Language: Marathi is the official language. Hindi and English are widely understood
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Maharashtra is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
