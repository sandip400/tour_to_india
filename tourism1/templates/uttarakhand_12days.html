Explore the Land of Gods

A comprehensive 12-day journey through Uttarakhand's mountains, valleys, and spiritual havens

Uttarakhand: Abode of the Gods

Uttarakhand, nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, offers a perfect blend of spiritual experiences, adventure activities, and natural beauty. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you from the sacred rivers of Rishikesh and Haridwar to the snow-capped peaks of Auli, the serene lakes of Nainital, and the wildlife-rich forests of Jim Corbett National Park.

Day 1

Arrival in Dehradun & Local Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun or at the Dehradun Railway Station. Transfer to your hotel and check-in. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Hyatt Regency, Lemon Tree, or Madhuban Resort for luxury stays; Hotel Softel Plaza or Hotel Viceroy Inn for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Robber's Cave & Tapkeshwar Temple

Visit Robber's Cave (Guchhupani), a unique river cave formation. Continue to Tapkeshwar Temple, a sacred Shiva temple where water drips from the ceiling onto the Shivalinga.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Garhwali cuisine at Kalsang or Orchard Restaurant.

Evening: Forest Research Institute

Visit the Forest Research Institute, a colonial-era building with beautiful architecture set in a sprawling campus. The institute houses various forestry museums. End your day with a stroll at Paltan Bazaar for some local shopping and dinner.

Dinner: Try Kumar Sweet House for local sweets and snacks or Moti Mahal for North Indian cuisine.

Robber's Cave

Forest Research Institute

Day 1 Tips
  • Dehradun's weather is pleasant most of the year, but carry a light jacket during winter months.
  • The Forest Research Institute is closed on Mondays and public holidays.
  • Robber's Cave requires walking through ankle-deep water, so wear appropriate footwear.
  • Tapkeshwar Temple requires modest dress (shoulders and knees covered).
Day 2

Mussoorie - Queen of Hills

Morning: Drive to Mussoorie

After breakfast, drive to Mussoorie (approximately 1.5 hours, 35 km). Known as the "Queen of Hills," Mussoorie offers stunning views of the Himalayan ranges. Check into your hotel and prepare for a day of exploration.

Accommodation: Welcomhotel by ITC Hotels, JW Marriott Mussoorie, or Fortune Resort Grace for luxury stays; Hotel Brentwood or Country Inn for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Kempty Falls & Company Garden

Visit the famous Kempty Falls, a cascading waterfall where you can enjoy a refreshing dip (weather permitting). Continue to Company Garden, a beautifully maintained park with a variety of flowers and a small artificial lake.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Cafe Ivy or Lovely Omelette Centre, famous for its variety of egg dishes.

Evening: Mall Road & Gun Hill

Take a cable car ride to Gun Hill, the second highest point in Mussoorie, offering panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges. Spend the evening strolling along the famous Mall Road, the heart of Mussoorie, lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Dinner: Try Cafe Landour for continental cuisine or Kalsang for Tibetan and Chinese dishes.

Day 3

Mussoorie to Rishikesh

Morning: Lal Tibba & Camel's Back Road

Visit Lal Tibba, the highest point in Mussoorie offering spectacular views of the Himalayan ranges. Continue to Camel's Back Road, a 3 km stretch named after a rock formation resembling a camel's back, perfect for a morning walk with beautiful mountain views.

Activity: Visit Sir George Everest's House, the former residence of the Surveyor General of India after whom Mount Everest is named.

Afternoon: Drive to Rishikesh

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Rishikesh (approximately 3 hours, 90 km). Known as the "Yoga Capital of the World," Rishikesh is situated on the banks of the holy Ganges River. Check into your hotel and prepare for an evening of exploration.

Accommodation: Taj Rishikesh Resort & Spa, Ananda in the Himalayas, or Ganga Kinare for luxury stays; Divine Ganga Cottage or Aloha on the Ganges for mid-range options.

Evening: Triveni Ghat & Ganga Aarti

Visit Triveni Ghat, a sacred bathing spot at the confluence of three rivers. Attend the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti (prayer ceremony) where priests perform rituals with fire and music to honor the sacred river.

Dinner: Try Chotiwala or Ganga Beach Restaurant for riverside dining with a view of the illuminated ghats.

Lal Tibba

Triveni Ghat

About Rishikesh

Rishikesh, located in the foothills of the Himalayas, is a sacred town known for its spiritual significance and as a center for yoga and meditation. The Beatles visited the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's ashram here in 1968, bringing international attention to the town. Today, it's a popular destination for spiritual seekers, adventure enthusiasts, and travelers looking to experience the serene beauty of the Ganges River.

Yoga and meditation capital
Sacred Ganges River
Adventure sports hub
Day 4

Rishikesh Exploration

Morning: Yoga & Meditation

Start your day with a yoga and meditation session at one of the many ashrams or yoga centers in Rishikesh. Many places offer drop-in classes suitable for beginners to advanced practitioners.

Recommendation: Parmarth Niketan, Sivananda Ashram, or Yoga Niketan offer authentic yoga experiences.

Afternoon: Laxman Jhula & Ram Jhula

Visit the iconic suspension bridges across the Ganges - Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula. Explore the nearby temples including the Trayambakeshwar Temple (13-story temple) and Geeta Bhawan, which houses paintings depicting scenes from the Bhagavad Gita.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Little Buddha Cafe or Ramana's Organic Cafe, both offering a mix of Indian and international cuisine.

Evening: Adventure Activities

Experience white water rafting on the Ganges River (if time permits and depending on the season) or enjoy cliff jumping and body surfing. Alternatively, explore the Beatles Ashram (Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's ashram), where the famous band stayed in 1968.

Dinner: Try Bistro Nirvana or The Sitting Elephant for a relaxed dining experience.

Yoga Session

Laxman Jhula

Adventure Activities Information
  • White water rafting is available in different grades (1-4) depending on your experience level.
  • Most rafting operators provide all necessary safety equipment and instructions.
  • The best time for rafting is from September to June (avoid monsoon season).
  • Cliff jumping is available at specific spots with proper supervision.
  • Always book adventure activities through reputable operators who prioritize safety.
Day 5

Rishikesh to Haridwar

Morning: Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

Visit the sacred Neelkanth Mahadev Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, located about 12 km from Rishikesh. According to mythology, this is where Lord Shiva consumed poison during the ocean churning, turning his throat blue (hence the name Neelkanth, meaning "blue throat").

Note: The temple is situated at a higher altitude and requires a drive through forested hills. The journey itself is scenic with beautiful views.

Afternoon: Drive to Haridwar

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Haridwar (approximately 30 minutes, 25 km). Known as the "Gateway to God," Haridwar is one of the seven holiest places for Hindus. Check into your hotel and prepare for an evening of spiritual experiences.

Accommodation: Haveli Hari Ganga, Radisson Blu, or Ambrosia Sarovar Portico for comfortable stays.

Evening: Har Ki Pauri & Ganga Aarti

Visit Har Ki Pauri, the most sacred ghat in Haridwar, where it's believed that Lord Vishnu left his footprint. Attend the spectacular Ganga Aarti ceremony at sunset, where priests perform rituals with large fire lamps accompanied by bells, drums, and religious chants.

Dinner: Try Mohan Puri Wala or Hoshiyarpuri for authentic local cuisine after the aarti.

Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri

About Haridwar

Haridwar, meaning "Gateway to God," is one of the seven holiest places for Hindus. It's one of the four sites where drops of the elixir of immortality (Amrit) accidentally spilled over from the pitcher carried by the celestial bird Garuda during the Samudra Manthan (churning of the ocean). The city marks the point where the Ganges descends from the mountains to the plains, making it a significant pilgrimage site.

Day 6

Haridwar to Chopta

Morning: Haridwar Temples

After breakfast, visit some of Haridwar's important temples including Mansa Devi Temple, accessible by cable car and offering panoramic views of the city, and Chandi Devi Temple, situated on Neel Parvat. Both temples are part of the Siddh Peetha (places of worship dedicated to Goddess Shakti).

Activity: Take a holy dip in the Ganges at Har Ki Pauri if you wish to participate in this sacred ritual.

Afternoon: Drive to Chopta

After lunch, begin your journey to Chopta (approximately 6-7 hours, 220 km), often referred to as the "Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand." The drive takes you through beautiful Garhwal landscapes with changing scenery from plains to mountains, passing through Devprayag, the confluence of rivers Alaknanda and Bhagirathi.

En route: Stop at Devprayag to witness the sacred confluence and take photographs of the beautiful meeting point of the rivers.

Evening: Arrival in Chopta

Arrive in Chopta by evening. Check into your hotel or camp. Enjoy the serene surroundings and prepare for the next day's trek. Chopta is situated at an altitude of about 2,700 meters and offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks.

Accommodation: The Meadows Chopta, Magpie Jungle Camp, or similar camping options as Chopta has limited luxury accommodations.

Mansa Devi Temple

Chopta Landscape

About Chopta

Chopta is a picturesque hamlet nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas, known for its pristine beauty and panoramic views of the snow-capped mountains. It serves as the base for the trek to Tungnath, the highest Shiva temple in the world, and Chandrashila peak. The region is rich in flora and fauna, with rhododendron and deodar forests, and offers a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Base for Tungnath & Chandrashila trek
Snow-covered landscapes (winter)
Camping and stargazing
Day 7

Chopta to Auli

Morning: Tungnath & Chandrashila Trek

Start early for the trek to Tungnath Temple and Chandrashila Peak. Tungnath is the highest Shiva temple in the world at an altitude of 3,680 meters. Continue to Chandrashila Peak (4,000 meters) for panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba.

Note: The trek is moderately difficult and takes about 3-4 hours to reach Chandrashila from Chopta. Carry water, snacks, and wear appropriate trekking shoes.

Afternoon: Drive to Auli

After returning from the trek and having lunch, begin your journey to Auli (approximately 3-4 hours, 100 km), a famous ski destination in winter and a beautiful meadow with panoramic Himalayan views in summer. The drive takes you through Joshimath, an important pilgrimage center.

En route: Stop at Joshimath to visit the ancient Narsingh Temple and enjoy the views of Nanda Devi peak.

Evening: Arrival in Auli

Check into your hotel in Auli. Rest and relax after the trek and journey. If time and energy permit, take a short stroll around to enjoy the beautiful surroundings and sunset views over the Himalayan peaks.

Accommodation: Cliff Top Club, The Tattva Resort, or GMVN Ski Resort for comfortable stays.

Tungnath Temple

Auli Landscape

Trekking Information
  • The trek to Tungnath and Chandrashila is moderately difficult and suitable for beginners with reasonable fitness.
  • Start early to avoid afternoon clouds that may block the views from Chandrashila.
  • Carry sufficient water, energy bars, and light snacks.
  • Wear layers as the temperature can vary significantly with altitude.
  • The temple remains closed during winter (November to April) due to heavy snowfall, but the trek can still be done.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for a safer and more informative experience.
Day 8

Auli Exploration

Morning: Gondola Ride

Take the Auli Gondola (cable car), one of the longest in Asia, from Joshimath to Auli for breathtaking views of the Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi, Kamet, and Dronagiri. The 4 km ride offers spectacular panoramic vistas.

Activity: If visiting in winter (December to March), enjoy skiing on the slopes of Auli, known as one of the best skiing destinations in India.

Afternoon: Gurso Bugyal Trek

Embark on a short trek to Gurso Bugyal, a beautiful meadow offering panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks. The trek is relatively easy and takes about 2 hours round trip from Auli.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or return to your hotel for lunch before the afternoon activities.

Evening: Sunset Views & Relaxation

Enjoy the sunset views over the Himalayan peaks from your hotel or a nearby viewpoint. Spend the evening relaxing at your hotel, perhaps with a bonfire if the weather permits.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel, as Auli has limited dining options outside of hotels.

Auli Gondola

Gurso Bugyal

About Auli

Auli, situated at an altitude of 2,500-3,000 meters, is a popular ski destination and hill station in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand. Originally developed as a paramilitary base, it was later transformed into a ski resort by the government. The pristine slopes, covered with oak and coniferous forests, offer a perfect setting for skiing in winter and trekking in summer. The panoramic views of Himalayan peaks like Nanda Devi (India's second-highest peak), Kamet, and Mana Parvat make it a photographer's paradise.

Premier skiing destination (winter)
Panoramic Himalayan views
Beautiful trekking trails
Day 9

Auli to Binsar

Morning: Auli Artificial Lake

Visit the Auli Artificial Lake, created for making artificial snow for the ski slopes. The lake reflects the surrounding Himalayan peaks, creating a picturesque setting. Take a leisurely walk around the lake and enjoy the serene environment.

Activity: Visit the Hanuman Temple nearby for panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges.

Afternoon: Drive to Binsar

After lunch, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Binsar (approximately 8-9 hours, 300 km), a serene hill station known for its wildlife sanctuary and panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks. The drive takes you through changing landscapes from the Garhwal to the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand.

En route: Stop at Karnprayag, one of the Panch Prayag (five confluences) of the Alaknanda River, for a short break and to witness the sacred confluence.

Evening: Arrival in Binsar

Arrive in Binsar by evening. Check into your resort or lodge, most of which are located within or near the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary. Rest and relax after the long journey, enjoying the peaceful surroundings and clean mountain air.

Accommodation: Mary Budden Estate, Binsar Forest Retreat, or KMVN Tourist Rest House for a comfortable stay.

Auli Artificial Lake

Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary

About Binsar

Binsar, located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, is known for its wildlife sanctuary and stunning views of the Himalayan peaks. The Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary, spread over 45 square kilometers, is home to over 200 bird species and various animals like leopards, deer, and wild boars. The sanctuary is covered with oak, rhododendron, and pine forests, offering a pristine natural environment. From Zero Point, the highest point in Binsar, one can enjoy panoramic views of Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Panchachuli.

Bird watching paradise
Panoramic Himalayan views
Rich biodiversity
Day 10

Binsar to Nainital

Morning: Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary

Explore the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary with a guided nature walk. Visit Zero Point, the highest point in Binsar, offering 360-degree views of the Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Panchachuli. The sanctuary is home to various bird species, making it a paradise for bird watchers.

Activity: Visit the ancient Bineshwar Mahadev Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, located within the sanctuary.

Afternoon: Drive to Nainital

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Nainital (approximately 3-4 hours, 120 km), a charming hill station built around a pear-shaped lake. The drive takes you through beautiful Kumaon landscapes with changing scenery.

En route: Stop at Almora, a picturesque town known for its cultural heritage, for a short break and to explore the local market.

Evening: Naini Lake & Mall Road

Check into your hotel in Nainital. Spend the evening strolling along the Mall Road and Naini Lake, enjoying the cool mountain air and beautiful lake views illuminated in the evening. Take a boat ride on the lake if time permits.

Accommodation: The Naini Retreat, Vivanta Nainital, or Manu Maharani for luxury stays; Hotel Himalaya or Alka The Lake Side Hotel for mid-range options.

Zero Point, Binsar

Naini Lake

About Nainital

Nainital, known as the "Lake District of India," is a popular hill station in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. Built around the pear-shaped Naini Lake, the town is surrounded by seven hills, locally known as "Sapta-Shring" (seven peaks). According to Hindu mythology, the lake is believed to be one of the 64 Shakti Peeths, where the eyes (naina) of Goddess Sati fell when Lord Shiva carried her body.

Day 11

Nainital to Jim Corbett National Park

Morning: Nainital Sightseeing

Take the cable car to Snow View Point for panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi. Visit Naina Devi Temple, dedicated to the patron goddess of the town. Enjoy a boat ride on Naini Lake, the centerpiece of the town.

Activity: Visit Tiffin Top (Dorothy's Seat) for beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Afternoon: Drive to Jim Corbett National Park

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Jim Corbett National Park (approximately 3 hours, 100 km), India's oldest national park known for its Bengal tiger reserve. The drive takes you through beautiful forests and rural landscapes.

En route: Stop at Corbett Museum in Kaladhungi, the former winter home of Jim Corbett, now converted into a museum showcasing his life and work.

Evening: Arrival at Jim Corbett

Check into your jungle resort. Attend a briefing about the park and safari rules. Enjoy the evening at your resort, which often organizes wildlife documentaries or cultural performances.

Accommodation: Taj Corbett Resort & Spa, Jim's Jungle Retreat, or Namah Resort for luxury stays; Corbett River Creek or Corbett Machaan Resort for mid-range options.

Snow View Point

Jim Corbett National Park

About Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park, established in 1936, is India's oldest national park and was named after the famous British-Indian hunter and conservationist Jim Corbett. Spread across 520 square kilometers, the park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the endangered Bengal tiger. The park is divided into five zones: Bijrani, Dhikala, Jhirna, Domunda, and Sonanadi, each offering different wildlife experiences.

Day 12

Jim Corbett Safari & Departure

Early Morning: Jungle Safari

Wake up early for a thrilling jeep safari into the heart of Jim Corbett National Park. The early morning is the best time to spot wildlife including tigers, elephants, deer, and various bird species. The safari lasts for about 3-4 hours with a naturalist guide who will help you spot and identify wildlife.

Note: Safari bookings should be made in advance as they get filled quickly, especially during peak season.

Late Morning: Breakfast & Check-out

Return to your resort for a hearty breakfast. Check out from your resort and prepare for your onward journey. If time permits, visit the Garjiya Devi Temple, a sacred temple dedicated to Goddess Parvati, located on a large rock in the middle of the Kosi River.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at your resort or at a local restaurant before departing.

Afternoon: Departure to Delhi

Begin your journey to Delhi (approximately 6-7 hours, 260 km). The drive takes you through the plains of Uttar Pradesh before reaching the national capital.

Alternative: If your flight departs the next day, consider staying overnight in Delhi.

Jeep Safari in Jim Corbett National Park

Safari Information
  • Morning safaris typically run from 6:00 AM to 10:00 AM.
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing (olive green, brown, beige) to blend with the surroundings.
  • Carry binoculars, camera with zoom lens, hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen.
  • Follow all instructions given by your guide and maintain silence during wildlife sightings.
  • Tiger sightings are not guaranteed but the park offers diverse wildlife experiences.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • March to June: Ideal for hill stations like Mussoorie and Nainital
  • September to November: Perfect for wildlife safaris at Jim Corbett
  • December to February: Best for skiing in Auli and snow views
  • Avoid: Monsoon season (July-August) as landslides are common in hilly regions

Transportation

  • Air: Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun connects to major Indian cities
  • Train: Dehradun, Haridwar, and Kathgodam (for Nainital) are major railway stations
  • Road: Well-connected highway network; buses available from Delhi to major towns
  • Local Transport: Taxis, shared jeeps, and local buses connect major tourist spots
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers for varying temperatures; warm clothes for higher altitudes
  • Footwear: Comfortable trekking shoes and waterproof footwear
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills, basic first aid
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Binoculars for wildlife viewing, water bottle, flashlight

Food & Cuisine

Uttarakhand offers a diverse culinary experience:

Garhwali Cuisine
  • Mandua ki Roti (finger millet bread)
  • Jhangora ki Kheer (barnyard millet pudding)
  • Kafuli (spinach dish)
  • Chainsoo (black gram soup)
  • Phaanu (lentil dish)
  • Bhatt ki Churkani (black soybean dish)
Kumaoni Cuisine
  • Aloo ke Gutke (spicy potato dish)
  • Singori (sweet dish wrapped in malu leaf)
  • Bal Mithai (chocolate-like sweet)
  • Kumaoni Raita (yogurt with cucumber)
  • Bhang ki Chutney (hemp seed chutney)
  • Dubuk (lentil soup)

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is widely spoken. Garhwali and Kumaoni are regional languages. English is understood in tourist areas.
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns.
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs.
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas.
  • Safety: Uttarakhand is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions.
  • Altitude: Some areas are at high altitude. Acclimatize properly to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Permits: No special permits required for the places in this itinerary.
  • Festivals: Kumbh Mela (every 12 years in Haridwar), Nanda Devi Raj Jat (every 12 years), and Uttarayani (January) are major celebrations.
