Andaman & Nicobar Islands: Paradise in the Bay of Bengal

This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you on an in-depth exploration of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. From the historical sites of Port Blair to the pristine beaches of Havelock and Neil Islands, from the mysterious limestone caves of Baratang to the untouched beauty of Little Andaman, this journey covers both popular destinations and hidden gems of this tropical paradise.

Highlights of the Tour

World-class beaches and pristine shores
Crystal clear turquoise waters
Vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life
Colonial history and heritage sites
Off-the-beaten-path adventures
Natural limestone caves and mud volcanoes

Quick Facts

  • Duration: 12 Days
  • Starting Point: Port Blair
  • Ending Point: Port Blair
  • Languages: Hindi, English, Bengali

Day 1

Arrival in Port Blair & City Introduction

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair. Transfer to your hotel and check-in. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: SeaShell Hotel, Fortune Resort Bay Island, or Sinclairs Bayview for luxury stays; Hotel Sentinel or J Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Cellular Jail Visit

Visit the historic Cellular Jail National Memorial, a colonial prison used by the British to exile political prisoners. Explore the museum and learn about India's freedom struggle. The jail's architecture with its seven wings radiating from a central tower is unique.

Activity: Don't miss the Sound and Light show in the evening that narrates the saga of the Indian freedom struggle through a moving light and sound spectacle.

Evening: Corbyn's Cove Beach

Visit Corbyn's Cove Beach, a serene coconut-palm fringed beach just 7 km from Port Blair. Enjoy the sunset views and relax by the shore. You can also try water sports like jet skiing and banana boat rides if available.

Dinner: Enjoy your first taste of Andamanese cuisine at a local restaurant like New Lighthouse Restaurant or Annapurna Restaurant, known for fresh seafood dishes.

Cellular Jail

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry your ID proof as it's required for entry to the Andaman Islands.
  • Book the Cellular Jail Light and Sound show tickets in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Apply sunscreen before heading to the beach, as the tropical sun can be intense.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting historical sites.
Day 2

Port Blair Exploration

Morning: Ross Island & North Bay

Take a ferry to Ross Island (now Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Island), the former administrative headquarters of the British. Explore the ruins of colonial buildings reclaimed by nature, including the church, bakery, and officers' quarters.

Activity: Continue to North Bay Island, famous for its coral reefs. Enjoy snorkeling or take a glass-bottom boat ride to view the vibrant underwater marine life.

Afternoon: Museums & Cultural Sites

Visit the Anthropological Museum to learn about the indigenous tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Continue to the Samudrika Naval Marine Museum to explore exhibits on the islands' marine life, history, and geography.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant like Annapurna or Gagan Restaurant for authentic South Indian and Bengali cuisine.

Evening: Aberdeen Bazaar & Marina Park

Explore Aberdeen Bazaar, the main shopping area in Port Blair. Shop for souvenirs like seashell crafts, pearl jewelry, and wooden artifacts. Later, visit Marina Park and enjoy the waterfront promenade with views of the harbor.

Dinner: Try seafood specialties at New Lighthouse Restaurant or Something Different restaurant.

Day 3

Port Blair to Havelock Island

Morning: Ferry to Havelock Island

Check out from your Port Blair hotel and head to the Phoenix Bay Jetty to catch a ferry to Havelock Island (now officially renamed Swaraj Dweep). The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours depending on the type of ferry.

Travel tip: Book your ferry tickets in advance, especially during peak season. Private ferries like Makruzz or Green Ocean offer more comfortable journeys than government ferries.

Afternoon: Check-in & Beach Visit

Arrive at Havelock Island and transfer to your resort. After check-in and lunch, head to Kalapathar Beach, known for its black rocks, white sand, and turquoise waters. The beach is perfect for a relaxing afternoon and photography.

Accommodation: Taj Exotica, Barefoot Resort, or Symphony Palms Beach Resort for luxury stays; Silver Sand Beach Resort or Havelock Island Beach Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: Sunset at Radhanagar Beach

Visit the world-famous Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7), consistently rated among Asia's best beaches. Enjoy the spectacular sunset views and take a leisurely walk along the pristine shoreline with its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your resort or try local seafood at restaurants like Full Moon Café or Something Different.

Ferry to Havelock Island

About Havelock Island

Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep) is the most popular island in the Andaman archipelago, known for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and lush forests. It's a paradise for beach lovers, water sports enthusiasts, and nature admirers.

The island is home to some of Asia's best beaches, including Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7), which was named "Asia's Best Beach" by TIME Magazine. The island offers excellent opportunities for snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and trekking through its tropical forests.

Day 4

Havelock Island Beaches & Water Activities

Morning: Snorkeling or Scuba Diving

Start your day with an underwater adventure at Elephant Beach, known for its rich coral reefs and vibrant marine life. Choose between snorkeling or scuba diving based on your preference and experience level.

Activity: For certified divers, explore dive sites like The Wall, Dixon's Pinnacle, or Aquarium. First-time divers can opt for a Discover Scuba Diving (DSD) program with professional instructors.

Afternoon: Elephant Beach Exploration

After your underwater adventure, spend time exploring Elephant Beach. Enjoy the pristine shoreline, relax on the white sand, or try other water activities like sea walking, banana boat rides, or jet skiing if available.

Lunch: Enjoy a beach picnic or return to your resort for lunch.

Evening: Kayaking & Beach Time

In the late afternoon, try kayaking through the mangroves near Beach No. 5 (Vijaynagar Beach) or simply relax at your resort's beach. For the adventurous, a night kayaking experience in the bioluminescent waters can be arranged (subject to availability and conditions).

Dinner: Enjoy a beachside dinner at your resort or visit Red Snapper or Anju Coco for fresh seafood and international cuisine.

Marine Life of Andaman

The Andaman Islands are home to one of the richest coral reef ecosystems in the world. The waters around Havelock Island host over 200 species of coral and countless marine creatures including:

  • Colorful reef fish like clownfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish
  • Larger marine life such as reef sharks, turtles, and rays
  • Rare species like dugongs and giant clams
  • Vibrant hard and soft corals forming underwater gardens

The best visibility for underwater activities is typically from November to May, with water temperatures ranging from 26°C to 30°C (79°F to 86°F).

Day 5

Havelock Island Water Activities

Morning: Advanced Diving or Trekking

For certified divers, enjoy a morning dive at some of Havelock's premier dive sites like Johnny's Gorge or Dixon's Pinnacle. Non-divers can opt for a trek through the island's lush interior forests to spot endemic birds and wildlife.

Activity: Experienced divers might encounter larger marine life like reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, or even manta rays at deeper sites.

Afternoon: Beach Hopping

Explore some of Havelock's less-visited beaches like Beach No. 3 (Govind Nagar Beach) and Beach No. 5 (Vijaynagar Beach). Each beach has its own unique character and marine ecosystem. Enjoy swimming, sunbathing, or simply relaxing under the shade of coconut palms.

Lunch: Try a beachside shack for fresh seafood caught by local fishermen.

Evening: Sunset Cruise & Dinner

Take a sunset cruise around the island to enjoy panoramic views of Havelock's coastline as the sun sets over the Andaman Sea. Some cruises include snacks and beverages.

Dinner: Enjoy a special dinner at your resort or at one of the beachfront restaurants like Anju Coco or Something Different, reflecting on your Havelock experiences.

Diving in Andaman

The Andaman Islands offer some of the best diving experiences in Asia with over 50 dive sites across the archipelago. Havelock Island is the main diving hub with several professional dive centers offering courses and guided dives.

Popular dive sites around Havelock include:

  • Dixon's Pinnacle: An underwater mountain with abundant soft corals and pelagic fish
  • The Wall: A dramatic vertical drop-off with gorgonian fans and barrel sponges
  • Johnny's Gorge: Known for encounters with reef sharks and eagle rays
  • Aquarium: Perfect for beginners with shallow depths and colorful reef fish

Water temperatures range from 26-30°C year-round, making it comfortable for diving with just a thin wetsuit or rash guard.

Day 6

Havelock to Neil Island

Morning: Leisure Time & Check-out

Enjoy a leisurely morning at your resort. Take a final stroll on the beach or relax by the pool. Check out from your Havelock Island resort and prepare for your journey to Neil Island (now officially renamed Shaheed Dweep).

Activity: If time permits, visit the local market near the jetty to buy souvenirs or fresh tropical fruits.

Afternoon: Ferry to Neil Island

Board a ferry from Havelock to Neil Island. The journey takes approximately 1-1.5 hours. Upon arrival, transfer to your resort and check-in. Neil Island is smaller and more laid-back than Havelock, offering a peaceful retreat.

Accommodation: Summer Sands Beach Resort, Tango Beach Resort, or Pearl Park Beach Resort for comfortable stays; Sea Shell Neil or Silver Sand for mid-range options.

Evening: Natural Bridge & Sunset

Visit the Natural Coral Bridge at Bharatpur Beach, a unique natural formation created by coral deposits. Continue to Laxmanpur Beach to witness a spectacular sunset. The beach is known for its pristine shoreline and shallow waters perfect for wading.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your resort or try local seafood at restaurants like Tango Cafe or Welcome Restaurant.

Ferry to Neil Island

About Neil Island

Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep) is a small but beautiful island known for its pristine beaches, clear waters, and laid-back atmosphere. The island is named after a British brigadier and is often called the "vegetable bowl" of Andaman due to its extensive farming activities.

The island is less crowded than Havelock and offers a more relaxed experience. Its main attractions include the Natural Coral Bridge, beaches named after characters from the Ramayana (Bharatpur, Laxmanpur, Sitapur), and excellent snorkeling opportunities in its crystal-clear waters.

Day 7

Neil Island Exploration

Morning: Beach Hopping & Snorkeling

Spend the morning exploring Neil Island's beautiful beaches. Start with Bharatpur Beach, ideal for snorkeling due to its rich coral reefs. Continue to Sitapur Beach on the eastern coast, known for its stunning sunrise views (if you can wake up early).

Activity: Rent snorkeling gear to explore the vibrant underwater world or simply relax on the pristine beaches.

Afternoon: Island Exploration

Rent a bicycle to explore this small, flat island at your own pace. Visit local villages, paddy fields, and vegetable plantations that give Neil Island its nickname "vegetable bowl of Andaman." Stop by the local market to sample fresh tropical fruits.

Lunch: Enjoy a seafood lunch at a beachside restaurant or try a local eatery in the main market area.

Evening: Stargazing at Laxmanpur Beach

Return to Laxmanpur Beach in the evening for stargazing. Due to minimal light pollution, Neil Island offers excellent opportunities to view the night sky. If you're lucky and visiting during the right season, you might witness the magical bioluminescent phytoplankton that make the shoreline glow at night.

Dinner: Enjoy a relaxed dinner at your resort or try one of the local restaurants in the market area.

Cycling in Neil Island

Neil Island's Natural Beauty

Despite its small size (just 13.7 sq km), Neil Island packs a punch when it comes to natural beauty. The island features:

  • Pristine beaches with different colored sands
  • Vibrant coral reefs accessible from the shore
  • Unique natural rock formations like the Howrah Bridge
  • Lush tropical vegetation and agricultural fields
  • Clear night skies perfect for stargazing

The island's small size makes it perfect for exploration by bicycle, which can be rented for around ₹100-150 per day from shops near the jetty or from your resort.

Day 8

Neil Island to Baratang

Morning: Return to Port Blair

Check out from your Neil Island resort and catch an early morning ferry back to Port Blair. The journey takes approximately 2-2.5 hours. Upon arrival, have a quick lunch in Port Blair before continuing your journey.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a restaurant in Port Blair before heading to Baratang.

Afternoon: Journey to Baratang Island

Embark on a journey to Baratang Island, approximately 100 km from Port Blair. The trip involves a drive through the Jarawa Tribal Reserve (where photography is strictly prohibited), followed by a ferry crossing. The entire journey takes about 3-4 hours.

Note: The convoy through the Jarawa Reserve operates at specific times, so plan accordingly. Respect the privacy and culture of the indigenous Jarawa tribe by following all regulations.

Evening: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Baratang Island and check into your accommodation. Options are limited on this less-touristy island, with basic government guesthouses and a few private options available. Spend the evening relaxing and preparing for tomorrow's exploration.

Accommodation: Forest Guest House, PWD Guest House, or private homestays are the main options in Baratang.

Ferry Journey

Baratang Island

About Baratang Island

Baratang Island is one of the less-visited but geologically fascinating islands in the Andaman archipelago. It's known for its natural limestone caves, mud volcanoes, and mangrove creeks. The island offers a more authentic and off-the-beaten-path experience compared to the more touristy islands.

The journey to Baratang passes through the Jarawa Tribal Reserve, home to the indigenous Jarawa tribe, one of the oldest human populations on Earth. To protect their way of life, strict regulations are in place, including convoy-only travel through their reserve and a complete ban on photography.

Important Advisory

When traveling through the Jarawa Tribal Reserve, it is strictly prohibited to interact with, photograph, or give any items to the tribal people. These regulations are in place to protect their indigenous culture and prevent the spread of diseases. Violations can result in severe penalties including imprisonment.

Day 9

Baratang to Port Blair

Morning: Limestone Caves

Take a boat ride through dense mangrove forests to reach the famous limestone caves of Baratang. These natural caves feature stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations created over thousands of years. A local guide will explain the geological significance of these formations.

Activity: The boat journey through the mangroves itself is an experience, offering opportunities to spot various bird species and occasionally saltwater crocodiles.

Afternoon: Mud Volcano

Visit the unique mud volcanoes, one of the few places in India where this geological phenomenon can be observed. These are not actual volcanoes but natural formations where mud is pushed up by natural gases from underground.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at a local eatery before beginning your journey back to Port Blair.

Evening: Return to Port Blair

Check out from your Baratang accommodation and begin the journey back to Port Blair. The return journey also involves a convoy through the Jarawa Reserve and takes approximately 3-4 hours. Check into your Port Blair hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Return to one of the hotels in Port Blair where you stayed earlier in the trip.

Geological Wonders of Baratang

Baratang Island is a geological treasure trove featuring two fascinating natural phenomena:

  • Limestone Caves: These natural caves were formed over millions of years as rainwater dissolved the limestone rock. The resulting stalactites (hanging from the ceiling) and stalagmites (rising from the floor) create spectacular formations that continue to grow at a rate of about 1cm per 100 years.
  • Mud Volcanoes: These rare geological features occur when natural gases and hot water deep within the Earth force their way up through the soil, creating small volcano-like cones of mud. They're believed to be connected to tectonic activity in the region and occasionally become more active after earthquakes.

Both these natural wonders provide insights into the dynamic geological processes that have shaped the Andaman Islands over millions of years.

Day 10

Little Andaman

Morning: Ferry to Little Andaman

Take an early morning ferry from Haddo Wharf in Port Blair to Little Andaman Island (Hut Bay). The journey takes approximately 5-6 hours depending on sea conditions. Little Andaman is the fourth largest island in the Andaman group and offers a more pristine, less commercialized experience.

Travel tip: The ferry journey can be rough during monsoon season. Carry motion sickness medication if needed and bring snacks and water for the journey.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Hut Bay Jetty and transfer to your accommodation. Options are limited on Little Andaman, with government guesthouses and a few private resorts being the main choices. After check-in and lunch, take some time to rest after the long ferry journey.

Accommodation: Blue View Resort, Wild Orchid Resort, or government guesthouses like Tourism Cottage and PWD Guest House.

Evening: Butler Bay Beach

Visit Butler Bay Beach on the western coast of Little Andaman, known for its pristine shoreline and surfing opportunities. Enjoy the sunset and the peaceful atmosphere of this less-visited island. If you're interested in surfing, you can rent boards from local operators.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your resort or at a local eatery in Hut Bay market area.

About Little Andaman

Little Andaman Island, despite its name, is the fourth largest island in the Andaman archipelago with an area of about 734 sq km. It's separated from the main Andaman group by the Duncan Passage and offers a more off-the-beaten-path experience for travelers seeking pristine nature and fewer crowds.

The island is known for its:

  • Beautiful, uncrowded beaches ideal for surfing
  • Spectacular waterfalls like White Surf and Whisper Wave
  • Elephant training camp where you can see Asian elephants
  • Red Oil Palm Plantation, one of the largest in Asia
  • Rich biodiversity including endemic bird species

Little Andaman was affected by the 2004 tsunami but has since recovered, though infrastructure remains basic compared to the more developed islands.

Day 11

Little Andaman to Port Blair

Morning: White Surf Waterfall

Visit White Surf Waterfall, a beautiful cascade located about 6.5 km from Hut Bay. The waterfall is surrounded by lush rainforest and offers a refreshing swimming opportunity. The journey to the waterfall involves a short trek through the forest.

Activity: Swimming in the natural pool at the base of the waterfall. Carry a change of clothes and a towel.

Afternoon: Elephant Training Camp

Visit the Elephant Training Camp where you can see Asian elephants that were once used for logging operations. The camp now focuses on conservation and offers visitors a chance to learn about these magnificent animals and their role in the island's history.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local eatery before heading to the jetty.

Evening: Return to Port Blair

Check out from your accommodation and catch the afternoon ferry back to Port Blair. The journey takes approximately 5-6 hours. Upon arrival in Port Blair, transfer to your hotel for your final night in the Andaman Islands.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a restaurant in Port Blair, reflecting on your island adventures.

Natural Wonders of Little Andaman

Little Andaman offers some of the most pristine natural environments in the Andaman archipelago. The island's limited development has preserved its natural beauty, including:

  • Waterfalls: White Surf and Whisper Wave waterfalls flow year-round but are most spectacular during and after the monsoon season
  • Forests: The island is covered with dense tropical rainforest home to numerous bird species and other wildlife
  • Beaches: Butler Bay and other beaches offer some of the best surfing opportunities in India, with waves suitable for both beginners and experienced surfers
  • Coral Reefs: The surrounding waters host vibrant coral reefs that can be explored through snorkeling

The island's remoteness means these natural attractions are far less crowded than those on the main Andaman Islands, offering a more intimate experience with nature.

Day 12

Departure

Morning: Chidiya Tapu

If your flight is in the evening, visit Chidiya Tapu (Bird Island), about 25 km from Port Blair. Known as the "Sunset Point" of Andaman, it offers beautiful views and is home to many endemic bird species. Explore the small beach and the Biological Park if time permits.

Activity: Bird watching and nature photography. The area is home to over 40 species of endemic birds.

Afternoon: Last-Minute Shopping

Return to Port Blair for some last-minute souvenir shopping at Sagarika Emporium (Government Handicraft Shop) or Aberdeen Bazaar. Look for pearls, seashell crafts, wooden artifacts, and spices unique to the islands.

Lunch: Enjoy a final meal of local Andamanese cuisine at a restaurant of your choice.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Veer Savarkar International Airport for your departure flight. Bid farewell to the beautiful Andaman Islands, carrying memories of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and unique experiences from your comprehensive 12-day journey.

Note: Ensure you reach the airport at least 2 hours before your flight departure time as security procedures can be time-consuming.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: Ideal months with pleasant weather and clear skies
  • December to February: Peak season with perfect conditions for water activities
  • Avoid June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall and rough seas
  • For diving: February to May offers the best visibility
  • For budget travelers: October and November (shoulder season) offer good weather with fewer crowds

Transportation

  • Air: Direct flights to Port Blair from major Indian cities
  • Inter-island: Government and private ferries connect the islands
  • Port Blair: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and local buses available
  • Havelock & Neil: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and rental scooters/bicycles
  • Ferry booking: Reserve in advance during peak season
  • Private boats: Can be hired for island hopping and fishing trips

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes, swimwear, cover-ups
  • Footwear: Flip-flops, water shoes, comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun protection: Sunscreen (reef-safe), hat, sunglasses
  • Insect repellent: Mosquitoes can be present, especially in evenings
  • First aid: Basic medications, motion sickness pills for ferry rides
  • Electronics: Camera (waterproof if possible), power bank
  • Cash: ATMs are limited on smaller islands

Food & Cuisine

Andaman cuisine is a unique blend of Indian, Bengali, South Indian, and seafood influences:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Fresh seafood (fish, prawns, lobster, crab)
  • Fish curry with coconut milk
  • Grilled fish with local spices
  • Amritsari fish (popular in Port Blair)
  • Macher Jhol (Bengali fish curry)
  • Coconut prawn curry
Dining Tips
  • Seafood is the specialty across the islands
  • Vegetarian options are available but limited
  • Beach shacks offer fresh catches at reasonable prices
  • Resorts offer multi-cuisine options
  • Try fresh tropical fruits like coconut and pineapple
  • Carry water bottles to stay hydrated

Practical Information

  • Permits: All visitors need to obtain a permit (issued on arrival)
  • ID proof: Carry government-issued photo ID at all times
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). Limited ATMs outside Port Blair
  • Network: BSNL offers the best coverage; limited on remote islands
  • Internet: Wi-Fi available at most resorts but can be slow
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Power outages can occur on smaller islands
  • Restricted areas: Some tribal areas are off-limits to tourists
  • Marine conservation: Do not collect coral or shells from beaches
  • Alcohol: Available at licensed shops and resorts
