Kerala: God's Own Country

Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. From the serene backwaters to the misty hill stations, this 12-day itinerary covers the best of what this southern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Kochi & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Cochin International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in Fort Kochi. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Brunton Boatyard or Taj Malabar for luxury stays; Old Harbour Hotel or Fort House for heritage options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Fort Kochi

Enjoy your first Kerala meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Fort House Restaurant' or 'Oceanos'. Visit the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets along the shoreline of Fort Kochi, a legacy of ancient Chinese explorers.

Lunch recommendation: Try Kerala classics like Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish), Avial (mixed vegetables), and Payasam (sweet dessert).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit St. Francis Church, the oldest European church in India. Take a leisurely stroll along the Vasco da Gama Square. End your day with a traditional Kathakali performance at the Kerala Kathakali Centre.

Evening activity: Kathakali is a classical dance form known for its elaborate costumes and makeup. Arrive early to watch the artists prepare.

Chinese Fishing Nets

Kathakali Performance

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Fort Kochi is best explored on foot. Wear comfortable walking shoes.
  • Book your Kathakali show tickets in advance during peak season.
  • Try the fresh seafood at restaurants near the Chinese fishing nets.
Day 2

Kochi Heritage Tour

Morning: Jewish Quarter & Synagogue

Start your day with a visit to the Paradesi Synagogue in Jew Town, built in 1568. Explore the surrounding antique shops and spice markets that showcase Kerala's trading history.

Activity: Shop for spices, antiques, and handcrafted items in the markets of Jew Town.

Afternoon: Mattancherry Palace & Museums

Visit the Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace) with its remarkable murals depicting scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata. Continue to the Kerala Folklore Museum to learn about the state's rich cultural heritage.

Lunch: Try a traditional Kerala Sadhya (feast) served on a banana leaf at a local restaurant.

Evening: Sunset Cruise

Take a sunset cruise on the Kochi backwaters. Enjoy the panoramic views of the harbor, Marine Drive, and the city skyline as the sun sets.

Dinner: Enjoy fresh seafood at a waterfront restaurant like 'Fusion Bay' or 'Malabar Junction'.

Day 3

Kochi to Alleppey

Morning: Departure to Alleppey

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Alleppey (approximately 1.5 hours), known as the "Venice of the East" for its network of canals and backwaters. Alleppey is famous for its houseboat cruises through the serene backwaters.

Note: Pre-book your houseboat for an overnight stay. Various categories are available from standard to luxury.

Afternoon: Houseboat Check-in & Cruise

Board your traditional Kerala houseboat (Kettuvallam) and begin your cruise through the backwaters. Enjoy lunch prepared by the onboard chef featuring local specialties. Cruise through narrow canals, passing villages, paddy fields, and coconut groves.

Lunch: Traditional Kerala cuisine prepared fresh on the houseboat.

Evening: Sunset on the Backwaters

Experience the magical sunset over the backwaters from the deck of your houseboat. As night falls, the boat will dock at a quiet spot. Enjoy dinner and overnight stay on the houseboat.

Experience: The peaceful ambiance of the backwaters at night, with stars overhead and the gentle sounds of nature.

Traditional Kerala Houseboat

Important Information
  • Houseboats are equipped with bedrooms, bathrooms, and a common sitting area.
  • Most houseboats have air conditioning that runs during the night (7 PM to 7 AM).
  • Carry insect repellent, sunscreen, hat, and light cotton clothes.
  • Mobile network is available in most areas of the backwaters.
  • The best time to experience the backwaters is from September to March.
Backwater Experience

The Kerala backwaters are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets formed by more than 900 km of waterways. The houseboat cruise offers a unique perspective of rural Kerala life that has remained unchanged for centuries. You'll see locals fishing, farming, and going about their daily activities along the water's edge.

Day 4

Alleppey Exploration

Early Morning: Sunrise on the Backwaters

Wake up early to witness the sunrise over the backwaters. Watch as the local life begins to stir with fishermen setting out in their small canoes and villagers starting their day.

Activity: Enjoy a cup of traditional Kerala coffee or tea on the deck as you take in the peaceful morning atmosphere.

Morning: Breakfast & Disembarkation

Enjoy breakfast on the houseboat before disembarking around 9:30 AM. Check into a hotel in Alleppey town for your second night.

Accommodation: Ramada Alleppey, Punnamada Resort, or Lemon Tree Vembanad Lake Resort for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Village Experience

Take a guided village tour or bicycle ride through the rural areas around Alleppey. Visit local coir-making units, toddy shops (local palm wine taverns), and interact with villagers to understand their way of life.

Cultural experience: Learn about traditional occupations like coir-making, fishing, and farming that sustain the local economy.

Evening: Alleppey Beach

Visit Alleppey Beach to watch the sunset. The 150-year-old pier extending into the sea is a popular spot for photography. Enjoy dinner at a beachside restaurant serving fresh seafood.

Dinner: Try Kerala-style fish curry, prawns, and other seafood delicacies.

Sunrise on the Backwaters

Village Life in Alleppey

About Alleppey

Alleppey (Alappuzha) is known as the "Venice of the East" due to its extensive network of canals and backwaters. The town is famous for its houseboat cruises, coir industry, and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race held on the second Saturday of August. The backwaters of Alleppey offer a glimpse into rural Kerala life that has remained largely unchanged for generations.

Extensive network of backwaters and canals
Famous for traditional houseboats (Kettuvallams)
Home to the prestigious Nehru Trophy Boat Race
Day 5

Alleppey to Kumarakom

Morning: Journey to Kumarakom

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Kumarakom (approximately 1 hour), a cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake. Kumarakom is known for its bird sanctuary and luxury backwater resorts.

Note: The journey offers beautiful views of paddy fields, coconut groves, and backwater stretches.

Afternoon: Resort Check-in & Relaxation

Check into your lakeside resort in Kumarakom. Most resorts here offer a blend of traditional Kerala architecture with modern amenities. Spend the afternoon relaxing by the pool or indulging in an Ayurvedic spa treatment.

Accommodation: Kumarakom Lake Resort, Coconut Lagoon, or Zuri Kumarakom for luxury stays.

Evening: Bird Sanctuary Visit

Visit the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, spread across 14 acres. The sanctuary is home to numerous migratory birds including Siberian storks, egrets, darters, herons, and waterfowl. The best time to visit is early morning or late evening.

Dinner: Enjoy a traditional Kerala dinner at your resort, often featuring fresh catch from the lake.

Lakeside Resort in Kumarakom

About Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a picturesque cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake in Kerala. It's part of the Kuttanad region, known as the 'Rice Bowl of Kerala' due to its vast paddy fields. The destination offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, bird watching opportunities, and luxury resorts that specialize in Ayurvedic treatments.

Famous for its bird sanctuary with rare migratory birds
Known for luxury resorts offering Ayurvedic treatments
Located on the banks of Vembanad Lake, the largest lake in Kerala
Day 6

Kumarakom to Thekkady

Morning: Ayurvedic Experience

Start your day with an optional Ayurvedic massage or wellness treatment at your resort. Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine, has deep roots in Kerala.

Activity: If time permits, take a short canoe ride through the narrow canals to observe local village life.

Afternoon: Journey to Thekkady

After lunch, check out and begin your journey to Thekkady (approximately 4 hours drive). The route takes you through rubber plantations, spice gardens, and the Western Ghats mountain range with its winding roads and scenic views.

En route: Stop at a spice plantation for a guided tour to learn about various spices grown in Kerala.

Evening: Arrival in Thekkady

Arrive in Thekkady, home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary. Check into your hotel and rest. If time permits, visit the local market to shop for spices and handicrafts.

Accommodation: Spice Village, Cardamom County, or Greenwoods Resort for comfortable stays amidst nature.

Ayurvedic Treatment

Spice Plantation

About Thekkady

Thekkady is home to the Periyar National Park, one of India's most well-known wildlife sanctuaries. The park is centered around the artificial Periyar Lake formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam. The region is famous for its spice plantations, growing cardamom, cinnamon, pepper, cloves, and nutmeg. Thekkady offers a perfect blend of wildlife experiences and cultural insights into Kerala's spice trade history.

Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary
Famous for spice plantations
Located in the Western Ghats mountain range
Day 7

Thekkady to Munnar

Morning: Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Early morning boat safari on Periyar Lake to spot wildlife including elephants, sambar deer, wild boar, and if you're lucky, tigers. The sanctuary is also home to over 265 species of birds.

Alternative: If you prefer, you can opt for a guided jungle walk or bamboo rafting instead of the boat safari.

Afternoon: Cultural Show & Departure

Watch a Kalaripayattu performance, Kerala's ancient martial art form. After lunch, begin your journey to Munnar (approximately 4 hours drive), a hill station known for its tea plantations.

En route: The journey offers breathtaking views of the Western Ghats, waterfalls, and tea estates. Make stops for photography.

Evening: Arrival in Munnar

Arrive in Munnar, situated at an altitude of 1,600 meters above sea level. Check into your hotel and rest. If time permits, take a short walk around the town center.

Accommodation: Windermere Estate, Tea County, or The Tall Trees for comfortable stays with mountain views.

Boat Safari on Periyar Lake

Munnar Tea Plantations

About Munnar

Munnar is a picturesque hill station in the Western Ghats mountain range. It was once the summer resort of the British government in South India. The town is famous for its vast tea plantations, rolling hills, and cool climate. The name 'Munnar' means 'three rivers' as it is situated at the confluence of three mountain streams - Mudrapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala.

Famous for vast tea plantations
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Home to Anamudi, the highest peak in South India
Day 8

Munnar Exploration

Morning: Tea Plantation Tour

Visit the Tata Tea Museum to learn about the history and process of tea production. Take a guided tour of a tea plantation to see how tea is grown, harvested, and processed. Enjoy tea tasting sessions to appreciate different varieties.

Activity: Try plucking tea leaves alongside the workers for a hands-on experience (if permitted).

Afternoon: Scenic Spots

Visit Mattupetty Dam and Lake, a popular picnic spot with boating facilities. Continue to Echo Point, known for its natural echo phenomenon, and Top Station, which offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats and neighboring Tamil Nadu.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local restaurant during your tour.

Evening: Eravikulam National Park

Visit Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr (mountain goat). The park also has a variety of flora including the Neelakurinji flower that blooms once every 12 years, covering the hills in blue.

Note: The park may be closed during the breeding season of the Nilgiri Tahr (January-March). Check before planning your visit.

Day 9

Munnar to Wayanad

Morning: Optional Activities

Choose from optional activities like a short trek to nearby hills, visit to Attukal Waterfalls, or explore Blossom Park. If you prefer a relaxed morning, enjoy the amenities at your hotel.

Activity: Early morning yoga or meditation session with mountain views can be arranged at most hotels.

Afternoon: Journey to Wayanad

After lunch, check out and begin your journey to Wayanad (approximately 5-6 hours drive). The route takes you through winding mountain roads, forests, and small towns, offering spectacular views.

En route: Make stops at viewpoints for photography. The journey crosses from the eastern to the western side of the Western Ghats.

Evening: Arrival in Wayanad

Arrive in Wayanad, a rural district known for its wildlife, spice plantations, and tribal heritage. Check into your resort or homestay and rest after the long journey.

Accommodation: Vythiri Resort, Wayanad Wild, or Tranquil Resort for nature-focused stays; numerous homestays also offer authentic experiences.

Lush Green Landscape of Wayanad

About Wayanad

Wayanad is a rural district in Kerala located on the southern tip of the Deccan Plateau. The name 'Wayanad' is derived from 'Vayal Nadu' which means 'land of paddy fields'. The region is known for its tribal heritage, spice plantations, wildlife sanctuaries, and ancient caves with prehistoric carvings. Wayanad's landscape is characterized by mist-clad mountains, dense forests, and terraced paddy fields.

Home to Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary
Rich tribal heritage and culture
Famous for coffee, tea, and spice plantations
Day 10

Wayanad Exploration

Morning: Pookode Lake & Waterfalls

Visit Pookode Lake, a freshwater lake surrounded by forests and hills. Enjoy boating and visit the freshwater aquarium. Continue to Soochipara Waterfalls (also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls), a three-tiered waterfall perfect for a refreshing dip.

Activity: Short trek to reach the base of the waterfall through coffee and spice plantations.

Afternoon: Edakkal Caves

After lunch, visit the Edakkal Caves, famous for their ancient petroglyphs (rock carvings) dating back to the Neolithic age. The caves are reached after a moderate trek up Ambukuthi Hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Note: The trek to the caves takes about 45 minutes and involves climbing steps. Wear comfortable shoes and carry water.

Evening: Plantation Tour

Visit a spice plantation to learn about various spices grown in Wayanad including pepper, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Many plantations also grow coffee, tea, and vanilla. End your day with a traditional Kerala dinner at your accommodation.

Cultural experience: Some resorts arrange cultural performances showcasing tribal dances of Wayanad.

Day 11

Wayanad to Kovalam

Morning: Wildlife Sanctuary

Early morning visit to Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary (Muthanga), part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. Take a jeep safari through the sanctuary to spot elephants, deer, tigers, and various bird species.

Note: Wildlife sightings are not guaranteed and depend on various factors including season and time of day.

Afternoon: Journey to Kozhikode

After breakfast, check out and drive to Kozhikode (Calicut) airport (approximately 3 hours). Take a flight to Trivandrum (approximately 1 hour), the capital city of Kerala.

Alternative: If you prefer, you can drive the entire distance (approximately 8-9 hours), though this would be a long journey.

Evening: Arrival in Kovalam

From Trivandrum airport, drive to Kovalam (approximately 30 minutes), a beach town known for its crescent-shaped beaches. Check into your beach resort and relax after the journey.

Accommodation: The Leela Kovalam, Taj Green Cove Resort, or Niraamaya Retreats for luxury beach stays.

Kovalam Beach

About Kovalam

Kovalam is a beach town located on the Arabian Sea coast in Kerala. The name 'Kovalam' means 'grove of coconut trees', and the town is known for its three adjacent crescent-shaped beaches. The most famous is Lighthouse Beach, named after the 30-meter high lighthouse which offers panoramic views of the coastline. Kovalam was once a calm fishing village that transformed into one of India's most popular beach destinations.

Famous for its crescent-shaped beaches
Known for Ayurvedic treatments and resorts
Popular for water sports and activities
Day 12

Kovalam & Departure

Morning: Beach Time

Spend a relaxed morning at Kovalam Beach. Take a dip in the Arabian Sea, sunbathe on the golden sands, or try water sports like surfing, parasailing, or jet skiing if you're feeling adventurous.

Activity: Visit the iconic red and white striped lighthouse at Lighthouse Beach for panoramic views of the coastline.

Afternoon: Ayurvedic Spa

Indulge in an Ayurvedic massage or treatment at one of Kovalam's renowned wellness centers. Kerala is famous for its Ayurvedic traditions, and this is a perfect way to end your trip.

Shopping: Pick up some souvenirs from the beachside shops. Handicrafts, spices, and Ayurvedic products make great mementos.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Trivandrum International Airport (approximately 30 minutes) for your onward journey. If your flight is the next day, you can extend your stay in Kovalam or explore Trivandrum city.

Optional: If time permits, visit the Padmanabhaswamy Temple or Napier Museum in Trivandrum before your departure.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to February: The best time with pleasant weather across Kerala
  • March to May: Summer season, good for hill stations like Munnar and Wayanad
  • June to September: Monsoon season, lush green landscapes but frequent rainfall
  • Backwaters: Best experienced from August to March
  • Wildlife: October to March offers the best sightings
  • Ayurvedic treatments: Monsoon season (June-August) is traditionally considered ideal

Transportation

  • Air: International airports at Kochi, Trivandrum, and Kozhikode
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities and towns
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in cities; auto-rickshaws for short distances
  • Road Trips: Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience
  • Boats: Essential for exploring the backwaters and reaching some coastal areas
  • Public Transport: Kerala has a good network of government buses

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for plains; light woolens for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and flip-flops for beaches
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Umbrella during monsoon, modest clothing for temple visits

Food & Cuisine

Kerala offers a diverse culinary experience:

Kerala Specialties
  • Appam with Stew
  • Kerala Sadhya (traditional feast)
  • Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish)
  • Malabar Biryani
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Payasam (sweet dessert)
Regional Variations
  • Malabar: Influenced by Arab traders
  • Central Kerala: Traditional vegetarian dishes
  • Coastal areas: Seafood specialties
  • Beverages: Fresh coconut water, Sulaimani tea
  • Street food: Pazham Pori, Unnakaya, Kozhikkodan Halwa
  • Spices: Black pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves

Practical Information

  • Language: Malayalam is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Kerala is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
  • Festivals: Onam (August-September), Vishu (April), and Thrissur Pooram (April-May) are major celebrations
