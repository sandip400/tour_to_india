Odisha: The Soul of Incredible India

Odisha (formerly Orissa) offers a perfect blend of ancient temples, pristine beaches, serene lakes, and vibrant tribal culture. From the magnificent temples of Bhubaneswar to the golden beaches of Puri, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this eastern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Bhubaneswar & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar. Transfer to your hotel in the city center. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Mayfair Lagoon or Trident Bhubaneswar for luxury stays; Ginger Bhubaneswar or Empires Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Ekamra Haat

Enjoy your first Odia meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Dalma' or 'Odisha Hotel'. Visit Ekamra Haat, a handicraft market where you can explore local arts and crafts of Odisha.

Lunch recommendation: Try Dalma (lentil and vegetable curry), Pakhala (fermented rice) or Machha Besara (fish curry) with steamed rice.

Evening: Lingaraj Temple & Bindu Sagar

Visit the magnificent Lingaraj Temple (from outside, as non-Hindus are not allowed inside) dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the oldest temples in Bhubaneswar. Continue to Bindu Sagar, a sacred lake surrounded by numerous temples.

Evening activity: Take a leisurely walk around the temple area to admire the architecture and spiritual ambiance. Non-Hindus can view the temple from a designated viewing platform.

Day 1 Tips
  • Bhubaneswar is known as the "Temple City of India" with over 700 temples.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Photography may be restricted at certain religious sites.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
Day 2

Bhubaneswar Temple Tour & Caves

Morning: Temple Circuit

Embark on a temple tour of Bhubaneswar. Visit the exquisite Mukteshwar Temple known for its ornate gateway, the Rajarani Temple famous for its sculptural excellence, and the Brahmeswara Temple with its intricate carvings.

Activity: Consider hiring a local guide who can explain the historical and architectural significance of these ancient temples.

Afternoon: Udayagiri & Khandagiri Caves

After lunch, visit the Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves, ancient Jain rock-cut shelters dating back to the 1st century BCE. Explore the intricate carvings and inscriptions that depict Jain mythology and royal scenes.

Note: Wear comfortable shoes as there will be some climbing involved to reach the caves.

Evening: Odisha State Museum & Dinner

Visit the Odisha State Museum to learn about the rich cultural heritage of the state through its collection of sculptures, paintings, manuscripts, and tribal artifacts. End your day with dinner at a local restaurant.

Dinner: Try Odia thali at Dalma Restaurant or enjoy seafood at Mayfair Kitchen.

Day 3

Bhubaneswar to Puri

Morning: Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Visit Dhauli Hill, the site of the historic Kalinga War where Emperor Ashoka embraced Buddhism. Explore the Peace Pagoda (Shanti Stupa) built in collaboration with Japan and the ancient rock edicts of Ashoka.

Activity: Take in the panoramic views of the Daya River and surrounding landscapes from the hilltop.

Afternoon: Drive to Puri

After lunch, drive to Puri (approximately 1.5 hours, 60 km), one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Mayfair Heritage or Toshali Sands for luxury; Hotel Holiday Resort or Sterling Puri for mid-range options.

Evening: Puri Beach & Market

Visit the golden beaches of Puri to enjoy the sunset. Take a leisurely walk along the beach and watch local fishermen with their traditional boats. Explore the local markets for souvenirs, especially the famous Puri paintings and seashell crafts.

Dinner: Try seafood at a beachside restaurant like Wildgrass or Chung Wah.

Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Puri Beach

About Puri

Puri is one of the oldest cities in eastern India and a major pilgrimage destination. It is famous for the Jagannath Temple, dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Vishnu. The city hosts the famous Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) annually, which attracts millions of devotees from around the world.

Besides its religious significance, Puri is known for its beautiful beaches, traditional arts and crafts, and delicious seafood cuisine.

Important Note

Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the Jagannath Temple. However, you can view the temple from the roof of the nearby Raghunandan Library. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and respect local customs.

Day 4

Puri & Konark

Morning: Jagannath Temple & Puri Heritage Walk

Take a heritage walk around the Jagannath Temple complex (non-Hindus can view from outside). Learn about the temple's history, architecture, and cultural significance. Explore the narrow lanes of the old town with its many maths (monasteries) and smaller temples.

Activity: Visit the Raghunandan Library for a view of the temple from its roof.

Afternoon: Konark Sun Temple

Drive to Konark (approximately 35 km from Puri) to visit the magnificent Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This 13th-century temple is designed in the form of a colossal chariot with intricately carved stone wheels, pillars, and walls.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Konark like Yatri Nivas or pack a lunch from Puri.

Evening: Chandrabhaga Beach & Return to Puri

Visit Chandrabhaga Beach near Konark, known for its clean shores and beautiful sunset views. Return to Puri for dinner and overnight stay.

Dinner: Try the local delicacy Khaja (a sweet pastry) and Chhena Poda (cheese dessert) at a local sweet shop in Puri.

Konark Sun Temple

Historical Significance

The Konark Sun Temple was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. It is designed as a massive chariot for the sun god Surya, with 12 pairs of elaborately carved wheels pulled by seven horses. The temple is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture and showcases the artistic excellence of ancient Odisha.

The temple's name is derived from two Sanskrit words: 'Kona' (corner) and 'Arka' (sun), as it was built to worship the Sun God. Despite its partial ruins, the temple remains one of India's most stunning architectural marvels.

Day 5

Puri to Chilika Lake

Morning: Raghurajpur Crafts Village

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Raghurajpur, a heritage crafts village famous for Pattachitra paintings (traditional cloth-based scroll paintings) and other handicrafts. Meet local artisans and watch them create intricate artwork.

Activity: Participate in a short Pattachitra painting workshop if available.

Afternoon: Drive to Chilika Lake

Continue your journey to Chilika Lake (approximately 2 hours from Raghurajpur), Asia's largest brackish water lagoon and a wetland of international importance. Check into your accommodation at Satapada or Barkul.

Accommodation: Panthanivas Satapada or Chilika Dhaba for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Sunset Boat Ride

Take a boat ride on Chilika Lake to enjoy the beautiful sunset views. Look for Irrawaddy dolphins, which are a special attraction of the lake. The lake is also home to numerous migratory birds during winter months.

Dinner: Enjoy fresh seafood at a local restaurant near the lake or at your accommodation.

Raghurajpur Crafts Village

Chilika Lake

About Chilika Lake

Chilika Lake is Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, spreading across the districts of Puri, Khurda, and Ganjam. It is the largest wintering ground for migratory birds on the Indian subcontinent, hosting over 160 species of birds during the winter months.

The lake is also home to the rare Irrawaddy dolphins, which can be spotted during boat rides. The Nalabana Bird Sanctuary within the lake is a designated Ramsar site (wetland of international importance).

Wildlife Spotting

Best time for bird watching: November to February

Best time for dolphin spotting: Early morning or late afternoon

Remember to maintain silence during wildlife spotting and follow responsible tourism practices.

Day 6

Chilika Lake to Gopalpur

Morning: Bird Watching & Dolphin Spotting

Take an early morning boat ride to Nalabana Bird Sanctuary within Chilika Lake for bird watching. During winter months (November to February), the lake hosts millions of migratory birds from as far as Siberia and the Caspian Sea. Continue to the outer channel for dolphin spotting.

Activity: Bring binoculars for better wildlife viewing and a camera with zoom lens for photography.

Afternoon: Drive to Gopalpur

After lunch, check out from your accommodation and drive to Gopalpur-on-Sea (approximately 2.5 hours, 100 km), a serene beach town on the Bay of Bengal. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Mayfair Palm Beach Resort or Swosti Palm Resort for luxury; Hotel Moti or Sea Pearl for mid-range options.

Evening: Gopalpur Beach

Spend the evening at Gopalpur Beach, known for its tranquil atmosphere and golden sands. Watch the sunset over the Bay of Bengal and enjoy the cool sea breeze. The beach is less crowded compared to Puri, offering a more peaceful experience.

Dinner: Enjoy seafood at a beachside restaurant or at your hotel.

Migratory Birds at Chilika

Gopalpur Beach

About Gopalpur

Gopalpur was once a bustling seaport trading with Southeast Asian countries like Burma (Myanmar), Java, and Sumatra. Today, it's a quiet beach town known for its pristine beaches and colonial charm. The old lighthouse, built during British rule, is a notable landmark.

The town has a laid-back atmosphere, making it perfect for those seeking relaxation away from crowded tourist destinations. The beach is known for its gentle waves, though swimming should be done with caution as the currents can be strong at times.

Day 7

Gopalpur to Bhubaneswar & Departure

Morning: Sunrise at Gopalpur Beach

Wake up early to witness the beautiful sunrise over the Bay of Bengal. Take a morning walk along the beach and visit the old lighthouse if time permits.

Activity: Collect seashells as souvenirs or simply enjoy the peaceful morning atmosphere.

Afternoon: Return to Bhubaneswar

After breakfast and check-out, begin your journey back to Bhubaneswar (approximately 4 hours, 180 km). Depending on your departure time, you may check into a hotel for a short rest or head directly to the airport.

En route: Consider stopping at Taptapani Hot Springs if time permits, located about halfway between Gopalpur and Bhubaneswar.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Bhubaneswar for souvenirs like Pattachitra paintings, Sambalpuri textiles, or silver filigree work.

Departure: Transfer to Biju Patnaik International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Winter months with pleasant temperatures ideal for sightseeing
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional heavy rainfall
  • Avoid April to June: Summer months with high temperatures and humidity
  • For bird watching at Chilika: November to February
  • For Rath Yatra in Puri: June/July (dates vary as per Hindu calendar)

Transportation

  • Air: Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar has good connectivity
  • Train: Bhubaneswar and Puri are well-connected by railways
  • Road: Good highway network connecting major destinations
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes, modest attire for temples
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits
  • Beach essentials: Swimwear, sunscreen, hat, sunglasses
  • For bird watching: Binoculars, zoom lens camera
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, insect repellent
  • Other: Power bank, universal adapter, water bottle

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pakhala (fermented rice)
  • Machha Besara (fish curry)
  • Chhena Poda (cheese dessert)

  • Kankada Jhola (crab curry)

  • Khaja (sweet pastry from Puri)
