Manipur: The Land of Jewels

Manipur offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. From the vibrant city of Imphal to the floating islands of Loktak Lake, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northeastern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Imphal & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Imphal International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Imphal. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Classic Hotel, Hotel Imphal, or The Classic Grande for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Lunch & Kangla Fort

Enjoy your first Manipuri meal at a traditional restaurant. Visit Kangla Fort, the ancient seat of Manipuri kings, with its moat, temples, and historical structures.

Lunch recommendation: Try Manipuri classics like Eromba (vegetable dish with fermented fish), Chamthong (vegetable stew), and Singju (spicy salad).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit Shri Govindajee Temple, a significant religious site with twin domes. Explore Ima Keithel (Mother's Market), one of the largest all-women markets in Asia. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Evening activity: If available, catch a traditional Manipuri dance performance at the Manipur Dance Academy.

Kangla Fort

Ima Keithel (Mother's Market)

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use local taxis or auto-rickshaws for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites like Shri Govindajee Temple.
  • Ima Keithel is closed on Sundays, plan accordingly.
Day 2

Imphal Museums & War Memorials

Morning: Manipur State Museum

Start your day with a visit to the Manipur State Museum, which houses a rich collection of historical artifacts, tribal costumes, weapons, and paintings that showcase the cultural heritage of Manipur.

Activity: Learn about the state's royal history and diverse tribal cultures through the exhibits.

Afternoon: INA Memorial & Japanese War Memorial

Visit the INA Memorial Complex in Moirang (a short drive from Imphal), where the flag of the Indian National Army was first hoisted on Indian soil. Continue to the India Peace Memorial (Japanese War Memorial) at Red Hill, commemorating the Battle of Imphal during World War II.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Moirang.

Evening: Imphal War Cemetery & Local Market

Visit the Imphal War Cemetery, maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission. Explore the local markets of Imphal for handicrafts, handloom products, and souvenirs.

Dinner: Try local street food like Paknam (fish cake), Morok Metpa (chili chutney), and Chak-hao Kheer (black rice pudding).

Day 3

Loktak Lake & Keibul Lamjao National Park

Morning: Drive to Loktak Lake

Early morning departure from Imphal to Loktak Lake (approximately 45 minutes by road). Loktak Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India and is famous for its floating islands called 'phumdis'.

Note: Pre-book your boat ride on Loktak Lake to explore the phumdis and visit the floating Keibul Lamjao National Park.

Afternoon: Boat Safari & National Park

Enjoy a boat safari through Loktak Lake, observing the unique ecosystem of floating phumdis. Visit Keibul Lamjao National Park, the world's only floating national park and home to the endangered Sangai deer (brow-antlered deer).

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at a local eatery near the lake or pack a picnic lunch.

Evening: Sendra Island & Overnight Stay

Visit Sendra Island, a hillock within Loktak Lake that offers panoramic views. Stay overnight at a homestay or resort near Loktak Lake to experience the serene environment.

Accommodation options: Sendra Resort, Loktak Aquamarine Floating Homestay, or local homestays in Moirang.

Loktak Lake with Phumdis

Important Information
  • Carry insect repellent, sunscreen, hat, and light cotton clothes.
  • Photography is allowed but be respectful of the natural environment.
  • Mobile network may be limited around Loktak Lake.
  • Follow all safety instructions given by your guide during the boat ride.
  • The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant.
Wildlife Spotting

Keibul Lamjao National Park is home to the endangered Sangai deer, found only in Manipur. While sightings are not guaranteed, you might also spot various bird species, wild boars, and other wildlife. Early morning and late afternoon are the best times for wildlife spotting.

Day 4

Moirang & Return to Imphal

Morning: Sunrise at Loktak Lake

Early morning visit to witness the sunrise over Loktak Lake. The morning light creates a magical atmosphere as it illuminates the phumdis and surrounding hills.

Activity: Take a morning walk along the lakeshore or enjoy a short boat ride to capture the beauty of the lake at dawn.

Afternoon: Moirang Cultural Sites

Visit the Moirang Thangjing Temple, dedicated to the deity Thangjing. Explore the INA Museum in Moirang, which houses artifacts and documents related to the Indian National Army and Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Manipuri lunch at a local restaurant in Moirang.

Evening: Return to Imphal

Return to Imphal by road (approximately 45 minutes). Check into your hotel in Imphal. Spend the evening relaxing or exploring the local markets for handicrafts and souvenirs.

Accommodation: Return to your hotel in Imphal for the night.

About Moirang

Moirang is a historic town in Manipur, significant for being the place where the flag of the Indian National Army was first hoisted on Indian soil on April 14, 1944. It is also associated with the legendary love story of Khamba and Thoibi, which is an important part of Manipuri folklore and culture.

Day 5

Ukhrul & Shirui Peak

Morning: Drive to Ukhrul

Early morning departure from Imphal to Ukhrul (approximately 3 hours by road), a picturesque hill station and home to the Tangkhul Naga tribe. The drive offers beautiful views of terraced fields and rolling hills.

Note: The road to Ukhrul can be winding, so take motion sickness medication if needed.

Afternoon: Shirui Peak Trek

After lunch in Ukhrul, embark on a trek to Shirui Peak (weather permitting). Shirui Peak is famous for the rare and endangered Shirui Lily (Lilium mackliniae), which blooms between May and June. The trek offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Note: If visiting during May-June, you might be lucky to see the Shirui Lily in bloom.

Evening: Cultural Experience & Overnight Stay

Return to Ukhrul town. Experience the local Tangkhul Naga culture through traditional music and dance performances. Stay overnight at a homestay or guesthouse in Ukhrul.

Accommodation options: Ukhrul Inn, local homestays, or government guesthouses.

Day 6

Dzüko Valley Trek

Morning: Drive to Dzüko Valley Base

Early morning departure from Ukhrul to Viswema/Jakhama village (approximately 4 hours by road), which serves as the base for the Dzüko Valley trek. The Dzüko Valley is known for its rolling hills, meadows, and seasonal wildflowers.

Note: Pack light for the trek. Carry water, snacks, and essential supplies.

Afternoon: Trek to Dzüko Valley

Begin the trek to Dzüko Valley (approximately 2-3 hours one way). The initial climb is steep but rewards you with breathtaking views of the valley. Explore the valley with its unique landscape of rolling hills and seasonal flowers.

Lunch: Packed lunch to be enjoyed in the valley.

Evening: Return to Imphal

Return trek to the base and drive back to Imphal (approximately 3 hours). Check into your hotel in Imphal for the night.

Note: This is a long day with significant travel time. An alternative is to camp overnight in Dzüko Valley (requires prior arrangements) and return to Imphal the next day.

Dzüko Valley

Trekking Information
  • Difficulty Level: Moderate (suitable for those with basic fitness)
  • Duration: 2-3 hours one way
  • Best Time: April-June for wildflowers, October-December for clear views
  • What to Carry: Water, snacks, rain jacket, camera, first aid kit
  • Footwear: Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
Valley of Flowers

If you visit during May-July, Dzüko Valley transforms into a carpet of colorful wildflowers, including the rare Dzüko Lily. The valley is often referred to as the "Valley of Flowers of the Northeast" and offers spectacular photo opportunities.

Day 7

Imphal & Departure

Morning: Handicraft Shopping

Visit the Manipur Handloom & Handicrafts Development Corporation showroom to shop for traditional Manipuri textiles, including the famous Phanek (traditional sarong), Wangkhei Phee (traditional shawl), and other handloom products.

Also visit: Khwairamband Bazaar for local handicrafts and souvenirs.

Afternoon: Farewell Lunch

Enjoy a farewell lunch featuring traditional Manipuri cuisine. Visit any remaining sites of interest in Imphal, such as the RKCS Art Gallery or the Second World War Cemetery.

Lunch recommendation: Try a traditional Manipuri thali with various local dishes.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Imphal International Airport for your departure flight. If your flight is the next day, enjoy a relaxed evening in Imphal, perhaps catching a cultural performance or enjoying dinner at a local restaurant.

Note: Confirm your flight details and arrange for airport transfer in advance.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Ideal time with pleasant weather and clear skies
  • April to June: Good for seeing wildflowers, including the Shirui Lily (May-June)
  • July to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall (not recommended)
  • Festivals: Plan your visit during Sangai Festival (November) or Lai Haraoba (April-May) for cultural experiences

Transportation

  • Air: Imphal International Airport has direct flights from major cities like Delhi, Kolkata, and Guwahati
  • Road: Connected to neighboring states via NH-2 and NH-37
  • Local Transport: Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and local buses within Imphal
  • Recommendation: Hire a private car with a local driver for exploring areas outside Imphal

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light to medium weight clothing, with a light jacket for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and trekking shoes for hikes
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Documents: ID proof, travel insurance, emergency contacts

Food & Cuisine

Manipuri cuisine is unique and flavorful, with a focus on organic ingredients:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Eromba (vegetable dish with fermented fish)
  • Chamthong (vegetable stew)
  • Singju (spicy salad)
  • Paknam (fish cake)
  • Morok Metpa (chili chutney)
  • Chak-hao Kheer (black rice pudding)
Dining Tips
  • Most dishes are mildly spicy
  • Rice is the staple food
  • Fermented fish (Ngari) is a common ingredient
  • Try local fruits like heikha (passion fruit)
  • Black rice is a specialty of Manipur
  • Local rice beer (Yu) is a traditional drink

Practical Information

  • Language: Manipuri (Meiteilon) is the primary language. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in Imphal but limited in remote areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Imphal; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Manipur is generally safe for tourists. Stay updated on local conditions
  • Inner Line Permit: Not required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists need to register with the Foreigner Registration Officer in Imphal
  • Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly, especially at religious sites. Ask permission before photographing people
