Rajasthan: The Land of Kings

Rajasthan offers a mesmerizing blend of royal heritage, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes. From the pink city of Jaipur to the blue city of Jodhpur and the romantic lakes of Udaipur, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northwestern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Jaipur & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Jaipur International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Jaipur. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Rambagh Palace or Oberoi Rajvilas for luxury stays; Jai Mahal Palace or Samode Haveli for heritage options.

Afternoon: Lunch & City Palace

Enjoy your first Rajasthani meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Spice Court' or 'Niros'. Visit the City Palace, a stunning complex of courtyards, gardens, and buildings right in the heart of the Old City.

Lunch recommendation: Try Rajasthani classics like Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, and Ker Sangri.

Evening: Jantar Mantar & Markets

Visit Jantar Mantar, an astronomical observation site from the 18th century. Take a leisurely stroll through the vibrant markets of the Pink City. Enjoy dinner at Chokhi Dhani, a village-themed resort offering authentic Rajasthani cuisine and cultural performances.

Evening activity: Watch the light and sound show at Amer Fort if time permits.

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Wear comfortable footwear as you'll be walking a lot.
  • Respect dress codes when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain at local markets but be respectful.
Day 2

Jaipur Forts & Monuments

Morning: Amer Fort

Start your day with a visit to the magnificent Amer Fort, located on a hill overlooking Maota Lake. Take an elephant ride up to the fort (optional) or go by jeep. Explore the stunning Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Sukh Niwas, and Jai Mandir.

Activity: Don't miss the panoramic views of the surrounding hills and Jaipur city from the fort ramparts.

Afternoon: Jaigarh & Nahargarh Forts

Visit Jaigarh Fort, known for housing the world's largest cannon on wheels, Jaivana. Continue to Nahargarh Fort, which offers spectacular views of the city. Enjoy lunch at Padao restaurant inside Nahargarh Fort.

Note: These three forts (Amer, Jaigarh, and Nahargarh) form a complex and are connected to each other.

Evening: Jal Mahal & Cultural Show

Visit Jal Mahal (Water Palace) located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake for sunset views. Attend a cultural show at Govind Dev Ji Temple or enjoy shopping at Johari Bazaar for traditional Rajasthani jewelry.

Dinner: Try a rooftop restaurant like Peacock Restaurant or Handi for authentic Rajasthani cuisine with views of the illuminated city.

Day 3

Jaipur to Jodhpur

Morning: Drive to Jodhpur

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Jodhpur (approximately 5-6 hours). En route, you can stop at Ajmer and visit the famous Ajmer Sharif Dargah, a Sufi shrine.

Note: The drive offers beautiful views of the Rajasthani countryside. You can also stop at roadside dhabas (eateries) for authentic local food.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive in Jodhpur, known as the "Blue City" for its blue-painted houses. Check into your hotel and take some time to rest.

Accommodation: Umaid Bhawan Palace or Taj Hari Mahal for luxury stays; Ratan Vilas or Pal Haveli for heritage options.

Evening: Clock Tower & Markets

Visit the Clock Tower and explore the vibrant Sardar Market surrounding it. This is a great place to shop for textiles, spices, and handicrafts. Enjoy dinner at a rooftop restaurant with views of the illuminated Mehrangarh Fort.

Dinner recommendation: Try Indique or On the Rocks for a memorable dining experience with fort views.

Blue City of Jodhpur

Important Information
  • The drive from Jaipur to Jodhpur is long, so start early.
  • Keep some snacks and water for the journey.
  • Jodhpur can be extremely hot in summer, so dress accordingly.
  • The narrow lanes of the old city can be confusing, consider hiring a local guide.
About Jodhpur

Jodhpur, the second largest city in Rajasthan, was founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, chief of the Rathore clan. The city is known for its magnificent Mehrangarh Fort, blue-painted houses, and its rich cultural heritage. Jodhpur is also famous for its spicy snacks, sweets, handicrafts, and textiles.

Day 4

Jodhpur to Udaipur

Morning: Mehrangarh Fort

Visit the imposing Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India. Explore its various palaces, courtyards, and museums. Don't miss the panoramic views of the blue city from the fort ramparts.

Activity: Take an audio guide for a more informative experience.

Afternoon: Jaswant Thada & Drive to Udaipur

Visit Jaswant Thada, a marble memorial built in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II. After lunch, begin your journey to Udaipur (approximately 4-5 hours).

En route: You can stop at Ranakpur Jain Temple, known for its architectural marvel with 1444 marble pillars, each uniquely carved.

Evening: Arrival in Udaipur

Arrive in Udaipur, known as the "City of Lakes" or "Venice of the East." Check into your hotel and relax. If time permits, take a stroll around Lake Pichola.

Accommodation: Taj Lake Palace or The Oberoi Udaivilas for luxury stays; Amet Haveli or Jagat Niwas Palace for heritage options with lake views.

Day 5

Udaipur Exploration

Morning: City Palace

Visit the magnificent City Palace, the largest palace complex in Rajasthan, built on the banks of Lake Pichola. Explore its various courtyards, pavilions, terraces, corridors, rooms, and hanging gardens.

Note: Don't miss the Crystal Gallery and Vintage Car Collection within the palace complex.

Afternoon: Saheliyon Ki Bari & Jagdish Temple

Visit Saheliyon Ki Bari (Garden of the Maidens), featuring fountains, kiosks, marble elephants, and a lotus pool. Continue to Jagdish Temple, a large Hindu temple in the middle of Udaipur.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Ambrai Restaurant or Upre by 1559 AD, both offering stunning lake views.

Evening: Boat Ride & Cultural Show

Take a boat ride on Lake Pichola during sunset to enjoy views of the City Palace, Lake Palace, and Jag Mandir. In the evening, attend a cultural show at Bagore Ki Haveli featuring traditional Rajasthani folk dances and music.

Dinner: Enjoy a rooftop dinner at Raas Leela or Tribute Restaurant with views of the illuminated palaces.

Day 6

Udaipur to Jaisalmer

Morning: Monsoon Palace

Visit the Monsoon Palace (Sajjangarh Palace) located on a hilltop with panoramic views of Udaipur's lakes, palaces, and surrounding countryside. This palace was used as a monsoon retreat and for hunting by the royal family.

Note: The drive up to the palace offers beautiful views, so keep your camera ready.

Afternoon: Flight to Jaisalmer

After lunch, head to Udaipur Airport to catch a flight to Jaisalmer. Alternatively, you can take a train or drive, but these options take much longer (10-12 hours).

Note: Flights between Udaipur and Jaisalmer may not operate daily, so check the schedule in advance.

Evening: Arrival in Jaisalmer

Arrive in Jaisalmer, known as the "Golden City" for its yellow sandstone architecture. Check into your hotel and relax. If time permits, enjoy the sunset views of Jaisalmer Fort.

Accommodation: Suryagarh or The Oberoi Vanyavilas for luxury stays; Hotel Killa Bhawan or Nachana Haveli for heritage options.

About Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer, founded in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal, is a city in the heart of the Thar Desert. It's famous for its magnificent Jaisalmer Fort (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), intricate Havelis (mansions), and the surrounding sand dunes. The city's remote location and desert landscape give it a magical, otherworldly feel.

Day 7

Jaisalmer to Jaipur & Departure

Morning: Jaisalmer Fort & Havelis

Explore Jaisalmer Fort, one of the largest fully preserved fortified cities in the world. Visit the ornate Havelis (mansions) like Patwon Ki Haveli, Salim Singh Ki Haveli, and Nathmal Ki Haveli, known for their intricate carvings and architecture.

Activity: Shop for local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs within the fort area.

Afternoon: Return to Jaipur

After lunch, head to Jaisalmer Airport to catch a flight back to Jaipur. Alternatively, you can fly to Delhi or Mumbai for your international connection, depending on your onward journey.

Note: If you have an evening flight out of Jaipur, you can spend more time exploring Jaisalmer in the morning.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Jaipur. Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar are good options for traditional Rajasthani souvenirs, textiles, and jewelry.

Departure: Transfer to Jaipur International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: The best time to visit Rajasthan with pleasant weather
  • November to February: Peak tourist season with cool temperatures
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional rainfall, less crowded
  • Avoid summer months (April to June) as temperatures can exceed 45Â°C (113Â°F)

Transportation

  • Air: Major cities like Jaipur, Jodhpur, and Udaipur have airports with good connectivity
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities and towns
  • Road: Well-maintained highways, but distances can be long
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes, but also carry a light jacket for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for exploring forts and palaces
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, lip balm
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, hand sanitizer
  • Others: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Rajasthan offers a rich culinary experience with dishes that have evolved due to the scarcity of water and fresh vegetables in the desert region:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Dal Baati Churma
  • Gatte ki Sabzi
  • Ker Sangri
  • Laal Maas (spicy meat curry)
  • Pyaaz Kachori
  • Mawa Kachori (sweet)
Dining Experiences
  • Chokhi Dhani (village-themed resort)
  • Rooftop restaurants with fort views
  • Heritage hotel dining
  • Street food in old city markets
  • Desert camps with cultural performances
  • Roadside dhabas for authentic flavors

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is the primary language. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Rajasthan is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing is recommended, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Bargaining: Expected in markets and with street vendors, but be respectful
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
