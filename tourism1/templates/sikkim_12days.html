Explore the Wonders of Sikkim

A 12-day comprehensive journey through mountains, monasteries, and magical landscapes

Start Planning

Sikkim: A Himalayan Paradise

Sikkim offers a perfect blend of breathtaking mountain landscapes, rich Buddhist culture, and pristine natural beauty. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary allows you to explore the state in depth, from the vibrant capital of Gangtok to the remote lakes of North Sikkim and the tranquil monasteries of West Sikkim.

Highlights of the Tour

Panoramic views of Kanchenjunga
Ancient Buddhist monasteries
Sacred lakes and hot springs
Indo-China border at Nathula Pass
Valley of Flowers at Yumthang
Authentic Sikkimese cuisine
Local handicrafts and culture
Short treks and nature walks

Quick Facts

  • Duration: 12 Days
  • Starting Point: Bagdogra/NJP
  • Ending Point: Bagdogra/NJP
  • Languages: Nepali, Bhutia, Lepcha, English
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹)

Day 1

Arrival & Gangtok

Morning: Arrival & Journey to Gangtok

Arrive at Bagdogra Airport or New Jalpaiguri (NJP) Railway Station. Begin your scenic drive to Gangtok (approximately 4-5 hours), the capital city of Sikkim. Enjoy the beautiful landscapes along the Teesta River.

Note: Foreign nationals need Inner Line Permit (ILP) to enter Sikkim, which can be obtained at the Rangpo border checkpoint.

Afternoon: Check-in & Rest

Arrive in Gangtok and check into your hotel. Take some time to rest and acclimatize to the altitude (Gangtok is at 5,410 feet above sea level).

Accommodation: Mayfair Spa Resort & Casino or Elgin Nor-Khill for luxury stays; Summit Namnang Courtyard & Spa or Denzong Regency for mid-range options.

Evening: MG Marg Exploration

Visit MG Marg, the heart of Gangtok and a lively pedestrian-only zone. Explore the shops, cafes, and restaurants. Enjoy your first taste of Sikkimese cuisine for dinner.

Dinner recommendation: Try local specialties like momos (dumplings), thukpa (noodle soup), and Sikkimese noodles at restaurants like Taste of Tibet or Roll House.

Teesta River

MG Marg, Gangtok

Day 1 Tips
  • Keep your ID proof and permit documents handy during the journey.
  • Stay hydrated but avoid excessive water to help with altitude acclimatization.
  • Wear comfortable clothes as the temperature can vary during the drive.
  • MG Marg is pedestrian-only, so enjoy the traffic-free stroll.
Day 2

Gangtok Sightseeing

Morning: Monastery Tour

Start your day with a visit to Rumtek Monastery, one of the largest and most significant monasteries in Sikkim. Continue to Enchey Monastery, known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance.

Note: Foreign nationals need additional permits for Rumtek Monastery, which should be arranged in advance.

Afternoon: Botanical Gardens & Ropeway

Visit the Flower Exhibition Centre to see a variety of orchids and rhododendrons. Take a ride on the Gangtok Ropeway for panoramic views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Baker's Cafe or Gangtok Groove, both offering a mix of local and international cuisine.

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the Namgyal Institute of Tibetology to learn about Buddhist philosophy, art, and culture. Explore the Do Drul Chorten, a stupa surrounded by prayer wheels.

Dinner: Try traditional Sikkimese cuisine at Nepali Food Restaurant or The Tibetan Kitchen.

Hotel → Rumtek Monastery (24 km, 1 hour) → Enchey Monastery (7 km, 20 min) → Lunch break → Flower Exhibition Centre (walking distance) → Gangtok Ropeway (10 min) → Namgyal Institute of Tibetology (15 min) → Do Drul Chorten (walking distance) → Hotel

Day 3

Tsomgo Lake & Baba Mandir

Early Morning: Departure to Tsomgo Lake

Early morning departure from Gangtok to Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake), a glacial lake at an altitude of 12,400 feet. The drive takes approximately 2 hours through winding mountain roads with breathtaking views.

Note: Special permits are required for visiting Tsomgo Lake and Baba Mandir, which should be arranged a day in advance through your hotel or tour operator.

Morning: Tsomgo Lake Experience

Explore the sacred Tsomgo Lake, which remains frozen during winter months. Enjoy yak rides around the lake and take in the stunning alpine scenery. Visit the small Shiva temple on the lakeside.

Activity: Yak riding is a popular activity here, costing around ₹300-500 per ride.

Afternoon: Baba Mandir Visit

Continue to Baba Mandir, a shrine dedicated to Baba Harbhajan Singh, a soldier who is revered as the "Hero of Nathula". Learn about the interesting legends associated with this shrine.

Lunch: Enjoy packed lunch or eat at one of the small eateries near Tsomgo Lake or Baba Mandir.

Evening: Return to Gangtok

Return to Gangtok by evening. Spend the evening at leisure, perhaps exploring local markets for souvenirs or enjoying a traditional Sikkimese dinner.

Shopping: Look for Sikkimese handicrafts, prayer flags, and traditional jewelry at the Directorate of Handicrafts and Handloom or Lal Market.

Tsomgo Lake

Baba Mandir

Important Information
  • Carry warm clothes as temperatures at Tsomgo Lake can be much colder than Gangtok.
  • Altitude sickness is possible; move slowly and stay hydrated.
  • Photography is allowed but be respectful at religious sites.
  • The road to Tsomgo Lake can close during heavy snowfall or adverse weather.
  • Carry your original ID proof and permit copies during the trip.
Day 4

Nathula Pass

Morning: Journey to Nathula Pass

Early morning departure from Gangtok to Nathula Pass, located at an altitude of 14,140 feet on the Indo-China border. This historic pass was part of the ancient Silk Route connecting India and Tibet.

Note: Special permits are required for Nathula Pass, which are only issued to Indian nationals. Foreign tourists are not allowed. Permits should be arranged at least a day in advance.

Afternoon: Border Experience

Explore the Indo-China border at Nathula Pass. Witness the ceremonial exchange between Indian and Chinese soldiers. Enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges.

Important: Photography is restricted in certain areas. Follow all instructions given by the military personnel.

Evening: Return to Gangtok

Return to Gangtok by evening. Rest and relax at your hotel after a day at high altitude. Prepare for your journey to North Sikkim the next day.

Dinner: Enjoy a hearty dinner at one of Gangtok's restaurants. Try Marwari Restaurant or Chopsticks for a variety of options.

Nathula Pass

Historical Significance

Nathula Pass has significant historical importance as it was part of the ancient Silk Route connecting India and Tibet. The pass was closed after the 1962 Sino-Indian War and reopened in 2006 as a symbol of improving relations between India and China. Today, it serves as an official Border Personnel Meeting point between the Indian Army and the People's Liberation Army of China.

Altitude Warning

Nathula Pass is at an elevation of 14,140 feet (4,310 meters), where oxygen levels are significantly lower. Altitude sickness is common. Move slowly, stay hydrated, and inform your guide immediately if you experience severe headache, nausea, or breathing difficulties.

Day 5

Gangtok to Lachung

Morning: Journey to North Sikkim

After breakfast, begin your journey to Lachung, a picturesque village in North Sikkim (approximately 6-7 hours drive). The route offers spectacular views of mountains, valleys, and waterfalls.

Note: North Sikkim requires special permits which should be arranged in advance through your tour operator.

En Route: Scenic Stops

Make stops at Seven Sisters Waterfall, a magnificent cascade of seven different falls. Visit Singhik viewpoint for breathtaking views of Mount Kanchenjunga, the third highest mountain in the world.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Mangan or carry packed lunch from Gangtok.

Evening: Arrival in Lachung

Arrive in Lachung by evening and check into your hotel or homestay. Lachung is a small mountain village at an altitude of about 8,610 feet, known for its apple orchards and pristine beauty.

Accommodation: Yarlam Resort, Lachung Residency, or local homestays for an authentic experience.

Dinner: Most accommodations in Lachung provide dinner, typically featuring local Sikkimese cuisine.

Road Condition: Winding mountain roads, some sections may be rough

Day 6

Yumthang Valley & Zero Point

Early Morning: Journey to Yumthang Valley

After an early breakfast, drive to Yumthang Valley (approximately 1.5 hours from Lachung), also known as the 'Valley of Flowers'. Located at an elevation of 11,800 feet, this valley is a paradise of alpine flowers, hot springs, and yaks grazing on rolling meadows.

Note: The valley blooms with rhododendrons, primulas, and many other flowers during spring (March to June).

Morning: Yumthang Exploration

Explore the beautiful Yumthang Valley and visit the natural hot springs known for their medicinal properties. The hot springs are surrounded by snow-capped mountains, creating a surreal landscape.

Activity: Take a dip in the hot springs (if facilities are available) or simply enjoy the panoramic views of the valley.

Afternoon: Zero Point

Continue your journey to Zero Point (Yumesamdong), about 23 km from Yumthang Valley at an altitude of 15,300 feet. This is the last outpost before the Indo-China border and offers breathtaking views of snow-covered mountains.

Lunch: Enjoy packed lunch or have hot noodles and tea available at small stalls along the way.

Evening: Return to Lachung

Return to Lachung by evening. Spend the evening relaxing at your accommodation or take a stroll around the village to experience local life.

Dinner: Enjoy a warm dinner at your hotel or homestay, perfect after a day in the cold mountain air.

Yumthang Valley

Zero Point

About Yumthang Valley

Yumthang Valley, also known as the 'Valley of Flowers', is a nature sanctuary with rivers, hot springs, yaks, and grazing pastures all around. During spring, the valley erupts in a riot of colors as rhododendrons, primulas, poppies, and many other alpine flowers bloom, creating a magical landscape against the backdrop of snow-capped mountains.

Day 7

Lachung to Lachen

Morning: Journey to Lachen

After breakfast, check out from your accommodation in Lachung and begin your journey to Lachen (approximately 4-5 hours drive), another beautiful village in North Sikkim at an altitude of 8,838 feet.

Note: The journey involves retracing your route back to Chungthang and then taking the road to Lachen.

En Route: Scenic Stops

Make stops at scenic viewpoints along the way. The route offers beautiful views of the Teesta River and surrounding mountains. Stop at Chungthang, the confluence of Lachen and Lachung rivers, which form the Teesta River.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Chungthang or carry packed lunch from Lachung.

Evening: Arrival in Lachen

Arrive in Lachen by evening and check into your hotel or homestay. Lachen is a small village known for its pristine beauty and as the gateway to Gurudongmar Lake.

Accommodation: Apple Orchard Resort, Lachen, Fortuna Resort, or local homestays for an authentic experience.

Evening activity: Rest and acclimatize to the altitude. Early dinner and early to bed as the next day starts very early for the Gurudongmar Lake visit.

Chungthang

Lachen Village

About Lachen

Lachen, which means "big pass", is a small village in North Sikkim. The village follows a unique self-governing social system called the "Dzumsa," similar to Lachung. The pristine beauty, traditional lifestyle, and proximity to Gurudongmar Lake make it a must-visit destination in North Sikkim.

Elevation: 8,838 feet (2,694 meters)
Population: Approximately 1,000
Climate: Cold throughout the year
Day 8

Gurudongmar Lake & Return to Gangtok

Very Early Morning: Journey to Gurudongmar Lake

Start extremely early (around 3-4 AM) for Gurudongmar Lake, one of the highest lakes in the world at an altitude of 17,800 feet. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours through some of the most challenging but scenic roads in Sikkim.

Note: Foreign nationals are not allowed to visit Gurudongmar Lake. They can visit Tso Lhamu Lake instead, which is also beautiful but at a lower altitude.

Morning: Gurudongmar Lake Experience

Arrive at Gurudongmar Lake by morning. The lake is considered sacred by both Buddhists and Sikhs. Spend time taking in the surreal beauty of the crystal-clear blue waters surrounded by snow-capped mountains.

Important: Due to the extremely high altitude, it's recommended to spend no more than 30 minutes at the lake to avoid altitude sickness.

Afternoon: Return Journey

Begin your return journey to Lachen. After a short rest and lunch in Lachen, continue your journey to Gangtok (approximately 6-7 hours drive).

Lunch: Have lunch at your hotel in Lachen before departing for Gangtok.

Evening: Arrival in Gangtok

Arrive in Gangtok by evening. Check into your hotel and rest after the long journey.

Accommodation: Return to your previous hotel in Gangtok or try a new one for a different experience.

Gurudongmar Lake

Legend of Gurudongmar Lake

According to legend, when Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) passed by the lake, local people approached him with the problem that the lake remained frozen most of the year, limiting their access to water. The Guru touched a part of the lake, which now never freezes even in extreme winter. Another legend associates the lake with Guru Nanak, the first Sikh Guru, who is said to have visited the lake during his travels.

Extreme Altitude Warning

Gurudongmar Lake is at an elevation of 17,800 feet (5,425 meters), where oxygen levels are extremely low. Severe altitude sickness is common. It's recommended to spend no more than 30 minutes at the lake. People with heart conditions, respiratory problems, or high blood pressure should consult a doctor before planning this trip.

Day 9

Gangtok to Ravangla

Morning: Relaxed Start

After the intense journey of the previous day, enjoy a relaxed morning in Gangtok. Have a late breakfast and spend some time shopping for souvenirs or simply relaxing at your hotel.

Shopping: MG Marg and the surrounding areas offer good options for buying local handicrafts, thangka paintings, and traditional jewelry.

Afternoon: Journey to Ravangla

After lunch, begin your journey to Ravangla (approximately 3-4 hours drive), a small town in South Sikkim known for its breathtaking views of the Himalayan range.

En route: Make stops at scenic viewpoints along the way. The route offers beautiful views of terraced fields, forests, and distant mountains.

Evening: Arrival in Ravangla

Arrive in Ravangla by evening and check into your hotel. If time permits, visit the Buddha Park (Tathagata Tsal) to see the massive Buddha statue and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Accommodation: Mount Narsing Resort, Hotel Sikkim Continental, or other comfortable options in Ravangla.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try local restaurants in Ravangla for authentic Sikkimese cuisine.

Ravangla View

Buddha Park, Ravangla

About Ravangla

Ravangla (also spelled Rabongla) is a small town situated at an elevation of 7,000 feet in South Sikkim. It offers spectacular views of the greater Himalayan range including Kanchenjunga, Pandim, Siniolchu, and Kabru peaks. The town is known for its Buddha Park, tea gardens, and as a base for treks to Maenam Hill and Tendong Hill.

Elevation: 7,000 feet (2,134 meters)
Known for tea gardens and cardamom plantations
Base for several treks in South Sikkim
Day 10

Ravangla to Pelling

Morning: Ravangla Exploration

Start your day with a visit to the Buddha Park (if you didn't visit the previous evening). Continue to Ralong Monastery, one of the most important monasteries of the Karma Kagyu sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

Also visit: Bon Monastery, the only monastery of the Bon religion (pre-Buddhist Tibetan religion) in Sikkim.

Afternoon: Journey to Pelling

After lunch, begin your journey to Pelling (approximately 3 hours drive), a small town in West Sikkim known for offering some of the most spectacular views of Mount Kanchenjunga.

En route: Make a stop at Temi Tea Garden, the only tea estate in Sikkim known for producing some of the finest tea in the world.

Evening: Arrival in Pelling

Arrive in Pelling by evening and check into your hotel. Enjoy the panoramic views of Mount Kanchenjunga from your hotel (if weather permits).

Accommodation: The Elgin Mount Pandim, Norbu Ghang Resort, or Magpie Chestnut Resort for comfortable stays.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel while taking in the mountain views.

Ralong Monastery

Temi Tea Garden

About Pelling

Pelling is a small town in West Sikkim, famous for its panoramic views of the Kanchenjunga range. At an altitude of 6,800 feet, it offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, historical significance with ancient monasteries, and adventure opportunities. The town is divided into three areas: Lower, Middle, and Upper Pelling, each offering different views and experiences.

Photography Tip

The best time for photography in Pelling is early morning when the first rays of the sun illuminate the Kanchenjunga range, creating a golden glow on the snow-capped peaks. Set your alarm early to catch this magical moment!

Day 11

Pelling Sightseeing

Early Morning: Sunrise View

Wake up early to catch the sunrise over Mount Kanchenjunga from your hotel or a nearby viewpoint. The golden rays illuminating the snow-capped peaks create a magical scene.

Morning: Historical Sites

After breakfast, visit the key attractions around Pelling. Start with Pemayangtse Monastery, one of the oldest and most important monasteries in Sikkim. Continue to the ruins of Rabdentse Palace, the second capital of the former Kingdom of Sikkim.

Note: The walk to Rabdentse ruins is through a beautiful forest trail and takes about 30 minutes one way.

Afternoon: Kanchenjunga Falls

Visit the magnificent Kanchenjunga Falls, a powerful waterfall named after the mountain. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Pelling.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Sikkimese dishes at The Melting Pot or Mountain Cafe.

Late Afternoon: Khecheopalri Lake

Visit Khecheopalri Lake, a sacred lake for both Buddhists and Hindus, known for its pristine waters where leaves are not allowed to float. The lake is surrounded by dense forests and has a peaceful atmosphere.

Note: It's about a 30-40 minute drive from Pelling.

Evening: Leisure Time

Return to Pelling and spend the evening at leisure. Enjoy the sunset views of Kanchenjunga from your hotel or a nearby viewpoint.

Dinner: Enjoy a farewell dinner at your hotel or a local restaurant, reflecting on your Sikkim journey.

Pemayangtse Monastery

Khecheopalri Lake

Historical Significance

Rabdentse was the second capital of the former Kingdom of Sikkim from 1670 to 1814. The ruins of the palace and the monastery complex are now preserved as an archaeological site by the Archaeological Survey of India. The site offers not only historical insights but also panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Legend of Khecheopalri Lake

According to local legend, Khecheopalri Lake is considered a wish-fulfilling lake. It is believed that birds pick up any leaf that falls on the water surface, keeping the lake pristine. The lake is shaped like a footprint, which is considered to be the footprint of Goddess Tara.

Day 12

Pelling to Bagdogra/NJP & Departure

Morning: Leisurely Breakfast

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel, taking in the final views of the mountains. Check out from your hotel and prepare for your return journey.

Afternoon: Return Journey

Begin your return journey to Bagdogra Airport or New Jalpaiguri (NJP) Railway Station (approximately 5-6 hours drive). The route takes you through the beautiful landscapes of West Bengal.

Lunch: Stop for lunch at a restaurant in Jorethang or Melli.

Evening: Departure

Arrive at Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station for your onward journey, carrying memories of your Sikkim adventure.

Note: Ensure you have ample time between your arrival at Bagdogra/NJP and your flight/train departure, considering potential delays due to road conditions.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March to May): Ideal for flower blooms, especially rhododendrons
  • Summer (June to September): Pleasant weather but monsoon season brings rain
  • Autumn (October to November): Clear skies and perfect for mountain views
  • Winter (December to February): Cold with snowfall, some areas may be inaccessible
  • Avoid monsoon season (June to September) as landslides can disrupt travel plans

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airport is Bagdogra (125 km from Gangtok)
  • Train: Nearest railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP, 125 km from Gangtok)
  • Local Transport: Shared jeeps, taxis, and private vehicles
  • Helicopter service is available from Bagdogra to Gangtok (weather permitting)
  • For North Sikkim and remote areas, hiring a private vehicle with a local driver is recommended

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers are key; thermal wear for higher altitudes
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes with good grip
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, lip balm
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Reusable water bottle, torch, rain cover

Food & Cuisine

Sikkim offers a unique blend of Tibetan, Nepali, and indigenous flavors:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Momos (steamed or fried dumplings)
  • Thukpa (noodle soup)
  • Gundruk (fermented leafy greens)
  • Phagshapa (pork with radish)
  • Sel Roti (sweet rice bread)
  • Chhurpi (traditional cheese)
Beverages
  • Butter Tea (Po Cha)
  • Chang (millet beer)
  • Sikkim Tea
  • Rhododendron juice
  • Tongba (millet-based alcoholic drink)

Practical Information

  • Permits: Indian nationals need Inner Line Permit (ILP) for restricted areas; foreign nationals need Protected Area Permit (PAP)
  • Language: Nepali, Bhutia, Lepcha, and English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in Gangtok and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Gangtok; limited in remote areas
  • Altitude Sickness: Move slowly, stay hydrated, and consider medication if going to high altitude areas
  • Respect: Sikkim is an eco-friendly state with strict plastic bans; respect local customs and religious sites
