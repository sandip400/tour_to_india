Culture of Telangana

Telangana has a rich and diverse culture, shaped by its history, literature, art, music, festivals, and traditions. Here's an overview of some key aspects of its culture:

1. Language

  • Telugu is the official language and a major part of the cultural identity of Telangana. It's one of the classical languages of India with a rich literary heritage. The Telangana dialect of Telugu has its own unique vocabulary and expressions influenced by Urdu and Marathi.

2. Literature

  • Telangana has a rich literary tradition with poets like Kaloji Narayana Rao, Dasarathi Krishnamacharyulu, and Gaddar who wrote extensively about social issues and the Telangana movement.
  • Folk literature including ballads, songs, and stories form an important part of Telangana's cultural heritage.


3. Art and Craft

  • The state is known for its diverse range of art forms, including Nirmal paintings, Pembarthi metal craft, and Cheriyal scroll paintings.
  • Bidri work, a metal handicraft inlaid with silver, is a specialty of Hyderabad region.
  • Pochampally Ikat, a unique style of tie and dye weaving, is internationally renowned.

4. Music

  • Carnatic music has a strong presence in Telangana, especially in Hyderabad.
  • Folk music forms like Oggukatha, Chindu Bhagavatham, and Perini Shivatandavam are unique to the region.
  • Qawwali, influenced by Sufi traditions, is popular in Hyderabad.
  • Burrakatha, a storytelling form with music, is widely practiced in rural areas.

5. Dance

  • Perini Shivatandavam, an ancient dance form performed by male dancers, was revived in Telangana.
  • Gusadi Dance, performed by Gond tribes, is a vibrant folk dance form.
  • Bathukamma dance, performed during the Bathukamma festival, is unique to Telangana.

6. Cuisine

  • Telangana cuisine is known for its spicy and tangy flavors, with dishes like Hyderabadi Biryani, Haleem, and Qubani ka Meetha.
  • Traditional Telangana dishes include Sakinalu, Sarva Pindi, and Jonna Rotte (jowar roti).
  • Street food in Hyderabad, such as Irani chai with Osmania biscuits, is famous throughout India.


7. Theatre and Cinema

  • Telangana has a rich tradition of folk theater forms like Oggu Katha and Gollasuddulu.
  • Hyderabad is home to Tollywood, the Telugu film industry, which produces numerous films annually.
  • Ramoji Film City in Hyderabad, the world's largest film studio complex, attracts filmmakers from across India.

8. Architecture

  • Telangana's architectural style blends Hindu, Islamic, and contemporary influences. Notable structures include the Charminar, Golconda Fort, Qutb Shahi Tombs, and Thousand Pillar Temple in Warangal.

9. Religious Diversity

  • The state is home to a variety of religions, with Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, and Buddhism having a significant presence. This diversity is reflected in the state's festivals, rituals, and cultural practices. The Ganga-Jamuni tehzeeb (Hindu-Muslim cultural synthesis) is particularly evident in Hyderabad.

10. Folk Traditions

  • Telangana is famous for its rich folk traditions, including Bonalu, Bathukamma, and tribal celebrations.
  • Storytelling traditions like Oggu Katha and Burrakatha preserve ancient myths and legends.

11. Philosophy

  • The region has been influenced by various philosophical traditions including Vedanta, Sufism, and the Bhakti movement. The teachings of saints like Yogi Vemana have had a profound impact on Telangana's cultural ethos.

12. Handicrafts

  • Telangana is known for its handicrafts such as Bidriware, Dokra metal casting, Nirmal paintings, and Pochampally Ikat textiles.


Telangana's culture is deeply rooted in its history, and it continues to evolve while maintaining a close connection to its heritage. It's a region where modernity and tradition coexist beautifully!



Tradition of Telangana

Telangana has a rich and diverse tradition deeply rooted in its history, culture, and social practices. Here's an overview of some key traditional aspects of the state:

1. Clothing Tradition

Men

  • Dhoti and Kurta: Traditional Telangana men wear white or cream-colored dhoti with a kurta, especially during religious ceremonies, weddings, and festivals.
  • Turban (Pagdi): Worn for special occasions and ceremonies.

Women

  • Saree: Telangana women traditionally wear sarees like Pochampally Ikat, Gadwal, and Narayanpet. During Bathukamma, colorful sarees are commonly worn.
  • Jewelry: Traditional gold jewelry including Kasulu (bangles), Jhumkas (earrings), and Vaddanam (waistband) are worn during festivals and ceremonies.

2. Wedding Traditions

  • Pelli Koduku: Pre-wedding ceremony for the groom.
  • Jeelakarra Bellam: The bride and groom taste a mixture of cumin seeds and jaggery symbolizing the sweet and sour experiences of married life.
  • Talambralu: The couple showers each other with rice mixed with turmeric and saffron.
  • Arundhati Nakshatra: The couple looks at the Arundhati star, symbolizing marital fidelity.
  • Mangalsutra: The groom ties the sacred thread around the bride's neck, symbolizing marriage.

3. Music & Dance Traditions

  • Perini Shivatandavam: An ancient dance form performed to worship Lord Shiva before warriors went to battle.
  • Oggu Katha: A traditional storytelling form with music and dance elements.
  • Burrakatha: A folk storytelling tradition with musical accompaniment.
  • Gusadi Dance: A tribal dance form performed by the Gond tribe with elaborate costumes and headgear.

4. Social & Religious Traditions

  • Numaish: Annual industrial exhibition in Hyderabad that has become a cultural tradition.
  • Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb: The syncretic Hindu-Muslim culture especially prevalent in Hyderabad.
  • Muharram: Observed with processions and rituals, particularly in Hyderabad.

5. Architectural & Handicraft Traditions

  • Kakatiya Temples: Warangal is famous for its stunning stone temples with intricate carvings.
  • Cheriyal Scroll Painting: Traditional narrative scroll paintings depicting mythological themes.
  • Bidriware: Metal handicraft with silver inlay work, originated in Bidar but flourished in Hyderabad.
  • Pembarthi Metal Craft: Intricate sheet metal work used for decorative items and temple decorations.

6. Sports & Games

  • Kabaddi: Traditional rural game played widely across Telangana villages.
  • Kho-Kho: Popular traditional game in rural areas.
  • Marbles & Gilli-Danda: Traditional children's games still played in villages.


Telangana's traditions beautifully combine history, art, food, music, and festivals, making it one of the most culturally vibrant states in India.



Festivals of Telangana

Telangana is known for its vibrant and diverse festivals, which reflect its rich cultural heritage, religious harmony, and artistic traditions. Here's a list of some of the major festivals celebrated in the state:

1. Bathukamma (Biggest Festival of Telangana)

  • The most significant festival unique to Telangana, celebrated with floral arrangements, songs, and dances.
  • Women arrange seasonal flowers in a conical shape to worship Goddess Gauri.
  • The festival lasts 9 days, culminating in the immersion of the floral arrangements in water bodies.
  • Celebrated with colorful processions and traditional songs.

üìç Time: September - October (Dussehra season)


2. Bonalu

  • A traditional Hindu festival dedicated to Goddess Mahakali.
  • Women carry pots (Bonalu) filled with offerings on their heads to temples.
  • Features the Potharaju dance and processions with decorated elephants and bulls.
  • Famous places: Golconda Fort, Ujjaini Mahakali Temple, Secunderabad.

üìç Time: July - August


3. Sankranti

  • Marks the harvest festival and is widely celebrated across Telangana.
  • People fly kites, prepare special foods like Pongali and Ariselu.
  • Homes are decorated with colorful Rangoli and Muggu (floor designs).
  • Cockfights and bull races are traditional in rural areas.

üìç Time: January 14 - 15


4. Dussehra

  • Celebrated with great enthusiasm, especially in Hyderabad.
  • The Golconda Fort and Ujjaini Mahakali Temple host special celebrations.
  • The famous Alai Balai event brings together people from all communities.

üìç Time: September - October


5. Nagula Chavithi

  • A festival dedicated to the worship of snakes, considered sacred in Hindu tradition.
  • Women offer milk, turmeric, and flowers at snake pits and anthills.

üìç Time: October - November

6. Medaram Jatara

  • One of Asia's largest tribal festivals, held biennially in Warangal district.

7. Ugadi

8. Ramzan/Eid-ul-Fitr

Foods of Telangana

1. Staple Food of Telangana

  • Sarva Pindi: A savory pancake made with rice flour, peanuts, and spices.

  • Haleem: A slow-cooked stew of meat, lentils, and pounded wheat, especially popular during Ramzan.

  • Double ka Meetha: Bread pudding dessert with saffron and nuts.

Vegetarian Dishes

  • Sarvapindi: A spicy rice flour pancake with peanuts and sesame seeds.
  • Boti Curry: A spicy curry made with vegetables or meat.
  • Bachali Kura: Malabar spinach curry with tamarind.

Meat Dishes

  • Talakaya Kura: A spicy goat head curry.
  • Pachi Pulusu: A tangy tamarind soup with meat.
  • Saoji Mutton: Spicy mutton curry with distinctive flavors.

Dal & Accompaniments

  • Pappu Charu: A tangy lentil soup with tamarind.
  • Gongura Pachadi: Tangy chutney made from sorrel leaves, a Telangana specialty.

4. Telangana Sweets & Desserts

  • Qubani ka Meetha: Apricot dessert served with cream, a Hyderabadi specialty.
  • Double ka Meetha: Bread pudding with saffron and nuts.

History of Telangana

Telangana has a rich and diverse history that spans ancient civilizations, medieval kingdoms, Nizam rule, and the modern era. The region has played a significant role in India's political, cultural, and intellectual history.

1. Ancient Telangana (Before 6th Century CE)

  • Prehistoric Period: Archaeological evidence at sites like Nagarjunakonda shows human habitation dating back to 10,000 BCE.
  • Satavahana Empire (230 BCE - 220 CE): Ruled from their capital at Amaravati, they were patrons of Buddhism and trade.
  • Ikshvaku Dynasty (225 CE - 325 CE): Successors to the Satavahanas, known for Buddhist monuments.

2. Medieval Telangana (6th - 16th Century)

Chalukya Dynasty (6th - 12th Century):

  • The Eastern Chalukyas ruled parts of Telangana and were patrons of art and architecture.
  • Built temples and promoted Telugu literature.

Kakatiya Dynasty (1083 - 1323):

  • Golden age of Telangana with Warangal as their capital.
  • Built magnificent temples, tanks, and forts like the Thousand Pillar Temple and Warangal Fort.
  • Queen Rudrama Devi, one of India's few female rulers, led the kingdom during this period.

Delhi Sultanate Rule (1323 - 1347):

  • Alauddin Khilji's general Malik Kafur invaded and defeated the Kakatiyas.
  • The region came under the Delhi Sultanate's control.

Bahmani Sultanate (1347 - 1518):

  • Broke away from the Delhi Sultanate and established an independent kingdom.
  • Promoted Persian culture and architecture in the Deccan.

Qutb Shahi Dynasty (1518 - 1687):

  • Founded Hyderabad in 1591 around the Charminar.
  • Promoted a syncretic culture blending Persian, Telugu, and local traditions.
  • Built monuments like Golconda Fort, Charminar, and Qutb Shahi Tombs.

3. Mughal & Asaf Jahi Rule (1687 - 1948)

  • Aurangzeb conquered the Qutb Shahi kingdom in 1687, bringing it under Mughal control.
  • Asaf Jah I, a Mughal governor, established the Asaf Jahi dynasty (Nizams) in 1724.
  • The Nizams ruled Hyderabad State, which included Telangana, as a princely state.

4. British Colonial Influence & Nizam Rule (1798 - 1948)

Subsidiary Alliance

  • The Nizam signed a subsidiary alliance with the British East India Company in 1798.
  • While maintaining nominal independence, the Nizams were under British influence.

Nizam's Dominions

  • Hyderabad State became the largest princely state in British India.
  • The Nizams were among the wealthiest people in the world.

Peasant Revolts & Freedom Movement

  • Telangana Peasant Armed Struggle (1946-1951):
    • One of the largest peasant movements against feudal landlords and the Nizam's rule.
    • Led by Communist Party of India against the oppressive Jagirdari system.
  • Anti-Nizam Movements:
    • Andhra Mahasabha and other organizations fought for civil liberties and democratic rights.
    • The Hyderabad State Congress led movements against the Nizam's autocratic rule.

Operation Polo & Integration with India

  • After India's independence in 1947, the Nizam refused to join either India or Pakistan.
  • The Indian Army conducted "Operation Polo" in September 1948 to integrate Hyderabad State into India.
  • Hyderabad State was officially merged with the Indian Union on September 17, 1948.

5. Post-Independence & Formation of Telangana (1948 - Present)

States Reorganization

  • In 1956, the States Reorganization Act merged Telangana with Andhra State to form Andhra Pradesh.
  • The merger included safeguards for Telangana known as the "Gentlemen's Agreement."

Telangana Movement

  • 1969 Movement: First major agitation for a separate Telangana state.
  • 2000s Revival: K. Chandrashekar Rao formed the Telangana Rashtra Samithi (TRS) in 2001 to fight for statehood.
  • 2009-2014: Intensified movement with protests, strikes, and political pressure.

Formation of Telangana State

  • On June 2, 2014, Telangana was officially formed as the 29th state of India.
  • K. Chandrashekar Rao became the first Chief Minister of the new state.

6. Present-Day Telangana

  • IT Hub of India: Hyderabad is a major center for information technology and pharmaceuticals.
  • Economic Growth: Focus on irrigation projects, agriculture, and industrial development.
  • Tourist Attractions: Charminar, Golconda Fort, Ramoji Film City, Warangal Fort, and numerous temples and lakes.


Telangana has witnessed ancient empires, Islamic rule, Nizam governance, integration with India, and finally the formation of a separate state. It remains a center of culture, education, technology, and politics in India. üö©üìú
