Jharkhand: Land of Forests

Jharkhand offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich tribal heritage, and historical significance. From the bustling streets of Ranchi to the serene landscapes of Netarhat, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this eastern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Ranchi & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Birsa Munda Airport, Ranchi. Transfer to your hotel in central Ranchi. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Capitol Residency, Radisson Blu Hotel, or Hotel Ashoka for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Lunch & Ranchi Lake

Enjoy your first Jharkhand meal at a local restaurant. Visit Ranchi Lake, a man-made lake built by a British agent in 1842, surrounded by gardens and offering boating facilities.

Lunch recommendation: Try local dishes like Dhuska (fried rice pancake), Pittha (rice flour dumplings), and Handia (rice beer).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the Tribal Research Institute and Museum to learn about the various tribes of Jharkhand. Explore the local market area and shop for tribal handicrafts.

Evening activity: If available, catch a tribal dance performance at a cultural center.

Ranchi Lake

Tribal Research Institute

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs or auto-rickshaws for convenient transportation around the city.
  • The Tribal Research Institute is closed on Mondays, plan accordingly.
  • Main Road and Firayalal areas are good for shopping and dining.
Day 2

Ranchi Waterfalls Tour

Morning: Hundru Falls

Start your day with a visit to Hundru Falls, one of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand where the Subarnarekha River falls from a height of 98 meters creating a spectacular sight.

Activity: Take a dip in the natural pool formed at the base of the waterfall (if water conditions permit).

Afternoon: Jonha Falls & Lunch

Visit Jonha Falls (Gautam Dhara), another beautiful waterfall where you need to descend about 700 steps to reach the base. Enjoy a packed lunch amidst nature.

Note: Wear comfortable shoes as there's a fair amount of walking involved.

Evening: Dassam Falls & Sunset

Visit Dassam Falls (Dassam Ghagh), where the Kanchi River cascades from a height of 44 meters. Enjoy the sunset views before returning to Ranchi.

Dinner: Try local cuisine at restaurants like The Tribal Rasoi or Kaveri Restaurant in Ranchi.

Day 3

Ranchi to Netarhat

Morning: Jagannath Temple

Visit the Jagannath Temple in Ranchi, which is a replica of the famous Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha. The temple is located on a hilltop offering panoramic views of the city.

Also visit: Pahari Mandir, another hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Afternoon: Drive to Netarhat

After lunch, drive to Netarhat (approximately 3-4 hours), known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur" for its scenic beauty. Check into your accommodation in Netarhat.

Accommodation: Netarhat Tourist Complex, Prabhat Vihar Resort, or local guesthouses.

Evening: Sunset Point

Visit the Sunset Point in Netarhat to witness a spectacular sunset over the hills. The viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding forests and valleys.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple dinner at your accommodation or at a local eatery.

Sunset at Netarhat

Important Information
  • Netarhat is at a higher elevation, so carry warm clothes, especially for evenings.
  • Mobile network can be limited in Netarhat.
  • ATM facilities are limited, carry sufficient cash.
  • The road to Netarhat passes through forests and can be bumpy in sections.
  • Accommodation options are limited, so book in advance.
About Netarhat

Netarhat, situated at an altitude of 3,700 feet, is known for its pristine beauty, dense forests, and spectacular sunrise and sunset views. The name "Netarhat" is believed to have been derived from the words "Netar" (eyes) and "Hat" (market), meaning a place that is a feast for the eyes.

Day 4

Netarhat to Betla National Park

Early Morning: Sunrise at Magnolia Point

Wake up early to witness the sunrise from Magnolia Point, one of the most beautiful sunrise points in Jharkhand. The first rays of the sun illuminating the valley create a magical atmosphere.

Note: This is weather-dependent. Carry warm clothes as mornings can be chilly.

Morning: Upper & Lower Ghaghri

Visit the Upper and Lower Ghaghri waterfalls near Netarhat. These twin waterfalls are surrounded by dense forests and offer a serene environment.

Activity: Short trek through the forest to reach the waterfalls.

Afternoon: Drive to Betla National Park

After lunch, drive to Betla National Park (approximately 3-4 hours), part of the Palamau Tiger Reserve. Check into your accommodation in Betla.

Accommodation: Betla Forest Rest House, Van Vihar Resort, or nearby hotels in Daltonganj.

Evening: Betla Fort

Visit the ancient Betla Fort located inside the national park. The fort dates back to the medieval period and offers a glimpse into the region's history.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your accommodation and prepare for an early morning safari the next day.

Wildlife at Betla

Betla National Park is home to a variety of wildlife including:

  • Tigers (rare sightings)
  • Leopards
  • Elephants
  • Sloth Bears
  • Sambar Deer
  • Spotted Deer
  • Wild Boars
  • Various Bird Species
Day 5

Betla to Hazaribagh

Early Morning: Jungle Safari

Embark on an early morning jungle safari in Betla National Park. The park is known for its population of tigers, leopards, elephants, and various deer species. Morning is the best time to spot wildlife.

Note: Safari timings and availability depend on the season. Pre-book your safari through the forest department.

Late Morning: Breakfast & Check-out

Return to your accommodation for breakfast. Check out and prepare for your journey to Hazaribagh.

Afternoon: Drive to Hazaribagh

Drive to Hazaribagh (approximately 4-5 hours), known for its wildlife sanctuary and natural beauty. Check into your accommodation in Hazaribagh.

Accommodation: Hazaribagh Tourist Complex, Hotel Mayfair, or other local hotels.

Evening: Hazaribagh Lake

Visit Hazaribagh Lake, a beautiful man-made lake surrounded by hills. Enjoy a peaceful evening with boating facilities and scenic views.

Dinner: Try local cuisine at restaurants in Hazaribagh town.

Day 6

Hazaribagh to Deoghar

Morning: Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary

Visit the Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary, home to various species of wildlife including tigers, panthers, and sambhar deer. Take a guided tour through the sanctuary to learn about the local flora and fauna.

Also visit: Canary Hill for panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Afternoon: Drive to Deoghar

After lunch, drive to Deoghar (approximately 3-4 hours), known for the famous Baidyanath Jyotirlinga Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India. Check into your accommodation in Deoghar.

Accommodation: Hotel Yatri Niwas, Hotel Shiv Darshan, or other local hotels.

Evening: Baidyanath Temple Visit

Visit the sacred Baidyanath Temple complex, which houses the main Jyotirlinga temple and 21 other temples. The temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus, especially devotees of Lord Shiva.

Evening activity: Attend the evening aarti (prayer ceremony) at the temple.

About Deoghar

Deoghar, also known as "Baidyanath Dham," is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in India. The name "Deoghar" means "abode of the gods." During the month of Shravan (July-August), thousands of devotees undertake the Kanwar Yatra, carrying holy water from the Ganges to offer at the Baidyanath Temple.

Home to one of the 12 Jyotirlingas
Major pilgrimage center
Famous for Peda (milk-based sweet)
Day 7

Deoghar to Ranchi & Departure

Morning: Naulakha Temple

Visit the Naulakha Temple, known for its beautiful architecture and the fact that it was built at a cost of nine lakh rupees (hence the name). The temple is dedicated to Lord Radha-Krishna.

Also visit: Trikut Hill, which can be reached by ropeway and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, drive back to Ranchi (approximately 5-6 hours). If time permits, make a stop at Parasnath Hill, an important Jain pilgrimage site.

Alternative: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Ranchi.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Ranchi. The Main Road area and Firayalal Chowk are good options for souvenirs and tribal handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Birsa Munda Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Overall: October to March (winter months)
  • Waterfalls: July to October (post-monsoon)
  • Wildlife: November to February
  • Avoid the peak summer months (April to June) as temperatures can be very high
  • Monsoon season (June to September) makes some areas difficult to access

Transportation

  • Air: Birsa Munda Airport in Ranchi connects to major cities
  • Train: Ranchi, Dhanbad, and Jamshedpur have major railway stations
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws and taxis in cities; shared jeeps in rural areas
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip as public transport between tourist spots can be limited
  • Road conditions can vary, especially in remote areas

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for summer; warm layers for winter
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and hiking boots
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, torch
  • Miscellaneous: Reusable water bottle, snacks for long journeys

  • Arsa (rice flour sweet)

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is the primary language. English is understood in urban areas and tourist spots
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities but limited in rural areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Exercise normal precautions. Some remote areas may have Naxalite activity, so check current conditions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
