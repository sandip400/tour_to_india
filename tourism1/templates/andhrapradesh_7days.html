Andhra Pradesh: Land of Ancient Temples and Natural Beauty

Andhra Pradesh offers a perfect blend of pristine beaches, ancient temples, lush valleys, and rich cultural heritage. From the bustling port city of Visakhapatnam to the sacred hills of Tirupati, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this southern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Visakhapatnam & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Visakhapatnam International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Vizag. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Novotel Visakhapatnam or The Park for luxury stays; Keys Select or Fortune Inn Sree Kanya for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Beach Visit

Enjoy your first Andhra meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Raju Gari Dhaba' or 'Spicy Venue'. Visit the beautiful Ramakrishna Beach and enjoy the sea breeze.

Lunch recommendation: Try Andhra specialties like Pulihora (tamarind rice), Gongura Pachadi (sorrel leaf chutney), and Royyala Vepudu (prawn fry).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the Kailasagiri Hill Park for panoramic views of the city and coastline. Enjoy the sunset and take a ride on the ropeway. Have dinner at a beachside restaurant.

Evening activity: Take a stroll along the Beach Road and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.

Ramakrishna Beach

Kailasagiri Hill

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Kailasagiri Hill Park is open until 8:00 PM.
  • Beach Road comes alive in the evening with numerous food stalls and activities.
Day 2

Visakhapatnam Attractions

Morning: Submarine Museum

Start your day with a visit to the INS Kurusura Submarine Museum, the first of its kind in Asia. Explore this decommissioned submarine that has been converted into a museum.

Activity: Learn about the life of naval officers and the history of the Indian Navy's submarine operations.

Afternoon: Beaches & Yarada Hill

Visit Rushikonda Beach, known for its golden sands and water sports activities. Continue to Yarada Beach, a secluded beach surrounded by hills offering breathtaking views.

Lunch: Enjoy seafood at a beachside restaurant near Rushikonda.

Evening: Simhachalam Temple

Visit the ancient Simhachalam Temple, dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located on a hill and offers panoramic views of the surroundings.

Dinner: Try traditional Andhra thali at 'Subbayya Gari Hotel' or 'Minerva Coffee Shop'.

Day 3

Araku Valley

Early Morning: Journey to Araku

Take the early morning train to Araku Valley (approximately 4 hours), enjoying the scenic route through tunnels and bridges in the Eastern Ghats. Alternatively, you can drive to Araku, which takes about 3 hours.

Note: The Vistadome train offers panoramic views and is highly recommended if available.

Afternoon: Coffee Museum & Tribal Museum

Visit the Araku Coffee Museum to learn about coffee cultivation and processing. Continue to the Tribal Museum to understand the culture and lifestyle of local tribes.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional tribal meal at a local restaurant.

Evening: Accommodation in Araku

Check into your accommodation in Araku Valley. Enjoy the cool climate and serene atmosphere. Visit a local tribal village if time permits.

Accommodation options: Haritha Valley Resort (APTDC), Araku Jungle Bells Resort, or local homestays.

Scenic Araku Valley

Important Information
  • Book your train tickets in advance, especially for the Vistadome coach.
  • Carry light woolens as evenings can be cool in Araku Valley.
  • Mobile network might be limited in some areas of Araku.
  • Respect tribal customs and ask permission before taking photographs in tribal villages.
  • The best time to visit is from October to March.
Araku Coffee

Araku Valley is famous for its organic coffee, which has gained international recognition. Don't miss the opportunity to taste and purchase some authentic Araku coffee as a souvenir. The coffee museum offers a detailed insight into the coffee-making process and its significance to the local economy.

Day 4

Araku to Rajahmundry

Morning: Borra Caves

Visit the million-year-old Borra Caves, one of the largest caves in India. Explore the fascinating stalactite and stalagmite formations illuminated by colorful lights.

Activity: Take photographs of the natural limestone formations and learn about their geological significance.

Afternoon: Journey to Rajahmundry

Return to Visakhapatnam and then proceed to Rajahmundry (approximately 5 hours by road). Rajahmundry is known as the "Cultural Capital of Andhra Pradesh" and is located on the banks of the Godavari River.

Travel option: You can also take a train from Visakhapatnam to Rajahmundry, which takes about 3-4 hours.

Evening: Godavari Riverfront

Check into your hotel in Rajahmundry. Visit the Godavari Riverfront and enjoy the sunset. Take a short boat ride if time permits.

Accommodation: The Gateway Hotel, River Bay Resort, or Hotel Anand Regency.

Borra Caves

Godavari River

About Rajahmundry

Rajahmundry, founded in the 11th century, is one of the oldest cities in South India. It is situated on the banks of the Godavari River and is known as the "Cultural Capital of Andhra Pradesh." The city is famous for its literary heritage, ancient temples, and the magnificent Godavari Bridge.

Birthplace of Telugu literature
Home to the historic Godavari Bridge
Known as the "Gateway to the Godavari"
Day 5

Rajahmundry to Vijayawada

Morning: Godavari River Cruise

Take a morning cruise on the Godavari River to Papi Hills and Pattiseema. Enjoy the scenic beauty of the river and surrounding landscapes.

Activity: Look out for local fishermen using traditional fishing methods and various bird species along the riverbanks.

Afternoon: Journey to Vijayawada

After lunch, drive to Vijayawada (approximately 3-4 hours), the commercial capital of Andhra Pradesh. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Murali Park, Novotel Vijayawada, or Hotel DV Manor.

Evening: Kanaka Durga Temple

Visit the famous Kanaka Durga Temple, dedicated to the goddess Durga, located on the Indrakeeladri Hill overlooking the Krishna River. The temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Andhra Pradesh.

Dinner: Try local Andhra cuisine at 'Babai Hotel' or 'RR's Durbar' known for authentic flavors.

Day 6

Vijayawada to Tirupati

Morning: Undavalli Caves

Visit the Undavalli Caves, a monolithic example of Indian rock-cut architecture dating back to the 4th-5th centuries. The caves are carved out of solid sandstone on a hillside.

Highlight: Don't miss the huge reclining statue of Lord Vishnu carved out of a single block of granite.

Afternoon: Journey to Tirupati

After lunch, drive to Tirupati (approximately 5-6 hours), the temple town at the foothills of Tirumala. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Select Grand Ridge, Marasa Sarovar Premiere, or TTDC Hotel Tamil Nadu.

Evening: Tirupati Town

Explore Tirupati town and visit the Sri Govindaraja Swamy Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Prepare for the early morning visit to Tirumala the next day.

Note: If you plan to visit the Tirumala temple, book your darshan tickets in advance through the official TTD website.

Tirupati Town

About Tirupati

Tirupati is one of the most sacred pilgrimage centers in India. The town is famous for the Sri Venkateswara Temple (also known as Tirumala Temple) dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located on the seven hills of Tirumala and is one of the most visited religious sites in the world.

One of the richest temples in the world
Located on the seven hills of Tirumala
Receives millions of pilgrims annually
Day 7

Tirupati & Departure

Early Morning: Tirumala Temple

Early morning visit to the Sri Venkateswara Temple in Tirumala. The temple is located on a hill and can be reached by road or by climbing the steps (approximately 3,500 steps).

Note: Follow all temple rules and dress code. Men should wear traditional dhoti or pants, and women should wear saree or salwar kameez.

Afternoon: ISKCON Temple & Padmavathi Temple

Return to Tirupati and visit the ISKCON Temple. Also visit the Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple in Tiruchanur, dedicated to Goddess Padmavathi, the consort of Lord Venkateswara.

Lunch: Try the famous Tirupati laddu and other prasadam items.

Evening: Departure

Do some last-minute shopping for souvenirs and religious items. Transfer to Tirupati Airport or Railway Station for your onward journey.

Note: Tirupati is well-connected by air and rail to major cities in India.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Coastal Areas: October to March (winter months)
  • Araku Valley: September to February
  • Tirupati: Year-round, but avoid summer (March to June) due to extreme heat
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) in coastal areas

Transportation

  • Air: Major airports in Visakhapatnam, Vijayawada, and Tirupati
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs, auto-rickshaws, and local buses
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for coastal areas; light woolens for Araku Valley
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Andhra Pradesh is known for its spicy and flavorful cuisine:

Andhra Cuisine
  • Pulihora (tamarind rice)
  • Gongura Pachadi (sorrel leaf chutney)
  • Andhra Chicken Curry
  • Pesarattu (green gram dosa)
  • Hyderabadi Biryani
  • Pootharekulu (sweet rice paper rolls)
Coastal & Tribal Cuisine
  • Seafood dishes in coastal areas
  • Bamboo Chicken in Araku Valley
  • Ragi Sangati (finger millet porridge)
  • Tirupati Laddu
  • Kakinada Kaja (sweet)
  • Araku Coffee

Practical Information

  • Language: Telugu is the primary language. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Andhra Pradesh is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
