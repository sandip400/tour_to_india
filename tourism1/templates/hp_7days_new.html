Himachal Pradesh: The Land of Snow

Himachal Pradesh offers breathtaking mountain landscapes, lush valleys, and rich cultural heritage. From the colonial charm of Shimla to the adventure hub of Manali, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Shimla & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Shimla by road from Chandigarh or Delhi. Transfer to your hotel in central Shimla. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Oberoi Cecil or Wildflower Hall for luxury stays; Hotel Willow Banks or Clarkes Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Mall Road

Enjoy your first Himachali meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Himachali Rasoi' or 'Cafe Sol'. Explore the famous Mall Road, the main street of Shimla lined with shops, cafes, and colonial buildings.

Lunch recommendation: Try Himachali classics like Madra (chickpeas in yogurt gravy), Chana Madra, and Sidu (local bread).

Evening: Ridge & Christ Church

Visit the Ridge, a large open space in the heart of Shimla offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. Explore the neo-gothic Christ Church, one of the oldest churches in North India.

Evening activity: Enjoy the sunset views from the Ridge and take a leisurely stroll along the Mall Road as it lights up in the evening.

Mall Road, Shimla

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially due to the high altitude.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes as Shimla involves a lot of walking.
  • Mall Road is pedestrian-only, so be prepared to walk.
  • Acclimatize to the altitude by taking it easy on your first day.

Day 2

Shimla Heritage Tour

Morning: Viceregal Lodge

Start your day with a visit to the Viceregal Lodge (now Indian Institute of Advanced Study), a magnificent British-era building with beautiful gardens. Take a guided tour to learn about its historical significance.

Activity: Explore the surrounding botanical gardens and enjoy the colonial architecture.

Afternoon: Jakhu Temple & Hanuman Statue

Visit the Jakhu Temple, dedicated to Lord Hanuman, situated at the highest point in Shimla. The temple houses a 108-feet tall statue of Lord Hanuman, one of the tallest in the world.

Note: Be cautious of the monkeys around the temple area. Avoid carrying food items or shiny objects.

Evening: Lakkar Bazaar & Local Cuisine

Explore Lakkar Bazaar, famous for its wooden crafts and souvenirs. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant trying authentic Himachali dishes.

Dinner: Try local specialties like Dham (traditional festive meal), Tudkiya Bhath (spiced rice), and Chha Gosht (lamb curry).

Day 3

Kufri & Naldehra

Morning: Excursion to Kufri

After breakfast, drive to Kufri (approximately 30 minutes from Shimla), a small hill station known for its scenic beauty and adventure activities. Visit the Himalayan Wildlife Zoo which houses various Himalayan species.

Activity: Enjoy horseback riding to Mahasu Peak for panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Afternoon: Naldehra Golf Course

Continue to Naldehra, home to one of the oldest and highest golf courses in India, designed by Lord Curzon. Enjoy the beautiful cedar forests and panoramic views.

Lunch: Have a picnic lunch amidst the beautiful surroundings or eat at a local restaurant.

Evening: Return to Shimla

Return to Shimla by evening. Spend the evening at leisure, perhaps enjoying a cultural show or shopping for local handicrafts.

Dinner: Try a different local restaurant in Shimla for dinner, perhaps sampling some Tibetan influences like momos and thukpa.

Scenic Landscape of Kufri

Important Information
  • Kufri can be quite crowded during peak season, try to reach early.
  • Horseback riding in Kufri is optional but highly recommended for the views.
  • Naldehra Golf Course has an entry fee even if you're not playing golf.
  • Weather can change quickly in the mountains, carry a light jacket.
Scenic Views

Kufri and Naldehra offer some of the most spectacular views of the Himalayan ranges. On a clear day, you can see snow-capped peaks stretching across the horizon. Don't forget your camera!

Day 4

Shimla to Manali

Morning: Departure to Manali

After an early breakfast, check out from your hotel in Shimla and begin your journey to Manali (approximately 7-8 hours drive). The route takes you through beautiful landscapes, including the Kullu Valley.

Stops: Make brief stops at scenic viewpoints along the way for photographs and refreshments.

Afternoon: Lunch at Kullu

Stop at Kullu for lunch and a short break. If time permits, visit the Kullu Shawl factories or shops to see the famous Kullu shawls being made.

Shopping: Kullu is known for its handwoven shawls, caps, and other woolen items.

Evening: Arrival in Manali

Reach Manali by evening and check into your hotel. Rest and relax after the long journey. If energy permits, take a short stroll along the Mall Road in Manali.

Accommodation: The Himalayan, Span Resort & Spa, or Manuallaya Resort for luxury stays; Honeymoon Inn or Johnson Lodge for mid-range options.

Day 5

Manali Exploration

Morning: Hadimba Temple

After breakfast, visit the ancient Hadimba Temple, a wooden temple dedicated to Hadimba Devi, wife of Bhima from the Mahabharata. The temple is surrounded by a beautiful cedar forest.

Next: Visit the Vashisht Temple and Hot Springs, known for their healing properties. You can take a dip in the hot springs if you wish.

Afternoon: Manu Temple & Tibetan Monasteries

Visit the Manu Temple, dedicated to sage Manu, the creator of the world according to Hindu mythology. Continue to the Tibetan Monasteries in Manali to learn about Buddhist culture and traditions.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant, trying Tibetan specialties like momos, thukpa, and tingmo.

Evening: Mall Road & Local Shopping

Spend the evening exploring Mall Road, the main shopping street in Manali. Shop for local handicrafts, woolen items, and souvenirs. Enjoy dinner at a restaurant on Mall Road.

Try: Local Himachali dishes or enjoy international cuisine available at various restaurants.

Day 6

Solang Valley

Morning: Solang Valley Adventure

After breakfast, drive to Solang Valley (approximately 30 minutes from Manali), a popular spot for adventure activities. Depending on the season, you can enjoy paragliding, zorbing, skiing (in winter), or simply enjoy the scenic beauty.

Activities: Try paragliding for breathtaking aerial views of the valley (weather permitting).

Afternoon: Picnic Lunch & More Activities

Enjoy a picnic lunch in the valley or eat at one of the small eateries. Continue with more adventure activities or simply relax and enjoy the mountain views.

Note: Adventure activities are subject to weather conditions and may require additional fees.

Evening: Riverside Dinner

Return to Manali by evening. Enjoy a special dinner at a riverside restaurant, listening to the soothing sound of the Beas River.

Recommendation: Try Johnson's Cafe or River Music Cafe for a memorable dining experience.

About Solang Valley

Solang Valley, situated between Solang village and Beas Kund, offers panoramic views of the surrounding snow-capped mountains and glaciers. It's a hub for adventure sports in both summer and winter.

Elevation: 2,560 meters (8,400 ft)
Winter activities: Skiing, snowboarding
Summer activities: Paragliding, zorbing, trekking

Day 7

Manali & Departure

Morning: Nature Walk

Take a morning nature walk along the Beas River or in the nearby Van Vihar National Park, a municipal garden with tall deodar trees.

Activity: Enjoy the fresh mountain air and peaceful surroundings before your departure.

Afternoon: Last-Minute Shopping

After checking out from your hotel, spend some time doing last-minute shopping for souvenirs and local products. The Tibetan Market and Mall Road are good options.

Souvenirs: Consider buying Kullu shawls, Himachali caps, wooden crafts, or local fruit products like apple jam.

Evening: Departure

Depart from Manali to your next destination or return journey. If heading to Delhi or Chandigarh, overnight buses are a popular option.

Alternative: If your schedule allows, you can extend your stay in Manali or visit nearby destinations like Kasol or Naggar.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Pleasant weather, blooming flowers
  • Summer (June-August): Peak tourist season, ideal for escaping the heat
  • Autumn (September-November): Clear skies, perfect for photography
  • Winter (December-February): Snowfall, skiing, and winter sports
  • Avoid monsoon season (July-August) as landslides can disrupt travel

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airports are Chandigarh and Bhuntar (Kullu)
  • Train: Nearest railway stations are Kalka (for Shimla) and Pathankot (for Manali)
  • Road: Well-connected by road from Delhi, Chandigarh, and other major cities
  • Local Transport: Taxis, local buses, and shared jeeps available
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers are key; pack for varying temperatures
  • Summer: Light woolens, comfortable walking shoes
  • Winter: Heavy woolens, thermals, gloves, caps, mufflers
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, sunscreen, lip balm, moisturizer
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Himachal Pradesh offers a diverse culinary experience:

Himachali Cuisine
  • Dham (traditional festive meal)
  • Madra (chickpeas in yogurt gravy)
  • Chha Gosht (lamb curry)
  • Sidu (local bread)
  • Babru (black gram fritters)
  • Tudkiya Bhath (spiced rice)
Other Influences
  • Tibetan: Momos, thukpa, tingmo
  • Punjabi: Various vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes
  • Street Food: Chana bhatura, aloo tikki
  • Beverages: Kangra tea, apple cider
  • Desserts: Mittha (sweet rice), fruit wines

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is widely spoken. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs available in major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in towns; limited in remote areas
  • Altitude: Be aware of altitude sickness in higher regions
  • Safety: Himachal Pradesh is generally safe for tourists
  • Permits: Inner Line Permits required for certain areas like Spiti and Kinnaur
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
