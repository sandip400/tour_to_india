Punjab: The Land of Five Rivers

Punjab offers a perfect blend of spiritual heritage, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine. From the sacred Golden Temple in Amritsar to the modern city of Chandigarh, this 12-day comprehensive itinerary covers the best of what this northern Indian state and its neighboring regions have to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Amritsar & Golden Temple

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport, Amritsar. Transfer to your hotel in central Amritsar. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Swarna or Hyatt Amritsar for luxury stays; Golden Tulip or Ramada Amritsar for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Local Exploration

Enjoy your first Punjabi meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Brothers Dhaba' or 'Kesar Da Dhaba'. Visit the Partition Museum, the world's first museum dedicated to the Partition of India in 1947.

Lunch recommendation: Try Amritsari Kulcha, a local specialty of stuffed bread served with chole (chickpea curry) and fresh yogurt.

Evening: Golden Temple

Visit the magnificent Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), the holiest shrine of Sikhism. Experience the serene atmosphere and spiritual ambiance. Participate in the evening Palki Sahib ceremony when the holy book Guru Granth Sahib is carried to its night-time abode.

Evening activity: Have dinner at the langar (community kitchen) in the Golden Temple complex, where free meals are served to all visitors regardless of religion, caste, or background.

Golden Temple

Amritsari Kulcha

Day 1 Tips
  • Cover your head with a scarf or use the free head coverings provided when visiting the Golden Temple.
  • Remove shoes and wash your feet before entering the temple complex.
  • Photography is allowed in most areas of the Golden Temple, but be respectful.
  • The Golden Temple is open 24 hours, but the evening time with illumination is particularly beautiful.
Day 2

Amritsar Historical Sites & Wagah Border

Morning: Golden Temple Sunrise & Jallianwala Bagh

Experience the Golden Temple during sunrise when the golden structure reflects beautifully in the sacred pool. After breakfast, visit Jallianwala Bagh, the historical site of the 1919 massacre that played a pivotal role in India's independence movement.

Activity: Take a guided tour of the Golden Temple complex including the Sikh Museum to learn about Sikh history and traditions.

Afternoon: Gobindgarh Fort & Lunch

Visit the historic Gobindgarh Fort, a 18th-century military fort that now houses museums and cultural exhibits. Enjoy a traditional Punjabi lunch at a local restaurant.

Note: Don't miss the sound and light show at Gobindgarh Fort if your schedule permits.

Evening: Wagah Border Ceremony

Travel to the Wagah Border (about 30 km from Amritsar) to witness the famous Beating Retreat ceremony. This daily military practice takes place at the India-Pakistan border with a synchronized parade of soldiers from both countries.

Dinner: Return to Amritsar and enjoy dinner at Bharawan Da Dhaba or try street food at Hall Bazaar.

Day 3

Amritsar to Pathankot

Morning: Local Markets & Departure

After breakfast, explore the local markets of Amritsar like Hall Bazaar and Katra Jaimal Singh Market for traditional Punjabi clothing, handicrafts, and souvenirs. Check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Pathankot (approximately 2.5 hours, 120 km).

Shopping tip: Look for phulkari embroidery, juttis (traditional footwear), and Punjabi suits as souvenirs.

Afternoon: Arrival in Pathankot & Lunch

Arrive in Pathankot, a city located at the foothills of the Himalayas and serving as a gateway to Himachal Pradesh and Jammu & Kashmir. Check into your hotel and enjoy lunch.

Accommodation: Country Inn & Suites or Hotel Savera for comfortable stays.

Evening: Ranjit Sagar Dam

Visit the Ranjit Sagar Dam (also known as Thein Dam), one of the highest earth-filled dams in India. Enjoy the scenic beauty of the reservoir surrounded by hills. Return to Pathankot for dinner.

Dinner: Try local Punjabi cuisine at a restaurant like Haveli or Punjabi Rasoi.

Ranjit Sagar Dam

About Pathankot

Pathankot is a city in the Punjab state of India, located in the foothills of the Himalayas. It serves as an important gateway to the three northern states of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, and Punjab. The city has historical significance and was once part of the ancient Audumbar kingdom.

The region around Pathankot offers beautiful natural landscapes with rivers, hills, and forests. It's also known for its military significance, hosting an important base of the Indian Air Force.

Travel Advisory

Pathankot is close to the international border, so always carry valid identification. Some areas may have security restrictions. It's advisable to check the current situation before planning visits to remote areas around Pathankot.

Day 4

Pathankot to Dharamshala

Morning: Mukteshwar Temple

After breakfast, visit the ancient Mukteshwar Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Ravi River. The temple is known for its unique architecture and peaceful surroundings.

Activity: Take a short walk along the riverbank to enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Afternoon: Drive to Dharamshala

Begin your journey to Dharamshala (approximately 2.5 hours, 90 km), located in the Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh. The drive offers beautiful views of the Dhauladhar mountain range. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Park Moksha or Radisson Blu for luxury; Hotel Udechee Huts or Pride Surya Mountain Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: McLeod Ganj Exploration

Visit McLeod Ganj, the upper part of Dharamshala and home to the Tibetan government-in-exile and the Dalai Lama. Explore the local market and enjoy the Tibetan atmosphere. Take a leisurely walk around the area to acclimatize to the altitude.

Dinner: Try Tibetan cuisine at restaurants like Tibet Kitchen or Lung Ta.

Mukteshwar Temple

McLeod Ganj

About Dharamshala

Dharamshala is a city in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Surrounded by cedar forests on the edge of the Himalayas, it's home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. The city is divided into two parts: Lower Dharamshala (the main town) and Upper Dharamshala (also known as McLeod Ganj).

Elevation: 1,457 meters (4,780 ft)
Cool climate throughout the year
Strong Tibetan cultural influence
Day 5

Dharamshala Exploration

Morning: Namgyal Monastery & Tsuglagkhang Complex

Visit the Namgyal Monastery and Tsuglagkhang Complex, the main temple complex which houses the residence of the Dalai Lama, a museum, and several Buddhist temples. Experience the peaceful atmosphere and learn about Tibetan Buddhism.

Activity: Attend a meditation session or a teaching if available. Check the schedule at the monastery.

Afternoon: Bhagsu Waterfall & Lunch

Evening: Tibet Museum & Shopping

Day 6

Dharamshala to Chandigarh

Morning: Dharamshala Cricket Stadium

Visit the HPCA Cricket Stadium, one of the most picturesque cricket stadiums in the world, set against the backdrop of the Dhauladhar mountain range. Take a tour of the stadium and enjoy the panoramic views.

Activity: Don't miss the stadium museum that showcases the history of cricket in Himachal Pradesh.

Afternoon: Drive to Chandigarh

After lunch, begin your journey to Chandigarh (approximately 5-6 hours, 240 km), the capital of Punjab and Haryana. The drive offers beautiful landscapes through the foothills of the Himalayas. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: JW Marriott or Taj Chandigarh for luxury; Hotel Mountview or Lemon Tree for mid-range options.

Evening: Sukhna Lake

If time permits, visit Sukhna Lake, an artificial reservoir that is a popular recreational spot in Chandigarh. Enjoy a leisurely walk along the promenade or take a boat ride on the lake.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Pal Dhaba or Sagar Ratna for authentic North Indian cuisine.

HPCA Cricket Stadium

About Chandigarh

Chandigarh is a union territory and the capital of two states - Punjab and Haryana. It was designed by the famous Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier and is known for its urban planning, architecture, and modern amenities. The city is divided into sectors, each with its own markets, parks, and facilities.

India's first planned city post-independence
Known as "The City Beautiful"
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Day 7

Chandigarh Exploration

Morning: Rock Garden

Visit the famous Rock Garden, a unique garden created by artist Nek Chand using industrial and urban waste. The garden features sculptures, waterfalls, and amphitheaters made from recycled materials.

Tip: The garden is spread over 40 acres, so wear comfortable walking shoes and allocate at least 2 hours for the visit.

Afternoon: Capitol Complex & Lunch

Explore the Capitol Complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site designed by Le Corbusier. The complex includes the High Court, Secretariat, and Assembly buildings, showcasing modernist architecture. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

Note: Visitors need to obtain a permit to visit the Capitol Complex. This can be arranged through your hotel or a local travel agency.

Evening: Rose Garden & Sector 17

Visit the Zakir Hussain Rose Garden, Asia's largest rose garden with over 1,600 species of roses. Later, explore Sector 17, the city's main commercial hub, for shopping and dining.

Dinner: Try Punjabi cuisine at Ghazal Restaurant or enjoy international cuisine at Virgin Courtyard.

Day 8

Chandigarh to Patiala

Morning: Government Museum & Art Gallery

Visit the Government Museum and Art Gallery, which houses a rich collection of Gandharan sculptures, Pahari and Rajasthani miniature paintings, and contemporary Indian art. The museum also has artifacts from the Indus Valley Civilization.

Activity: Don't miss the Le Corbusier Centre nearby, which showcases the life and works of the architect who designed Chandigarh.

Afternoon: Drive to Patiala

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Patiala (approximately 1.5 hours, 70 km), a city known for its rich cultural heritage, palaces, and the famous Patiala peg. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Neemrana's Baradari Palace or Hotel Iqbal Inn for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Qila Mubarak

Visit Qila Mubarak, a 17th-century fort and palace complex that was the residence of the royal family of Patiala. Explore the various sections including the Darbar Hall, Sheesh Mahal, and the museum.

Dinner: Enjoy traditional Punjabi dinner at Patiala Shahi or The Yellow Chilli.

Government Museum

Qila Mubarak

About Patiala

Patiala is a historic city founded in 1763 by Baba Ala Singh, the founder of the Patiala royal dynasty. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, magnificent palaces, and distinctive cuisine. It has given its name to several items including the Patiala salwar (a type of loose-fitting trousers), the Patiala peg (a generous measure of whisky), and Patiala shahi turban (a style of turban).

Former princely state with royal heritage
Home to the Patiala Gharana of classical music
Famous for Patiala salwar and juttis (footwear)
Day 9

Patiala to Ludhiana

Morning: Moti Bagh Palace & Sheesh Mahal

Visit the Moti Bagh Palace, which now houses the National Institute of Sports, and the beautiful Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors). The palace is surrounded by well-maintained gardens and features a medal gallery showcasing achievements of Indian athletes.

Activity: Take a stroll through the Baradari Gardens, a beautiful garden complex with fountains and pavilions.

Afternoon: Drive to Ludhiana

After lunch, begin your journey to Ludhiana (approximately 1.5 hours, 90 km), the largest city in Punjab and an important industrial center. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Hyatt Regency or Park Plaza for luxury; Keys Hotel or Maharaja Regency for mid-range options.

Evening: Shopping & Local Markets

Explore the vibrant markets of Ludhiana, known for textiles, hosiery, and fashion. Visit Chaura Bazaar, Ghumar Mandi, or Mall Road for shopping. Ludhiana is famous for its woolen garments, shawls, and traditional Punjabi outfits.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Baba Chicken or Bistro Flamme Bois for authentic Punjabi cuisine.

Moti Bagh Palace

Ludhiana Markets

About Ludhiana

Ludhiana is the largest city in Punjab and an important industrial hub, often called the "Manchester of India" due to its industrial importance. The city is known for its hosiery industry, bicycle manufacturing, and textile production. It's also a major educational center with several prestigious institutions.

Industrial capital of Punjab
Famous for textiles and woolen garments
Major bicycle manufacturing center
Day 10

Ludhiana to Jalandhar

Morning: Punjab Agricultural University & Museum

Visit the Punjab Agricultural University, one of the oldest agricultural universities in India with beautiful campus grounds. Explore the Rural Museum within the campus that showcases traditional Punjabi rural life, agricultural implements, and crafts.

Activity: Take a walk through the botanical gardens and experimental fields if time permits.

Afternoon: Drive to Jalandhar

After lunch, drive to Jalandhar (approximately 1 hour, 60 km), one of the oldest cities in Punjab and a major industrial center known for sports goods manufacturing. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Radisson Hotel or Ramada Jalandhar City Centre for luxury; Maya Hotel or Hotel Maharaja for mid-range options.

Evening: Devi Talab Mandir & Local Markets

Visit the Devi Talab Mandir, a historic Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga. Later, explore the local markets like Model Town Market or Rainak Bazaar for shopping.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Haveli Restaurant or Rangla Punjab for authentic Punjabi cuisine.

Punjab Agricultural University

Devi Talab Mandir

About Jalandhar

Jalandhar is one of the oldest cities in Punjab with a history dating back to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization. Today, it's a major industrial center known for sports goods manufacturing, hand tools, and leather products. The city is also an important educational hub with numerous colleges and universities.

Sports goods manufacturing capital of India
Famous for hand tools production
Rich historical heritage
Day 11

Jalandhar to Kapurthala

Morning: Sports Goods Industry Tour

Take a guided tour of a sports goods manufacturing unit to see how cricket bats, footballs, and other sports equipment are made. Jalandhar is India's sports goods manufacturing hub, exporting to countries worldwide.

Shopping: Purchase high-quality sports equipment directly from manufacturers at factory outlets.

Afternoon: Drive to Kapurthala

After lunch, drive to Kapurthala (approximately 30 minutes, 20 km), known as the "Paris of Punjab" due to its Indo-French architecture. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Hotel Saffron or Hotel Savera for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Jagatjit Palace & Gardens

Visit the magnificent Jagatjit Palace, built in French Renaissance style and now housing the Sainik School. Explore the beautiful gardens and architecture that reflect the Indo-French design.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant trying Kapurthala's unique cuisine that has French influences.

Sports Goods Manufacturing

Jagatjit Palace

About Kapurthala

Kapurthala was once a princely state ruled by the Ahluwalia dynasty. The city is known for its unique Indo-French architecture, particularly from the reign of Maharaja Jagatjit Singh who was greatly influenced by French culture. The city earned the nickname "Paris of Punjab" due to its European-style buildings and gardens.

Known for Indo-French architecture
Rich royal heritage
Home to the Moorish Mosque, modeled after a mosque in Morocco
Day 12

Kapurthala to Amritsar & Departure

Morning: Moorish Mosque & Shalimar Gardens

Visit the Moorish Mosque, built in the early 20th century and inspired by the Grand Mosque of Marrakesh in Morocco. Continue to the Shalimar Gardens, modeled after the famous gardens in Kashmir.

Activity: Take a guided tour to learn about the unique architectural features and history of these sites.

Afternoon: Return to Amritsar

After lunch, begin your journey back to Amritsar (approximately 1.5 hours, 80 km). Depending on your departure time, you may check into a hotel for a short rest or head directly to the airport.

Optional: If time permits, visit the Khalsa College in Amritsar, known for its stunning colonial architecture.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Amritsar for souvenirs like phulkari embroidery, juttis, or Punjabi food items.

Departure: Transfer to Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Winter months with pleasant temperatures
  • April to June: Summer with hot temperatures, but good for visiting hill stations
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional heavy rainfall
  • Festival seasons: Baisakhi (April), Lohri (January), and Diwali (Oct/Nov) are particularly vibrant times to visit

Transportation

  • Air: Amritsar and Chandigarh have international airports with good connectivity
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Road: Well-maintained highways and state roads
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for summer, warm layers for winter
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes
  • Religious sites: Head covering (scarf or handkerchief) for visiting gurudwaras
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit
  • Other: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
