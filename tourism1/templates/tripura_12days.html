Tripura: Land of Cultural Heritage

Tripura, one of India's smallest states nestled in the northeastern corner of the country, offers a unique blend of tribal culture, royal heritage, and natural beauty. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the state's highlights and hidden gems, from the magnificent palaces of Agartala to the ancient rock carvings of Unakoti, the serene beauty of Jampui Hills, and the lesser-known archaeological sites and natural wonders.

Day 1

Arrival in Agartala

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport in Agartala. Transfer to your hotel and check-in. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Ginger Agartala, Hotel Welcome Palace, or Polo Towers for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Ujjayanta Palace

Visit the magnificent Ujjayanta Palace, the former royal palace of Tripura's Manikya dynasty, now housing the Tripura Government Museum. Explore its Indo-Saracenic architecture, beautiful gardens, and the museum's collection showcasing the state's rich cultural heritage.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Bengali cuisine at Kurry Klub or Ginger Hotel restaurant.

Evening: Jagannath Temple & Local Market

Visit the Jagannath Temple, a significant Hindu temple with distinctive architecture. Later, explore the local markets around Battala or Gol Bazaar to get a feel of the local culture and perhaps pick up some handicrafts.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Shankar Restaurant or Aahar Restaurant, trying local specialties like Chakhwi (bamboo shoot curry) or Mui Borok (a traditional rice dish).

Ujjayanta Palace

Jagannath Temple

Day 1 Tips
  • Agartala has a tropical climate, so dress accordingly with light, breathable clothing.
  • The Ujjayanta Palace Museum is closed on Mondays, so plan your visit accordingly.
  • Photography is allowed in most areas of the palace but may be restricted in certain sections of the museum.
  • Carry water and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
Day 2

Exploring Agartala

Morning: Heritage Park & Tribal Museum

Visit the Heritage Park, which showcases the cultural heritage of Tripura. Continue to the Tribal Museum to learn about the 19 different tribal communities of the state, their customs, traditions, and artifacts.

Activity: Participate in a guided tour of the museum to gain deeper insights into Tripura's tribal heritage.

Afternoon: Nehru Park & Fourteen Gods Temple

Relax at Nehru Park, a beautiful garden in the heart of Agartala. Visit the Fourteen Gods Temple (Chaturdasha Devata Mandir), dedicated to the fourteen deities who are considered the tribal gods of the royal family of Tripura.

Lunch: Try local cuisine at Rajdhani Restaurant or Hotel Sonar Tori.

Evening: Cultural Show & Local Cuisine

Attend a cultural show showcasing traditional Tripuri dance forms like Hojagiri, Lebang Boomani, or Mamita. These performances offer insights into the rich cultural heritage of the state.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Basil Restaurant or try street food at Maharaja Chaat Bhandar.

Tribal Museum

Fourteen Gods Temple

About Tripura's Tribal Culture

Tripura is home to 19 different tribal communities, each with their unique customs, traditions, and languages. The major tribes include Tripuri, Reang, Jamatia, Noatia, and Halam. These communities have preserved their rich cultural heritage through traditional dance forms, music, handicrafts, and festivals. The tribal communities primarily practice jhum cultivation (slash and burn agriculture) and are known for their bamboo and cane crafts.

Day 3

Tripura Sundari Temple & Udaipur

Morning: Drive to Udaipur

After breakfast, drive to Udaipur (approximately 55 km from Agartala), the ancient capital of Tripura. The town is known for its temples and lakes, offering a glimpse into the state's rich history.

En route: Enjoy the scenic countryside and rural landscapes of Tripura.

Afternoon: Tripura Sundari Temple

Visit the sacred Tripura Sundari Temple, one of the 51 Shakti Peethas in Hindu tradition. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari and is an important pilgrimage site. Explore the temple complex and learn about its historical and religious significance.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple vegetarian lunch near the temple.

Evening: Kalyan Sagar & Jagannath Bari

Visit Kalyan Sagar, a large lake adjacent to the Tripura Sundari Temple. According to legend, the lake was created by cutting a hill. Continue to Jagannath Bari, another important temple in Udaipur dedicated to Lord Jagannath.

Accommodation: Stay overnight in Udaipur at a local guesthouse or return to Agartala (recommended if you prefer better accommodation options).

Tripura Sundari Temple

Kalyan Sagar

About Tripura Sundari Temple

The Tripura Sundari Temple, also known as Tripureshwari Temple, is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas in Hindu tradition. According to mythology, the right foot of Goddess Sati fell here when Lord Shiva carried her body after her self-immolation. The temple was built by Maharaja Dhanya Manikya in 1501 AD and is considered one of the holiest Hindu shrines in Northeast India. The temple houses a small idol of Goddess Tripura Sundari made of red stone.

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas

Day 4

Neermahal Palace

Morning: Drive to Melaghar

After breakfast, drive to Melaghar (approximately 53 km from Agartala) to visit Neermahal, the stunning water palace built by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya in 1930.

En route: Enjoy the scenic countryside and rural landscapes of Tripura.

Afternoon: Neermahal Palace

Take a boat ride to Neermahal, located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake. This architectural marvel combines Hindu and Muslim styles. Explore the palace's beautiful architecture, gardens, and the surrounding lake.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at a local eatery near the lake, trying fresh fish preparations.

Evening: Rudrasagar Lake

Spend time exploring the Rudrasagar Lake, a Ramsar site known for its biodiversity. Enjoy the serene environment and observe various bird species that inhabit the area. Return to Agartala by evening.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try a local restaurant like Punjabi Dhaba or Momo House.

Neermahal Palace

Rudrasagar Lake

About Neermahal

Neermahal, meaning "Water Palace," is the former summer residence of the Tripura royal family. Built in 1930 by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya, it's one of only two water palaces in India (the other being Jal Mahal in Rajasthan). The palace combines Hindu and Muslim architectural styles and features a central courtyard, several rooms, and beautiful gardens. The annual Neermahal Water Festival held here attracts tourists from across the country.

Unique water palace architecture
Located in Rudrasagar Lake

Day 5

Dumboor Lake

Morning: Drive to Dumboor Lake

After breakfast, drive to Dumboor Lake (approximately 120 km from Agartala), a large reservoir in the Gandacherra subdivision of Dhalai district. The lake is named after its shape, which resembles a musical instrument called 'Dumboor' (drum).

En route: Pass through beautiful landscapes and tribal villages, offering glimpses of rural life in Tripura.

Afternoon: Boat Ride & Island Exploration

Take a boat ride on Dumboor Lake, which has 48 small islands scattered across its expanse. These islands are home to various bird species and offer beautiful natural scenery. Visit some of the accessible islands and enjoy the serene environment.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a simple local eatery near the lake.

Evening: Return to Agartala

Return to Agartala by evening. If time permits, stop at local villages en route to interact with tribal communities and learn about their lifestyle and culture.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try a local restaurant in Agartala.

Dumboor Lake

Islands in Dumboor Lake

About Dumboor Lake

Dumboor Lake is a large reservoir formed by the Raima and Sarma rivers in the Dhalai district of Tripura. The lake gets its name from its shape, which resembles a musical instrument called 'Dumboor' (drum). Spread over an area of 41 sq km, the lake has 48 small islands scattered across its expanse, earning it the nickname "Mini Andaman." The lake is a paradise for bird watchers and nature lovers, with various migratory birds visiting during winter. It's also home to diverse aquatic life and surrounded by lush forests.

Day 6

Pilak Archaeological Site

Morning: Drive to Pilak

After breakfast, drive to Pilak (approximately 100 km from Agartala), an important archaeological site dating back to the 8th-9th centuries. The site contains Hindu and Buddhist sculptures and is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region.

En route: Pass through scenic landscapes and rural areas, offering glimpses of daily life in Tripura.

Afternoon: Explore Pilak Archaeological Site

Explore the Pilak archaeological site, which features sculptures and artifacts from both Hindu and Buddhist traditions. The site includes stone and terracotta plaques, sculptures of various deities, and structural remains of temples.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a simple local eatery near the site.

Evening: Return to Agartala

Return to Agartala by evening. If time permits, visit the Boxanagar Archaeological Site en route, which contains the ruins of a Buddhist monastery dating back to the 8th-12th centuries.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try a local restaurant in Agartala.

Pilak Archaeological Site

Ancient Sculptures at Pilak

About Pilak

Pilak is an important archaeological site located in the South Tripura district, dating back to the 8th-9th centuries AD. The site contains sculptures and artifacts from both Hindu and Buddhist traditions, indicating the religious harmony that existed during that period. The sculptures found here show influences of the Pala and Samatata styles of Bengal. Notable findings include images of Avalokiteshvara, Narasimha, Mahishasuramardini, and various other deities. The Archaeological Survey of India has been conducting excavations at the site, uncovering new artifacts and structures regularly.

8th-9th century archaeological site
Hindu and Buddhist sculptures
Rich cultural heritage
Day 7

Journey to Unakoti

Morning: Drive to Unakoti

After an early breakfast, begin your journey to Unakoti (approximately 178 km from Agartala), known for its ancient rock carvings and stone images dating back to the 7th-9th centuries. The drive takes you through beautiful landscapes of rural Tripura.

En route: Make a brief stop at Kailashahar, a historic town with colonial-era buildings.

Afternoon: Unakoti Archaeological Site

Explore the fascinating Unakoti archaeological site, featuring massive rock-cut sculptures of Hindu deities carved into the hillside. The name "Unakoti" means "one less than a crore" (9,999,999), referring to the legend that this many deities gathered here.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a simple local eatery near the site.

Evening: Accommodation in Unakoti/Kailashahar

Check into your accommodation in Unakoti or nearby Kailashahar. Spend the evening relaxing and reflecting on the day's experiences.

Accommodation: Unakoti Tourist Lodge or a guesthouse in Kailashahar. Note that accommodations are basic in this area.

Unakoti Rock Carvings

Unakoti Landscape

About Unakoti

Unakoti is an ancient Shaivite (dedicated to Lord Shiva) pilgrimage site featuring massive rock-cut sculptures and stone images dating back to the 7th-9th centuries. The central attraction is a 30-foot high carving of Lord Shiva's head, known as "Unakotiswara Kal Bhairava." The site also features carvings of Goddess Durga, Lord Ganesha, and other Hindu deities. According to legend, Lord Shiva was traveling to Kashi with 9,999,999 gods and goddesses but stopped here for the night. He asked everyone to wake up before sunrise, but as no one except Shiva woke up, he left them behind, turning them into stone images.

Day 8

Journey to Jampui Hills

Morning: Drive to Jampui Hills

After breakfast, drive to Jampui Hills (approximately 150 km from Unakoti), known as the "Eternal Spring" of Tripura for its pleasant climate year-round. The journey takes you through picturesque landscapes and tribal villages.

En route: Stop at viewpoints along the way to enjoy panoramic vistas of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Reach Jampui Hills and check into your accommodation. Rest and acclimatize to the higher altitude and cooler climate of the hills.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at your accommodation or at a local eatery.

Evening: Sunset Views

Visit a nearby viewpoint to witness a spectacular sunset over the rolling hills. The orange hues painting the sky above the hills create a mesmerizing sight.

Accommodation: Jampui Tourist Lodge or a homestay in the area. Note that accommodations are basic but offer an authentic experience.

Jampui Hills Landscape

Sunset at Jampui Hills

About Jampui Hills

Jampui Hills, located in the North Tripura district along the Tripura-Mizoram border, is a range of hills known for its pleasant climate, stunning views, and orange plantations. The hills are home to the Mizo tribal community, who have preserved their unique culture and traditions. Jampui Hills is famous for its Orange Festival held in November-December, celebrating the harvest of the region's sweet oranges. The area offers excellent opportunities for trekking, bird watching, and experiencing tribal culture.

Famous for sweet oranges

Day 9

Exploring Jampui Hills

Morning: Betlingchhip Trek

After breakfast, embark on a trek to Betlingchhip, the highest point in Tripura offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. On a clear day, you can see as far as the plains of Bangladesh.

Activity: Enjoy bird watching along the trek route, as the area is home to various bird species.

Afternoon: Mizo Village Visit

Visit a local Mizo tribal village to experience their unique culture and lifestyle. Interact with the villagers, learn about their customs, traditions, and perhaps witness traditional crafts like bamboo and cane work.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at a local home or eatery, trying traditional Mizo cuisine.

Evening: Orange Orchards

Visit the famous orange orchards of Jampui Hills (seasonal, best during November-December). Learn about orange cultivation and the importance of this crop to the local economy. If in season, taste the sweet oranges directly from the orchards.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your accommodation, perhaps trying more local dishes.

View from Betlingchhip

Traditional Mizo Village

Jampui Hills Tips
  • Carry warm clothes as evenings can get cool, especially during winter months.
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes for the Betlingchhip trek.
  • Carry a good camera to capture the stunning landscapes and sunrise/sunset views.
  • Respect local customs and traditions when visiting tribal villages.
  • If visiting during the Orange Festival (November-December), book accommodation well in advance.
Day 10

Chabimura

Morning: Drive to Chabimura

After breakfast, begin your journey back towards Agartala. En route, visit Chabimura (approximately 200 km from Jampui Hills), also known as Debtamura, famous for its panel of rock carvings on a steep cliff on the banks of the Gomati River.

En route: Enjoy the scenic drive through the hills and forests of Tripura.

Afternoon: Chabimura Rock Carvings

Take a boat ride on the Gomati River to view the magnificent rock carvings at Chabimura. The carvings, dating back to the 15th-16th centuries, depict various Hindu deities, primarily Lord Shiva. The panels are carved on a 30-meter high cliff face rising from the river.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a simple local eatery near the site.

Evening: Drive to Amarpur

Continue your journey to Amarpur (approximately 30 km from Chabimura) and check into your accommodation. Spend the evening relaxing after the long day of travel.

Accommodation: Stay at a local guesthouse or hotel in Amarpur. Note that accommodations are basic in this area.

Chabimura Rock Carvings

Gomati River at Chabimura

About Chabimura

Chabimura, also known as Debtamura, is a significant archaeological site featuring rock carvings on a steep cliff along the Gomati River. The carvings, dating back to the 15th-16th centuries, depict various Hindu deities, primarily Lord Shiva, along with Vishnu, Kartika, Mahishasuramardini, and others. The panels are carved on a 30-meter high cliff face rising from the river and can only be viewed properly from a boat. The site is not only of archaeological importance but also offers beautiful natural scenery with the river flowing through a gorge surrounded by lush forests.

Day 11

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Morning: Drive to Sepahijala

After breakfast, drive to Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary (approximately 150 km from Amarpur), home to various species of primates, birds, and the state animal of Tripura, the Phayre's Langur.

En route: Enjoy the scenic countryside and rural landscapes of Tripura.

Afternoon: Wildlife Exploration

Explore the Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary, visiting the zoo, botanical garden, and the Rubber Plantation. Take a boat ride on the sanctuary's lake and visit the Clouded Leopard National Park within the sanctuary.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at the sanctuary's canteen or a nearby eatery.

Evening: Return to Agartala

Continue your journey to Agartala (approximately 35 km from Sepahijala). Check into your hotel and spend the evening relaxing or exploring the local markets for souvenirs.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a restaurant like Ginger or Manik's Kitchen, trying local specialties you might have missed earlier.

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Phayre's Langur

About Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary, spread over 18.53 sq km, is a major wildlife conservation area in Tripura. It's home to various species of primates, including the endangered Phayre's Langur (locally known as "Chashma Bandar" or spectacled monkey), which is also the state animal of Tripura. The sanctuary features a zoo, botanical garden, and a lake where visitors can enjoy boat rides. It's also known for its rubber plantations and successful breeding programs for endangered species.

Home to Phayre's Langur
Botanical garden and zoo
Lake with boat rides
Day 12

Departure

Morning: Last-Minute Shopping

Spend the morning picking up souvenirs and local handicrafts. Tripura is known for its bamboo and cane products, handloom textiles, and tribal artifacts. Visit Purbasha Handicraft Emporium or local markets for authentic items.

Recommended purchases: Bamboo handicrafts, Rignai and Risa (traditional Tripuri garments), tribal jewelry, and handloom products.

Afternoon: Leisure Time

Enjoy a leisurely lunch at a restaurant of your choice, perhaps trying any local dishes you haven't yet experienced. Spend some time relaxing or visiting any attractions in Agartala you might have missed.

Lunch recommendation: Try Kasturi Restaurant or Hotel Sonar Tori for a final taste of local cuisine.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport for your departure flight. Bid farewell to the beautiful state of Tripura, taking with you memories of its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality.

Note: Ensure you reach the airport at least 2 hours before your flight departure time.

Departure Tips
  • Confirm your flight details a day before departure.
  • Pack any fragile handicrafts carefully in your check-in luggage.
  • Keep some local currency for last-minute expenses at the airport.
  • Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport is relatively small, so services may be limited.
  • Consider booking a pre-paid taxi to the airport to avoid any hassles.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: The best time with pleasant weather and minimal rainfall
  • April to May: Summer season with moderate temperatures
  • June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall, not ideal for travel
  • Plan your visit around festivals like Garia Puja (April) or Orange Festival (November-December) for a cultural experience

Transportation

  • Air: Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport in Agartala connects to major Indian cities
  • Train: Agartala Railway Station connects to Guwahati and other major cities
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, cycle-rickshaws, and taxis within cities
  • Road: State buses connect major towns, but services may be limited
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience, especially for remote areas

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes; a light jacket for evenings in hill areas
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit, anti-diarrheal medicine
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Hand sanitizer, wet wipes, toilet paper, flashlight

Food & Cuisine

Tripura offers a unique culinary experience influenced by Bengali and tribal traditions:

Bengali Influence
  • Fish curry with mustard
  • Shorshe Ilish (Hilsa fish in mustard sauce)
  • Chingri Malai Curry (Prawn curry)
  • Mishti Doi (Sweet yogurt)
  • Rosogolla and Sandesh (Sweets)
Tribal Specialties
  • Mui Borok (Rice dish)
  • Chakhwi (Bamboo shoot curry)
  • Gudok (Vegetable stew)
  • Bangui (Rice beer)
  • Berma (Fermented fish)
  • Wahan Mosdeng (Pork chutney)

Practical Information

  • Language: Bengali and Kokborok are the main languages. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities but may be limited in remote areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities, limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Tripura is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign nationals need to register with the Foreigner Registration Office within 24 hours of arrival
  • Local Customs: Respect tribal customs and traditions. Ask permission before photographing people or religious ceremonies
  • Accommodation: Limited options outside Agartala. Book in advance, especially during peak season
