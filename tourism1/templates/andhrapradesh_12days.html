Day 1

Arrival in Visakhapatnam & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Visakhapatnam International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Vizag. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Novotel Visakhapatnam or The Park for luxury stays; Keys Select or Fortune Inn Sree Kanya for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Beach Visit

Enjoy your first Andhra meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Raju Gari Dhaba' or 'Spicy Venue'. Visit the beautiful Ramakrishna Beach and enjoy the sea breeze.

Lunch recommendation: Try Andhra specialties like Pulihora (tamarind rice), Gongura Pachadi (sorrel leaf chutney), and Royyala Vepudu (prawn fry).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the Kailasagiri Hill Park for panoramic views of the city and coastline. Enjoy the sunset and take a ride on the ropeway. Have dinner at a beachside restaurant.

Evening activity: Take a stroll along the Beach Road and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.

Day 2

Visakhapatnam Attractions

Morning: Submarine Museum

Start your day with a visit to the INS Kurusura Submarine Museum, the first of its kind in Asia. Explore this decommissioned submarine that has been converted into a museum.

Activity: Learn about the life of naval officers and the history of the Indian Navy's submarine operations.

Afternoon: Beaches & Yarada Hill

Visit Rushikonda Beach, known for its golden sands and water sports activities. Continue to Yarada Beach, a secluded beach surrounded by hills offering breathtaking views.

Lunch: Enjoy seafood at a beachside restaurant near Rushikonda.

Evening: Simhachalam Temple

Visit the ancient Simhachalam Temple, dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located on a hill and offers panoramic views of the surroundings.

Dinner: Try traditional Andhra thali at 'Subbayya Gari Hotel' or 'Minerva Coffee Shop'.

Day 3

Journey to Araku Valley

Early Morning: Train to Araku

Take the early morning train to Araku Valley (approximately 4 hours), enjoying the scenic route through tunnels and bridges in the Eastern Ghats. Alternatively, you can drive to Araku, which takes about 3 hours.

Note: The Vistadome train offers panoramic views and is highly recommended if available.

Afternoon: Coffee Museum & Tribal Museum

Visit the Araku Coffee Museum to learn about coffee cultivation and processing. Continue to the Tribal Museum to understand the culture and lifestyle of local tribes.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional tribal meal at a local restaurant.

Evening: Accommodation in Araku

Check into your accommodation in Araku Valley. Enjoy the cool climate and serene atmosphere. Visit a local tribal village if time permits.

Accommodation options: Haritha Valley Resort (APTDC), Araku Jungle Bells Resort, or local homestays.

Scenic Train Journey to Araku Valley

Important Information
  • Book your train tickets in advance, especially for the Vistadome coach.
  • Carry light woolens as evenings can be cool in Araku Valley.
  • Mobile network might be limited in some areas of Araku.
  • Respect tribal customs and ask permission before taking photographs in tribal villages.
  • The best time to visit is from October to March.
Araku Coffee

Araku Valley is famous for its organic coffee, which has gained international recognition. Don't miss the opportunity to taste and purchase some authentic Araku coffee as a souvenir. The coffee museum offers a detailed insight into the coffee-making process and its significance to the local economy.

Day 4

Exploring Araku Valley

Morning: Borra Caves

Visit the million-year-old Borra Caves, one of the largest caves in India. Explore the fascinating stalactite and stalagmite formations illuminated by colorful lights.

Activity: Take photographs of the natural limestone formations and learn about their geological significance.

Afternoon: Padmapuram Gardens

Visit the Padmapuram Gardens, originally created during World War II to supply vegetables to soldiers. Today, it's a beautiful botanical garden with tree-top cottages and a nursery.

Lunch: Enjoy a picnic lunch at the gardens or try local cuisine at a nearby restaurant.

Evening: Chaparai Waterfalls

Visit the Chaparai Waterfalls (also known as Dumbriguda Waterfalls), a natural cascade with flat rocks where you can enjoy the flowing water. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

Dinner: Return to your accommodation for dinner and overnight stay in Araku Valley.

Adventure Activities

Araku Valley offers several adventure activities for thrill-seekers:

  • Trekking: Several trails through the Eastern Ghats
  • Zip-lining: Available at certain locations in the valley
  • Tribal Village Tours: Guided tours to authentic tribal settlements
  • Photography: Abundant opportunities for nature and landscape photography

These activities can be arranged through your accommodation or local tour operators.

Day 5

Araku to Rajahmundry

Morning: Ananthagiri Coffee Plantations

Visit the Ananthagiri Coffee Plantations to see how coffee is grown and harvested. The lush green plantations offer beautiful views and photo opportunities.

Activity: Take a guided tour of the plantations and learn about sustainable coffee farming practices.

Afternoon: Return to Visakhapatnam

Take the afternoon train or drive back to Visakhapatnam. Enjoy the scenic journey through the Eastern Ghats once more.

Lunch: Pack a lunch or eat at a restaurant in Araku before departure.

Evening: Drive to Rajahmundry

From Visakhapatnam, drive to Rajahmundry (approximately 5 hours). Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: The Gateway Hotel, River Bay Resort, or Hotel Anand Regency.

About Rajahmundry

Rajahmundry, founded in the 11th century, is one of the oldest cities in South India. It is situated on the banks of the Godavari River and is known as the "Cultural Capital of Andhra Pradesh." The city is famous for its literary heritage, ancient temples, and the magnificent Godavari Bridge.

Day 6

Godavari River Exploration

Morning: Godavari River Cruise

Take a morning cruise on the Godavari River to Papi Hills and Pattiseema. Enjoy the scenic beauty of the river and surrounding landscapes.

Activity: Look out for local fishermen using traditional fishing methods and various bird species along the riverbanks.

Afternoon: Dowleswaram Barrage

Visit the Dowleswaram Barrage, an engineering marvel built by Sir Arthur Cotton in the 19th century. Learn about its significance in irrigation and flood control.

Lunch: Enjoy a riverside lunch at a local restaurant.

Evening: Temples of Rajahmundry

Visit the ancient temples of Rajahmundry, including the Markandeya Temple and the Kotilingeshwara Temple. These temples showcase the rich religious heritage of the region.

Dinner: Try local Andhra cuisine at a traditional restaurant in Rajahmundry.

Godavari River Cruise

The Godavari River

The Godavari is the second longest river in India after the Ganges. Known as the "Dakshina Ganga" (Ganges of the South), it holds immense cultural and religious significance. The river is the lifeline of Andhra Pradesh, providing water for irrigation, drinking, and industrial purposes.

The Godavari River Basin is rich in biodiversity and supports a variety of flora and fauna. The river cruise offers a unique perspective of the river ecosystem and the lives of people who depend on it.

Day 7

Rajahmundry to Vijayawada

Morning: Godavari Bridge

Visit the iconic Godavari Bridge, also known as the Havelock Bridge, one of the oldest railway bridges in India. The bridge offers stunning views of the river and surrounding landscape.

Activity: Take photographs of this engineering marvel and learn about its historical significance.

Afternoon: Journey to Vijayawada

After lunch, drive to Vijayawada (approximately 3-4 hours), the commercial capital of Andhra Pradesh. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Murali Park, Novotel Vijayawada, or Hotel DV Manor.

Evening: Kanaka Durga Temple

Visit the famous Kanaka Durga Temple, dedicated to the goddess Durga, located on the Indrakeeladri Hill overlooking the Krishna River. The temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Andhra Pradesh.

Dinner: Try local Andhra cuisine at 'Babai Hotel' or 'RR's Durbar' known for authentic flavors.

About Vijayawada

Vijayawada is the third largest city in Andhra Pradesh and serves as the commercial capital of the state. Located on the banks of the Krishna River, the city is known for its historical, cultural, and religious significance. Vijayawada means "The Place of Victory" and has a history dating back to the 5th century BCE.

The city is surrounded by hills and caves with ancient temples and rock-cut structures. It's also a major transportation hub with excellent connectivity to other parts of India.

Day 8

Vijayawada & Amaravati

Morning: Undavalli Caves

Visit the Undavalli Caves, a monolithic example of Indian rock-cut architecture dating back to the 4th-5th centuries. The caves are carved out of solid sandstone on a hillside.

Highlight: Don't miss the huge reclining statue of Lord Vishnu carved out of a single block of granite.

Afternoon: Amaravati Stupa

Drive to Amaravati (approximately 1 hour) to visit the ancient Buddhist site. Explore the Amaravati Stupa and the Archaeological Museum, which houses sculptures and relics from the 3rd century BCE to the 12th century CE.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at a local restaurant in Amaravati.

Evening: Prakasam Barrage

Return to Vijayawada and visit the Prakasam Barrage, built across the Krishna River. Enjoy the sunset views and the illuminated barrage in the evening.

Dinner: Have dinner at a restaurant overlooking the Krishna River.

Amaravati Stupa

Historical Significance

Amaravati was once a major Buddhist center and the capital of the Satavahana dynasty. The Amaravati Stupa, built during the 3rd century BCE, was one of the largest stupas in India. The sculptures and reliefs from Amaravati are considered masterpieces of Indian art and are displayed in museums around the world, including the British Museum in London.

Amaravati is also the name of the planned capital city of Andhra Pradesh, which is being developed near the ancient site.

Day 9

Vijayawada to Guntur & Ongole

Morning: Drive to Guntur

After breakfast, drive to Guntur (approximately 1 hour), known for its chili production and historical sites. Visit the Kondaveedu Fort, a 14th-century hill fortress with panoramic views.

Activity: Explore the ancient fort and its ruins, including temples, pillars, and water tanks.

Afternoon: Guntur Chili Market

Visit the famous Guntur Chili Market, one of the largest chili markets in Asia. Learn about different varieties of chilies and their uses in Andhra cuisine.

Lunch: Try the spicy Guntur cuisine at a local restaurant.

Evening: Drive to Ongole

Continue your journey to Ongole (approximately 2-3 hours), known for its cattle breed and beaches. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Hotel Haritha, Hotel Dwaraka, or similar mid-range options.

Kondaveedu Fort

Guntur Chilies

Guntur is known as the "Chili Capital of India" and produces some of the spiciest chilies in the world. The Guntur Sannam chili is famous for its heat and flavor, and is a key ingredient in authentic Andhra cuisine.

The chili market in Guntur is a vibrant place where farmers, traders, and exporters gather to buy and sell different varieties of chilies. The market is at its busiest during the harvest season from January to March.

Day 10

Ongole to Nellore

Morning: Ongole Cattle Farm

Visit an Ongole cattle farm to see the famous Ongole breed, known for its size, strength, and milk production. Learn about the significance of this breed in Indian agriculture.

Activity: Interact with farmers and learn about traditional cattle rearing practices.

Afternoon: Vodarevu Beach

Visit the Vodarevu Beach, a pristine beach with golden sands and clear waters. Enjoy the peaceful atmosphere and watch local fishermen at work.

Lunch: Enjoy fresh seafood at a beachside restaurant.

Evening: Drive to Nellore

Drive to Nellore (approximately 2 hours), known for its spicy cuisine and agricultural produce. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Select Grand Ridge, Hotel Athidi Grand, or similar options.

Coastal Drive

The drive from Ongole to Nellore along the coastal highway offers beautiful views of the Bay of Bengal. You'll pass through small fishing villages, salt pans, and agricultural fields.

This stretch of the East Coast Road is less crowded compared to other coastal highways in India, making it a pleasant driving experience. Consider making stops at viewpoints along the way to enjoy the coastal scenery.

Day 11

Nellore to Tirupati

Morning: Ranganatha Temple

Visit the Sri Ranganatha Swamy Temple in Nellore, a 14th-century temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Admire the Dravidian architecture and intricate carvings.

Activity: Participate in the morning rituals and prayers if possible.

Afternoon: Nelapattu Bird Sanctuary

Visit the Nelapattu Bird Sanctuary, home to various species of migratory and resident birds, including the rare Spot-billed Pelican.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local restaurant before heading to the sanctuary.

Evening: Drive to Tirupati

Drive to Tirupati (approximately 3 hours), the temple town at the foothills of Tirumala. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Fortune Select Grand Ridge, Marasa Sarovar Premiere, or TTDC Hotel Tamil Nadu.

About Tirupati

Tirupati is one of the most sacred pilgrimage centers in India. The town is famous for the Sri Venkateswara Temple (also known as Tirumala Temple) dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located on the seven hills of Tirumala and is one of the most visited religious sites in the world.

Day 12

Tirupati & Departure

Early Morning: Tirumala Temple

Early morning visit to the Sri Venkateswara Temple in Tirumala. The temple is located on a hill and can be reached by road or by climbing the steps (approximately 3,500 steps).

Note: Follow all temple rules and dress code. Men should wear traditional dhoti or pants, and women should wear saree or salwar kameez.

Afternoon: ISKCON Temple & Padmavathi Temple

Return to Tirupati and visit the ISKCON Temple. Also visit the Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple in Tiruchanur, dedicated to Goddess Padmavathi, the consort of Lord Venkateswara.

Lunch: Try the famous Tirupati laddu and other prasadam items.

Evening: Departure

Do some last-minute shopping for souvenirs and religious items. Transfer to Tirupati Airport or Railway Station for your onward journey.

Note: Tirupati is well-connected by air and rail to major cities in India.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Coastal Areas: October to March (winter months)
  • Araku Valley: September to February
  • Tirupati: Year-round, but avoid summer (March to June) due to extreme heat
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) in coastal areas

Transportation

  • Air: Major airports in Visakhapatnam, Vijayawada, and Tirupati
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs, auto-rickshaws, and local buses
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for coastal areas; light woolens for Araku Valley
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
