Meghalaya: The Abode of Clouds

Meghalaya offers a perfect blend of natural wonders, living traditions, and unique landscapes. From the misty hills of Shillong to the crystal-clear waters of Dawki, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northeastern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Guwahati & Drive to Shillong

Morning: Arrival at Guwahati

Arrive at Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, Assam. Meet your driver and prepare for the journey to Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya.

Travel tip: Guwahati is the nearest airport to Meghalaya. Pre-arrange your transportation to Shillong for a hassle-free experience.

Afternoon: Drive to Shillong

Embark on a scenic 3-hour drive (approximately 100 km) from Guwahati to Shillong. En route, stop at Umiam Lake (Barapani), a vast reservoir surrounded by hills, for photographs and a quick refreshment.

Attraction: Umiam Lake offers breathtaking panoramic views and watersport activities (if time permits).

Evening: Check-in & Police Bazaar

Check into your hotel in Shillong. After freshening up, visit Police Bazaar, the main commercial hub of Shillong, for shopping and dinner. Experience the vibrant local atmosphere and try some Khasi cuisine.

Accommodation: Tripura Castle, Hotel Polo Towers, or Ri Kynjai for luxury stays; Hotel Alpine Continental or Hotel Rockski for mid-range options.

Day 1 Tips
  • The drive from Guwahati to Shillong offers beautiful views, so keep your camera handy.
  • Carry some snacks and water for the journey.
  • All visitors to Meghalaya need to register on the Meghalaya Tourism app or website.
  • Shillong has a pleasant climate year-round, but evenings can be cool, so carry a light jacket.
Day 2

Shillong Exploration

Morning: Elephant Falls & Shillong Peak

Start your day with a visit to Elephant Falls, a three-tier waterfall located on the outskirts of the city. Next, head to Shillong Peak, the highest point in Shillong offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

Activity: At Shillong Peak, which is a sacred site for the local Khasi tribe, enjoy the magnificent bird's-eye view of Shillong and take memorable photographs.

Afternoon: Cathedral & Don Bosco Museum

Visit the Mary Help of Christians Cathedral, an architectural marvel with beautiful stained glass windows. Continue to Don Bosco Museum, a seven-story museum showcasing the culture, heritage, and history of the northeastern states of India.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant like Cafe Shillong or ML05 Cafe for authentic Khasi cuisine or continental options.

Evening: Ward's Lake & Local Market

Spend the evening at Ward's Lake, a picturesque man-made lake with a botanical garden where you can enjoy boating or a leisurely walk. Later, explore the local markets for traditional Khasi handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Dinner: Try Jadoh (rice and meat dish) or Dohneiihong (pork with black sesame) at local eateries like Trattoria or City Hut Dhaba.

Day 3

Shillong to Cherrapunji

Morning: Mawphlang Sacred Forest

Check out from your Shillong hotel and drive to Mawphlang Sacred Grove (about 25 km from Shillong). Take a guided tour through this ancient forest, considered sacred by the Khasi people and preserved for centuries through traditional conservation practices.

Activity: Learn about the rich biodiversity and cultural significance of the forest while exploring its trails with a local guide.

Afternoon: Drive to Cherrapunji

After lunch, continue your journey to Cherrapunji (Sohra), one of the wettest places on Earth, approximately 54 km from Shillong. En route, stop at various viewpoints to admire the cascading waterfalls and lush green valleys.

Accommodation: Polo Orchid Resort or Kutmadan Resort for comfort; Sa-I-Mika Park or Coniferous Resort for budget options.

Evening: Seven Sisters Waterfall

Visit the Seven Sisters Waterfall (Nohsngithiang Falls), a seven-segmented waterfall that plunges down from a height of 315 meters. Enjoy the sunset views as the last rays of the sun illuminate the waterfall.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your resort or at a local restaurant in Cherrapunji, trying Khasi specialties like Tungrymbai (fermented soybean dish) or Nakham Bitchi (dried fish chutney).

About Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji (locally known as Sohra) once held the record for receiving the highest rainfall on Earth and is now the second wettest place after nearby Mawsynram. The region is known for its stunning landscapes, deep gorges, majestic waterfalls, and the unique living root bridges cultivated by the Khasi tribe.

The area's limestone caves, dense forests, and breathtaking viewpoints offer visitors a glimpse into nature's raw beauty and the traditional way of life of the indigenous Khasi people.

Weather Advisory

Cherrapunji receives heavy rainfall, especially during the monsoon season (June to September). Carry rain gear, waterproof bags for electronics, and appropriate footwear regardless of when you visit, as sudden showers are common.

Day 4

Cherrapunji & Nongriat Trek

Morning: Nohkalikai Falls

Visit Nohkalikai Falls, India's tallest plunge waterfall with a height of 340 meters. Learn about the tragic legend behind the waterfall's name and enjoy the breathtaking views from the viewing platform.

Activity: Photography and admiring the magnificent views of the waterfall and surrounding landscapes.

Afternoon: Trek to Nongriat Double Decker Root Bridge

Drive to Tyrna village (about 12 km from Cherrapunji) and begin the trek to Nongriat village to see the famous Double Decker Living Root Bridge. The trek involves descending approximately 3,500 steps into the valley and then climbing back up.

Note: This is a moderate to difficult trek requiring good physical fitness. If you prefer, you can opt for easier alternatives like the Single Root Bridge at Mawlynnong (which we'll visit on Day 5).

Evening: Return to Cherrapunji

Return to Cherrapunji after the trek. Spend the evening relaxing at your resort, sharing experiences with fellow travelers, and enjoying the serene atmosphere of Cherrapunji.

Option: You can choose to stay overnight in Nongriat village for a more immersive experience, but this would require adjusting the itinerary.

Living Root Bridges

Living root bridges are unique bio-engineering marvels created by the Khasi and Jaintia tribes of Meghalaya. The tribes train the aerial roots of the Indian rubber tree (Ficus elastica) to grow along hollow betel nut trunks that span streams and rivers.

Over 15-30 years, these roots form a sturdy, living bridge that can support the weight of several people at once. Some of these bridges are over 150 years old and grow stronger with time, unlike conventional bridges that weaken.

The Double Decker Root Bridge in Nongriat village is a rare example where roots have been trained to form two bridges, one above the other.

Day 5

Cherrapunji to Mawlynnong

Morning: Mawsmai Cave

Visit Mawsmai Cave, a limestone cave with impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations. Explore the illuminated sections of the cave that have been made accessible to tourists.

Activity: Take a guided tour through the cave to learn about its geological significance and marvel at the natural limestone formations.

Afternoon: Drive to Mawlynnong

After lunch, check out from your Cherrapunji accommodation and drive to Mawlynnong (approximately 90 km, 3-hour drive), recognized as "Asia's Cleanest Village" by Discover India Magazine in 2003.

Accommodation: Mawlynnong has limited accommodation options. Stay at local homestays like Hala Tyngkong Homestay or Ilajong Guest House for an authentic experience.

Evening: Village Exploration

Explore the pristine village of Mawlynnong, known for its cleanliness, community-based sanitation system, and unique living practices. Visit the Sky View platform, a bamboo structure offering panoramic views of the Bangladesh plains.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple homemade dinner at your homestay, typically consisting of local Khasi dishes prepared with fresh, organic ingredients.

Mawlynnong Village

About Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong gained international recognition as "Asia's Cleanest Village" and "God's Own Garden." The village showcases the Khasi community's commitment to cleanliness and environmental preservation.

All homes in Mawlynnong use eco-friendly disposal systems, and waste is collected in bamboo dustbins placed throughout the village. The community works together to maintain cleanliness, with even young children participating in daily cleaning routines.

Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and contribute to maintaining the village's pristine environment.

Day 6

Mawlynnong & Dawki

Morning: Living Root Bridge

Visit the Single Decker Living Root Bridge at Riwai, a short walk from Mawlynnong. This natural bridge, formed by training the roots of the Indian rubber tree, is over a century old and demonstrates the ingenious engineering skills of the Khasi tribe.

Activity: Walk across the bridge and follow the trail that leads to a stream where you can dip your feet in the cool water.

Afternoon: Dawki & Umngot River

Drive to Dawki (approximately 35 km from Mawlynnong) to see the crystal-clear Umngot River. The river's transparency is so remarkable that boats appear to be floating on air rather than water.

Activity: Take a boat ride on the Umngot River and enjoy the serene atmosphere. If time permits, visit the Bangladesh border which is very close to Dawki.

Evening: Shnongpdeng

Visit Shnongpdeng, a riverside village known for water activities like snorkeling, cliff jumping, and kayaking. Enjoy the scenic beauty of the river and surrounding mountains as the sun sets.

Option: You can choose to stay overnight in a tent by the riverside in Shnongpdeng or return to Mawlynnong for the night.

Best Time to Visit Dawki

The best time to visit Dawki is from October to March when the water is at its clearest. During these months, the river's legendary transparency is at its peak, offering breathtaking views of the riverbed.

During monsoon (June to September), the river level rises and the water can become muddy, diminishing the clarity. However, the surrounding landscapes become lush and green during this period.

If you're visiting during peak tourist season (December to January), arrive early in the morning to avoid crowds and get the best experience on the river.

Day 7

Return to Guwahati & Departure

Morning: Leisurely Start

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your homestay and take some time to soak in the final views of the pristine village of Mawlynnong. Bid farewell to your hosts and begin your journey back to Guwahati.

Activity: Take some final photographs of the beautiful surroundings and purchase any local handicrafts or organic products as souvenirs.

Afternoon: Drive to Guwahati

Embark on the return journey to Guwahati (approximately 170 km, 5-6 hours). The drive takes you through scenic landscapes, giving you time to reflect on your Meghalaya experience.

En route: Stop at convenient locations for lunch and short breaks. If time permits, visit Barapani Lake once more for a final view.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits before your departure, visit the local markets in Guwahati for some last-minute shopping. Pick up regional specialties like Khasi hand-woven textiles, bamboo crafts, or local spices.

Departure: Transfer to Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport for your onward journey, carrying memories of Meghalaya's pristine landscapes and unique culture.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: Ideal months with pleasant weather and clear skies
  • June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall (great for waterfall viewing but trekking can be challenging)
  • December to February: Cold but clear, with temperatures dropping to 4-5Â°C in some areas
  • For river activities: October to March when waters are clearest
  • For cultural festivals: November (Wangala) and April (Shad Suk Mynsiem)

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airport is in Guwahati, Assam (3 hours from Shillong)
  • Train: Nearest major railway station is in Guwahati
  • Road: Well-connected via NH-40 from Guwahati
  • Local Transport: Shared sumos, taxis, and local buses available
  • Recommended: Hire a private car with driver for the entire trip
  • For trekking: Local guides necessary for living root bridge treks

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Lightweight with warm layers for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Rain gear: Waterproof jacket, umbrella, waterproof covers for bags
  • For treks: Backpack, water bottle, energy bars, first aid kit
  • Documentation: ID proof (required for domestic tourists)
  • Health: Personal medications, insect repellent
  • Other: Power bank, camera with waterproof case, torch

Food & Cuisine

Meghalaya offers a unique culinary experience with dishes that showcase local ingredients and traditional cooking methods:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Jadoh (rice and meat dish)
  • Dohneiihong (pork with black sesame)
  • Tungrymbai (fermented soybean dish)
  • Nakham Bitchi (dried fish chutney)
  • Putharo (rice pancakes)
  • Kyat (local rice beer)
Dining Tips
  • Most villages offer simple, home-cooked meals
  • Shillong has a range of restaurants serving local and international cuisine
  • Try orange honey and tea from Khasi Hills
  • Carry snacks for long drives and treks
  • Vegetarian options are limited in rural areas
  • Try the local pineapples and oranges when in season

Practical Information

  • Language: Khasi is the main language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs available in Shillong but limited in rural areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Power outages can occur, so carry a power bank
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Shillong; limited in remote areas
  • Registration: All tourists need to register on the Meghalaya Tourism app
  • Protected areas: Some areas require special permits for foreign nationals
  • Local customs: Respect tribal customs and ask before photographing people
  • Plastic ban: Single-use plastics are banned in many areas of Meghalaya
  • Emergency: Limited medical facilities outside Shillong; carry basic medicines
