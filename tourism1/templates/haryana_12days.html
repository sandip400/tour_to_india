Haryana: The Cradle of Indian History

Haryana offers a fascinating blend of ancient history, spiritual heritage, and modern development. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the major cities and hidden gems of this North Indian state, from the corporate hub of Gurugram to the sacred battlefields of Kurukshetra and the serene Morni Hills.

Day 1

Arrival in Delhi & Transfer to Gurugram

Morning: Arrival & Transfer

Arrive at Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi. Transfer to your hotel in Gurugram (approximately 30-45 minutes depending on traffic). Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Oberoi or Leela Ambience for luxury stays; Lemon Tree or Radisson for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Cyber Hub

Enjoy your first meal at the famous Cyber Hub, a premium food and entertainment destination in Gurugram. Explore this modern complex that showcases Gurugram's cosmopolitan culture.

Lunch recommendation: Try restaurants like Dhaba, Farzi Café, or SodaBottleOpenerWala for a mix of Indian and fusion cuisine.

Evening: Ambience Mall & Leisure Valley

Visit Ambience Mall, one of India's largest shopping centers. Later, take a leisurely stroll in Leisure Valley Park, a green oasis in the concrete jungle of Gurugram.

Dinner: Try Kingdom of Dreams for a theatrical dining experience with cultural performances if available, or head to Sector 29 market for diverse dining options.

Cyber Hub

Ambience Mall

Day 1 Tips
  • Delhi-Gurugram traffic can be heavy, especially during peak hours. Plan your transfer accordingly.
  • Cyber Hub is vast, so wear comfortable footwear.
  • Keep yourself hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation within the city.
Day 2

Gurugram Exploration

Morning: Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary

Start your day with a visit to Sultanpur National Park and Bird Sanctuary, located about 15 km from Gurugram. This wetland is home to over 250 species of birds, including migratory birds during winter months.

Activity: Bird watching and nature photography. Carry binoculars if you have them.

Afternoon: Sheetla Mata Temple & Lunch

Visit the revered Sheetla Mata Temple, an important religious site in Gurugram. Continue to the Kingdom of Dreams, India's first live entertainment and leisure destination, combining Indian culture, heritage, and art with technology.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Culture Gully inside Kingdom of Dreams, offering cuisines from different Indian states.

Evening: Damdama Lake

Visit Damdama Lake, located about 24 km from Gurugram. This natural lake offers boating, camping, and other adventure activities. Enjoy the sunset views and peaceful environment.

Dinner: Return to Gurugram and try local Haryanvi cuisine at Dhaba Estd 1986 or similar authentic restaurants.

Day 3

Gurugram to Faridabad

Morning: Drive to Faridabad

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Faridabad (approximately 1 hour), one of Haryana's major industrial hubs. The city is known for its industries and proximity to Delhi.

Note: The drive offers views of the developing urban landscape of the National Capital Region.

Afternoon: Check-in & Raja Nahar Singh Palace

Arrive in Faridabad and check into your hotel. After lunch, visit the historic Raja Nahar Singh Palace, a 18th-century palace that now houses a museum showcasing artifacts from the region's history.

Accommodation: Radisson or Park Plaza for comfortable stays in Faridabad.

Evening: Badkhal Lake & Local Markets

Visit Badkhal Lake, once a popular picnic spot (note: the lake may be dry depending on the season). Continue to explore the local markets of Faridabad like Sector 15 Market or Crown Interiorz Mall for shopping and dinner.

Dinner: Try local North Indian cuisine at restaurants like Coriander Leaf or The Great Kabab Factory.

Raja Nahar Singh Palace

Important Information
  • Faridabad is an industrial city, so air quality can be poor. Carry masks if you have respiratory issues.
  • Badkhal Lake has faced water depletion issues in recent years. Check current status before visiting.
  • Raja Nahar Singh Palace may have specific visiting hours. Confirm before planning your visit.
  • Faridabad can get very hot in summer months (April-June). Carry water and sun protection.
About Faridabad

Faridabad, founded in 1607 by Sheikh Farid, treasurer to Jahangir, is one of the largest cities in Haryana and part of the National Capital Region. It's a major industrial hub with numerous manufacturing units. Despite its industrial character, the city has historical significance with sites like Raja Nahar Singh Palace and religious places like Shirdi Sai Baba Temple and ISKCON Temple.

Day 4

Faridabad to Kurukshetra

Morning: Drive to Kurukshetra

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Kurukshetra (approximately 3-4 hours), the land of the Mahabharata war and one of India's most significant spiritual and historical destinations.

En route: You can stop at Panipat, known for its three historic battles, for a short break and visit to the Panipat Museum if time permits.

Afternoon: Check-in & Brahma Sarovar

Arrive in Kurukshetra and check into your hotel. After lunch, visit the sacred Brahma Sarovar, a vast water tank believed to have been excavated by King Kuru. It's especially beautiful during evening aarti (prayer ceremony).

Accommodation: Haryana Tourism's Neelkanthi or Jyotisar Tourist Complex; Hotel Saffron for private options.

Evening: Sannihit Sarovar & Light and Sound Show

Visit Sannihit Sarovar, another sacred tank that attracts pilgrims during solar eclipses. In the evening, attend the Light and Sound Show at Kurukshetra Panorama and Science Centre, which narrates the story of the Mahabharata.

Dinner: Try simple, sattvic food at local eateries near Brahma Sarovar or at your hotel.

Day 5

Kurukshetra Exploration

Morning: Jyotisar & Krishna Museum

Visit Jyotisar, the sacred site where Lord Krishna delivered the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna. See the ancient banyan tree under which the sermon is believed to have been delivered. Continue to Sri Krishna Museum, which houses artifacts related to Lord Krishna and the Mahabharata.

Activity: Take time to meditate or reflect at Jyotisar, one of the most spiritually significant sites in India.

Afternoon: Kurukshetra Panorama & Science Centre

Visit the Kurukshetra Panorama and Science Centre, which houses a panoramic depiction of the Mahabharata war and various scientific exhibits. Continue to Bhishma Kund and Bhadrakali Temple, other important sites associated with the Mahabharata.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple vegetarian thali at local eateries near the tourist sites or at Haryana Tourism's restaurant.

Evening: Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple & Ghat

Visit the ancient Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the oldest temples in Haryana. Later, attend the evening aarti at Brahma Sarovar and take a boat ride if available.

Dinner: Try local Haryanvi cuisine at your hotel or nearby restaurants.

Day 6

Kurukshetra to Panchkula & Morni Hills

Morning: Drive to Panchkula

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Panchkula (approximately 2-3 hours), a planned city that serves as a gateway to the Morni Hills, the only hill station in Haryana.

Note: The drive offers beautiful views of the changing landscape from plains to foothills.

Afternoon: Morni Hills

After checking into your hotel in Panchkula, drive to Morni Hills (approximately 1 hour). Explore this picturesque hill station with its twin lakes and panoramic views of the Shivalik Range. Visit the Morni Fort for historical insights.

Lunch: Enjoy a picnic lunch amidst nature or try the local dhabas for authentic Haryanvi food.

Evening: Tikkar Taal & Return to Panchkula

Visit the twin lakes of Tikkar Taal, surrounded by lush greenery. Enjoy boating if available. Return to Panchkula by evening and relax at your hotel.

Accommodation: Hotel KC Residency or The Cove in Panchkula; Haryana Tourism's Dabchick Tourist Complex in Morni Hills for a more immersive experience.

Morni Hills

Tikkar Taal

About Morni Hills

Morni Hills, named after Queen Morni who once ruled the area, is Haryana's only hill station. Located at an altitude of about 1,220 meters above sea level, it offers a refreshing escape from the plains with its cool climate, dense forests, and twin lakes. The area is rich in flora and fauna and offers activities like trekking, bird watching, and boating. The 17th-century Morni Fort stands as a testament to the region's historical significance.

Day 7

Panchkula to Pinjore & Ambala

Morning: Pinjore Gardens

After breakfast, visit the magnificent Pinjore Gardens (Yadavindra Gardens), a perfect example of the Mughal style of architecture. These 17th-century terraced gardens feature water fountains, pavilions, and a mini zoo.

Activity: Take a leisurely stroll through the seven terraces of the garden and visit the small heritage museum inside.

Afternoon: Drive to Ambala

After lunch, drive to Ambala (approximately 1 hour), known for its military cantonment and as a major railway junction. Check into your hotel and rest.

Accommodation: Hotel Puran Palace or Hotel Noor Mahal in Ambala.

Evening: Ambala Exploration

Explore Ambala Cantonment area and visit the local markets like Cloth Market and Sadar Bazaar, known for textiles, spices, and handicrafts. Visit the historic Ambala Church if time permits.

Dinner: Try local Punjabi-influenced cuisine at restaurants like Puran Dhaba or Hotel Noor Mahal's restaurant.

Day 8

Ambala to Panipat

Morning: Temples of Ambala

Start your day with visits to some of Ambala's religious sites like Badshahi Bagh Gurudwara and Manji Sahib Gurudwara. These sites hold historical and spiritual significance for the Sikh community.

Activity: Participate in the langar (community meal) at one of the gurudwaras for an authentic cultural experience.

Afternoon: Drive to Panipat

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Panipat (approximately 2 hours), known as the "City of Weavers" and famous for three historic battles that shaped India's history.

En route: You can stop at Karnal for a short break if needed.

Evening: Panipat Museum & Kabuli Bagh

Visit the Panipat Museum, which houses artifacts related to the three historic battles of Panipat. Continue to Kabuli Bagh, a garden complex built by Emperor Babur to commemorate his victory in the First Battle of Panipat.

Accommodation: Hotel KC Plaza or Skylark Hotel in Panipat.

Badshahi Bagh Gurudwara

Panipat Museum

About Panipat

Panipat, often called the "City of Weavers," holds immense historical significance as the site of three pivotal battles that shaped India's destiny. The First Battle of Panipat (1526) established Mughal rule in India, the Second Battle (1556) confirmed it, and the Third Battle (1761) marked the decline of Maratha power. Today, Panipat is known for its textile industry, particularly handloom products, carpets, and blankets. The city blends its rich historical heritage with industrial progress.

Day 9

Panipat to Karnal

Morning: Panipat Handloom Industry

Explore Panipat's famous handloom industry. Visit the local textile markets and factories to see how the traditional handloom products, carpets, and blankets are made. This is a great opportunity to purchase authentic Haryanvi textiles.

Activity: Shop for handloom products, which make excellent souvenirs.

Afternoon: Drive to Karnal

After lunch, drive to Karnal (approximately 1 hour), known as the "Rice Bowl of India" and named after Raja Karna from the Mahabharata. Check into your hotel and rest.

Accommodation: Noor Mahal Palace Hotel for luxury; Hotel Jewels or Vivaan Hotel for mid-range options.

Evening: Karnal Exploration

Visit the Cantonment Church Tower, a remnant of British colonial architecture. Explore the local markets and enjoy the evening at Karna Lake, a popular recreational spot maintained by Haryana Tourism.

Dinner: Try local Haryanvi cuisine at Zhilmil or Noor Mahal's restaurant.

Haryanvi Cuisine

Haryanvi cuisine is known for its simplicity, nutritional value, and rich flavors. During your stay in Karnal, don't miss these local specialties:

  • Kachri Ki Sabzi: A traditional dish made from wild melon
  • Bajre Ki Khichdi: A nutritious porridge made from millet
  • Methi Gajar: A vegetable dish made with fenugreek leaves and carrots
  • Hara Dhania Cholia: Green coriander with chickpeas
  • Malpua: Sweet pancakes soaked in sugar syrup
  • Lassi: Traditional yogurt-based drink
Day 10

Karnal to Hisar

Morning: National Dairy Research Institute

Visit the National Dairy Research Institute (NDRI), one of the premier dairy research institutions in Asia. The institute has a museum that showcases the evolution of dairy farming in India.

Activity: Learn about India's White Revolution and the importance of dairy farming in Haryana's economy.

Afternoon: Drive to Hisar

After lunch, drive to Hisar (approximately 2-3 hours), one of the important historical cities of Haryana with structures dating back to the Mughal and Firoz Shah Tughlaq era.

En route: You can stop at small towns like Jind for a short break and to experience rural Haryana.

Evening: Firoz Shah Palace Complex

Visit the Firoz Shah Palace Complex, built in the 14th century by Firoz Shah Tughlaq. The complex includes structures like Lat ki Masjid, Gujari Mahal, and Baradari.

Accommodation: Hotel Rajhans or Hotel Sagar in Hisar.

National Dairy Research Institute

Firoz Shah Palace Complex

About Hisar

Hisar, meaning 'fort' in Persian, has a rich historical legacy dating back to the 1st century. The city was significantly developed during the reign of Firoz Shah Tughlaq in the 14th century, who built several monuments that stand today. In modern times, Hisar has emerged as an important agricultural and industrial center in Haryana. The city is home to Chaudhary Charan Singh Haryana Agricultural University, one of Asia's largest agricultural universities.

Day 11

Hisar to Rohtak

Morning: Haryana Agricultural University

Visit the Chaudhary Charan Singh Haryana Agricultural University, one of the largest agricultural universities in Asia. Explore its campus, museum, and botanical gardens.

Activity: Learn about agricultural innovations and research that have contributed to Haryana's status as an agricultural powerhouse.

Afternoon: Drive to Rohtak

After lunch, drive to Rohtak (approximately 2 hours), an ancient city mentioned in the Mahabharata and now an educational hub of Haryana. Check into your hotel and rest.

Accommodation: Hotel Rivoli or Hotel Horizon in Rohtak.

Evening: Rohtak Exploration

Visit the ancient Shiva Temple and Gokaran Tank, believed to be built during the Mahabharata period. Explore the local markets and enjoy the evening at Tilyar Lake, a popular recreational spot with a mini zoo and boating facilities.

Dinner: Try local Haryanvi cuisine at restaurants like Choudhary Dhaba or Hotel Rivoli's restaurant.

Haryana Agricultural University

Tilyar Lake

Educational Hub

Rohtak has emerged as a major educational center in Haryana, often called the "Educational City of Haryana." It is home to prestigious institutions like Maharshi Dayanand University, National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT), and several medical and engineering colleges. The city's focus on education has contributed significantly to its development and has made it a hub for students from across the region seeking quality education.

Day 12

Rohtak to Sultanpur & Return to Delhi

Morning: Maharshi Dayanand University

Visit the Maharshi Dayanand University campus, one of the largest universities in Haryana. Explore its vast campus and architectural buildings.

Activity: Take a morning walk in the university's botanical garden if time permits.

Afternoon: Drive to Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary

After checking out from your hotel, drive to Sultanpur National Park and Bird Sanctuary (approximately 2-3 hours). This is your second visit to the sanctuary, but this time you'll approach it from a different route, offering new perspectives.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a roadside dhaba or carry packed food.

Evening: Return to Delhi

After exploring the bird sanctuary, continue your journey to Delhi (approximately 1 hour). Depending on your onward travel plans, you can either check into a hotel in Delhi or head directly to the airport.

Departure: Transfer to Indira Gandhi International Airport for your onward journey or extend your stay in Delhi if desired.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: The best time to visit Haryana with pleasant weather
  • November to February: Peak tourist season with cool temperatures
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional rainfall, less crowded
  • Avoid summer months (April to June) as temperatures can exceed 45°C (113°F)

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest major airport is in Delhi; Hisar has a small domestic airport
  • Train: Good rail connectivity with major stations in Ambala, Kurukshetra, Karnal, and Panipat
  • Road: Excellent highway network connecting all major cities
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes for summer; warm clothes for winter
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for exploring historical sites
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, lip balm
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, hand sanitizer
  • Others: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is the primary language. English is understood in urban areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are widely available in cities and towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; may be limited in rural areas
  • Safety: Haryana is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing is recommended, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Festivals: Teej, Lohri, Baisakhi, and Gugga Naumi are major festivals
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
