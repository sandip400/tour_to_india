Odisha: The Soul of Incredible India

Odisha (formerly Orissa) offers a perfect blend of ancient temples, pristine beaches, serene lakes, vibrant tribal culture, and rich wildlife. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you on a complete journey through this eastern Indian state, covering both popular destinations and hidden gems.

Day 1

Arrival in Bhubaneswar & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar. Transfer to your hotel in the city center. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Mayfair Lagoon or Trident Bhubaneswar for luxury stays; Ginger Bhubaneswar or Empires Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Ekamra Haat

Enjoy your first Odia meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Dalma' or 'Odisha Hotel'. Visit Ekamra Haat, a handicraft market where you can explore local arts and crafts of Odisha.

Lunch recommendation: Try Dalma (lentil and vegetable curry), Pakhala (fermented rice) or Machha Besara (fish curry) with steamed rice.

Evening: Lingaraj Temple & Bindu Sagar

Visit the magnificent Lingaraj Temple (from outside, as non-Hindus are not allowed inside) dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the oldest temples in Bhubaneswar. Continue to Bindu Sagar, a sacred lake surrounded by numerous temples.

Evening activity: Take a leisurely walk around the temple area to admire the architecture and spiritual ambiance. Non-Hindus can view the temple from a designated viewing platform.

Day 1 Tips
  • Bhubaneswar is known as the "Temple City of India" with over 700 temples.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Photography may be restricted at certain religious sites.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
Day 2

Bhubaneswar Temple Tour & Caves

Morning: Temple Circuit

Embark on a temple tour of Bhubaneswar. Visit the exquisite Mukteshwar Temple known for its ornate gateway, the Rajarani Temple famous for its sculptural excellence, and the Brahmeswara Temple with its intricate carvings.

Activity: Consider hiring a local guide who can explain the historical and architectural significance of these ancient temples.

Afternoon: Udayagiri & Khandagiri Caves

After lunch, visit the Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves, ancient Jain rock-cut shelters dating back to the 1st century BCE. Explore the intricate carvings and inscriptions that depict Jain mythology and royal scenes.

Note: Wear comfortable shoes as there will be some climbing involved to reach the caves.

Evening: Odisha State Museum & Dinner

Visit the Odisha State Museum to learn about the rich cultural heritage of the state through its collection of sculptures, paintings, manuscripts, and tribal artifacts. End your day with dinner at a local restaurant.

Dinner: Try Odia thali at Dalma Restaurant or enjoy seafood at Mayfair Kitchen.

Day 3

Bhubaneswar to Puri

Morning: Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Visit Dhauli Hill, the site of the historic Kalinga War where Emperor Ashoka embraced Buddhism. Explore the Peace Pagoda (Shanti Stupa) built in collaboration with Japan and the ancient rock edicts of Ashoka.

Activity: Take in the panoramic views of the Daya River and surrounding landscapes from the hilltop.

Afternoon: Drive to Puri

After lunch, drive to Puri (approximately 1.5 hours, 60 km), one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Mayfair Heritage or Toshali Sands for luxury; Hotel Holiday Resort or Sterling Puri for mid-range options.

Evening: Puri Beach & Market

Visit the golden beaches of Puri to enjoy the sunset. Take a leisurely walk along the beach and watch local fishermen with their traditional boats. Explore the local markets for souvenirs, especially the famous Puri paintings and seashell crafts.

Dinner: Try seafood at a beachside restaurant like Wildgrass or Chung Wah.

Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Puri Beach

About Puri

Puri is one of the oldest cities in eastern India and a major pilgrimage destination. It is famous for the Jagannath Temple, dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Vishnu. The city hosts the famous Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) annually, which attracts millions of devotees from around the world.

Besides its religious significance, Puri is known for its beautiful beaches, traditional arts and crafts, and delicious seafood cuisine.

Day 4

Puri & Konark

Morning: Jagannath Temple & Puri Heritage Walk

Take a heritage walk around the Jagannath Temple complex (non-Hindus can view from outside). Learn about the temple's history, architecture, and cultural significance. Explore the narrow lanes of the old town with its many maths (monasteries) and smaller temples.

Activity: Visit the Raghunandan Library for a view of the temple from its roof.

Afternoon: Konark Sun Temple

Drive to Konark (approximately 35 km from Puri) to visit the magnificent Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This 13th-century temple is designed in the form of a colossal chariot with intricately carved stone wheels, pillars, and walls.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Konark like Yatri Nivas or pack a lunch from Puri.

Evening: Chandrabhaga Beach & Return to Puri

Visit Chandrabhaga Beach near Konark, known for its clean shores and beautiful sunset views. Return to Puri for dinner and overnight stay.

Dinner: Try the local delicacy Khaja (a sweet pastry) and Chhena Poda (cheese dessert) at a local sweet shop in Puri.

Jagannath Temple

Konark Sun Temple

Historical Significance

The Konark Sun Temple was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. It is designed as a massive chariot for the sun god Surya, with 12 pairs of elaborately carved wheels pulled by seven horses. The temple is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture and showcases the artistic excellence of ancient Odisha.

The temple's name is derived from two Sanskrit words: 'Kona' (corner) and 'Arka' (sun), as it was built to worship the Sun God. Despite its partial ruins, the temple remains one of India's most stunning architectural marvels.

Day 5

Puri to Chilika Lake

Morning: Raghurajpur Crafts Village

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and drive to Raghurajpur, a heritage crafts village famous for Pattachitra paintings (traditional cloth-based scroll paintings) and other handicrafts. Meet local artisans and watch them create intricate artwork.

Activity: Participate in a short Pattachitra painting workshop if available.

Afternoon: Drive to Chilika Lake

Continue your journey to Chilika Lake (approximately 2 hours from Raghurajpur), Asia's largest brackish water lagoon and a wetland of international importance. Check into your accommodation at Satapada or Barkul.

Accommodation: Panthanivas Satapada or Chilika Dhaba for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Sunset Boat Ride

Take a boat ride on Chilika Lake to enjoy the beautiful sunset views. Look for Irrawaddy dolphins, which are a special attraction of the lake. The lake is also home to numerous migratory birds during winter months.

Dinner: Enjoy fresh seafood at a local restaurant near the lake or at your accommodation.

Raghurajpur Crafts Village

Chilika Lake

About Chilika Lake

Chilika Lake is Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, spreading across the districts of Puri, Khurda, and Ganjam. It is the largest wintering ground for migratory birds on the Indian subcontinent, hosting over 160 species of birds during the winter months.

The lake is also home to the rare Irrawaddy dolphins, which can be spotted during boat rides. The Nalabana Bird Sanctuary within the lake is a designated Ramsar site (wetland of international importance).

Day 6

Chilika Lake to Gopalpur

Morning: Bird Watching & Dolphin Spotting

Take an early morning boat ride to Nalabana Bird Sanctuary within Chilika Lake for bird watching. During winter months (November to February), the lake hosts millions of migratory birds from as far as Siberia and the Caspian Sea. Continue to the outer channel for dolphin spotting.

Activity: Bring binoculars for better wildlife viewing and a camera with zoom lens for photography.

Afternoon: Drive to Gopalpur

After lunch, check out from your accommodation and drive to Gopalpur-on-Sea (approximately 2.5 hours, 100 km), a serene beach town on the Bay of Bengal. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Mayfair Palm Beach Resort or Swosti Palm Resort for luxury; Hotel Moti or Sea Pearl for mid-range options.

Evening: Gopalpur Beach

Spend the evening at Gopalpur Beach, known for its tranquil atmosphere and golden sands. Watch the sunset over the Bay of Bengal and enjoy the cool sea breeze. The beach is less crowded compared to Puri, offering a more peaceful experience.

Dinner offering a more peaceful experience.

Dinner: Enjoy seafood at a beachside restaurant or at your hotel.

Migratory Birds at Chilika

Gopalpur Beach

About Gopalpur

Gopalpur was once a bustling seaport trading with Southeast Asian countries like Burma (Myanmar), Java, and Sumatra. Today, it's a quiet beach town known for its pristine beaches and colonial charm. The old lighthouse, built during British rule, is a notable landmark.

The town has a laid-back atmosphere, making it perfect for those seeking relaxation away from crowded tourist destinations. The beach is known for its gentle waves, though swimming should be done with caution as the currents can be strong at times.

Day 7

Gopalpur to Rayagada

Morning: Sunrise at Gopalpur Beach

Wake up early to witness the beautiful sunrise over the Bay of Bengal. Take a morning walk along the beach and visit the old lighthouse if time permits.

Activity: Collect seashells as souvenirs or simply enjoy the peaceful morning atmosphere.

Afternoon: Drive to Rayagada

After breakfast and check-out, begin your journey to Rayagada (approximately 5-6 hours, 250 km), a district known for its tribal culture and natural beauty. The drive takes you through scenic landscapes of Eastern Ghats. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Hotel Sai International or Hotel Raj Residency for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Local Market Exploration

Explore the local markets of Rayagada to get a glimpse of tribal life and culture. The markets are good places to see tribal people in their traditional attire and to purchase authentic tribal handicrafts.

Dinner: Try local cuisine at your hotel or at a nearby restaurant.

Sunrise at Gopalpur Beach

Rayagada Landscape

About Rayagada

Rayagada is a district in southern Odisha known for its diverse tribal population, including the Kondh, Soura, and Dongria Kondh tribes. The region is characterized by hills, forests, and valleys, offering a glimpse into the traditional tribal way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries.

The district is also known for its natural beauty, with waterfalls, rivers, and lush green landscapes. It's an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers authentic cultural experiences away from mainstream tourism.

Tribal Area Advisory

When visiting tribal areas, please be respectful of local customs and traditions. Always ask permission before taking photographs of tribal people. It's advisable to hire a local guide who understands the tribal culture and can facilitate interactions.

Day 8

Rayagada Tribal Tour

Morning: Chatikona Market

Visit Chatikona weekly market (if your visit coincides with market day, usually Wednesday), where Dongria Kondh tribals come down from the hills to sell their produce and handicrafts. This is one of the best places to observe tribal life and culture.

Activity: Look for traditional tribal jewelry, especially the distinctive Dongria Kondh necklaces and earrings.

Afternoon: Tribal Village Visit

With a local guide, visit a nearby tribal village to learn about their traditional lifestyle, housing, agricultural practices, and crafts. Interact with the villagers and gain insights into their unique culture and customs.

Note: Always respect tribal customs and privacy. Ask permission before taking photographs and follow your guide's instructions.

Evening: Minajhola Waterfall

Visit the scenic Minajhola waterfall, located amidst lush green forests. The area is known for its natural beauty and is a perfect place to relax after a day of cultural exploration.

Dinner: Return to Rayagada for dinner at your hotel or a local restaurant.

Chatikona Tribal Market

Tribal Village

Tribal Culture

Odisha is home to 62 different tribal communities, each with their own distinct culture, language, and traditions. The Dongria Kondh tribe, often called the "Hill People," are known for their distinctive jewelry and colorful attire. They practice sustainable agriculture on the slopes of the Niyamgiri hills and are known for their strong connection to the land.

Tribal communities in Odisha have preserved their traditional knowledge systems, including herbal medicine, sustainable farming practices, and handicrafts. Their art forms, including pithora paintings and metal crafts, reflect their deep connection with nature and spiritual beliefs.

Day 9

Rayagada to Daringbadi

Morning: Drive to Daringbadi

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Daringbadi (approximately 5-6 hours, 200 km), often called the "Kashmir of Odisha" due to its cool climate and beautiful landscapes. The drive takes you through scenic hills and forests.

En route: Stop at small villages and viewpoints along the way to enjoy the natural beauty of the Eastern Ghats.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive in Daringbadi and check into your accommodation. Take some time to rest and acclimatize to the cooler temperatures of this hill station, which sits at an altitude of about 3,000 feet.

Accommodation: Hill View Resort or Daringbadi Nature Camp for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Pine Forest & Sunset Point

Visit the pine forest near Daringbadi, a peaceful spot for a leisurely walk amidst tall pine trees. Continue to a nearby sunset point to enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys as the sun sets.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your accommodation, which typically serves simple but delicious local cuisine.

Daringbadi Landscape

Pine Forest

About Daringbadi

Daringbadi is a hill station in the Kandhamal district of Odisha, known for its cool climate, coffee plantations, pine forests, and waterfalls. It experiences occasional snowfall during winter months, earning it the nickname "Kashmir of Odisha."

The region is home to Kandha and Kutia Kondh tribal communities and offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. The area is also known for its organic turmeric, honey, and coffee production.

Weather Advisory

Daringbadi has a cooler climate compared to other parts of Odisha. Temperatures can drop significantly in the evenings, especially during winter months (November to February). Carry warm clothing, especially if visiting during winter.

Day 10

Daringbadi to Cuttack

Morning: Daringbadi Waterfalls

Visit the beautiful waterfalls around Daringbadi, including Putudi Waterfall and Dasingbadi Waterfall. Take a refreshing walk through coffee plantations and spice gardens that dot the landscape.

Activity: Learn about organic coffee cultivation and processing from local farmers.

Afternoon: Drive to Cuttack

After lunch, check out from your accommodation and begin your journey to Cuttack (approximately 6-7 hours, 250 km), one of the oldest cities in Odisha and known for its silver filigree work. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: OYO Townhouse or Hotel Manorama for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Cuttack Local Food

Explore the local food scene of Cuttack, known for its street food and sweets. Visit Chhatra Bazar, one of the oldest and largest markets in the city, to sample local delicacies.

Dinner: Try Dahibara Aludam (a popular street food) and Chhena Poda (a cheese-based dessert) at local eateries.

Putudi Waterfall

Cuttack City

About Cuttack

Cuttack, founded in 989 AD, is one of the oldest cities in India and was the capital of Odisha for almost nine centuries before Bhubaneswar. The city is known as the "Silver City" due to its famous silver filigree work, a delicate form of jewelry making.

The city is situated at the confluence of the Mahanadi and Kathajodi rivers, forming a delta area known as the "Millennium City." Cuttack is also known for its vibrant Durga Puja celebrations and the historic Barabati Fort.

Day 11

Cuttack to Bhitarkanika

Morning: Silver Filigree Workshop

Visit a traditional silver filigree (Tarakasi) workshop in Cuttack to see artisans creating intricate jewelry and decorative items. This ancient craft involves twisting thin silver wires into delicate patterns.

Activity: Purchase authentic silver filigree items directly from artisans as souvenirs.

Afternoon: Drive to Bhitarkanika

After lunch, check out from your hotel and drive to Bhitarkanika National Park (approximately 4-5 hours, 150 km), one of India's most impressive wildlife sanctuaries and a Ramsar wetland of international importance. Check into your accommodation near the park.

Accommodation: Dangmal Forest Rest House or nearby eco-resorts for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Nature Walk

Take a guided nature walk around your accommodation to get acquainted with the mangrove ecosystem. Learn about the unique biodiversity of Bhitarkanika, which is home to saltwater crocodiles, various bird species, and diverse flora.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple dinner at your accommodation, typically featuring local seafood dishes.

Silver Filigree Work

Bhitarkanika Mangroves

About Bhitarkanika

Bhitarkanika National Park is the second largest mangrove ecosystem in India after the Sundarbans. It's home to the endangered saltwater crocodile, with some specimens reaching over 20 feet in length, making them among the largest reptiles in the world.

The park is also known for its rich avian fauna, with over 215 species of birds, including many migratory birds that visit during winter. The dense mangrove forests, crisscrossed by numerous creeks and mudflats, create a unique ecosystem that supports diverse wildlife.

Eco-Tourism

Bhitarkanika promotes responsible eco-tourism. Follow all park rules, avoid plastic usage, and respect wildlife by maintaining a safe distance and not disturbing their natural behavior.

Day 12

Bhitarkanika to Bhubaneswar & Departure

Morning: Boat Safari in Bhitarkanika

Take an early morning boat safari through the creeks and rivers of Bhitarkanika. Look for saltwater crocodiles basking on the banks, monitor lizards, and various bird species in the mangrove forests.

Activity: Visit Dangmal Crocodile Research Centre to learn about conservation efforts for the saltwater crocodile.

Afternoon: Return to Bhubaneswar

After lunch, check out from your accommodation and begin your journey back to Bhubaneswar (approximately 4-5 hours, 150 km). Depending on your departure time, you may check into a hotel for a short rest or head directly to the airport.

En route: Reflect on the diverse experiences of your Odisha journey, from ancient temples to pristine beaches, tribal culture to wildlife.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Bhubaneswar for souvenirs like Pattachitra paintings, Sambalpuri textiles, or silver filigree work.

Departure: Transfer to Biju Patnaik International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Winter months with pleasant temperatures ideal for sightseeing
  • July to September: Monsoon season with occasional heavy rainfall
  • Avoid April to June: Summer months with high temperatures and humidity
  • For bird watching at Chilika: November to February
  • For wildlife at Bhitarkanika: December to February
  • For Rath Yatra in Puri: June/July (dates vary as per Hindu calendar)

Transportation

  • Air: Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar has good connectivity
  • Train: Bhubaneswar, Puri, and Cuttack are well-connected by railways
  • Road: Good highway network connecting major destinations
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs in cities
  • Remote areas: Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience
  • Boat: Required for exploring Chilika Lake and Bhitarkanika

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes, modest attire for temples
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits
  • Beach essentials: Swimwear, sunscreen, hat, sunglasses
  • For wildlife areas: Binoculars, zoom lens camera, insect repellent
  • For tribal areas: Modest clothing, small gifts for children
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, water purification tablets
  • Other: Power bank, universal adapter, water bottle
