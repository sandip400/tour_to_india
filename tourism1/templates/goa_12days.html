Goa: India's Coastal Paradise

Goa offers a perfect blend of pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, rich Portuguese heritage, and lush landscapes. From the bustling markets of Panjim to the serene shores of South Goa, this 12-day itinerary covers the best of what this beautiful coastal state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Panjim & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Dabolim International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in Panjim, Goa's capital city. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Vivanta, Goa Marriott Resort, or The Crown for luxury stays; Hospedaria Abrigo De Botelho or Panjim Inn for heritage options.

Afternoon: Lunch & City Walk

Enjoy your first Goan meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Viva Panjim' or 'Ritz Classic'. Take a leisurely walk along the Mandovi River promenade and visit the iconic Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Church with its distinctive white faÃ§ade and symmetrical staircases.

Lunch recommendation: Try Goan classics like Fish Curry Rice, Prawn BalchÃ£o, or Pork Vindaloo.

Evening: Sunset Cruise

Take a sunset cruise on the Mandovi River. These popular cruises feature Goan folk music, dance performances, and beautiful views of Panjim's waterfront. After the cruise, enjoy dinner at one of the restaurants along the riverfront.

Evening activity: If interested, you can visit one of Goa's famous floating casinos on the Mandovi River for some entertainment.

Day 2

Old Goa Heritage Tour

Morning: Basilica of Bom Jesus

After breakfast, head to Old Goa, the former capital of Portuguese India. Start with the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Basilica of Bom Jesus, which contains the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier. Explore this 16th-century masterpiece of baroque architecture.

Activity: Attend a morning mass if you're interested in experiencing the spiritual atmosphere.

Afternoon: Old Goa Churches

Continue your exploration with a visit to SÃ© Cathedral, the largest church in Asia, and the Church of St. Francis of Assisi. Also visit the Archaeological Museum to learn about Goa's rich history.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local Goan restaurant near Old Goa like 'Mum's Kitchen' which serves authentic Goan cuisine.

Evening: Dona Paula Viewpoint

Visit the Dona Paula Viewpoint, known for its breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and the popular legend surrounding it. Enjoy a relaxed evening by the jetty and watch the sunset. Return to Panjim for dinner at a local seafood restaurant.

Dinner: Try authentic Goan seafood at 'Ritz Classic' or 'Mum's Kitchen' in Panjim.

Day 3

North Goa Beach Hopping

Morning: Calangute & Baga Beaches

After an early breakfast, head to North Goa's popular beaches. Start with Calangute, known as the 'Queen of Beaches', followed by its neighbor Baga Beach. Enjoy sunbathing, swimming, or simply relaxing by the shore.

Activity: Try some water sports like parasailing, jet skiing, or banana boat rides if you're interested.

Afternoon: Anjuna Beach & Flea Market

Head to Anjuna Beach, famous for its laid-back hippie culture and vibrant flea market (on Wednesdays). Explore the market for souvenirs, handicrafts, clothing, and accessories. Enjoy lunch at one of the beach shacks serving fresh seafood.

Lunch recommendation: Try a beach shack like 'Curlies' or 'Shiva Valley' for fresh seafood and amazing views.

Evening: Vagator & Chapora Fort

Visit Vagator Beach with its red cliffs and then climb up to Chapora Fort for stunning sunset views over the Arabian Sea. This fort gained popularity after being featured in the Bollywood film 'Dil Chahta Hai'.

Evening activity: If you enjoy nightlife, head to Tito's Lane in Baga for dinner and drinks, or for a more laid-back evening, enjoy a beachside dinner at Vagator.

Baga Beach

Chapora Fort

Important Information
  • Apply sunscreen liberally and stay hydrated while beach hopping.
  • Bargaining is expected at the Anjuna Flea Market - start at around 50% of the quoted price.
  • Water sports operators might not have proper safety equipment; ensure safety measures before participating.
  • Chapora Fort doesn't have proper lighting, so plan to leave before it gets dark.
  • Renting a scooter or motorcycle is the best way to explore the beaches (international driving permit required).
Day 4

Fontainhas & Panjim Exploration

Morning: Fontainhas Walking Tour

Explore Fontainhas, the Latin Quarter of Panjim and Goa's oldest residential district. Take a guided walking tour through its narrow streets lined with Portuguese-style colorful houses, art galleries, bakeries, and cafes.

Activity: Visit the Gitanjali Art Gallery, St. Sebastian Chapel, and try some Portuguese pastries at a local bakery.

Afternoon: Panjim Market & Lunch

Visit the Panjim Municipal Market to see the variety of spices, fresh produce, and seafood. Enjoy a authentic Goan lunch at a local restaurant. Visit the Goa State Museum to learn more about Goan history and culture.

Lunch: Try 'Hotel Venite', a charming old-world restaurant known for its authentic Goan cuisine and unique decor.

Evening: Shopping & Leisure

Spend the evening shopping for souvenirs at the local handicraft shops and boutiques in Panjim. Visit 18th June Road, Goa's premier shopping street. Enjoy dinner at one of Panjim's fine-dining restaurants.

Dinner recommendation: Try 'The Black Sheep Bistro' or 'Viva Panjim' for an exquisite dining experience featuring contemporary and traditional Goan cuisine.

About Fontainhas

Fontainhas was founded in the late 18th century and is famous for its colorful Portuguese-style buildings with red-tiled roofs, balconies with wrought iron railings, and narrow winding streets. It's one of the best-preserved Portuguese quarters in Asia and offers a unique glimpse into Goa's colonial past.

Day 5

Spice Plantation & Mapusa Market

Morning: Spice Plantation Tour

Take a day trip to one of Goa's famous spice plantations like Sahakari Spice Farm or Tropical Spice Plantation. Enjoy a guided tour learning about various spices, their cultivation, and medicinal properties. See vanilla, pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and other spices growing in their natural environment.

Experience: Participate in an elephant bathing session or take an elephant ride if offered. Enjoy a traditional Goan lunch served on a banana leaf at the plantation.

Afternoon: Mapusa Market

Head to Mapusa to visit its famous Friday Market (if your trip aligns). Even on other days, the market offers a glimpse into local Goan life with its variety of spices, clothing, household items, and fresh produce.

Shopping: Buy traditional Goan souvenirs like spices, cashew nuts, feni (local alcoholic beverage), handwoven mats, or Goan sausages.

Evening: Aguada Fort

Visit the 17th-century Fort Aguada built by the Portuguese as a defense against the Dutch and Marathas. Enjoy panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the sunset. The fort also houses a lighthouse, which is the oldest of its kind in Asia.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at one of the seafood restaurants near Candolim or Sinquerim Beach before returning to your hotel.

Day 6

Dudhsagar Falls Excursion

Early Morning: Departure

Early morning departure for Dudhsagar Falls, one of India's tallest waterfalls at 310 meters. The journey takes approximately 2-2.5 hours from Panjim. You'll need to join a jeep safari from Kulem to reach the falls, as private vehicles are not allowed beyond a certain point.

Note: It's best to book a guided tour in advance through your hotel or a reputable travel agency, as it includes transportation, jeep safari, lunch, and an experienced guide.

Mid-Morning to Afternoon: Dudhsagar Falls

Enjoy the thrilling jeep ride through streams and the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary to reach the falls. Spend time at Dudhsagar Falls, which means 'Sea of Milk' due to its white appearance. Take a refreshing dip in the natural pool at the base of the falls (if permitted) and enjoy the lush surroundings.

Lunch: Most tours include a packed lunch or a meal at a local restaurant on the way back.

Evening: Return & Relaxation

Return to your hotel by evening. Take some time to rest and relax after the adventurous day. Enjoy a leisurely dinner at your hotel or a nearby restaurant.

Evening activity: If you're not too tired, you can enjoy a casual stroll along Miramar Beach, which is close to Panjim.

Dudhsagar Falls

Important Information
  • Dudhsagar Falls is closed during the monsoon season (usually June to September) due to safety concerns.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and footwear suitable for trekking and getting wet.
  • Carry a change of clothes, towel, sunscreen, insect repellent, and water bottle.
  • Keep your valuables in a waterproof bag, as the jeep ride can be quite wet and bumpy.
  • Swimming may not be allowed if the current is strong - always follow guide instructions.
About Dudhsagar Falls

Dudhsagar Falls is located on the Mandovi River in the Western Ghats. The name "Dudhsagar" means "sea of milk" which comes from the white spray and foam that the cascading water creates. The falls are at their most spectacular during the monsoon season, though access is restricted. They are also visible from the railway track that runs across the falls, part of the Goa-Karnataka railway line.

Day 7

South Goa Relocation

Morning: Checkout & Breakfast

Check out from your North Goa hotel after breakfast. Today we'll be shifting to South Goa to experience its more serene and less crowded beaches. South Goa offers a different vibe compared to the more commercial North Goa.

Tip: Pack a small bag with essentials for a short stop at a beach before check-in at your South Goa hotel.

Afternoon: Majorda Beach & Lunch

On your way to South Goa, stop at Majorda Beach, known for its fine white sand and clear waters. This is also a beach where the Portuguese reportedly first learned the art of baking European breads in India. Enjoy lunch at a beach shack or at the famous Martin's Corner in Betalbatim.

Lunch recommendation: Martin's Corner is famous for its Goan and Portuguese cuisine, especially the seafood dishes like pomfret recheado and prawn curry rice.

Evening: Check-in & Relaxation

Check into your South Goa hotel in areas like Benaulim, Colva, or Cavelossim. These areas offer a range of accommodations from luxury resorts to boutique hotels. Spend the evening relaxing at your hotel or taking a leisurely stroll on the beach.

Accommodation options: The Leela Goa, Park Hyatt Goa Resort, Taj Exotica for luxury stays; Acacia Hotel & Spa, Beleza By The Beach, or Radisson Blu for mid-range options.

Day 8

Colva & Benaulim Beach Day

Morning: Colva Beach

After breakfast, head to Colva Beach, one of the largest and oldest beaches of South Goa. The beach is known for its white sand and clear blue waters. Spend the morning relaxing, swimming, or taking a leisurely walk along the shoreline.

Activity: If interested, you can take a boat ride to spot dolphins early in the morning, as they're commonly sighted in these waters.

Afternoon: Our Lady of Mercy Church & Benaulim

Visit the Our Lady of Mercy Church, also known as Colva Church, which was established in 1630 and houses the miraculous statue of Menino Jesus (Baby Jesus). After the church visit, head to Benaulim Beach, a fishing village and beach known for its laid-back atmosphere and less crowded shores.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local beach shack in Benaulim, trying the day's fresh catch prepared in Goan style.

Evening: Local Crafts & Dinner

Explore the local handicraft shops in Benaulim. The village is known for its skilled craftsmen who create traditional Goan handicrafts like shell work, pottery, and crochet items. Enjoy a relaxed dinner at a beach restaurant watching the sunset.

Dinner recommendation: The Fisherman's Wharf or Edwin's Bar & Restaurant offer excellent seafood and Goan specialties.

Benaulim's Fishing Heritage

Benaulim is one of Goa's oldest fishing villages with a rich cultural heritage. According to local legend, the village was formed when an arrow (Ban) shot by Lord Parashurama fell at this place, hence the name Banahalli, which later became Benaulim. The village maintains its traditional fishing culture to this day, and you can often see fishermen bringing in their catch in the early morning hours.

Day 9

Palolem & Agonda Beach Excursion

Morning: Drive to Palolem

After breakfast, drive to Palolem Beach in Canacona, about 40 km from Colva. Palolem is a curved bay of white sand with a calm sea, perfect for swimming. It's often called 'Paradise Beach' and is one of the most beautiful beaches in Goa.

Activity: Take a boat trip to Butterfly Beach or Honeymoon Beach, small secluded beaches accessible only by boat from Palolem.

Afternoon: Lunch & Beach Relaxation

Enjoy lunch at one of the many beach shacks in Palolem. The beach is lined with restaurants serving a variety of cuisines, but seafood is the specialty here. Spend some time relaxing on the beach, swimming in the calm waters, or kayaking around the bay.

Lunch recommendation: Magic Italy, Dropadi Inn, or CafÃ© Inn are popular choices for a beachside meal.

Evening: Agonda Beach Sunset

Head to nearby Agonda Beach, a quiet and picturesque 3 km stretch of beach known for its pristine shores and peaceful ambiance. It's the perfect place to watch the sunset. Agonda is also a nesting site for Olive Ridley turtles.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at one of the beachside restaurants in Agonda before driving back to your hotel. The White by Agonda or Agonda Sunset are good options for dinner with a view.

Day 10

Netravali & Tambdi Surla Temple

Morning: Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary

After breakfast, head to Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary, a biodiversity hotspot in the Western Ghats. Visit the Bubbling Lake (Zambaulim), a rare natural phenomenon where small bubbles constantly rise to the water's surface due to underground gases.

Activity: Explore the Savari Waterfall near Netravali, which offers a refreshing retreat into nature. A short trek through the forest will lead you to this beautiful waterfall.

Afternoon: Tambdi Surla Temple

Drive to Tambdi Surla Temple, a 12th-century Mahadeva Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is the oldest temple in Goa and the only Kadamba-period temple that survived the Portuguese destruction. Admire its intricate stone carvings and peaceful surroundings in the midst of a thick forest.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local restaurant in the vicinity.

Evening: Wellness & Relaxation

Return to your hotel in the evening. Treat yourself to a rejuvenating spa session at your hotel spa or a nearby wellness center. South Goa is known for its excellent ayurvedic and spa treatments.

Dinner: Enjoy a relaxed dinner at your hotel's restaurant or a nearby beachside restaurant.

Tambdi Surla Temple

About Tambdi Surla

The Tambdi Surla Temple is a small 12th-century Shaivite temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built from basalt and featuring intricate carvings, it's remarkably well-preserved because of its remote location in the forest. The temple escaped destruction during the Portuguese era when many Hindu temples were demolished. Its architecture represents the Kadamba-Yadava style with influence from the Chalukyan temple architecture.

Day 11

Water Sports & Leisure Day

Morning: Water Sports

Head to Bogmalo Beach or Hollant Beach, known for their water sports facilities. Try your hand at various water activities like jet skiing, banana boat rides, parasailing, windsurfing, or scuba diving. For scuba diving, consider the Grand Island trip, which offers the best diving spots in Goa.

Note: Always choose operators who provide proper safety equipment and briefings. Many resorts and hotels can arrange these activities for you.

Afternoon: Beach Relaxation & Lunch

After the adrenaline rush of water sports, relax on the beach and enjoy the sun. Have lunch at a beachside shack, indulging in fresh seafood and refreshing beverages.

Lunch recommendation: Try a traditional Goan fish thali, which typically includes fish curry, fried fish, rice, vegetables, and sol kadi (a digestive drink).

Evening: Farewell Dinner

As this is your last evening in Goa, enjoy a special farewell dinner at a high-end restaurant or beach shack of your choice. Consider places like Fisherman's Wharf, Martin's Corner, or Zeebop by the Sea for an authentic Goan dining experience.

Evening activity: If you're up for it, enjoy a quiet sunset walk on the beach or check out a local beach party if available.

Day 12

Departure Day

Morning: Leisure Breakfast

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel. Depending on your departure time, you might have a few hours to relax by the pool or take one last stroll on the beach.

Checkout: Most hotels have a checkout time of 11:00 AM or 12:00 PM, but they can usually store your luggage if your flight is later in the day.

Afternoon: Last-Minute Shopping

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping for souvenirs. You can pick up items like Goan spices, cashew nuts, feni, handicrafts, or beachwear from local markets or shops near your hotel.

Shopping suggestions: Spices from Goa, Cashew nuts, Goan feni (a local liquor made from cashew or coconut), handmade shell jewelry, and colorful sarongs.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Dabolim International Airport for your departure flight. It's advisable to reach the airport at least 2-3 hours before an international flight and 1-2 hours before a domestic flight.

Note: Traffic in Goa can be unpredictable, especially during the peak tourist season, so allow extra time for your journey to the airport.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Peak Season: October to March (winter months)
  • Monsoon Season: June to September (many attractions closed)
  • Shoulder Season: April-May (hot but less crowded)
  • For beach activities and water sports, November to February is ideal
  • Christmas and New Year period is festive but extremely crowded

Transportation

  • Air: Dabolim International Airport is well-connected to major Indian cities and some international destinations
  • Train: Madgaon and Thivim are the main railway stations
  • Local Transport: Rent a scooter/motorcycle (most popular), taxis, auto-rickshaws, or buses
  • Consider hiring a driver for the day for convenience
  • App-based taxi services like Goa Miles are available in many areas

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light, breathable fabrics; swimwear; light jacket for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable sandals, flip-flops, and walking shoes
  • Protection: High SPF sunscreen, sunglasses, hat, insect repellent
  • Medications: Personal medications, basic first aid kit including motion sickness pills
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Water gear: Waterproof phone case, dry bag for boat trips

Food & Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes
  • Fish Curry Rice (Goan staple)
  • Vindaloo (spicy curry dish)
  • Xacuti (coconut-based curry)
  • Sorpotel (spicy meat dish)
  • Bebinca (layered dessert)
  • Feni (cashew/coconut liquor)
Dining Tips
  • Beach shacks offer fresh seafood
  • Try Kings Beer, Goa's local brew
  • Sunday lunch at Goan homes is special
  • Sol Kadi is a great digestive drink
  • Seafood is freshest in morning catches
  • Try the Portuguese-influenced bakeries

Practical Information

  • Language: Konkani is the official language. English, Hindi, and Portuguese are widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in most tourist areas, but carry cash for beach shacks and small shops
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in tourist areas; many hotels, restaurants, and cafes offer free Wi-Fi
  • Safety: Goa is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions especially at night and isolated beaches
  • Bargaining: Expected at markets and with taxi drivers without meters; not applicable in formal shops
  • Water: Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Dress Code: Beachwear is fine for beaches, but dress modestly when visiting churches or temples
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
