Tamil Nadu: A Cultural Treasure

Tamil Nadu offers a perfect blend of ancient temples, colonial heritage, and stunning natural landscapes. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the best of Tamil Nadu and neighboring regions, from bustling cities to serene hill stations, ancient temples to wildlife sanctuaries.

Day 1

Arrival in Chennai

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chennai International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Chennai. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Leela Palace or ITC Grand Chola for luxury stays; Taj Coromandel or Trident Chennai for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Marina Beach

Visit Marina Beach, the second-longest urban beach in the world. Take a leisurely stroll along the promenade and enjoy the sea breeze. Visit the statues and monuments along the beach.

Lunch recommendation: Try South Indian classics like Idli, Dosa, Sambar, and Filter Coffee at Saravana Bhavan or Murugan Idli Shop.

Evening: Orientation Walk

Take an orientation walk around your hotel area. If staying near Mount Road (Anna Salai) or T. Nagar, explore the shopping districts. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Evening activity: If available, catch a classical Carnatic music or Bharatanatyam dance performance at Kalakshetra or Music Academy.

Chennai International Airport

Marina Beach

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Marina Beach is best visited in the evening when it's cooler and livelier.
  • Adjust to the local time zone to minimize jet lag.
Day 2

Chennai City Exploration

Morning: Mylapore Temple District

Visit the iconic Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Mylapore, a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture. Explore the surrounding area with its traditional markets and streets. Visit the San Thome Basilica, built over the tomb of St. Thomas the Apostle.

Activity: Take a guided walking tour of Mylapore to understand the local culture and traditions.

Afternoon: Colonial Heritage

Visit Fort St. George, the first English fortress in India, now housing the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly and a museum. Continue to the Government Museum and National Art Gallery in Egmore, which houses one of the finest collections of bronze sculptures.

Lunch: Try a traditional South Indian meal served on banana leaf at Annalakshmi or Dakshin.

Evening: Shopping & Dinner

Explore the bustling markets of T. Nagar, especially Pondy Bazaar and Ranganathan Street, known for textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts. Enjoy dinner at a contemporary South Indian restaurant.

Dinner recommendation: Try modern South Indian cuisine at Southern Spice (Taj Coromandel) or Dakshin (Crowne Plaza).

Day 3

Chennai to Mahabalipuram

Morning: Cholamandal Artists' Village

Visit the Cholamandal Artists' Village, one of the largest artists' communes in India. Explore the galleries showcasing contemporary art, sculptures, and paintings by local artists.

Activity: Interact with resident artists and learn about their creative process.

Afternoon: Drive to Mahabalipuram

Drive to Mahabalipuram (also known as Mamallapuram), approximately 60 km south of Chennai along the scenic East Coast Road. Stop at Muttukadu Boat House for a boat ride in the backwaters if time permits.

Accommodation: Radisson Blu Resort Temple Bay or InterContinental Chennai Mahabalipuram Resort for luxury; Ideal Beach Resort or Chariot Beach Resort for mid-range options.

Evening: Beach Sunset & Dinner

Relax at Mahabalipuram Beach and watch the sunset. Enjoy a seafood dinner at one of the beachside restaurants.

Dinner: Try fresh seafood preparations like prawn curry, fish fry, and crab masala at the local restaurants like Moonrakers or The Wharf.

Cholamandal Artists' Village

Important Information
  • The East Coast Road (ECR) offers beautiful views of the Bay of Bengal.
  • There are several beach resorts along the way where you can stop for lunch.
  • Mahabalipuram is known for its stone carving industry - you'll find many shops selling sculptures.
  • The beach at Mahabalipuram is generally safe, but swimming is not recommended due to strong currents.
About Mahabalipuram

Mahabalipuram was a 7th century port city of the South Indian dynasty of the Pallavas. It has been classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is famous for its shore temples, cave sanctuaries, giant open-air rock reliefs, and the Five Rathas (monolithic temples shaped as chariots).

Day 4

Mahabalipuram to Pondicherry

Morning: UNESCO Heritage Sites

Explore the UNESCO World Heritage monuments of Mahabalipuram, including the Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance (a massive rock relief), the Five Rathas (monolithic temples shaped as chariots), and Krishna's Butter Ball (a giant balancing rock).

Note: Hire a local guide to understand the historical and mythological significance of these 7th-century monuments.

Afternoon: Drive to Pondicherry

After lunch, drive to Pondicherry (now officially called Puducherry), approximately 95 km south of Mahabalipuram. Check into your hotel in Pondicherry.

Accommodation: La Villa, Palais de Mahe, or Le Pondy for heritage and luxury stays; Hotel Atithi or The Promenade for mid-range options.

Evening: French Quarter Walk

Take a leisurely walk through the charming French Quarter (White Town) with its colonial buildings, boutiques, and cafes. Stroll along the famous Promenade Beach at sunset.

Dinner: Enjoy French or Franco-Tamil fusion cuisine at restaurants like Villa Shanti, Le Dupleix, or Carte Blanche.

Day 5

Pondicherry to Thanjavur

Morning: Auroville & Sri Aurobindo Ashram

Visit Auroville, the international community founded in 1968, and see the Matrimandir (requires advance booking for inner chamber). Continue to Sri Aurobindo Ashram, a spiritual community founded by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother.

Note: Maintain silence at the Ashram and dress modestly.

Afternoon: Drive to Thanjavur

After lunch, embark on a longer drive to Thanjavur (formerly Tanjore), approximately 170 km southwest of Pondicherry. Check into your hotel in Thanjavur by evening.

Accommodation: Svatma or Ideal River View Resort for luxury; Sangam Hotel or Hotel Parisutham for mid-range options.

Evening: Rest & Relaxation

Rest at your hotel after the long drive. Enjoy a traditional Thanjavur dinner at your hotel or a nearby restaurant.

Try: Thanjavur's special dishes like Puliyodharai (tamarind rice), Vathal Kuzhambu (spicy gravy), and Thavala Adai (rice pancakes).

Day 6

Thanjavur to Chettinad

Morning: Brihadeeswarar Temple

Visit the magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century. Marvel at the 216-feet tall vimana (tower) and the massive Nandi (bull) statue carved from a single stone.

Also visit: The Thanjavur Royal Palace and Art Gallery, which houses an impressive collection of Chola bronzes and other artifacts.

Afternoon: Thanjavur Art

Visit a workshop to see the famous Thanjavur painting technique, known for its use of gold foil and embedded precious stones. You can also see demonstrations of the traditional bronze casting process that has been practiced here for centuries.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional South Indian thali (meal served on banana leaf) at a local restaurant.

Evening: Drive to Chettinad

Drive to Chettinad region, approximately 100 km southwest of Thanjavur. Check into your heritage hotel in Karaikudi or nearby.

Accommodation: The Bangala, Chidambara Vilas, or Visalam - all heritage mansions converted into hotels.

Chettinad Mansion

About Chettinad

Chettinad is the homeland of the Nattukottai Chettiars (Nagarathars), a prosperous merchant community. The region is famous for its palatial mansions with elaborate architecture, featuring materials imported from around the world, including marble from Italy, teak from Burma, and chandeliers from Europe. Chettinad is also renowned for its spicy, aromatic cuisine and handcrafted products like Athangudi tiles and Kottan baskets.

Day 7

Chettinad to Madurai

Morning: Chettinad Mansions

Explore the magnificent Chettinad mansions in Karaikudi and surrounding villages. Visit the Chettinad Palace (Kanadukathan Palace) and other heritage houses to admire their unique architecture, featuring Belgian glass, Italian marble, and Burma teak.

Also visit: The Athangudi tile-making units to see how the famous handmade Athangudi tiles are created.

Afternoon: Chettinad Cuisine

Enjoy an authentic Chettinad lunch, known for its aromatic and spicy flavors. The cuisine uses a variety of spices, including star anise, kalpasi (stone flower), and marathi mokku (dried flower pods).

Try: Chettinad Chicken/Mutton, Kuzhi Paniyaram (savory rice dumplings), and Kavuni Arisi (black rice pudding).

Evening: Drive to Madurai

Drive to Madurai, approximately 90 km west of Chettinad. Check into your hotel in Madurai.

Accommodation: Heritage Madurai, The Gateway Hotel Pasumalai, or Taj Madurai for luxury; JC Residency or Fortune Pandiyan Hotel for mid-range options.

Chettinad Mansion Interior

Chettinad Cuisine

Chettinad cuisine is one of the spiciest and most aromatic in India. The dishes use a variety of spices and are typically served on a banana leaf. Some popular dishes include:

  • Chettinad Chicken/Mutton Curry
  • Kuzhi Paniyaram (savory rice dumplings)
  • Kavuni Arisi (black rice pudding)
  • Chettinad Fish Fry
  • Vegetable Kola Urundai (vegetable balls)
  • Kandarappam (sweet rice flour cake)
Shopping in Chettinad

Chettinad is known for its unique handicrafts and antiques. Look for Athangudi tiles, Kottan baskets (palm leaf baskets), antique wooden artifacts, and Chettinad cotton sarees with checks and stripes.

Day 8

Madurai to Thekkady

Morning: Meenakshi Amman Temple

Visit the iconic Meenakshi Amman Temple, a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture with its towering gopurams (gateway towers) covered with thousands of colorful sculptures. Explore the thousand-pillar hall and the temple tank.

Note: Non-Hindus are not allowed in the innermost sanctum but can explore most parts of the temple complex.

Afternoon: Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace

Visit the Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace, built in 1636 by King Thirumalai Nayak in Indo-Saracenic style. Admire the massive pillars, stucco work, and the grand courtyard.

Lunch: Try Madurai's famous Kari Dosa (meat dosa) or Parottas with Mutton Curry at restaurants like Kumar Mess or Amma Mess.

Evening: Drive to Thekkady

Drive to Thekkady, located in the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary in Kerala, approximately 140 km southwest of Madurai. Enjoy the scenic drive through the Western Ghats. Check into your hotel in Thekkady.

Accommodation: Spice Village, Cardamom County, or Greenwoods Resort for luxury; Periyar Meadows or Wildernest for mid-range options.

About Thekkady

Thekkady is home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, one of India's most important tiger reserves. The sanctuary is centered around the artificial Periyar Lake, formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam. The area is known for its rich biodiversity, spice plantations, and cool climate. While it's in Kerala state, it's easily accessible from Tamil Nadu and is often included in Tamil Nadu tours.

Day 9

Thekkady to Munnar

Morning: Periyar Lake Boat Safari

Take an early morning boat safari on Periyar Lake to spot wildlife, including elephants, gaur (Indian bison), sambar deer, and if you're lucky, tigers. The animals often come to the lake's edge to drink water.

Alternative: If you prefer, you can opt for a guided nature walk or bamboo rafting in the sanctuary.

Afternoon: Spice Plantation Tour

Visit a spice plantation to learn about various spices like cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg. Understand how they are grown, harvested, and processed.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Kerala meal at a local restaurant or at the spice plantation.

Evening: Drive to Munnar

Drive to Munnar, approximately 100 km north of Thekkady. The scenic drive takes you through cardamom hills and tea plantations. Check into your hotel in Munnar.

Accommodation: Windermere Estate, Tea County, or Blackberry Hills for luxury; Deshadan Mountain Resort or KTDC Tea County for mid-range options.

Day 10

Munnar to Ooty

Morning: Munnar Sightseeing

Visit the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr (mountain goat). Continue to Top Station for panoramic views of the Western Ghats and neighboring Tamil Nadu. Visit the Tea Museum to learn about the history of tea plantations in Munnar.

Note: Eravikulam National Park is closed during the calving season of the Nilgiri Tahr (usually January-March).

Afternoon: Drive to Ooty

After lunch, embark on a scenic drive to Ooty (Udhagamandalam), approximately 160 km northwest of Munnar. The journey takes you through the Nilgiri Hills with breathtaking views. Check into your hotel in Ooty.

Accommodation: Taj Savoy, Destiny The Farmstay, or Sterling Ooty Fern Hill for luxury; Hotel Sinclairs or Fortune Resort Sullivan Court for mid-range options.

Evening: Ooty Lake

If time permits, visit Ooty Lake for a boat ride or a leisurely walk around the lake. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Try: Nilgiri specialties like Badaga cuisine or Anglo-Indian dishes that reflect Ooty's colonial heritage.

Munnar Tea Plantations

About Ooty

Ooty, officially known as Udhagamandalam, is a hill station in the Nilgiri Hills. Established as a summer retreat by the British during colonial times, it's known for its pleasant climate, tea gardens, colonial architecture, and beautiful botanical gardens. The town is also famous for the Nilgiri Mountain Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Elevation: 2,240 meters (7,350 ft)
Cool climate throughout the year

Day 11

Ooty to Coimbatore

Morning: Nilgiri Mountain Railway

Experience a ride on the famous Nilgiri Mountain Railway (toy train), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The train journey from Ooty to Coonoor offers breathtaking views of the Nilgiri Hills. (Note: Advance booking recommended)

Alternative: If train tickets are not available, you can drive to Coonoor and visit Sim's Park, a botanical garden with a diverse collection of plants.

Afternoon: Botanical Gardens & Tea Factory

Visit the Government Botanical Gardens, established in 1848, featuring terraced gardens, exotic and indigenous plants, and a fossilized tree estimated to be 20 million years old. Continue to a tea factory to learn about tea processing and enjoy a tea tasting session.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a colonial-style restaurant like Earl's Secret at King's Cliff or Nahar's Sidewalk CafÃ©.

Evening: Drive to Coimbatore

Drive to Coimbatore, approximately 85 km southeast of Ooty. The journey takes you through the scenic Nilgiri Hills and tea plantations. Check into your hotel in Coimbatore.

Accommodation: Vivanta by Taj Surya, Le Meridien, or Zone by The Park for luxury; The Residency or Fairfield by Marriott for mid-range options.

Day 12

Coimbatore & Departure

Morning: Coimbatore Temples

Visit the Marudamalai Temple, dedicated to Lord Murugan, located on a hill offering panoramic views of the city. Continue to the Perur Pateeswarar Temple, an ancient Shiva temple known for its beautiful architecture and sculptures.

Also visit: CODISSIA Trade Fair Complex, showcasing the industrial heritage of Coimbatore, if time permits.

Afternoon: Shopping & Lunch

Explore the local markets of Coimbatore for last-minute shopping. The city is known for its textiles, especially cotton products. Visit Brookefields Mall or Fun Republic Mall for a modern shopping experience.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional South Indian meal at Annapoorna or Sree Annapoorna, famous local restaurants.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Coimbatore International Airport for your onward journey. If you're returning to Chennai, you can take a flight (1 hour) or an overnight train.

Note: Ensure you reach the airport at least 2 hours before your domestic flight or 3 hours before an international flight.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Coastal areas: October to March (winter months)
  • Hill stations: March to June (summer) and September to November (autumn)
  • Temple towns: Year-round, but avoid summer (April-June) due to extreme heat
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) in most areas

Transportation

  • Air: Chennai, Madurai, Coimbatore, and Trichy have airports with good connectivity
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities and towns
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in cities; buses and auto-rickshaws widely available
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for coastal areas; warm layers for hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Tamil Nadu offers a diverse vegetarian and non-vegetarian culinary experience:

Vegetarian Specialties
  • Idli (steamed rice cakes)
  • Dosa (rice and lentil crepes)
  • Sambar (lentil and vegetable stew)
  • Rasam (tamarind soup)
  • Pongal (rice and lentil dish)
  • Filter Coffee
Regional Specialties
  • Chettinad Chicken/Mutton
  • Madurai Kari Dosa
  • Dindigul Biryani
  • Coimbatore's Annapoorna meals
  • Nilgiri's Anglo-Indian cuisine
  • Kerala-style seafood (in Thekkady/Munnar)

Practical Information

  • Language: Tamil is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Tamil Nadu is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Temple etiquette: Remove shoes before entering temples, dress modestly, and follow local customs
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
