Tripura: Northeast India's Hidden Gem

Tripura, one of India's smallest states, is a treasure trove of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and historical wonders. From magnificent royal palaces to ancient rock-cut sculptures, from serene hills to vibrant wildlife, this 7-day itinerary takes you through the highlights of this lesser-explored northeastern state.

Day 1

Arrival in Agartala & City Introduction

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport in Agartala. Transfer to your hotel and check-in. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Ginger Agartala, Hotel Welcome Palace, or Polo Towers for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Local Market Visit

Begin your Tripura experience with a visit to the Battala Market, one of the oldest and busiest markets in Agartala. Explore the vibrant stalls selling local handicrafts, bamboo products, and traditional textiles. This is a great place to get a feel for the local culture and daily life.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Bengali cuisine at Aahar Restaurant or Kurry Klub, sampling dishes like fish curry, mixed vegetables, and rice.

Evening: Heritage Park

Visit the Heritage Park (Purbasha), a cultural complex showcasing the diverse tribal heritage of Tripura. The park features replicas of traditional tribal houses, cultural artifacts, and beautiful landscaped gardens. Enjoy a leisurely stroll as you learn about the state's rich cultural diversity.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at Ginger or Shankar Restaurant, trying local specialties like Chakhwi (bamboo shoot curry) or Mui Borok (a traditional dish made with berma, a fermented fish).

Day 1 Tips
  • Agartala has a tropical climate, so dress in light, comfortable clothing.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • The local language is Bengali, but many people understand basic English, especially in tourist areas.
  • Exchange currency or withdraw cash from ATMs in Agartala as banking facilities may be limited in remote areas.
Day 2

Agartala City Tour

Morning: Ujjayanta Palace

Visit the magnificent Ujjayanta Palace, the former royal palace of Tripura's Manikya dynasty, now housing the Tripura State Museum. Explore the impressive Indo-Saracenic architecture, beautiful gardens, and the museum's extensive collection of artifacts showcasing the state's history, tribal culture, and royal heritage.

Activity: Take a guided tour of the museum to learn about Tripura's fascinating history and cultural diversity.

Afternoon: Temples & Gardens

Visit the Jagannath Temple, an important Hindu temple with beautiful architecture. Continue to the Fourteen Gods Temple (Chaturdasha Devata Mandir), dedicated to the fourteen deities who are considered the guardians of Tripura. Then explore the beautiful Sukanta Academy, a cultural center with lovely gardens.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Shehnaz or Kurry Klub, trying local Bengali and Tripuri dishes.

Evening: Astabal Maidan & Laxminarayan Bari

Visit Astabal Maidan, a large open ground that was once the royal stable and is now a popular recreational area. Continue to Laxminarayan Bari, a beautiful temple complex dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi. End your day with a visit to the illuminated Ujjayanta Palace grounds in the evening.

Dinner: Try Kashmiri or North Indian cuisine at Ginger or local Bengali food at Hotel Sonar Tori.

About Agartala

Agartala, the capital of Tripura, is a city with a rich royal heritage and cultural diversity. Once the seat of the Manikya dynasty that ruled Tripura for over 500 years, the city showcases a blend of Bengali and indigenous tribal influences. With its palaces, temples, and vibrant markets, Agartala offers visitors a glimpse into the unique culture and history of this northeastern state.

Day 3

Neermahal Palace & Surroundings

Morning: Drive to Melaghar

After breakfast, drive to Melaghar (approximately 53 km from Agartala), home to the famous Neermahal Palace. The journey takes you through picturesque countryside and small villages, offering glimpses of rural life in Tripura.

En route: Stop at local tea stalls to try Tripura's famous tea, grown in the hills of the state.

Afternoon: Neermahal Palace

Visit the stunning Neermahal Palace, also known as the "Lake Palace" of Tripura. Built in 1930 by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya, this white palace sits in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, combining Hindu and Muslim architectural styles. Take a boat ride to the palace and explore its beautiful halls, courtyards, and gardens.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple lunch at one of the local eateries near the lake, trying fresh fish curry, a specialty of the region.

Evening: Rudrasagar Lake

Spend the evening exploring the surroundings of Rudrasagar Lake, a Ramsar wetland site known for its biodiversity. Enjoy a boat ride on the lake to watch the sunset and possibly spot some of the many bird species that inhabit the area.

Return to Agartala: Drive back to Agartala in the evening. Dinner at your hotel or at a local restaurant like Shankar or Kurry Klub.

About Neermahal

Neermahal, meaning "Water Palace," is the former summer residence of Tripura's Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya. Built in 1930, it's one of only two water palaces in India (the other being Lake Palace in Udaipur). The palace beautifully combines Hindu and Muslim architectural elements and features a central courtyard, several rooms, and a beautiful garden. During the annual Neermahal Water Festival, the palace and lake become the center of cultural celebrations with boat races, swimming competitions, and folk performances.

Day 4

Unakoti Rock Carvings

Morning: Drive to Unakoti

After an early breakfast, embark on a journey to Unakoti (approximately 178 km from Agartala), one of Tripura's most significant archaeological sites. The drive takes about 4-5 hours through scenic landscapes, forests, and tribal villages.

En route: Make stops at viewpoints and small villages to stretch your legs and experience the rural beauty of Tripura.

Afternoon: Unakoti Rock Carvings

Explore the fascinating rock carvings of Unakoti, which means "one less than a crore" (9,999,999). These massive rock reliefs, dating back to the 7th-9th centuries, are carved on a hillside and depict various Hindu deities, with the massive Shiva head (Unakotiswara Kal Bhairava) being the most impressive.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a simple local eatery near the site.

Evening: Waterfalls & Return

Visit the beautiful waterfalls near the Unakoti site, which are particularly impressive during and after the monsoon season. Begin your return journey to Agartala in the late afternoon.

Accommodation: Return to your hotel in Agartala. Dinner at the hotel or at a local restaurant.

Unakoti Visit Tips
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes as you'll need to climb steps to see all the rock carvings.
  • Carry water, snacks, and insect repellent as facilities are limited at the site.
  • The best time to visit is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather is pleasant.
  • Photography is allowed, but be respectful as this is also a religious site.
  • Consider hiring a local guide to learn about the mythology and history behind the carvings.
About Unakoti

Unakoti, meaning "one less than a crore," is an ancient Shaivite pilgrimage site with massive rock-cut sculptures dating back to the 7th-9th centuries. According to legend, Lord Shiva was traveling to Kashi with a crore (10 million) gods and goddesses. They stopped at this site for the night, and Shiva instructed everyone to wake up before sunrise. Only Shiva woke up, and he left for Kashi, leaving behind the 9,999,999 gods and goddesses who were turned into stone images. The site is now an Archaeological Survey of India protected site and one of Tripura's most important historical attractions.

Day 5

Jampui Hills

Morning: Drive to Jampui Hills

After breakfast, embark on a journey to Jampui Hills (approximately 200 km from Agartala), known as the "Eternal Spring" of Tripura for its pleasant climate year-round. The drive takes about 5-6 hours through beautiful landscapes and tribal villages.

En route: Stop at Kailashahar, a historic town with colonial-era buildings, for a short break and lunch.

Afternoon: Vanghmun Village

Arrive at Jampui Hills and check into your accommodation in Vanghmun, the main village in the hills. Explore the village and interact with the local Mizo community, known for their warm hospitality and rich cultural traditions.

Activity: Visit a local orange orchard (if in season from November to January) or enjoy the panoramic views from various viewpoints.

Evening: Sunset at Betlingchhip

Visit Betlingchhip, the highest peak in Tripura at 3,600 feet above sea level. Enjoy the spectacular sunset views over the surrounding hills and valleys, with Bangladesh visible on clear days.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple dinner at your accommodation, possibly featuring local Mizo cuisine.

About Jampui Hills

Jampui Hills, located in the North Tripura district along the Tripura-Mizoram border, is a range of hills known for its pleasant climate, stunning views, and orange orchards. Home to the Mizo tribal community, the hills offer a unique cultural experience along with natural beauty. The area is famous for its orange festival held in November-December, celebrating the harvest of the region's sweet oranges. With an elevation ranging from 500-1000 meters, Jampui Hills provides a refreshing escape from the tropical heat of the plains.

Famous for sweet oranges

Day 6

Return to Agartala & Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Morning: Jampui Hills Exploration

Wake up early to catch the sunrise from a viewpoint near your accommodation. After breakfast, spend some time exploring more of Jampui Hills, visiting local villages, and interacting with the friendly Mizo people.

Activity: Visit a local handicraft center to see traditional Mizo weaving and bamboo crafts.

Afternoon: Return to Agartala

Begin your return journey to Agartala after an early lunch. The drive takes about 5-6 hours, with opportunities to stop at scenic spots along the way.

En route: Stop at Kailashahar or other small towns for refreshments and to stretch your legs.

Evening: Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

If time permits and you arrive back in Agartala with daylight remaining, visit the Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary, located about 25 km from the city. The sanctuary is home to various species of primates, including the endangered spectacled monkey, as well as clouded leopards, barking deer, and numerous bird species.

Dinner: Return to Agartala for dinner at your hotel or at a local restaurant like Ginger or Kurry Klub.

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

About Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary, spread over 18.53 sq km, is a major wildlife conservation area in Tripura. Established in 1987, the sanctuary is known for its rich biodiversity and is home to several endangered species. The sanctuary includes a zoo, botanical garden, and a lake, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and families. It's particularly famous for its spectacled langur (Phayre's leaf monkey) conservation program and the clouded leopard enclosures.

Home to rare spectacled langur
Over 150 bird species
Rich botanical diversity
Day 7

Tripura Sundari Temple & Departure

Morning: Tripura Sundari Temple

Visit the sacred Tripura Sundari Temple in Udaipur (approximately 55 km from Agartala), one of the 51 Shakti Peethas in Hindu tradition. The temple, built in 1501 by Maharaja Dhanya Manikya, is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari and is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Northeast India.

Activity: Participate in a morning prayer ceremony and explore the temple complex, which includes a sacred lake called Kalyan Sagar.

Afternoon: Souvenir Shopping

Return to Agartala and spend some time shopping for souvenirs. Visit the Tripura Government Emporium or local markets to purchase traditional bamboo and cane products, handloom textiles, tribal jewelry, and other handicrafts unique to Tripura.

Lunch: Enjoy a final lunch at a local restaurant, trying any Tripuri specialties you haven't yet sampled.

Evening: Departure

Transfer to Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport for your departure flight. If time permits before your flight, visit any attractions in Agartala that you might have missed earlier in the trip.

Note: If your flight departs the next day, consider visiting the Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary or Kalapania Nature Park for a relaxed end to your Tripura adventure.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to February: The best time with pleasant weather and minimal rainfall
  • March to May: Summer season with moderate temperatures
  • June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall, but lush green landscapes
  • Special Events: Orange Festival (Nov-Dec) in Jampui Hills, Kharchi Puja (July), and Garia Puja (April)

Transportation

  • Air: Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport in Agartala connects to major Indian cities
  • Train: Agartala Railway Station connects to Guwahati and other major cities
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and local buses within Agartala
  • Road: NH-8 connects Tripura to other northeastern states
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience, especially for remote areas

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes; a light jacket for evenings in hill areas
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Umbrella or raincoat (especially during monsoon), water bottle

Food & Cuisine

Tripura offers a unique culinary experience with influences from Bengali and tribal cuisines:

Bengali Influence
  • Fish Curry (Mach Bhaja)
  • Rice (Staple food)
  • Mixed Vegetables (Labra)
  • Chutney
  • Mishti Doi (Sweet yogurt)
  • Rosogolla (Sweet)
Tribal Specialties
  • Mui Borok (Berma with vegetables)
  • Chakhwi (Bamboo shoot curry)
  • Gudok (Mixed vegetable stew)
  • Bangui (Rice beer)
  • Awandru (Pork with bamboo shoot)
  • Apong (Rice wine)

Practical Information

  • Language: Bengali and Kokborok are the main languages. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in Agartala and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Agartala, limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Tripura is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign nationals need to register with the Foreigner Registration Office in Agartala
  • Local Customs: Respect local tribal customs and traditions. Ask permission before photographing people
  • Emergency Numbers: Police: 100, Ambulance: 108, Tourist Helpline: 1800-345-3665
