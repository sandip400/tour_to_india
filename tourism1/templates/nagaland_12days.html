Nagaland: The Land of Festivals

Nagaland offers a mesmerizing blend of tribal heritage, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through the major cities and hidden gems of India's northeastern state, from the bustling city of Kohima to the pristine Dzükou Valley and the remote villages of Mon.

Authentic Naga cuisine

Trekking in pristine valleys

Day 1

Arrival in Dimapur

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Dimapur Airport, the only airport in Nagaland. Transfer to your hotel in Dimapur and take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Hotel Acacia or The Gateway Hotel in Dimapur for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Kachari Ruins & Triple Falls

Visit the historic Kachari Ruins, remnants of the 13th-century Dimasa Kachari Kingdom. Continue to the picturesque Triple Falls, a series of three adjacent waterfalls surrounded by lush greenery.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Naga cuisine at Bambusa or Orami in Dimapur.

Evening: Hong Kong Market

Explore the vibrant Hong Kong Market, Dimapur's commercial hub where you can shop for clothing, electronics, and local products. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant featuring traditional Naga dishes.

Evening activity: Take a leisurely stroll around Dimapur city center to get oriented with Nagaland's commercial capital.

Kachari Ruins

Triple Falls

Day 1 Tips
  • Ensure you have your Inner Line Permit (ILP) ready upon arrival in Nagaland.
  • Exchange currency or withdraw cash in Dimapur as ATMs are limited in other parts of Nagaland.
  • Purchase any essential supplies in Dimapur as it has the best shopping options in the state.
  • Confirm your transportation arrangements for the next day's journey to Kohima.
Day 2

Dimapur to Kohima

Morning: Drive to Kohima

After breakfast, embark on a scenic 2-3 hour drive to Kohima, the capital city of Nagaland. The journey takes you through beautiful hills and valleys with several viewpoints along the way.

Activity: Stop at Chumoukedima viewpoint for panoramic views and photography.

Afternoon: Check-in & War Cemetery

Arrive in Kohima and check into your hotel. After lunch, visit the Kohima War Cemetery, a memorial dedicated to soldiers who died in the Battle of Kohima during World War II.

Accommodation: Hotel Japfü or Razhu Pru in Kohima for comfortable stays with good views.

Evening: Kohima Cathedral & Local Market

Visit the Kohima Cathedral, one of the largest cathedrals in Northeast India. Explore the local market where you can shop for traditional handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry.

Dinner: Try the popular Bamboo Shoot dishes and Smoked Pork at Chingtsuong Restaurant or Aradura Inn.

Day 3

Kohima Exploration

Morning: Nagaland State Museum

Visit the Nagaland State Museum to learn about the rich cultural heritage of the 16 major tribes of Nagaland. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, traditional attire, weapons, and handicrafts.

Activity: Take time to understand the unique cultural aspects of each Naga tribe displayed in the museum.

Afternoon: Japfü Peak Viewpoint

Visit the viewpoint for Japfü Peak, the second highest mountain in Nagaland. On clear days, you can see the magnificent peak and surrounding landscapes. Continue to the Kohima Village, one of the largest Angami villages.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at The Heritage or Orami in Kohima, both offering excellent local cuisine.

Evening: Cultural Show & Dinner

Attend a cultural show featuring traditional Naga dances and music if available. Many hotels and cultural centers organize these performances, especially during the tourist season.

Dinner: Experience a traditional Naga feast at Bamboo Hut or Kerünyü Restaurant, featuring a variety of local delicacies.

Nagaland State Museum

Cultural Etiquette
  • Always ask permission before taking photographs of people or cultural events.
  • Dress modestly when visiting villages and cultural sites.
  • Remove shoes when entering someone's home or certain religious places.
  • When offered food or drink in a village, it's polite to accept at least a small amount.
About Kohima

Kohima, the capital city of Nagaland, is perched at an altitude of 1,444 meters above sea level. The city gained historical significance during World War II when the Battle of Kohima was fought in 1944, considered the turning point of the Japanese invasion of India. Today, Kohima serves as the cultural and administrative center of Nagaland, showcasing a blend of traditional Naga heritage and modern development.

Day 4

Kohima to Khonoma Village

Morning: Drive to Khonoma

After breakfast, drive to Khonoma Village (approximately 20 km from Kohima), known as Asia's first green village. This picturesque Angami Naga village is famous for its terraced farming and rich cultural heritage.

Note: The drive offers beautiful views of the countryside and terraced fields. Keep your camera ready.

Afternoon: Village Tour & Homestay Check-in

Take a guided tour of Khonoma Village, learning about its history, traditional farming methods, and conservation efforts. Check into your homestay and interact with the local family.

Accommodation: Khonoma offers several homestay options where you can experience authentic Naga hospitality.

Evening: Cultural Exchange & Traditional Dinner

Participate in a cultural exchange with the villagers, learning about their traditions, stories, and songs. Join your host family for a traditional home-cooked Naga meal featuring organic local produce.

Activity: Learn about traditional Naga cooking methods and help prepare dinner with your host family if possible.

Khonoma Green Village

Terraced Fields

Homestay Experience

Staying in a Khonoma homestay offers a unique opportunity to experience authentic Naga village life. Most homestays are traditional houses that have been adapted to accommodate guests while maintaining their cultural integrity. Expect:

  • Basic but clean accommodations with shared bathrooms in some cases
  • Home-cooked meals using organic, locally-sourced ingredients
  • Opportunity to participate in daily village activities
  • Cultural exchanges with your host family and other villagers
  • Limited or no internet connectivity and intermittent electricity
Day 5

Khonoma to Dzükou Valley

Morning: Drive to Viswema Village

After an early breakfast, drive to Viswema Village (approximately 1 hour from Khonoma), which serves as the starting point for the Dzükou Valley trek. The valley is famous for its natural beauty and seasonal flowers.

Note: Pack light for the trek. Carry water, snacks, and a light lunch.

Afternoon: Trek to Dzükou Valley

Begin your trek to Dzükou Valley, which takes about 2-3 hours. The initial climb is steep but rewards you with breathtaking views. Explore the valley with its rolling hills, streams, and unique flora.

Activity: If visiting in season (June-September), look for the rare Dzükou Lily that only grows in this valley.

Evening: Overnight at Rest House

Check into the Dzükou Valley Rest House, a basic accommodation facility managed by local authorities. Enjoy the serene environment and prepare for a night in the wilderness.

Note: The rest house offers very basic facilities. Bring a sleeping bag, warm clothes, and necessary supplies.

Dzükou Valley Trek

Day 6

Dzükou Valley Exploration

Morning: Sunrise & Valley Exploration

Wake up early to witness the spectacular sunrise over the valley. After breakfast, spend the morning exploring deeper parts of the valley, including the stream that flows through it and the surrounding hills.

Activity: Photography, bird watching, and identifying local flora.

Afternoon: Cave Exploration

Hike to the natural caves in the valley area. These caves have historical significance and offer a different perspective of the valley's geological features.

Lunch: Enjoy a packed lunch amidst the natural beauty of the valley.

Evening: Campfire & Stargazing

Return to the rest house for dinner. Enjoy a campfire (if permitted) and take advantage of the clear night sky for stargazing, away from city lights.

Note: The valley gets quite cold at night, so ensure you have adequate warm clothing.

Sunrise at Dzükou Valley

Flora & Fauna

Dzükou Valley is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species. The valley is particularly famous for:

  • The rare Dzükou Lily (Lilium chitrangadae), which blooms between June and August
  • Rhododendrons that cover the hillsides with vibrant colors in spring
  • Various medicinal herbs used in traditional Naga medicine
  • Numerous bird species including the Blyth's Tragopan and Naga Wren Babbler
  • Small mammals like barking deer and wild boar
Day 7

Dzükou Valley to Mokokchung

Morning: Trek Back to Viswema

After an early breakfast, begin your trek back to Viswema Village. The descent is easier than the climb but still requires careful navigation.

Note: Take your time during the descent to avoid injuries on the steep sections.

Afternoon: Drive to Mokokchung

From Viswema, drive to Mokokchung (approximately 4-5 hours), the cultural center of the Ao tribe. The journey takes you through beautiful landscapes, villages, and forests.

Lunch: Stop for lunch at a roadside eatery or carry packed food for the journey.

Evening: Arrival & Rest

Arrive in Mokokchung and check into your hotel. Take the evening to rest and recover from the trek and journey.

Accommodation: Hotel Metsuben or Whispering Winds are good options in Mokokchung.

Day 8

Mokokchung Exploration

Morning: Mokokchung Town Tour

Explore Mokokchung town, the cultural center of the Ao tribe. Visit the District Museum to learn about Ao culture and history. Continue to the local market to see daily life and shop for handicrafts.

Activity: Interact with local artisans and learn about traditional Ao crafts.

Afternoon: Longkhum Village

Visit Longkhum Village near Mokokchung, known for its panoramic views and traditional Ao culture. This village is famous for its stone monoliths and the legend of lovers' stones.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Ao meal in the village, featuring dishes like smoked pork and bamboo shoot.

Evening: Cultural Experience

Return to Mokokchung for a cultural experience. If available, attend a traditional Ao cultural show featuring folk songs and dances. Alternatively, visit a local church to experience the strong Christian influence in Naga culture.

Dinner: Try traditional Ao cuisine at a local restaurant, featuring dishes like Pork with Bamboo Shoot and Axone (fermented soybean).

Longkhum Village

Ao Cultural Performance

About the Ao Tribe

The Ao tribe is one of the major tribes of Nagaland, primarily residing in the Mokokchung district. Known for their rich cultural heritage, the Aos have a strong oral tradition passed down through generations. They are famous for their colorful shawls, intricate wood carvings, and melodious folk songs. The tribe celebrates several festivals throughout the year, with Moatsu being the most significant one, observed in the first week of May after the sowing season.

Day 9

Mokokchung to Wokha

Morning: Drive to Wokha

After breakfast, drive to Wokha (approximately 3-4 hours), the homeland of the Lotha Naga tribe. The journey offers scenic views of mountains, valleys, and terraced fields.

En route: Stop at viewpoints along the way for photography and short breaks.

Afternoon: Arrival & Local Exploration

Arrive in Wokha and check into your accommodation. After lunch, explore the town center and visit the Lotha Tribal Museum to learn about the culture and traditions of the Lotha tribe.

Accommodation: Hotel Rhino or local guesthouses in Wokha.

Evening: Mount Tiyi

Visit Mount Tiyi, a sacred mountain for the Lotha tribe. According to local belief, the souls of the dead pass through this mountain on their journey to the afterlife. Enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Dinner: Try local Lotha cuisine at a restaurant in Wokha town.

Scenic Landscape of Wokha

Lotha Tribe Highlights
  • Tokhu Emong Festival: The main post-harvest festival celebrated in November
  • Traditional Attire: Distinctive shawls with intricate patterns and colorful designs
  • Folk Songs: Known for their melodious folk songs called "Ophirü"
  • Mount Tiyi: Sacred mountain with significant cultural and spiritual importance
  • Handicrafts: Skilled in bamboo and cane crafts, wood carving, and weaving
About Wokha

Wokha district is predominantly inhabited by the Lotha Naga tribe and is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. The district is famous for the Doyang River and Doyang Reservoir, which attracts the Amur Falcon birds in massive numbers during their migration, making it the "Falcon Capital of the World." The area is also known for its pineapple and orange plantations.

Day 10

Wokha to Mon

Morning: Doyang Reservoir

Visit the Doyang Reservoir, known for hosting thousands of migratory Amur Falcons during October-November. Even outside the migration season, the reservoir offers beautiful views and boating opportunities.

Activity: Bird watching and photography if visiting during the migration season.

Afternoon: Drive to Mon

After lunch, begin your journey to Mon (approximately 6-7 hours), home to the Konyak tribe, once famous headhunters. The drive is challenging but offers spectacular views of the Indo-Myanmar border region.

Note: The roads to Mon can be rough, so be prepared for a bumpy ride.

Evening: Arrival in Mon

Arrive in Mon and check into your accommodation. Rest and prepare for the next day's exploration of Konyak villages.

Accommodation: Helsa Cottage or Teihba Homestay in Mon offer basic but comfortable stays.

Doyang Reservoir

Road to Mon

Travel Advisory

The journey to Mon is one of the most challenging road trips in Nagaland:

  • Start early to ensure you reach Mon before dark
  • The road conditions can be poor, especially during or after the monsoon season
  • Carry motion sickness medication if you're prone to it
  • Keep some snacks and water for the journey as there are limited options along the way
  • Ensure your vehicle is in good condition, preferably a 4x4 or SUV
  • Mobile network coverage is limited on this route
Day 11

Mon Village Exploration

Morning: Longwa Village

Visit Longwa Village, where the Indo-Myanmar international border passes through the village chief's house. Meet the Angh (chief) and the tattooed elders of the village, the last generation with traditional facial tattoos.

Activity: Explore the village, visit a traditional Morung (communal house), and shop for authentic Konyak handicrafts.

Afternoon: Mon Village

Visit Mon Village to see the traditional Konyak houses and lifestyle. Interact with the villagers and learn about their unique customs and traditions.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional Konyak meal in the village.

Evening: Cultural Museum & Interaction

Visit the local cultural museum in Mon town to see artifacts related to the headhunting past of the Konyak tribe. Spend the evening interacting with locals and learning about their changing way of life.

Dinner: Try traditional Konyak cuisine at your accommodation or a local eatery.

Longwa Village

Konyak Elder with Tattoos

About the Konyak Tribe

The Konyak tribe is the largest of Nagaland's 16 tribes and was once known for headhunting practices, which ended in the 1960s. Today, they are recognized for their distinctive facial tattoos, brass ornaments, and woodcarvings. The elderly tattooed men, known as the last of the headhunters, are living repositories of a unique cultural history that is rapidly disappearing.

Day 12

Mon to Dimapur & Departure

Morning: Chi Village

Visit Chi Village near Mon, another traditional Konyak settlement known for its skilled craftsmen. Observe and learn about traditional blacksmithing and woodcarving techniques.

Activity: Shop for authentic Konyak handicrafts as souvenirs.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, begin your return journey to Dimapur (approximately 7-8 hours). Alternatively, you can fly from Mon to Dimapur if flights are available, saving considerable travel time.

Note: If driving, start early as the journey is long and you'll want to reach Dimapur before dark.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Dimapur for souvenirs. The Hong Kong Market and Super Market are good options for traditional Naga handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry.

Departure: Transfer to Dimapur Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: The best time to visit Nagaland with pleasant weather
  • December: Ideal for experiencing the Hornbill Festival
  • June to September: Monsoon season with lush landscapes but challenging travel conditions
  • April: Perfect for seeing rhododendrons in bloom in Dzükou Valley

Transportation

  • Air: Dimapur is the only city with an airport, connected to Kolkata and Guwahati
  • Train: Dimapur is the only railway station in Nagaland
  • Road: Shared taxis and buses connect major towns, but roads can be challenging
  • Local Transport: Shared taxis within towns; limited public transport
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers for varying temperatures; modest attire for village visits
  • Footwear: Sturdy hiking shoes for treks and village walks
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, rain gear (in monsoon)
  • Health: Personal medications, first aid kit, insect repellent
  • Others: Flashlight, power bank (electricity can be unreliable), toilet paper

Food & Cuisine

Nagaland offers a unique culinary experience with dishes that might be unfamiliar to most travelers:

Must-Try Dishes
  • Smoked Pork with Axone
  • Bamboo Shoot Curry
  • Galho (Rice Porridge)
  • Pork with Raja Mirchi (Ghost Pepper)
  • Zutho (Rice Beer)
  • Black Sticky Rice
Dining Experiences
  • Homestay meals for authentic local cuisine
  • Local restaurants in Kohima and Dimapur
  • Tribal food festivals
  • Street food in local markets
  • Community feasts during village visits
  • Note: Many dishes are spicy and meat-heavy

Practical Information

  • Permits: Inner Line Permit (ILP) required for all visitors, obtainable online or at entry points
  • Language: English is widely spoken; Nagamese is the common language among tribes
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs available in major towns but limited in remote areas
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Power cuts are common, especially in rural areas
  • Internet: Good connectivity in Kohima and Dimapur; limited in remote villages
  • Safety: Nagaland is generally safe for tourists. Respect local customs and traditions
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people or cultural events
  • Alcohol: Nagaland is officially a dry state, but local rice beer is available in villages
