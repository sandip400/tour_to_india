Haryana: The Cradle of Indian History

Haryana offers a perfect blend of ancient history, spiritual heritage, and modern urban experiences. From the corporate hub of Gurugram to the sacred battlefields of Kurukshetra, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Gurugram & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi. Transfer to your hotel in Gurugram. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: The Oberoi Gurugram or Leela Ambience for luxury stays; Lemon Tree or Radisson for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Cyber Hub

Enjoy your first meal at the famous Cyber Hub, a premium food and entertainment destination. Explore the vibrant atmosphere and diverse culinary options.

Lunch recommendation: Try North Indian cuisine at Dhaba or international options at SodaBottleOpenerWala.

Evening: Shopping & Leisure

Visit Ambience Mall, one of the largest shopping malls in India. Enjoy shopping, entertainment, and dinner at one of the many restaurants in the mall.

Evening activity: Catch a movie at PVR or enjoy bowling at Smaaash entertainment center.

Cyber Hub

Ambience Mall

Day 1 Tips
  • Traffic in Gurugram can be heavy during peak hours, plan your movements accordingly.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Cyber Hub is busiest in the evenings and on weekends.
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
Day 2

Gurugram Heritage & Culture

Morning: Kingdom of Dreams

Start your day with a visit to Kingdom of Dreams, India's first live entertainment and leisure destination. Explore Culture Gully, a mock Indian streetscape with crafts and cuisine from various states.

Activity: If available, book tickets for a Bollywood musical show for the evening.

Afternoon: Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary

Visit the Sultanpur National Park and Bird Sanctuary, located about 15 km from Gurugram. This wetland is home to over 250 species of birds, both resident and migratory.

Note: Best time to visit is from November to March when migratory birds arrive.

Evening: Cultural Show & Dinner

Return to Kingdom of Dreams for a spectacular Bollywood musical show at Nautanki Mahal. Enjoy dinner at one of the themed restaurants in Culture Gully.

Dinner: Try cuisines from different Indian states at the various food stalls in Culture Gully.

Day 3

Faridabad & Surajkund

Morning: Drive to Faridabad

After breakfast, drive to Faridabad (approximately 1 hour from Gurugram). Visit the Raja Nahar Singh Palace, a historical monument built in the 18th century that showcases Rajputana and Mughal architecture.

Note: The palace has been converted into a museum displaying artifacts from the region's history.

Afternoon: Surajkund Crafts Mela Site

Visit Surajkund, famous for its annual international crafts fair. Explore the permanent structures and the ancient Surajkund reservoir, a 10th-century water tank built by King Suraj Pal.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Dabchick restaurant near Surajkund, known for its peaceful ambiance and good food.

Evening: Badkhal Lake

Visit Badkhal Lake, a popular picnic spot surrounded by the Aravalli hills. Though the lake is now mostly dry, the area offers beautiful sunset views and recreational facilities.

Dinner: Return to Gurugram for dinner at Sector 29, a popular food hub with numerous dining options.

Surajkund Crafts Mela Site

Important Information
  • If your visit coincides with February, don't miss the Surajkund International Crafts Mela, one of the largest craft fairs in the world.
  • Raja Nahar Singh Palace is closed on Mondays.
  • Badkhal Lake is best visited during or just after the monsoon season when there might be some water.
  • Carry sunscreen, hat, and water as most sites involve outdoor exploration.
About Surajkund

Surajkund gets its name from the ancient amphitheater-shaped reservoir built in the 10th century by King Suraj Pal of the Tomar dynasty. The Surajkund Crafts Mela held annually in February showcases traditional crafts, handlooms, and cultural performances from India and around the world.

Day 4

Kurukshetra - The Land of Mahabharata

Morning: Drive to Kurukshetra

After an early breakfast, drive to Kurukshetra (approximately 2.5 hours from Gurugram). This ancient city is known as the battlefield of the epic Mahabharata war and holds immense historical and religious significance.

First stop: Visit Brahma Sarovar, a sacred water tank believed to have been created by Lord Brahma himself. Take a boat ride if available.

Afternoon: Sacred Sites

Visit the Kurukshetra Panorama and Science Centre which houses a multi-media presentation of the Mahabharata war. Continue to Jyotisar, the sacred site where Lord Krishna delivered the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna.

Lunch: Enjoy a simple vegetarian thali at one of the local restaurants near Brahma Sarovar.

Evening: More Exploration & Check-in

Visit the Krishna Museum which houses artifacts related to Lord Krishna and the Mahabharata. Also visit Bhadrakali Temple and Sheikh Chilli's Tomb, a beautiful example of Mughal architecture.

Accommodation: Check into a hotel in Kurukshetra for the night. Options include Neelkanthi Yatri Niwas or Hotel Saffron.

Brahma Sarovar

Jyotisar - Birthplace of Bhagavad Gita

Historical Significance

Kurukshetra is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus. According to the epic Mahabharata, this is where the 18-day war between the Pandavas and Kauravas was fought. It is also where Lord Krishna imparted the wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna before the battle began.

The city derives its name from King Kuru, the ancestor of the Kauravas and Pandavas, who performed penance at this site. Today, it houses numerous temples, tanks, and sites mentioned in ancient texts.

Day 5

Kurukshetra to Panchkula

Morning: Sunrise at Brahma Sarovar

Experience the spiritual ambiance of Brahma Sarovar during sunrise. Witness the morning rituals and prayers performed by devotees. After breakfast, visit any remaining sites in Kurukshetra you missed the previous day.

Afternoon: Drive to Panchkula

Drive to Panchkula (approximately 2 hours), a planned city adjacent to Chandigarh. Check into your hotel and have lunch.

Accommodation: The Bella Vista or Hotel KC Residency are good options in Panchkula.

Evening: Pinjore Gardens

Visit the magnificent Pinjore Gardens (Yadavindra Gardens), a 17th-century Mughal garden built by architect Nawab Fidai Khan. Explore the tiered gardens, fountains, and the mini-zoo.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at the heritage restaurant inside Pinjore Gardens or return to Panchkula for more dining options.

Day 6

Morni Hills Adventure

Morning: Drive to Morni Hills

After breakfast, drive to Morni Hills (approximately 1 hour from Panchkula), the only hill station in Haryana. Located in the Shivalik range, it offers beautiful views, trekking opportunities, and two natural lakes.

Activity: Trek to the Morni Fort, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Afternoon: Twin Lakes

Visit the twin lakes of Morni, a unique geographical phenomenon where two lakes exist on the same hill but maintain different water levels. Enjoy boating if available.

Lunch: Have a picnic lunch by the lakes or eat at the Haryana Tourism resort.

Evening: Nature & Wildlife

Explore the Morni Hills Wildlife Sanctuary which is home to various species of birds and animals. Return to Panchkula by evening.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a restaurant in Panchkula or Chandigarh's famous Sector 17 market area.

Morni Hills Landscape

Twin Lakes of Morni

About Morni Hills

Morni Hills is the only hill station in Haryana, situated at an altitude of 1,220 meters above sea level. The name 'Morni' is believed to have come from a queen who once ruled the area. The region is known for its lush greenery, cool climate, and the unique twin lakes that are connected by an underground channel despite having different water levels.

Elevation: 1,220 meters (4,003 ft)
Great for trekking and nature walks
Rich biodiversity and birdwatching opportunities
Day 7

Return to Gurugram & Departure

Morning: Chandigarh Visit

Before heading back, take a quick tour of Chandigarh, the well-planned city adjacent to Panchkula. Visit the famous Rock Garden, created by artist Nek Chand using industrial and urban waste.

Also visit: Sukhna Lake for a peaceful morning walk or boat ride.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch in Chandigarh, begin your return journey to Gurugram (approximately 4 hours drive).

En route: Stop at Murthal, famous for its dhabas (roadside eateries) serving delicious North Indian food, especially parathas.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

Reach Gurugram by evening. If time permits, do some last-minute shopping at DLF Galleria or MG Road markets.

Departure: Transfer to Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: The winter months offer pleasant weather for sightseeing
  • February: Ideal for attending the Surajkund International Crafts Mela
  • July to September: Monsoon season makes Morni Hills lush and beautiful
  • Avoid summer months (April to June) as temperatures can soar above 45°C

Transportation

  • Air: Indira Gandhi International Airport (Delhi) is the nearest major airport
  • Train: Well-connected railway network with stations in Gurugram, Faridabad, and Kurukshetra
  • Road: Excellent highway connectivity throughout the state
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs, auto-rickshaws, and local buses
  • Consider hiring a car with driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for summer; warm layers for winter
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration; hiking boots for Morni Hills
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Modest clothing: For temple visits in Kurukshetra

Food & Cuisine

Haryana offers a rich culinary experience with hearty, rustic flavors:

Traditional Dishes
  • Bajra Khichdi (pearl millet porridge)
  • Kadhi Pakora (gram flour dumplings in yogurt gravy)
  • Bathua Raita (chenopodium yogurt)
  • Methi Gajar (fenugreek and carrot dish)
  • Kachri Ki Sabzi (wild melon curry)
  • Singri Ki Sabzi (desert bean pods curry)
Breads & Sweets
  • Bajra Roti (pearl millet flatbread)
  • Besan Masala Roti (spiced gram flour bread)
  • Churma (crushed wheat sweet)
  • Malpua (sweet pancakes)
  • Kheer (rice pudding)
  • Ghevar (disc-shaped sweet cake)

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi and Haryanvi are the primary languages. English is understood in urban areas and tourist spots
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are widely available in cities and towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in urban areas; limited in rural regions
  • Safety: Generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Dress Code: Conservative in rural areas and religious sites; modern attire is acceptable in urban centers
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
  • Festivals: Teej, Lohri, Baisakhi, and Gugga Naumi are major festivals worth experiencing
