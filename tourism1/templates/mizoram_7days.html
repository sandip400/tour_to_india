Mizoram: The Land of Rolling Hills

Mizoram, the 'Land of the Hill People,' offers mesmerizing landscapes, vibrant tribal culture, and unexplored natural beauty. From the bustling streets of Aizawl to the serene vistas of Champhai, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this northeastern Indian state has to offer.

Highlights of the Tour

Breathtaking hilltop views
Authentic Mizo cuisine
Pristine lakes and waterfalls
Traditional Mizo dance and music
Lush bamboo forests
Warm Mizo hospitality

Quick Facts

  • Duration: 7 Days
  • Starting Point: Aizawl
  • Ending Point: Aizawl
  • Languages: Mizo, English

Day 1

Arrival in Aizawl & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Lengpui Airport, located about 32 km from Aizawl city. Transfer to your hotel in central Aizawl. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Hotel Regency, Hotel Chief, or Aizawl Club for comfortable stays.

Afternoon: Lunch & City Center

Enjoy your first Mizo meal at a local restaurant. Visit Solomon's Temple, an impressive white structure that offers panoramic views of Aizawl city.

Lunch recommendation: Try Mizo classics like Bai (vegetable stew), Vawksa Rep (smoked pork), and Misa Mach Poora (grilled shrimp).

Evening: Local Market Exploration

Visit the vibrant Bara Bazar (Main Market) to experience local life and shop for handloom products, bamboo crafts, and traditional Mizo attire. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance showcasing traditional Mizo dances like Cheraw (bamboo dance).

Day 1 Tips
  • Aizawl is built on steep hills, so be prepared for uphill walks.
  • Local taxis are the best way to get around the city.
  • Most shops close on Sundays due to the predominant Christian influence.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
Day 2

Aizawl Cultural Experience

Morning: Mizoram State Museum

Start your day with a visit to the Mizoram State Museum, which houses a rich collection of tribal artifacts, traditional costumes, and historical items that showcase Mizo culture and heritage.

Activity: Learn about the fascinating history of the Mizo tribes and their evolution over the centuries.

Afternoon: KV Paradise & Durtlang Hills

Visit KV Paradise, a memorial built by a husband in memory of his wife. Continue to Durtlang Hills for breathtaking views of Aizawl city and the surrounding valleys.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Durtlang Hills.

Evening: Handicraft Center & Local Cuisine

Visit the Mizoram Handloom & Handicraft Development Corporation showroom to see and purchase authentic Mizo handicrafts. Enjoy a traditional Mizo dinner.

Dinner: Try specialties like Sawhchiar (rice porridge with pork), Chhum Han (steamed vegetables), and Lu (a traditional herbal soup).

Day 3

Reiek Peak Day Trip

Morning: Drive to Reiek

After breakfast, drive to Reiek village, approximately 30 km from Aizawl. Reiek Peak is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Mizoram, offering spectacular views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

Note: The journey takes about 1.5 hours due to winding mountain roads.

Afternoon: Reiek Peak Trek

Embark on a trek to Reiek Peak (about 1-2 hours depending on your pace). The trail passes through tropical forests and offers stunning vistas. Enjoy a packed lunch at the peak while taking in the breathtaking panoramic views.

Lunch: Carry a packed lunch or enjoy a meal at the Tourist Resort.

Evening: Reiek Tourist Resort

Visit the Reiek Tourist Resort, which has a model Mizo village showcasing traditional Mizo houses and lifestyle. Return to Aizawl by evening.

Optional: If you prefer, you can stay overnight at the Reiek Tourist Resort to experience rural Mizoram.

Panoramic View from Reiek Peak

Important Information
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes and lightweight clothing.
  • Carry water bottles, snacks, insect repellent, and sunscreen.
  • The trek is of moderate difficulty and suitable for most fitness levels.
  • Photography is allowed but respect local customs.
  • The best time to visit is during clear weather for optimal views.
Flora & Fauna

Reiek Peak is home to diverse plant species including orchids and wild flowers. You might spot birds like the Peregrine Falcon, Mrs. Hume's Pheasant, and Blyth's Tragopan if you're lucky. Keep your eyes open for small mammals that inhabit these forests.

Day 4

Journey to Champhai

Morning: Drive to Champhai

After an early breakfast, embark on a scenic drive to Champhai, known as the 'Rice Bowl of Mizoram,' located near the Myanmar border. The journey takes approximately 6-7 hours through winding mountain roads and picturesque landscapes.

Note: Make several stops along the way to take in the beautiful scenery and stretch your legs.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Reach Champhai by afternoon. Check into your hotel and take some time to rest after the long journey. Have a late lunch at a local restaurant.

Accommodation: Hotel Champhai, The Grand Hotel Champhai, or a local homestay for an authentic experience.

Evening: Champhai Town Exploration

Take a leisurely stroll around Champhai town. Visit the local market and interact with the friendly locals. Enjoy the spectacular sunset views over the valleys.

Dinner: Try local Champhai specialties at a restaurant in town.

About Champhai

Champhai is a picturesque town near the Myanmar border known for its vast stretches of paddy fields, vineyards, and stunning natural beauty. The town serves as a major trading center between India and Myanmar and is home to fascinating historical sites including ancient menhirs (megalithic monuments).

Elevation: 1,678 meters (5,505 ft)
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Famous for vineyards and local wine production
Day 5

Champhai Exploration

Morning: Mura's Grave & Vangchhia

Visit Mura's Grave, a historical site related to a legendary Mizo princess. Continue to Vangchhia, an archaeological site with ancient megalithic monuments (menhirs) that date back several centuries.

Note: These sites provide fascinating insights into the ancient history and culture of the region.

Afternoon: Zokhawthar & Vineyard Visit

Visit Zokhawthar, the border town between India and Myanmar. Return via a local vineyard where you can learn about Champhai's growing wine industry and sample some local wines.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local eatery in Zokhawthar or carry a packed lunch.

Evening: Cultural Experience

Enjoy a cultural evening with traditional Mizo music and dance performances (if available). Interact with locals to learn more about their customs and traditions.

Dinner: Experience a traditional Mizo feast with dishes like Sanpiau (rice porridge with meat), Sarep (dried meat), and various bamboo shoot preparations.

Day 6

Champhai to Thenzawl

Morning: Journey to Thenzawl

After breakfast, begin your journey to Thenzawl, known for its beautiful handicrafts and waterfalls. The drive takes approximately 5-6 hours through scenic routes.

Note: Make stops at viewpoints along the way to capture the stunning landscapes.

Afternoon: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive in Thenzawl by afternoon. Check into your accommodation and refresh after the long journey.

Accommodation: Tourist Lodge Thenzawl or a local homestay.

Evening: Vantawng Falls

Visit Vantawng Falls, one of the highest waterfalls in Mizoram. Enjoy the natural beauty of the area and take a relaxing walk around the surroundings.

Dinner: Return to Thenzawl town for dinner at a local restaurant.

About Thenzawl

Thenzawl is a small town famous for its handloom industry and beautiful natural sites. It's a center for Mizo traditional textiles, particularly the Puanchei (traditional Mizo cloth) and other handwoven fabrics. The town is surrounded by hills and forests, offering plenty of opportunities for nature lovers.

Known for traditional Mizo handloom products
Home to Mizoram's first golf course
Gateway to beautiful waterfalls
Day 7

Thenzawl to Aizawl & Departure

Morning: Handloom Center

Visit the Handloom Center in Thenzawl to see weavers creating traditional Mizo textiles. This is an excellent opportunity to purchase authentic Mizo handloom products as souvenirs.

Activity: You might even get a chance to try weaving under the guidance of skilled artisans.

Afternoon: Return to Aizawl

After lunch, begin your journey back to Aizawl (approximately 3-4 hours). If time permits, make a stop at Tamdil Lake, a beautiful natural lake surrounded by lush forests.

Note: The road back to Aizawl offers scenic views of rolling hills and valleys.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Aizawl. The local markets offer a variety of handicrafts, bamboo products, and traditional Mizo attire.

Departure: Transfer to Lengpui Airport for your onward journey, or extend your stay in Mizoram if your schedule allows.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Perfect weather with clear skies and comfortable temperatures
  • March to May: Spring season with blooming flowers and pleasant climate
  • Avoid June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rainfall that can cause landslides
  • December: Experience the vibrant Chapchar Kut festival (spring festival) if you visit in early March

Transportation

  • Air: Lengpui Airport (near Aizawl) is connected to Kolkata, Guwahati, and Imphal
  • Road: Connected to neighboring states via NH-54. Shared taxis and buses available
  • Local Transport: Taxis are the primary mode of transport in cities
  • Rental Cars: Consider hiring a driver with a vehicle for your trip as mountain roads can be challenging

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light to medium warm clothes (layers), wind cheater/jacket
  • Footwear: Comfortable trekking shoes or boots with good grip
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit, altitude sickness medicine
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Documents: ID proof is required for Indian nationals; ILP (Inner Line Permit) for non-Mizos

Food & Cuisine

Mizoram offers a unique culinary experience with simple yet flavorful dishes:

Popular Dishes
  • Bai (vegetable stew)
  • Vawksa Rep (smoked pork)
  • Sa-um (fermented pork)
  • Chhum Han (steamed vegetables)
  • Misa Mach Poora (grilled shrimp)
  • Sawhchiar (rice porridge with pork)
Beverages & Snacks
  • Zu (local rice beer)
  • Champhai wine
  • Koat-pitha (rice pancakes)
  • Sanpiau (rice porridge with meat)
  • Bekang-um (fermented soybean)
  • Lu (traditional herbal soup)

Practical Information

  • Language: Mizo is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs available in Aizawl and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • ILP Requirement: Non-Mizos (including other Indian nationals) require an Inner Line Permit
  • Culture: Mizoram has a strong Christian influence. Most shops and establishments remain closed on Sundays
  • Safety: Mizoram is one of the safest states in India for tourists
  • Alcohol: Mizoram follows a partial prohibition policy; alcohol is available at licensed outlets in Aizawl
