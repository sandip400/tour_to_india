Day 1

Arrival in Leh & Acclimatization

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh. Transfer to your hotel in Leh city. Take ample rest to acclimatize to the high altitude (3,500 meters/11,500 feet).

Accommodation: The Grand Dragon Ladakh or Hotel Ladakh Palace for luxury stays; Hotel Omasila or Hotel Singge Palace for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Rest & Hydration

Continue resting and acclimatizing. Drink plenty of water and avoid physical exertion. You may experience mild altitude sickness symptoms which should subside with rest.

Health tip: Avoid alcohol and smoking. Eat light meals and stay hydrated with water and warm beverages like ginger tea.

Evening: Gentle Stroll

Take a gentle stroll around your hotel area if you're feeling up to it. Visit the Leh Market area for a brief introduction to local life. Return to your hotel for an early dinner and rest.

Evening activity: Enjoy a traditional Ladakhi dinner at your hotel or a nearby restaurant like Tibetan Kitchen or Lamayuru Restaurant.

Leh City View

Day 1 Tips
  • Acclimatization is crucial - don't rush or exert yourself.
  • Drink at least 3-4 liters of water throughout the day.
  • Apply sunscreen as UV radiation is intense at high altitudes.
  • Keep medications for altitude sickness handy (consult your doctor before the trip).
Day 2

Leh Local Sightseeing

Morning: Leh Palace & Shanti Stupa

After breakfast, visit the historic Leh Palace, a former royal residence built in the 17th century. Continue to Shanti Stupa, a white-domed Buddhist stupa offering panoramic views of Leh and surrounding mountains.

Activity: Take photographs of the stunning landscape from Shanti Stupa viewpoint.

Afternoon: Monastery Tour

Visit Spituk Monastery, located on a hilltop overlooking the Indus River. Continue to Thiksey Monastery, often compared to the Potala Palace in Tibet, known for its 12-story building complex and large Maitreya Buddha statue.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Thiksey village or carry packed lunch.

Evening: Hemis Monastery & Return

Visit Hemis Monastery, the largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh, famous for its annual Hemis Festival. Return to Leh by evening. Explore the Leh Main Market for souvenirs and local handicrafts.

Dinner: Try Ladakhi cuisine at Tibetan Kitchen or Summer Harvest restaurant in Leh.

Day 3

Leh to Nubra Valley via Khardung La

Morning: Drive to Khardung La

After breakfast, depart for Nubra Valley. Drive through the spectacular Khardung La Pass (5,359 meters/17,582 feet), one of the world's highest motorable passes. Enjoy breathtaking views and take photographs at the pass.

Note: Spend only 15-20 minutes at Khardung La due to the extreme altitude. Drink hot tea at the army-run cafÃ© to stay warm.

Afternoon: Descent to Nubra Valley

Continue your journey descending into the beautiful Nubra Valley, also known as the Valley of Flowers. Arrive at Diskit, the main town in Nubra Valley. Check into your accommodation.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Diskit or at your hotel.

Evening: Diskit Monastery

Visit the Diskit Monastery, home to a 32-meter tall Maitreya Buddha statue overlooking the valley. Enjoy the panoramic views of Nubra Valley from the monastery.

Accommodation: Desert Himalaya Resort, Nubra Organic Retreat, or similar hotels in Diskit or Hunder.

Khardung La Pass

Important Information
  • Carry your ID proof and Inner Line Permit (arranged by your tour operator).
  • The journey to Nubra Valley takes approximately 5-6 hours with stops.
  • Dress in layers as temperatures can vary significantly during the day.
  • Network connectivity is limited in Nubra Valley.
  • ATMs are scarce, so carry sufficient cash.
Nubra Valley

Nubra Valley is situated at the confluence of Shyok and Siachen Rivers. The valley was once part of the ancient Silk Route trading with Tibet. Today, it's known for its orchards, scenic beauty, Bactrian camels, and the contrasting landscape of sand dunes against snow-capped mountains.

Day 4

Nubra Valley Exploration

Morning: Hunder Sand Dunes

After breakfast, visit the unique Hunder Sand Dunes, a cold desert surrounded by mountains. Enjoy a Bactrian camel safari on the double-humped camels, a rare species found in this region.

Activity: Photography at the sand dunes with the backdrop of snow-capped mountains.

Afternoon: Yarab Tso & Panamik

Visit Yarab Tso, a small sacred lake near Sumur village. Continue to Panamik, known for its hot springs with medicinal properties. You can take a dip in the hot springs to relax your muscles.

Lunch: Enjoy a picnic lunch or eat at a local restaurant in Panamik.

Evening: Sumur Village & Samstanling Monastery

Visit Sumur village and the Samstanling Monastery, founded in 1841 by Lama Tsultim Nima. Return to your accommodation in Diskit or Hunder for dinner and overnight stay.

Evening activity: Stargazing in the clear Ladakhi night sky.

About Bactrian Camels

The double-humped Bactrian camels in Nubra Valley are believed to be descendants of the camels that once traveled on the ancient Silk Route. These camels are well-adapted to the extreme cold of the region and are now primarily used for tourism purposes. A camel ride costs approximately â‚¹300-500 per person for a 15-20 minute ride.

Day 5

Nubra Valley to Pangong Lake

Morning: Drive to Shyok Village

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and start your journey towards Pangong Lake. Take the direct route via Shyok village, which offers stunning views of the Shyok River and surrounding mountains.

Note: This route is weather-dependent and may be closed during heavy rainfall. The alternative route is via Leh, which is longer.

Afternoon: Scenic Drive

Continue your scenic drive through dramatic landscapes, crossing small streams and rugged terrain. Stop at viewpoints along the way for photographs. Enjoy a packed lunch or stop at a local dhaba (roadside restaurant) if available.

Travel time: The journey from Nubra Valley to Pangong Lake takes approximately 6-7 hours with stops.

Evening: Arrival at Pangong Lake

Arrive at Pangong Lake by late afternoon. Marvel at the changing colors of this stunning high-altitude lake (4,350 meters/14,270 feet). Check into your accommodation near the lake.

Accommodation: Pangong Sarai, Pangong Inn, or similar camps/guesthouses. Note that accommodations here are basic due to the remote location.

Pangong Lake

Important Information
  • Pangong Lake is partially in India and partially in China.
  • The lake changes colors throughout the day - from azure to blue to turquoise.
  • Nights can be extremely cold, even in summer. Pack warm clothes.
  • There is no mobile network at Pangong Lake.
  • Accommodation options are limited and basic - mostly camps and homestays.
Famous Location

Pangong Lake gained popularity after being featured in several Bollywood movies, most notably the climax scene of "3 Idiots." The lake is approximately 134 km long and extends from India to China. Only about 40% of the lake is in India. The brackish water of the lake remains unfrozen during summer, offering spectacular reflections of the surrounding mountains.

Day 6

Pangong Lake to Tso Moriri

Morning: Pangong Lake Sunrise

Wake up early to witness the magical sunrise over Pangong Lake. The changing colors of the lake during sunrise are truly mesmerizing. After breakfast, check out from your accommodation.

Activity: Morning walk along the lake shore and photography.

Afternoon: Drive to Tso Moriri

Begin your journey to Tso Moriri Lake via Chushul and Tsaga La. This remote route offers spectacular views of untouched landscapes and passes through high-altitude plains.

Lunch: Packed lunch or stop at a basic eatery in one of the small villages en route.

Evening: Arrival at Tso Moriri

Arrive at Tso Moriri Lake (4,522 meters/14,836 feet), a high-altitude lake in the Changthang region. Check into your accommodation in Korzok village, the only settlement near the lake.

Accommodation: Tso Moriri Camp, Nomadic Life Camp, or a homestay in Korzok village.

Day 7

Tso Moriri to Leh & Departure

Morning: Tso Moriri Exploration

Enjoy the serene beauty of Tso Moriri Lake in the morning light. Visit the Korzok Monastery, perched on a hill overlooking the lake. Look out for wildlife including bar-headed geese, Brahmin ducks, and if you're lucky, the elusive Tibetan wolves or snow leopards.

Activity: Bird watching and photography around the lake.

Afternoon: Return to Leh

After breakfast, begin your return journey to Leh via Chumathang, known for its hot springs. The route passes through the beautiful More Plains and Tanglang La Pass (5,328 meters/17,480 feet), the second highest motorable pass in Ladakh.

Lunch: Stop for lunch at Chumathang or Upshi.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

Arrive in Leh by evening. If time permits, do some last-minute shopping at Leh Market for souvenirs like Pashmina shawls, prayer wheels, Thangka paintings, and Ladakhi handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport for your departure flight, or extend your stay in Leh if desired.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Summer (May to September): Ideal time with pleasant weather and all routes open
  • Winter (October to April): Extremely cold with many routes closed due to snow
  • Peak Season: July and August (book accommodations in advance)
  • Festivals: Plan your visit during Hemis Festival (June/July) or Ladakh Festival (September) for cultural experiences

Transportation

  • Air: Direct flights to Leh from Delhi, Mumbai, Srinagar, and Chandigarh
  • Road: Manali-Leh Highway (473 km) and Srinagar-Leh Highway (434 km) open only in summer
  • Local Transport: Shared taxis, private taxis, and motorcycle rentals available in Leh
  • Recommendation: Hire a local driver with a 4x4 vehicle for the entire trip

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers, thermal wear, windproof jacket, warm hat, gloves
  • Footwear: Sturdy hiking boots, comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun Protection: Sunglasses (UV protected), sunscreen (SPF 50+), lip balm, hat
  • Medication: Altitude sickness pills (consult doctor), personal medications, first aid kit
  • Others: Water bottle, power bank, camera, torch, moisturizer, wet wipes

Food & Cuisine

Ladakh offers a unique culinary experience influenced by Tibetan and Central Asian cuisines:

Local Specialties
  • Thukpa (noodle soup)
  • Momos (dumplings)
  • Skyu (pasta with vegetables)
  • Chhutagi (pasta soup)
  • Butter tea (salty tea with yak butter)
  • Chang (local barley beer)
Dining Options
  • Tibetan restaurants in Leh
  • German bakeries for breakfast
  • Indian and international cuisine in Leh
  • Basic food options in remote areas
  • Homestay meals for authentic experience
  • Carry energy bars and snacks for long drives

Practical Information

  • Altitude Sickness: Spend at least 24 hours acclimatizing in Leh before any excursions
  • Permits: Inner Line Permits required for Nubra Valley, Pangong Lake, and Tso Moriri (arrange through your hotel or tour operator)
  • Currency: Limited ATMs in Leh city only, carry sufficient cash for remote areas
  • Electricity: Power cuts are common, carry a power bank
  • Internet: Limited connectivity, prepaid SIM cards work only in Leh
  • Water: Drink only bottled or purified water
  • Respect Local Culture: Dress modestly when visiting monasteries, ask permission before photographing locals
  • Environmental Responsibility: Carry back all non-biodegradable waste, use water sparingly
