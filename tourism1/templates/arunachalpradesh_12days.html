Comprehensive Exploration of Arunachal Pradesh

A 12-day journey through pristine landscapes, ancient monasteries, and diverse tribal cultures

Arunachal Pradesh: Land of the Dawn-Lit Mountains

Arunachal Pradesh, India's easternmost state, offers breathtaking Himalayan landscapes, ancient Buddhist monasteries, and diverse tribal cultures. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you through both the western and central regions of this pristine frontier state, allowing you to experience its rich cultural tapestry and natural wonders.

Day 1

Guwahati to Bhalukpong

Morning: Arrival & Departure

Arrive at Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, Assam. Meet your driver and begin your journey to Bhalukpong, the entry point to Arunachal Pradesh (approximately 5-6 hours drive).

Note: Ensure you have your Inner Line Permit (ILP) ready before entering Arunachal Pradesh.

Afternoon: Scenic Drive & Lunch

Enjoy the scenic drive through the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas. Stop for lunch at a roadside restaurant in Tezpur or along the way. Cross the Assam-Arunachal border and enter the beautiful Kameng region.

Lunch recommendation: Try local Assamese dishes like fish curry, bamboo shoot fry, or duck meat curry.

Evening: Bhalukpong Arrival

Arrive in Bhalukpong, a small town on the banks of the Kameng River. Check into your accommodation. If time permits, take a short walk along the riverside or visit the local market.

Accommodation: Prashanti Cottage, River View Resort, or similar riverside accommodations.

Kameng River, Bhalukpong

Arunachal Pradesh Entry Gate

Day 1 Tips
  • Start early from Guwahati to reach Bhalukpong before sunset.
  • Keep your Inner Line Permit (ILP) handy for verification at the check post.
  • Carry sufficient cash as ATMs are limited in remote areas.
  • Bhalukpong is known for fishing - you can arrange for angling if interested.
Day 2

Bhalukpong to Dirang

Morning: Departure to Dirang

After breakfast, begin your journey to Dirang (approximately 5-6 hours). The road winds through dense forests, picturesque valleys, and small villages. Stop at Tipi Orchid Centre, home to over 500 species of orchids.

Activity: Explore the orchid center and learn about the diverse flora of the region.

Afternoon: Bomdila & Lunch

Continue your journey, passing through Bomdila. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Bomdila. The landscape gradually changes as you gain altitude, with pine forests giving way to rhododendron groves.

Lunch: Try Tibetan dishes like thukpa (noodle soup) and momos (dumplings) at Bomdila.

Evening: Dirang Arrival & Exploration

Arrive in Dirang, a charming valley town. Check into your accommodation. Visit the Dirang Dzong (fort), an ancient structure showcasing traditional Monpa architecture. If time permits, explore the hot springs nearby.

Accommodation: Pemaling Hotel, Hotel Samdup Khang, or similar.

Day 3

Dirang to Tawang

Early Morning: Journey to Sela Pass

Start early for the journey to Tawang (approximately 6-7 hours). The highlight of today's journey is crossing the Sela Pass at 13,700 feet, one of the highest motorable passes in the world. The pass is often covered in snow during winter months.

Note: Carry warm clothes as temperatures at Sela Pass can be very low, even in summer.

Afternoon: Sela Lake & Jaswant Garh

Visit the pristine Sela Lake near the pass. Continue your journey, stopping at Jaswant Garh, a war memorial dedicated to the brave soldier who single-handedly held back enemy troops during the 1962 Indo-China War.

Lunch: Packed lunch or at a small eatery along the way.

Evening: Arrival in Tawang

Arrive in Tawang by evening. Check into your accommodation and rest. If time and energy permit, take a short stroll around the town to acclimatize to the high altitude.

Accommodation: Hotel Tashi Ga-Tsel, Hotel Mon Valley View, or similar.

Sela Pass (13,700 ft)

Important Information
  • The journey to Tawang is long and winding - take motion sickness medication if needed.
  • Altitude sickness is common - drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol.
  • Road conditions can be challenging, especially during monsoon or winter.
  • Mobile network is limited beyond Dirang - inform family/friends beforehand.
  • Carry essential medicines, as medical facilities are basic in remote areas.
Altitude Advisory

Tawang is situated at approximately 10,000 feet above sea level. Take it easy on your first day to acclimatize properly. Symptoms of altitude sickness include headache, nausea, and shortness of breath. If symptoms persist, seek medical attention immediately.

Day 4

Tawang Exploration

Morning: Tawang Monastery

Visit the Tawang Monastery (Galden Namgyal Lhatse), the largest monastery in India and the second largest in the world after the Potala Palace in Lhasa, Tibet. Founded in 1680, this magnificent structure houses valuable scriptures, thangkas, and a 28-foot high golden Buddha statue.

Activity: Witness the morning prayers and interact with the monks to learn about Buddhist traditions.

Afternoon: War Memorial & Craft Centre

Visit the Tawang War Memorial, dedicated to the soldiers who sacrificed their lives during the 1962 Indo-China War. Continue to the Craft Centre to see local handicrafts and traditional Monpa art.

Lunch: Enjoy local Monpa cuisine at a restaurant in Tawang town.

Evening: Pangong Tso Lake

Visit the serene Pangong Tso Lake (also known as Madhuri Lake), which gained popularity after being featured in a Bollywood movie. Return to Tawang town for dinner and overnight stay.

Dinner: Try traditional Monpa dishes like zan (local bread), churpi (cheese), and thukpa (noodle soup).

Day 5

Tawang to Zemithang

Morning: Journey to Zemithang

After breakfast, drive to Zemithang (approximately 4-5 hours), a remote village near the Indo-Tibet border. The journey takes you through pristine landscapes and traditional Monpa villages.

Note: Special permits are required for Zemithang, which should be arranged in advance.

Afternoon: Y-Junction & Lunch

Stop at the scenic Y-Junction, where three rivers meet. Enjoy a packed lunch amidst beautiful surroundings. Continue your journey to Zemithang.

Scenic stops: Make brief stops at viewpoints along the way to capture the breathtaking landscapes.

Evening: Arrival in Zemithang

Arrive in Zemithang by evening. Check into your basic accommodation or homestay. Explore the village and interact with the friendly Monpa people to learn about their traditional way of life.

Accommodation: Basic guesthouses or homestays are available. Expect simple facilities in this remote area.

Zemithang Valley

Traditional Monpa Village

About Zemithang

Zemithang is a remote village in the Tawang district, located just 40 km from the Indo-Tibet border. It is home to the Monpa tribe and is known for its pristine beauty, traditional culture, and the ancient Gorsam Chorten (stupa), which is believed to be over 500 years old and is the second largest in Asia after the Boudhanath Stupa in Nepal.

Elevation: 8,500 feet (2,600 meters)
Predominantly inhabited by Monpa tribe
Home to the ancient Gorsam Chorten
Day 6

Zemithang to Tawang

Morning: Gorsam Chorten

Visit the Gorsam Chorten, an ancient Buddhist stupa believed to be over 500 years old. It is the second largest stupa in Asia after the Boudhanath Stupa in Nepal. Explore the surrounding area and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.

Activity: Circumambulate the stupa as per Buddhist tradition and spin the prayer wheels.

Afternoon: Village Exploration & Return

Spend some time exploring the traditional Monpa villages around Zemithang. After lunch, begin your return journey to Tawang (approximately 4-5 hours).

Cultural experience: Interact with locals and learn about their traditional lifestyle, agriculture, and handicrafts.

Evening: Return to Tawang

Arrive back in Tawang by evening. Check into your accommodation and rest after the day's journey. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant in Tawang town.

Accommodation: Return to your previous hotel in Tawang or similar accommodation.

Day 7

Tawang to Bomdila

Morning: Departure to Bomdila

After breakfast, begin your return journey to Bomdila (approximately 6-7 hours). Retrace your route through the magnificent Sela Pass, enjoying different perspectives of the stunning landscapes.

Stop: Take a short break at Nuranang Waterfall (also known as Jang Falls), a beautiful 100-meter high waterfall.

Afternoon: Journey Continues

Continue your journey, stopping for lunch at a suitable location. The descent from the high mountains offers changing vegetation and climate zones.

Lunch: Packed lunch or at a local eatery along the way.

Evening: Arrival in Bomdila

Arrive in Bomdila by evening. Check into your accommodation. Visit the Bomdila Monastery if time permits, known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful atmosphere.

Accommodation: Hotel Tsepal Yangjom, Hotel Elysium, or similar.

Nuranang Waterfall

Bomdila Monastery

About Bomdila

Bomdila is a picturesque town situated at an altitude of about 8,500 feet in the West Kameng district of Arunachal Pradesh. It offers panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges and is home to the Bomdila Monastery, which belongs to the Gelugpa sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

Elevation: 8,500 feet (2,600 meters)
Predominantly inhabited by Monpa tribe
Known for traditional handicrafts
Day 8

Bomdila to Ziro

Morning: Bomdila Exploration

Spend the morning exploring Bomdila. Visit the Upper Gompa, Middle Gompa, and Lower Gompa (monasteries) to understand the different aspects of Buddhist culture. Explore the local market for traditional Arunachali handicrafts.

Shopping: Look for traditional items like Thangkas (Buddhist paintings), wooden masks, carpets, and tribal jewelry.

Afternoon: Journey to Ziro

After lunch, begin your journey to Ziro (approximately 7-8 hours), a beautiful valley in the Lower Subansiri district known for its paddy fields and the unique Apatani tribe. The route takes you through changing landscapes and tribal areas.

Stops: Make brief stops at scenic viewpoints and small villages along the way.

Evening: Arrival in Ziro

Arrive in Ziro by evening. Check into your accommodation and rest after the long journey. If energy permits, take a short stroll around the area.

Accommodation: Ziro Valley Resort, Siiro Resort, or homestays for a more authentic experience.

Bomdila Market

Ziro Valley

About Ziro

Ziro is a picturesque valley located in the Lower Subansiri district of Arunachal Pradesh. It is home to the Apatani tribe, known for their unique culture, sustainable agricultural practices, and distinctive facial tattoos among older women. The valley is famous for its paddy-cum-fish cultivation method and has been proposed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Elevation: 5,500 feet (1,700 meters)
Home to the Apatani tribe
Venue for the annual Ziro Music Festival
Day 9

Ziro Exploration

Morning: Apatani Villages

Visit traditional Apatani villages like Hong, Hari, Bulla, Dutta, and Hija. Observe the unique architecture of their houses, built on stilts with bamboo and wood. Learn about their sustainable agricultural practices, including the famous paddy-cum-fish cultivation.

Cultural experience: Interact with the Apatani people and learn about their traditions, language, and lifestyle.

Afternoon: Lunch & Craft Centre

Enjoy a traditional Apatani lunch at a local home or restaurant. Visit the Craft Centre to see and purchase local handicrafts, including bamboo and cane products, traditional textiles, and jewelry.

Lunch recommendation: Try local dishes like pike pila (fermented bamboo shoot), chicken/pork with bamboo shoot, and apong (rice beer).

Evening: Paddy Fields & Sunset

Take a leisurely walk through the picturesque paddy fields that surround the villages. Enjoy the sunset over the valley, offering stunning views of the terraced fields and surrounding hills.

Photography: This is an excellent opportunity for landscape and cultural photography.

Day 10

Ziro to Daporijo

Morning: Departure to Daporijo

After breakfast, begin your journey to Daporijo (approximately 5-6 hours), a town in the Upper Subansiri district and home to the Tagin tribe. The route takes you through beautiful landscapes, dense forests, and tribal villages.

Note: The road conditions can be challenging, especially during or after the monsoon season.

Afternoon: Scenic Drive & Lunch

Continue your journey, stopping for lunch at a suitable location. Enjoy the changing landscapes and the Subansiri River views along the way.

Lunch: Packed lunch or at a local eatery along the way.

Evening: Arrival in Daporijo

Arrive in Daporijo by evening. Check into your accommodation. If time permits, take a short walk around the town to get a feel for the place.

Accommodation: Hotel Singhik, Circuit House, or similar (accommodation options are limited in Daporijo).

Subansiri River

Daporijo Town

About Daporijo

Daporijo is the headquarters of the Upper Subansiri district of Arunachal Pradesh. It is home to the Tagin tribe, known for their unique customs and traditions. The town is situated on the banks of the Subansiri River and serves as a gateway to explore the remote tribal areas of Upper Subansiri.

Elevation: 1,800 feet (550 meters)
Home to the Tagin tribe
Located on the banks of Subansiri River
Day 11

Daporijo to Itanagar

Morning: Tagin Village Visit

Visit a traditional Tagin village near Daporijo to learn about their unique culture, traditions, and lifestyle. The Tagin tribe is known for their distinctive jewelry, traditional attire, and animistic beliefs.

Cultural experience: Interact with the villagers and observe their traditional houses, agricultural practices, and daily activities.

Afternoon: Journey to Itanagar

After lunch, begin your journey to Itanagar, the capital city of Arunachal Pradesh (approximately 7-8 hours). The route takes you through changing landscapes, from mountains to foothills.

Stops: Make brief stops at scenic viewpoints and small villages along the way.

Evening: Arrival in Itanagar

Arrive in Itanagar by evening. Check into your accommodation and rest after the long journey. If energy permits, take a short stroll around the city center.

Accommodation: Hotel Arun Subansiri, Hotel Donyi Polo Ashok, or similar.

Day 12

Itanagar Exploration & Departure

Morning: Itanagar Sightseeing

Visit the Ita Fort, a 14th-century brick structure that gave the city its name. Continue to the Jawaharlal Nehru State Museum to learn about the diverse tribal cultures of Arunachal Pradesh through artifacts, textiles, and handicrafts.

Also visit: Ganga Lake (Gyakar Sinyi), a beautiful natural lake surrounded by forests.

Afternoon: Buddhist Temple & Lunch

Visit the Buddha Vihar, a beautiful Buddhist temple with a large Buddha statue. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant, trying some Arunachali specialties.

Lunch recommendation: Try local dishes like bamboo shoot pork, smoked meat, or fish curry with local herbs.

Evening: Departure to Guwahati

Begin your return journey to Guwahati (approximately 6-7 hours). Alternatively, you can stay overnight in Itanagar and depart the next morning, depending on your flight schedule.

Note: If departing today, ensure you have an evening or night flight from Guwahati, or plan for an overnight stay in Guwahati.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to April: Ideal time with clear skies and pleasant weather
  • March to May: Spring season with blooming rhododendrons
  • September to November: Post-monsoon with lush green landscapes
  • Avoid monsoon season (June to August) as landslides are common
  • Winter (December to February) can be extremely cold with snowfall at higher elevations

Transportation

  • Air: Nearest airport is in Guwahati, Assam. Limited flights to Tezpur and Lilabari
  • Road: Private taxi is the best option for the mountain roads
  • Shared Sumos: Available between major towns but have fixed departure times
  • Helicopter Service: Available between select locations, weather permitting
  • Roads can be challenging - experienced drivers recommended

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Layers are key - thermal innerwear, fleece jackets, windproof outer layer
  • Footwear: Sturdy, waterproof hiking shoes with good grip
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, gloves, scarves, sunscreen (high SPF)
  • Medication: Personal medications, altitude sickness pills, basic first aid
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, torch, universal adapter
  • Documents: Inner Line Permit, ID proof, travel insurance

Food & Cuisine

Arunachal Pradesh offers diverse tribal cuisines:

Local Specialties
  • Thukpa (noodle soup)
  • Momos (dumplings)
  • Apong (rice beer)
  • Bamboo shoot dishes
  • Smoked meat preparations
  • Pika Pila (fermented soybean)
Dining Tips
  • Most hotels offer both Indian and Chinese cuisine
  • Local eateries serve authentic tribal food
  • Carry some packaged snacks for long journeys
  • Try local dairy products like churpi (cheese)
  • Drink only bottled or purified water

Practical Information

  • Permits: Inner Line Permit (ILP) is mandatory for all visitors. Can be obtained online or from designated offices
  • Language: English and Hindi are widely understood. Each tribe has its own dialect
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs available in major towns but carry sufficient cash
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Power cuts are common - carry a power bank
  • Internet & Mobile: Limited connectivity in remote areas. BSNL has better coverage
  • Safety: Generally safe for tourists. Inform your hotel about your daily plans
  • Local Customs: Respect tribal customs and traditions. Ask permission before photographing people
