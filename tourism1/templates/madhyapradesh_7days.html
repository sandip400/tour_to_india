Madhya Pradesh: The Heart of Incredible India

Madhya Pradesh offers a perfect blend of historical monuments, ancient temples, wildlife sanctuaries, and breathtaking landscapes. From the bustling city of Bhopal to the serene hills of Pachmarhi, this 7-day itinerary covers the best of what this central Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Bhopal & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Raja Bhoj Airport, Bhopal. Transfer to your hotel in central Bhopal. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Jehan Numa Palace or Noor-Us-Sabah Palace for luxury stays; Lago Villa or Hotel Lake View for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & Upper Lake

Enjoy your first Madhya Pradesh meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Under The Mango Tree' or 'Manohar Dairy'. Visit the Upper Lake (Bhojtal), one of the oldest man-made lakes in India, and enjoy a boat ride.

Lunch recommendation: Try local specialties like Bhutte Ka Kees (spiced corn dish), Dal Bafla (baked wheat balls with lentils), and Malpua (sweet pancakes).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the Tribal Museum to learn about the indigenous cultures of Madhya Pradesh. Explore the New Market area for some shopping. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant in MP Nagar or DB City Mall.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance at Bharat Bhavan, a multi-arts complex and museum.

Upper Lake (Bhojtal)

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Tribal Museum is closed on Mondays, plan accordingly.
  • Boat rides at Upper Lake are available until sunset.
Day 2

Sanchi Stupa & Bhimbetka Caves

Morning: Sanchi Stupa

Start your day with a visit to Sanchi Stupa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest stone structures in India. Built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, it houses relics of Buddha and features beautiful carvings and monolithic pillars.

Activity: Explore the Great Stupa, monasteries, and temples with a local guide to understand the historical significance.

Afternoon: Lunch & Travel to Bhimbetka

Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant near Sanchi. Then drive to the Bhimbetka Rock Shelters, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, located about 45 km from Bhopal.

Note: The drive offers beautiful views of the Vindhya mountain range.

Evening: Bhimbetka Rock Shelters

Explore the Bhimbetka Rock Shelters, featuring prehistoric cave paintings dating back to approximately 10,000 BCE. These rock shelters showcase the earliest traces of human life in India.

Dinner: Return to Bhopal for dinner at 'Bapu Ki Kutia' or 'Wind and Waves' restaurant overlooking the Upper Lake.

Day 3

Bhopal to Pachmarhi

Morning: Taj-ul-Masajid & Gohar Mahal

Visit Taj-ul-Masajid, one of the largest mosques in India, known for its impressive architecture. Then explore Gohar Mahal, a fusion of Hindu and Mughal architecture built by the first woman ruler of Bhopal.

Note: Dress modestly when visiting the mosque. Women should cover their heads.

Afternoon: Journey to Pachmarhi

After lunch, begin your journey to Pachmarhi, the only hill station in Madhya Pradesh, located in the Satpura Range (approximately 200 km, 4-5 hours drive). Check into your hotel upon arrival.

Accommodation: Champak Bungalow, Rock End Manor, or Welcome Heritage Golf View for comfortable stays.

Evening: Pachmarhi Town

Take a leisurely stroll around Pachmarhi town, known as "Queen of Satpura". Visit the local market and Christ Church, a beautiful colonial-era church.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at your hotel or try local restaurants like 'Misty Meadows' or 'Highland Restaurant'.

Taj-ul-Masajid

Pachmarhi Hill Station

About Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi, also known as "Satpura ki Rani" (Queen of Satpura), is Madhya Pradesh's only hill station. Located at an elevation of 1,067 meters, it was discovered by Captain James Forsyth of the British Army in 1857. The hill station is known for its ancient caves, waterfalls, and colonial architecture.

Elevation: 1,067 meters (3,501 ft)
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Part of Satpura National Park biosphere
Day 4

Pachmarhi Exploration

Morning: Bee Falls & Pandava Caves

Start your day with a visit to Bee Falls, one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Pachmarhi. Then explore the Pandava Caves, believed to have been used by the Pandavas during their exile.

Activity: Take a refreshing dip in the natural pool at the base of Bee Falls (if permitted and weather allows).

Afternoon: Dhoopgarh

After lunch, visit Dhoopgarh, the highest point in the Satpura Range offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys. It's an ideal spot to catch the sunset.

Note: Carry water, snacks, and wear comfortable shoes as there's some walking involved.

Evening: Pachmarhi Lake

Visit Pachmarhi Lake for a peaceful boat ride. Enjoy the serene atmosphere and beautiful surroundings. Return to your hotel for dinner.

Optional: If time permits, visit the Pachmarhi Cantonment area to see the colonial-era buildings.

Day 5

Pachmarhi to Jabalpur

Morning: Jata Shankar Caves

Visit Jata Shankar Caves, a sacred Hindu shrine with natural rock formations resembling the matted locks of Lord Shiva. The cave also has a natural spring.

Also visit: Priyadarshini (Forsyth Point) for panoramic views of the valley if time permits.

Afternoon: Journey to Jabalpur

After lunch, check out from your hotel and begin your journey to Jabalpur (approximately 250 km, 5-6 hours drive). Enjoy the scenic drive through the Satpura Range.

Note: Make stops at viewpoints along the way for photographs and refreshments.

Evening: Arrival in Jabalpur

Arrive in Jabalpur by evening. Check into your hotel and rest after the long journey. If energy permits, take a short stroll around the nearby area.

Accommodation: WelcomHeritage Narmada Jacksons, Vijan Mahal, or Fortune Landmark for comfortable stays.

Jata Shankar Caves

Jabalpur City

About Jabalpur

Jabalpur is a major city in Madhya Pradesh located on the shores of the Narmada River. Known as the "Sanskardhani" (Cultural Capital) of Madhya Pradesh, it's famous for its marble rocks at Bhedaghat, Dhuandhar Falls, and ancient temples. The city has a rich history dating back to the 12th century.

Located on the banks of Narmada River
Rich historical and cultural heritage
Famous for marble rocks and waterfalls
Day 6

Jabalpur Exploration

Morning: Bhedaghat & Marble Rocks

Visit Bhedaghat to see the magnificent Marble Rocks that rise to a height of 100 feet on either side of the Narmada River. Take a boat ride through the gorge to experience the beauty up close.

Activity: Boat ride on the Narmada River between the marble cliffs. The boatmen also sing traditional songs during the ride.

Afternoon: Dhuandhar Falls

Visit the Dhuandhar Falls (Smoke Cascade), where the Narmada River narrows down and plunges into a waterfall creating a smoke-like spray. Explore the nearby market for marble artifacts.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a riverside restaurant with views of the marble rocks.

Evening: Chausath Yogini Temple

Visit the Chausath Yogini Temple, one of the oldest heritage temples in India dating back to the 10th century. Located on a hilltop, it offers panoramic views of the surroundings.

Dinner: Return to Jabalpur city for dinner at a local restaurant like 'Samdareeya' or 'Arihant' for authentic Malwa cuisine.

Marble Rocks at Bhedaghat

Dhuandhar Falls

Visitor Tips
  • The boat ride at Bhedaghat is most magical during full moon nights when the marble rocks glow in the moonlight.
  • Photography is allowed but be careful with your equipment during the boat ride.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as there's walking involved at the Chausath Yogini Temple.
  • The best time to visit Dhuandhar Falls is during or just after the monsoon season when the waterfall is at its full glory.
  • Bargain when shopping for marble artifacts in the local market.
Day 7

Jabalpur to Bhopal & Departure

Morning: Madan Mahal Fort

Visit the Madan Mahal Fort, perched on top of a rocky hill. Built by the Gond ruler Raja Madan Shah in 1116 AD, it offers panoramic views of Jabalpur city.

Also visit: Rani Durgavati Museum if time permits, which houses a collection of sculptures, inscriptions, and prehistoric relics.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, check out from your hotel and begin your journey back to Bhopal (approximately 325 km, 6-7 hours drive). Alternatively, you can take a train or a flight from Jabalpur to Bhopal.

Note: If flying, the Jabalpur-Bhopal flight takes about 1 hour.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Bhopal. New Market and DB City Mall are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Raja Bhoj Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Ideal time with pleasant weather
  • July to September: Monsoon season - good for waterfalls but some activities may be restricted
  • April to June: Summer can be extremely hot in most parts except hill stations like Pachmarhi
  • Plan your visit around festivals like Khajuraho Dance Festival (February) or Tansen Music Festival (December) for a cultural experience

Transportation

  • Air: Major airports at Bhopal, Indore, Jabalpur, and Khajuraho
  • Train: Extensive rail network connecting major cities
  • Road: Well-connected highways but long distances between attractions
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in cities; local buses and auto-rickshaws available
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire trip for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for summer; warm layers for winter and hill stations
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

Madhya Pradesh offers a diverse culinary experience:

Malwa Region
  • Poha Jalebi (flattened rice with sweet jalebi)
  • Dal Bafla (baked wheat balls with lentils)
  • Bhuttey Ka Kees (spiced grated corn)
  • Malpua (sweet pancakes)
  • Chakki ki Shaak (wheat flour dumplings)
Bundelkhand Region
  • Kadhi Kachori
  • Rogan Josh (meat curry)
  • Arsa (sweet dish)
  • Paan (betel leaf preparation)
  • Gajak (sesame sweet)

Practical Information

  • Language: Hindi is the primary language. English is understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: Madhya Pradesh is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
